# EP001 style prompt contract

## Goal
Push EP001 away from grounded semi-realism and toward a mainstream Korean teen webtoon look while preserving the existing protagonist identity and exam-drama setting cues.

## Shared style lock
Use this language in every panel prompt:

`mainstream Korean teen webtoon aesthetic, clean Naver-style school drama lineart, polished digital manhwa illustration, stylized not photorealistic, youthful late-teen face proportions, soft cel shading, semi-flat clean colors, smooth even pale skin, grouped hair clumps instead of realistic strands, simplified Korean school or study-room background, mobile-readable vertical webtoon composition`

## Character lock
Keep the existing identity from `character_anchor.md`:

`same protagonist design in every panel, pale slim Korean male student, messy medium-length black layered hair with long bangs covering the eyebrows, narrow tired almond eyes with visible dark circles, small sharp face with pointed jawline, thin lips, dark plain sweatshirt or faded black hoodie, narrow shoulders, thin neck, quiet depressed intensity`

## Style cues to emphasize
- clean medium-thin outlines with slightly stronger outer silhouette
- youthful school-drama face design, not adult prestige-drama anatomy
- soft V-line to pointed jaw carryover from the anchor, without turning pretty-idol
- restrained soft blush or cool under-eye tint only when helpful
- selective prop detail for exam sheets, notebooks, prep books, phone screen, wall calendar
- negative space and readable acting at phone-screen size

## Negatives to repeat
Use strong anti-realism negatives in every panel:

`no photorealism, no live-action look, no cinematic prestige-drama lighting, no skin pores, no painterly skin texture, no realistic hair strands, no glossy idol styling, no mature adult facial structure, no broad shoulders, no thick neck, no gritty noir manhwa rendering, no western comic style, no 3D render, no watermark, no logo, no speech bubbles generated by the model`

## Setting guardrails
- Keep Korean exam-drama props and interiors grounded: mock exam papers, prep books, desk clutter, phone alarm screen, wall D-day calendar, apartment doorway, mirror, notebook.
- Backgrounds should be believable but simplified, never hyper-detailed.
- Use vertical webtoon framing with clean focal hierarchy before atmospheric detail.

## Panel writing rule
Each panel prompt should contain:
1. the shared style lock
2. the shared character lock if the protagonist is visible
3. one concise panel-specific action block
4. the shared anti-realism negatives

## Drift to reject
- protagonist becoming broad-shouldered, healthier, cleaner, or more adult
- eyes becoming round, oversized, or glossy-anime cute
- rendering slipping into photo-drama, cinematic poster art, or painterly realism
- hair becoming individually stranded, gelled, or idol-styled
- backgrounds stealing attention from the emotional beat
