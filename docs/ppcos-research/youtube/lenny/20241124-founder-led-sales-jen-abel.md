## Key Insights

### Why Founder-Led Sales Matters
- The founder IS the product in early stages - you have novel insight the market hasn't seen
- No one can speak to the vision like the founder can
- Founders see "budding moments" in conversations that salespeople miss
- Your day-one vision is never the same as what gets you to product-market fit
- Founder-led sales is about LEARNING, not revenue on day one

### The Sales Cycle Steps
1. **Finding leads** - Start with 30 people you're excited to learn from
2. **First call** - Be vulnerable, don't demo yet, understand their problem
3. **Second call** - Demo (leave them wanting more)
4. **Third call** - Walk through proposal/scope of work
5. **Fourth call** - Co-author the scope with them
6. **Fifth call** - Introduction to procurement
7. **Final** - Get signature (know who the actual signatory is)

### Outreach That Works
- **Relevancy over personalization** - Why are you reaching out to ME?
- **Counterintuitive insight** - Make them say "what?" or "how could that be?"
- **Focus on the problem** - Don't talk about your solution
- **Keep it short** - 3-4 sentences max, no scrolling on mobile
- **Avoid "better"** - Different beats better every time

### First Call Tactics
- **Be vulnerable**: "I'm an early stage startup, we have a lot to learn"
- **Don't show the demo** - The demo is your only carrot, save it
- **Ask about the problem**: How is this manifesting? Are you measuring it? Have you tried to solve it?
- **Look for buying signals**: They want to bring their boss to next call

### The Services Secret (40-50% of B2B startups need this)
- If they don't have a process to solve the problem, they can't buy technology yet
- Sell a 90-day service to educate them and set the stage
- You get: Logo, revenue, Intel, reference
- "Getting paid to educate them" - this is where the power lives
- Once you're in Enterprise, you're IN - it compounds

### Signs You're On To Something
1. Growing/widening problem (not fixed)
2. They're measuring or managing it
3. They've tried to solve it before
4. They bring other colleagues to calls
5. They want to co-author the solution with you

### Qualification is Everything
- "Everyone thinks they have a bottom of funnel problem. It's ALWAYS a top of funnel problem."
- Low conversion usually means: Wrong person, wrong message, problem not felt, or not different enough

### Enterprise Sales Reality
- 500+ employees, $50-200k initial contracts
- 6-12 months typical timeline
- Make procurement's job easy - fill out their forms for them
- Know who signs before you get there (CFO, legal, procurement, business unit?)
- Once you're in, you're IN - deals compound year over year

### Mindset Shifts
- Sales should feel FUN for the buyer
- Trust is the #1 currency
- Be honest about what you can/can't solve - they'll sell YOU on why they need it
- Don't negotiate with yourself on discounts
- Project manage the deal - keep calls tight, don't elongate
