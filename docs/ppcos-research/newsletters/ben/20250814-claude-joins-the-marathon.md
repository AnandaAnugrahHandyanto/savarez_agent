Gemini has had long context for a while, and GPT-4.1 joined it earlier this year. Sonnet 4 is Claude’s entry in the race.
Gemini is getting two new features [ https://substack.com/redirect/c6179d64-4001-4881-a4b5-1d843e2d4c95?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]: personal context (i.e. memory) and temporary chats. Personal Context has two parts: referencing past chats and saved bits of memory. You can turn off each part individually. Keshav uses Gemini a lot, his pov:
Gemini already has both of these, so this is likely just a rebrand of these features. But Gemini does it slightly different than ChatGPT. Instead of automatically saving info, Gemini saves it when you ask it to. Similarly, it searches for past chats when you ask it, instead of mysterious references like ChatGPT (which sometimes work, sometimes don’t). I still have the “saved bits” off in Gemini but I often ask it “remember we talked about this”.
— Keshav
Temporary chats are just one-off chats that don’t get saved in your chat history, like an incognito tab.
