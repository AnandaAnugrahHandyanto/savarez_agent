Hi, it’s Azeem with our weekend email.
The DeepSeek moment is becoming more than a moment. The nimble startup is challenging established AI labs with competitive models at a fraction of the cost and sending ripples across the industry. Meanwhile, the idea that liberalism must build to survive is going mainstream. Both stories highlight the inflection point that we’re here to understand: how we respond to technological and societal challenges will define our future. Thanks for reading!
Abundance
Ezra Klein  and Derek Thompson ’s new book is making the rounds. In Abundance [ https://substack.com/redirect/40b011e2-df68-47ac-ace4-9dbd08f62704?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], they issue a provocation to liberalism: if it can’t build, it can’t endure. Despite the immense wealth and technological prowess of the US, scarcity—of housing, energy, infrastructure and even opportunity—has become the defining constraint of its politics and future. This scarcity has fuelled zero-sum, illiberal populism but also eroded the liberal project from within.
In the words of Noah Smith  reviewing the book, “liberalism has forgotten how to build the things that people want”.
I’m writing this in the same week that saw the busiest airport in Europe, Heathrow, shut down because it relied on a single source of power. And when that power was no longer available, everything had to stop. What happened at Heathrow is a symptom of the scarcity mindset baked into modern systems [ https://substack.com/redirect/b55e54a3-27d2-4eb3-9590-b686ce0c2774?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] – scarcity of permission, initiative and ownership.
My view is that technology enables building. Technology is, after all, things getting cheaper; it is about reducing the resource cost of creating. But it’s only a catalyst. The necessary condition is the mindset.
See also, Packy McCormick  joined me on Friday to discuss the renewed culture of building [ https://substack.com/redirect/670c50d1-2213-44d9-8ac0-eca5f115a5c0?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]—across energy, infrastructure, AI and defense—and why we believe this can unlock an abundant future.
Sleepless nights
“I think Sam Altman is probably not sleeping well,” Kai-Fu Lee said a couple of days ago commenting on the threat [ https://substack.com/redirect/3d6369c9-5971-43b5-af87-3c72aab801af?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] DeepSeek presents to OpenAI. As a reminder, DeepSeek is delivering competitive models at a tiny portion of the spend compared to OpenAI and other major US labs (read our deep dive [ https://substack.com/redirect/54762c0d-860f-4f23-a4aa-2362d50b286c?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] for everything you need to know). And it’s causing ripple effects. Tencent is slowing down the pace of GPU roll-out [ https://substack.com/redirect/e5b37671-9dbf-49c1-bd0b-bd52fbb980ec?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] because of DeepSeek. Oil giant Saudi Aramco [ https://substack.com/redirect/784787fe-48ea-4066-819f-33f70ea25822?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]’s chief shared that DeepSeek is “really making a big difference” internally and companies across China [ https://substack.com/redirect/92eda94c-3d90-472e-9b17-9ff9648aaf2b?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] are steadily integrating its foundation models.
DeepSeek seems to have managed to create a research environment where the lab can out-innovate more heavily financed competitors. It only has160 employees, compared to over 2,000 at OpenAI and it is unburdened [ https://substack.com/redirect/516f4f94-e2d0-4c3f-80ef-ac4d79a246d4?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] by the strict commercial or investor pressures that typically demand rapid productization. DeepSeek’s billionaire founder, Liang Wenfeng, bought thousands of high-end chips ahead of export restrictions and deliberately limits commercial activities. He’s content with breaking even rather than chasing growth.
Meanwhile, OpenAI has pivoted towards product [ https://substack.com/redirect/54b251a9-3d57-4ed0-a576-ea538930cf17?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], as it hunts for a defensible moat and profitable market position. And the pressures are growing… Open-source may be the foundational kernel of building AI services. After all, the very successful Mac operating system is building on the open-source Linux kernel. And if that wasn’t enough, there’s the reality of end-to-end reinforcement learning [ https://substack.com/redirect/facf8a84-8094-438d-86a0-8619b081ad94?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] blurring the boundary between model and the product [ https://substack.com/redirect/14b53f28-ecf3-4ea0-9a16-0e402fba61dd?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] – the model is the product.
See also, what you should know about new models and AI development this week:
Models can improve not just by growing bigger or thinking longer, but also through search—generating multiple potential answers and selecting the best one. In an impressive demonstration [ https://substack.com/redirect/b9537c30-97ed-4d46-a1d0-45de8ee09cbd?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], Gemini 1.5 matched the specialized reasoning model o1’s performance by creating and evaluating 200 responses to a single query. Models excel at identifying their own flawed reasoning when comparing multiple outputs side by side.
Researchers in another part of Google suggest a scaling law for distributing training over multiple data centers [ https://substack.com/redirect/d820e74b-22f2-4781-b68f-14cb83bcb797?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. This could spell trouble for policymakers as control of large compute clusters was seen as one of the best methods of regulating AI [ https://substack.com/redirect/00e68375-5e43-491b-8ba3-a788d7807434?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
Another academic group found that different tasks—factual knowledge versus coding, for instance—require different scaling strategies [ https://substack.com/redirect/cd0dd9d3-3773-42ed-9894-cd422b1417b2?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. Knowledge-based tasks, such as factual question answering, are “capacity-hungry” - they benefit more from increasing the model's size and parameter count. In contrast, coding tasks are "data-hungry" - they improve more from seeing additional training examples. For model developers, this means the optimal trade-off between model size and training data can vary by up to 50% depending on which skills they prioritize.

It also released an o1-Pro API, but at $250 per million tokens [ https://substack.com/redirect/027981e5-1164-4840-a061-b6f2e8fb52f1?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], 250 times the price of DeepSeek R1, I can’t see many trying it. Mistral Small 3.1 [ https://substack.com/redirect/77adaabf-0709-46d1-9388-d15aff891028?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], Nvidia’s  “Llama Nemotron [ https://substack.com/redirect/a24c0f1b-daeb-494d-9bdf-1e7c374114a2?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ],” LG’s EXAONE [ https://substack.com/redirect/3252801a-0cb1-4b59-87cc-e1718b2227d8?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] (beating o1-mini on certain tasks) and Baidu’s Ernie 4.5 [ https://substack.com/redirect/b51864b1-1c61-4510-91bd-653b9e779045?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] (R1 level performance at half the cost).
Crossing the line
Meta stands accused of training its models on pirated ebooks from LibGen [ https://substack.com/redirect/fce9921e-d2ba-440b-b6aa-21db5e5b90a4?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], with court documents alleging employees torrented over 7.5 million books and 81 million research papers—potentially distributing them illegally via BitTorrent—after Mark Zuckerberg himself gave the green light. This extends beyond Meta to OpenAI and Google — which all argue that national competitiveness requires more permissive fair use interpretations, especially when autocratic regimes might ignore these constraints entirely.
The security implications compound these concerns – Anthropic is reporting “early warning” signs of AI systems developing concerning capabilities in cybersecurity and biology, while alignment techniques rapidly fail against determined attacks.  Miles Brundage , a former OpenAI policy lead, believes that AI will likely cause a “very serious incident” with billions in damage or hundreds dead this year [ https://substack.com/redirect/72bf1566-0002-4ca6-b9f3-f913a62180d1?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
This convergence of copyright disputes and security warnings reveals the tension facing policymakers. If their mindset is one of both economic, strategic and security competition, then to what extent do the legal protections previously afforded to creators get in the way of those priorities?
Here is how I look at it: copyright strikes a societal and economic balance—it incentivizes creators by granting temporary monopolies while curbing the downsides of those monopolies. AI systems, however, upend this balance by transforming how information is aggregated, analyzed, processed and consumed. This calls for a reassessment of the copyright framework to achieve its underlying objective while acknowledging the new technological reality. I suspect that in the current environment though, the rush of competition will set the direction.
