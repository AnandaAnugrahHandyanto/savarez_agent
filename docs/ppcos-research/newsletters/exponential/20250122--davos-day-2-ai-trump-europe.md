Hi all,
I’ve just wrapped up my second full day at the World Economic Forum in Davos. Three themes stand out: AI, Trump and Europe.
Watch my video for full commentary – abbreviated notes are available below.
AI
It’s impossible to take more than a few steps here without hearing about AI’s extraordinary trajectory. Dario Amodei of Anthropic has been reiterating his prediction [ https://substack.com/redirect/8f87a9e7-1956-4f4a-a2be-41eb92a58bea?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] that we could have “PhD-level” AI in just two to three years—vast, unprecedented capabilities that should make us all think hard about the implications. It strikes me that despite these rather jaw-dropping claims, the audience hasn’t fully absorbed the gravity of what he’s saying.
Meanwhile, Microsoft’s Satya Nadella reminded us of his fantastic metric—“tokens per dollar per watt”—to gauge AI’s efficiency and economic impact. It’s a clever way of capturing how quickly and cost-effectively AI systems can churn out useful results and I suspect it may shape how we track the progress of these technologies.
Trump
Although not physically present, Trump’s impact permeates the discussions.
People here hold mixed views. Some American business leaders appear buoyed by the economic growth they anticipate, while others—especially those outside the tech and business spheres—voice concern. This divide underscores just how far-reaching and polarising the Trump effect can be.
This takes me to…
Europe
There’s a special attention on Europe this year in the wake of Trump’s new term. There’s a sense of frustration among certain CEOs who find the continent’s policies slow to adapt, even going so far as to call the situation “embarrassing.”
Yet, I sense a spirited determination. Founders and executives are eager to prove that Europe can innovate just as swiftly as the United States and Asia, pointing to fast-growing sectors like fintech and defense tech (Helsing) as proof.
Ursula von der Leyen really came out yesterday in her speech [ https://substack.com/redirect/70848760-ec4d-44cd-889e-03797c32abed?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] to say that Europe needs to wake up and act in its full sovereignty:
Our capacity to invent and create is underappreciated – Europe’s global share of patent applications is on par with the US and China. But the world is changing. So must we. In the last 25 years, Europe has relied on the rising tide of global trade to drive its growth. It has relied on cheap energy from Russia. And Europe has too often outsourced its own security. But those days are gone. To sustain our growth in the next quarter of the century, Europe must shift gears.
