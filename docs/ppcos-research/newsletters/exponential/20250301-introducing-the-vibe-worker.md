# Introducing the vibe worker

**From:** "Azeem Azhar, Exponential View" <exponentialview@substack.com>
**Date:** 2025-03-01T00:58:38.000Z
**Folder:** exponential

View this post on the web at https://www.exponentialview.co/p/introducing-the-vibe-worker

Today, many people see AI as a tool primarily for automation, assuming it works best with structured, defined tasks. But the reality is quite different: AI is reshaping how we turn fuzzy intuitions into tangible, powerful ideas.
Your greatest notions are probably stuck in your head—too vague, too unstructured. Today’s AI will help you uncage them and turn them into reality.
Welcome to the world of the vibe worker.
If you are anything like me, vibes, half-baked thoughts based on snippets of evidence and tons of experience are constantly fizzing in your brain. They are inklings of ideas, essays to write, research to conduct, advice for a founder, new audiences to reach, new ways of doing things, gut feel, and judgment.
Vibes are the raw material of genius.
Historically, turning these vibes into something tangible—a detailed plan, a memo, a piece of code—has been a slog. That final 20% of clarity often demands 80% of the effort, as we wrestle our intuitions into structured form. But AI is changing that.
As LLMs improve, they’re becoming adept at deciphering our incoherent ramblings. You can throw a jumbled idea at them—“I want something that does this, kind of”—and they’ll figure out your real intention, delivering a workable starting point. It’s reminiscent of a parent interpreting a child’s babbling needs: it might sound fanciful, but it’s already happening. It is vibe working.
Here is a version one definition of vibe working:
Vibe working is using AI to turn fuzzy thoughts into structured outputs through iteration.
As with many work practices, distributed teams, asynchronous working, and agile, software developers have cottoned on to this early. “Vibe coding” is becoming a thing.
Here is Andrej Karpathy, one of the world’s top AI developers:
It is talking to your computer and getting it to build something that works. Coding lends itself to all of vibing. The code that the AIs write works or doesn’t work. You can test and iterate rapidly.
If you want to go deeper, Addy Osmani  has a good rundown of how to get started and some of the tools you need.
Vibe workers of the world arise
While developers pioneered this approach, the vibe working revolution extends far beyond code. Knowledge work of all kinds, marketing, strategy, budgeting, planning, research, and analysis, are fertile terrain.
Here are some of the things I do:
A daily check-in to Claude on my morning drive. I tell Claude what is top of mind. These could be details of an upcoming trip, the next big analysis I’m working on, a business problem I’m trying to crack, or my shopping list. It’s often inchoate as the coffee hasn’t settled in yet. I return to my desk, and a list of items to pass to the team or work on myself is ready.
Using SuperWhisper [ https://substack.com/redirect/b09967de-2ee7-4c3f-a3a0-1a6efc5cb551?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] or WisprFlow [ https://substack.com/redirect/e19c1ef6-0bf5-4da1-853e-5ffe42b21f55?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], I’ll fire off “vibey” queries to a waiting battery of LLMs, OpenAI’s o1-Pro with DeepResearch, Grok 3 with Deep Search and Perplexity Pro. I’ll compare answers and iterate again. Each iteration helps sharpen the next request until something like a prototype plan falls out.
Sometimes (but not consistently), I need to think through a problem “outside my head” by speaking it out loud and making notes. Now, I’ll speak out to an LLM and give it specific instructions: to transcribe and identify gaps, to challenge, to validate whether the argument is coherent and, if not, why not.
Once I’ve written something, I’ll send it to a different battery of LLMs, usually Claude and o1-Pro, and say, “What’s wrong with this? Give me ten weaknesses.” I can tackle those weaknesses and lather, rinse and repeat.
Vibes can be misunderstood.  Thomas à Becket found himself on the wrong end of history's most infamous misunderstanding. Nine hundred years ago, Henry II vibed [ https://substack.com/redirect/66a81657-101b-42ab-a07c-b178590de8ea?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], “Who will rid me of this turbulent priest?” The King didn’t actually order Becket's murder in 1170, but that frustrated outburst—his vibe—was interpreted by his knights as a royal edict. They promptly went and killed the Archbishop of Canterbury in his cathedral. History’s most infamous case of a misinterpreted vibe with fatal consequences. Your AI misunderstandings will hopefully be less dramatic.
Just like Henry’s knights misread his vibe, AI can misread ours. So, be prepared to  iterate to ensure the outputs don’t go off the rails.
You also need to be sensitive that many LLMs have been completely monged out by their builders. The AI companies engage in post-training, a process of reinforcement learning with human feedback, which gives models their particular “personality.” Character training has become a standard part of productising [ https://substack.com/redirect/19241fd0-da52-4aec-b4fc-c6191cd82a1f?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] an LLM, as Nathan Lambert  points out. Claude is friendly, almost family-friendly almost to a fault. Grok is supposed to be “based”. And ChatGPT was a bit cleverer than thou, although the latest model, GPT 4.5 seems more laidback.
Truth is that, as Nathan points out, “character remains more of an art… we don't know the trade-offs of what character training does”. If you are vibing, you need honest feedback. And your AI might not give you that.
One hint: tell it to “be real.”
This challenge of getting honest, unfiltered feedback is precisely why I rely on multiple AI systems rather than just one. Few real bits of work go through less than two or three different systems with numerous iterative queries for each of them. The diversity of perspectives helps counterbalance the individual quirks of each model.
From bottleneck to breakthrough
Why does this matter? Because so many of us are bottlenecked by how long it takes to express ideas. Like task switching or necessary meetings, traditional idea refinement simply adds to cognitive load. Cognitive load leads to fatigue and to mistakes. Vibe working creates space for deeper thinking.
Quality work isn’t just about vibes. It is also about analysis, detail and precision. And I’m not sure how you “vibe” precision. AI can’t replace the human gut entirely—sometimes I still need silence, pen and paper—but it does free up time for just that.
And, of course, a final output can’t be vibed. In my case, it often has to be written — as it is now, typed deep into the evening.
My colleague Nathan Warren  inspired me to put these words down when he showed me something that had taken him three hours, ‘including some mucking around”. I reckon it would have taken ten times as long in the old days. It’s a 10x outcome: the 10x vibe worker.
Vibe working is more than efficiency—it feels like a fundamental shift in cognitive work. By having AI handle the structuring, refinement and boring bits, we free our minds for what, for now, we excel at: intuition, creativity, and judgment.
The 10x gains aren’t just about speed; they're about quality. When I'm not drained from wrestling ideas into form, I produce work that better reflects my thinking. And, of course, it isn’t perfect, but the process is better than you’d expect.
Got a half-baked idea? Talk to your AI this weekend and watch it take shape. The vibe shift starts with you.
