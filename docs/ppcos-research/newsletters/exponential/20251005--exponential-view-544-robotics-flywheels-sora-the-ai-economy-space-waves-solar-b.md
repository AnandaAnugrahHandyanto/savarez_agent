AI shows up at work
This week, we shared our estimates for the 99% step-length – the number of actions an AI system can take at near-perfect reliability [ https://substack.com/redirect/d7d5a185-b7d6-41b5-9560-e198032e2b96?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] before a human must intervene. Today’s frontier systems reliably manage around 100 steps at that threshold. By our estimates, the number could exceed 10,000 by 2029. A couple of years later, they might have between 3x and 10x that range. At that scale, an AI system could operate for weeks, potentially months, without supervision.
Anthropic’s Claude 4.5 Sonnet [ https://substack.com/redirect/10c14320-db8c-412c-92bf-5123431abf54?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], the best-in-class coding model, can reportedly work autonomously for 30 hours on a codebase, a significant jump from the seven-hour ceiling just months ago.
Improvements are there, yet we’re far from saturation. On generous assumptions, the AI ecosystem processes roughly 100–200 trillion tokens per day. That may sound like a lot but it’s nowhere near the scale of a global utility. (Our colleague Nathan Warren  singlehandedly burned through 100 million tokens in one day using Codex.)

Monetizing the AI web
When Sam Altman hired [ https://substack.com/@exponentialview/note/c-162509474?utm_source=notes-share-action&r=f84r ] EV member Fidji Simo as CEO of Applications, I said it signalled OpenAI’s consumer ambitions. Sora 2 and the social app confirm it.
Sora 2’s video generation is impressive. But more than that, 56,000 iOS installs of the new Sora app on day one, despite invite-only, OS and geography constraints, tells me that the demand for AI-native social experiences is there and OpenAI is the best product launcher in the industry. Ben Thompson may have been right on point when he raised this “million billion trillion dollar” question [ https://substack.com/redirect/9c67a580-dca0-42cb-81a5-30a1740849ad?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]:
what is going to matter more in the long run, text or video? Sure, Google would like to dominate everything, but if it had to choose [...] video is, in the long run, much more compelling to many more people.
OpenAI are betting that they can build a creator economy without relying on YouTube, TikTok and others; they could own the generation layer, distribution layer and eventually a monetization channel. Will YouTube need Sora more than Sora needs YouTube? If OpenAI can crack retention and monetization, they own both the supply and demand side. That’s a different game than being a model provider.
Now, Sora is only half of OpenAI’s marketplace ambitions. This week they also announced the Agentic Commerce Protocol that will let users use ChatGPT as their shopping interface (and eventually, a shopping agent) [ https://substack.com/redirect/b1df6785-7363-4626-9256-cfd2f81a5fbf?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. They count on e-commerce contributing to $50 billion in revenues from new products by 2030. The unit economics could work for OpenAI if LLM discovery actually surfaces better options (early personal evidence is positive) and merchants compete for citations rather than purchases. Our researcher Chantal Smith  saved some $2000 on a travel package to Mongolia she found through Deep Research that traditional Google search did not even surface. And we’re already seeing people on our team hit 6-12 AI-mediated purchases in the last year. Google and Amazon are not sitting still – whoever becomes the default starting point for discovery holds the advantage.

Learn your lesson, or…
In a single decade, China applied a proven playbook to build a robotics ecosystem the West cannot easily match [ https://substack.com/redirect/6a0ac140-731f-4cc2-b51f-43d84b6e29d1?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. China overtook [ https://substack.com/redirect/496fbc56-8a74-46a8-b951-8f85f5719d42?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] the US in robot density in 2021, passed [ https://substack.com/redirect/2f34fe88-cc43-44f0-8573-d3db92474305?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] Japan and Germany in 2023 and now runs “lights out” production lines where robots operate without humans.
That ecosystem is using a playbook we know well from China’s breakthrough on solar panels, batteries, and EVs: compounding knowledge, falling costs and an untouchable competitive moat. The Kepler K2 “Bumblebee” humanoid robot [ https://substack.com/redirect/0d175c6f-8c32-4936-9419-57aed57c529f?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], now in mass production for $34,000, shows that the cycle is already spinning. Prices are low enough to expand adoption, which will generate more operational data and volume, which will further cut costs and widen the moat.
The West risks repeating the mistake it made with energy tech. China produces 80% of solar panels, supplies about 60% of wind turbines, 70% of EVs and 75% of batteries – all at a lower cost than the West. Several leading investors now consider those sectors “uninvestable” [ https://substack.com/redirect/8973412c-dc95-4681-a85e-4efdfe9a1534?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] in Europe and the US.
