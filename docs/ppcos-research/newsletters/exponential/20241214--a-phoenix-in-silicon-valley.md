Two years ago, it was all “Code Red” for Google. ChatGPT, the little chatbot that could, had shocked the behemoth [ https://substack.com/redirect/2e85b6c2-99cb-4d73-80a0-3dacc5ae8eb0?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]:
Although ChatGPT still has plenty of room for improvement, its release led Google’s management to declare a “code red.” For Google, this was akin to pulling the fire alarm. Some fear the company may be approaching a moment that the biggest Silicon Valley outfits dread — the arrival of an enormous technological change that could upend the business.
Google’s first response to ChatGPT, the long-discarded Bard, was mediocre. It seemed like the GPT tidal wave [ https://substack.com/redirect/0c5d9ff2-0417-4f7b-bb36-9f70ab215479?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] might overwhelm the dominant search engine, tangled in its bureaucracy, and nipped at by Anthropic, OpenAI and Perplexity.
From Code Red to renaissance
A trifecta of breakthroughs suggests that the search giant’s steadfast investments in fundamental research are bearing fruit. First, a quantum computing advance that could prove transformative [ https://substack.com/redirect/2f5af65e-e293-4f72-bfd9-bbeb7b581745?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. This was swiftly followed by vindication in autonomous vehicles, as its Waymo unit emerged triumphant while competitors retreated. [ https://substack.com/redirect/112aeb93-1d87-4b80-a7b0-1e3e6bc9721d?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] The coup de grâce arrived with significant improvements to its Gemini AI model, alongside a suite of practical applications that could reshape its core search business.
I want to turn to the most Googley of all the AI widgets they released: Gemini DeepResearch. [ https://substack.com/redirect/644e26e7-06c9-4c7f-8849-f19afe9455fd?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]
The product lets you do quite deep research on complex questions. It is the kind of thing I imagined when I looked at the evolution of knowledge technologies in 202 [ https://substack.com/redirect/53385297-3781-4dd6-aba1-ae0e5d3330b5?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]0. I suggested these systems would
make the process of synthesizing knowledge cheaper, which will have knock-on effects both substituting of that activity and spurring a range of new innovations around it.
When you use DeepResearch, it feels a little like science fiction. Feed it a query, and it springs into action, crafting a bespoke research strategy before methodically executing it. The process, which unfolds over several minutes, involves the AI prowling academic databases and authoritative Web pages before weaving its findings into a coherent synthesis.
Here is the question I asked.
Analyze the intersection of renewable energy adoption and economic development in emerging markets between 2020-2024, with particular focus on:
• Policy frameworks that have successfully accelerated clean energy transition
• Economic impacts on local communities and job markets
• Technology transfer mechanisms between developed and developing nations
• Financing models that have proven most effective Please include quantitative data on implementation costs, ROI metrics, and employment statistics where available.
My tireless research assistant produced 3,441 words, organised into 17 sections and a bibliography. As you can see below, tt was all rather impressive.
The full research dossier is here. [ https://substack.com/redirect/2d7d2caa-eff3-4b75-8de4-bf9650d14f6d?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]
The results are remarkable, if not revolutionary. What emerges after four minutes might take a competent graduate student a couple of days to produce—though seasoned academics may spot its limitations. The analysis tends towards description rather than insight, skating over contradictions and deeper structural factors. It faithfully reproduces conventional wisdom rather than challenging it.
Yet, at $20 monthly, such criticism feels rather churlish. The service offers what might be called the industrialisation of basic research: not quite artisanal quality, perhaps, but reliably adequate and available at a fraction of the traditional cost in time and money. For many, including me, that trade-off will prove irresistible. And in these exponential days, we can be sure the quality will only improve.
The rest of Google’s AI announcements are almost overwhelming [ https://substack.com/redirect/71e7a875-ead3-46b5-ad29-039289e76975?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. They do show the firm’s intent: a future of voice-controlled agents capable of undertaking complex real-world tasks. This demo video is cute and worth watching [ https://substack.com/redirect/09326170-6c10-416f-902b-91b1482fde9d?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
Taking stock
As its competitors have become more sought after, Alphabet’s stock had become relatively cheap compared to its peers.
Wall Street has noticed something after this week’s trifecta of initiatives. The company that once seemed wrong-footed by the AI revolution was now demonstrating mastery across multiple domains - from research tools to quantum computing. On Tuesday, the share price rose more than 10%, or more than $200bn.
Like the mythical phoenix, Alphabet appears to be rising from the ashes of its perceived irrelevance.  Whether it can is a multi-trillion-dollar question. This week, we saw signs that it might be ready to soar.
