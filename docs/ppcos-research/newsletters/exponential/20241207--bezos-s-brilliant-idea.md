I attended the pinnacle of the New York conference circuit, the DealBook Summit, this week. I am usually a gadfly at conferences, hovering in hallways and chatting to randos. At DealBook, I was glued to my seat and hardly even looking at my phone: Serena Williams, Jerome Powell, Sam Altman, Ken Griffin… and Jeff Bezos, among others.
Here are my five quick takeaways from the event.
The billionaire’s bonus
I hadn’t seen Jeff Bezos speak in person since 1997. That year, we bumped into each other at Boston’s Logan Airport and he gave me a ride into town. He’s built some interesting things since then.
In his discussion [ https://substack.com/redirect/f515ef79-e7ed-4b3f-a395-e98e721f115c?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] with Andrew Ross Sorkin, one thing made me take pause. He joked that rich lists should be ordered not by the amount of money a person had made for themselves but by the amount they had made for others. For the case of Bezos, this would be more than $2 trillion for Amazon shareholders alone. He owns roughly 10% of Amazon, which was enough, in his words, to keep him incentivised. Remember, Amazon was losing money for over a decade and was considered a turkey—or worse—by many analysts [ https://substack.com/redirect/badd9f68-45e9-4f68-b094-9e64879efb47?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
Ranking entrepreneurial endeavours less by what the entrepreneurs have accumulated and more by what they have enabled is a lovely way of looking at things. Perhaps something for inveterate list-makers at media firms to think about.
We’ll be surprised to the upside
I had a great discussion with IBM’s CEO, Arvind Krishna and Vanguard’s chief economist, Joe Davis, at a breakfast at the summit. We’re all quite bullish about the growth of AI in 2025 believing adoption in the enterprise will increase substantially. When I challenged Joe and Arvind about whether their projections for 2025 were more likely to be lower or higher than reality, both agreed that it was more likely that AI use will grow faster than they thought. It’s a familiar refrain I’m hearing from other conversations.
US bosses are optimistic and ambitious
A recurring theme with Sorkin’s guests was that the US economic growth is a coiled spring held back by onerous regulations and that if the Trump team successfully strips them back, growth will be unleashed.
To some extent, what else would these CEOs say? There will be a new boss in the Oval Office, so it’s wise to draft him into his wake. On the other hand, the alacrity with which company chiefs are all aligning behind the mantra that the US can grow much faster than it has and that the new White House can catalyse that growth is stark.
Europeans must work out exactly how they want their economies to grow. If the past is prologue, the gap with the US may only widen.
GLP-1s (and their successors) are going to be really huge
A discussion [ https://substack.com/redirect/1da42c68-6269-4749-beff-97ce57bf1132?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] with Dr Fatima Cody Stanford, an expert on obesity and David Ricks, the CEO of Eli Lilly, reinforced the EV house view that GLP-1s will be a vast market [ https://substack.com/redirect/35806eea-9dd9-4348-ac88-c7ef6162748a?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] well beyond diabetes. With more than 70% of Americans suffering from being overweight or having obesity, GLP-1s could improve more lives than statins. Forty million Americans are on statins. Lipitor, a statin, is the most valuable drug of all time, racking up nearly $100bn in sales in the US over its lifetime. GLP-1s and their successors will go further.
To win, know who you are
Two stand-out discussions were with Serena Williams [ https://substack.com/redirect/aa7f4f34-0b3b-4205-9c5e-f25205902680?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] and Alex Cooper [ https://substack.com/redirect/5a0668ee-0f39-4d39-95bf-db4b68dcbc0a?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], the podcaster. Both conversations zeroed in on what it takes to win. The short message: know who you are, be who you are and want it. The discussion with Alex gives valuable insights into the future of media—driven by highly-intentioned personalities who speak to (and for) a community.
DealBook is a brilliant event, and it’s hard to summarise. I’m very proud that at least one EV community member is instrumental in making the event happen.
