# 🔮 How to win as the world changes

Last week, I wrote to my portfolio companies with a simple message: the game is changing fast. The global order is being renegotiated in real time, driven by the inversion of globalization and the rise of AI.
These forces are creating confusion, hesitation and for the sharpest operators – opportunity.
If you’re a founder, executive or investor, this message is for you.
My message to portfolio companies
Sent on 6 April 2025
We are living through an incredibly weird time. Two massive waves are crashing down upon us: the accelerating force of artificial intelligence and the dramatic inversion of geoeconomics. Each of these forces is disruptive on its own – but together, they are increasing the stakes for everyone.
In this message, I will address:
The geoeconomics wave
The AI wave
My advice to founders and business leaders
Let’s start with geoeconomics and the shifting posture of the global order.
Globalization, as we’ve known it for the past three decades, is being renegotiated, restructured from the ground up. This isn’t just a US phenomenon. Governments around the world are adapting to this new paradigm, where industrial policy, national resilience and sovereign control take precedence over global efficiency.
As political winds shift and global instability intensifies, so too does the appetite for risk and experimentation. Uncertainty is now the defining condition.
The consequences?
Expect a deep chill in markets. Consumer spending is beginning to falter. Business decisions are dragging. Corporate leaders want much more certainty before making commitments. This hesitation is spreading to capital markets, where investors are increasingly going “risk-off.” I count myself among them.
Decision cycles are lengthening. Risk tolerance is falling. And in the near term, we may see a disproportionate negative impact on companies—particularly those in the climate space—where short-term returns are harder to prove.
Of course, it’s not all doom. There are tailwinds if you’re in hard tech and aligned with US strategic interests. But broadly speaking, I’m preparing for a tough year in the equity markets. I expect prolonged uncertainty and continued capital flight toward safe assets.
And if you’re listening closely, you’ll hear this same caution coming from government officials. “There will be pain before there’s gain.” That message has implications for all of us.
Then there’s AI. The other great wave. And unlike the drag of geoeconomic uncertainty, this one is accelerating. AI is working. The promises are materializing and market participants are responding with enthusiasm.
Venture capital is flowing into AI at an astonishing rate. By some estimates, 80 to 90 percent of all new VC money is heading toward AI firms, quarter after quarter. This is making it harder for companies that aren’t AI-native. It’s also warping expectations across the board.
In AI, we’re seeing firms growing at 50%, even 100% monthly. If you’re not part of that cohort, not only are you starved for capital, but you’re also now judged against these impossible growth trajectories. You may not even be in the AI sector but you’re being priced as if a competitor, powered by agents and workflows, could out-execute you in weeks.
Whether or not that’s true is beside the point. The belief is enough to shift sentiment, reshape valuations, and force new decisions.
Advice for founders
So what should you do in this environment?
Tough times make great companies. But let’s not romanticize it—being a founder or operator right now can be grim. What makes this moment unusual, though, is that it’s not uniformly tough. The AI sector is booming and capital is concentrating within it.
That creates an opportunity for everyone—regardless of your sector.
You need to scale, but not necessarily by increasing headcount. Instead, scale through synthetic capacity:
AI workflows.
AI agents.
AI tools.
In sales, that might mean using AI for prospecting, qualification and negotiation.
In marketing, it means testing 10, 100, even 500 variations of campaigns in parallel.
In customer support, it means replacing repetitive workflows.
And in your product and engineering teams, it means turning documents, specs, and tickets into faster build cycles—powered by AI. If your developers aren’t using Cursor or Windsurf (or something like them), they’re already falling behind.
Even non-technical teams need to embed agentic workflows using whatever stack suits them – Wordware, Lindy, Adept, open agents and so on.
I was speaking to a CEO a few weeks ago who grew twice as fast as he was expecting. He was able to deliver much more with much less and keep the very best people in his team in sales, marketing and customer support.
The opportunity is immense.
The message is clear. In a world of extended uncertainty, your money needs to last longer. Unless you’re hitting clear inflection points quarter after quarter, your only option is to scale capability—not cost.
The key takeaway
We can’t control the macro environment. But we can equip ourselves for it.
Two waves are colliding – one slowing us down, one accelerating change. In the face of geopolitical chill and market pessimism, AI offers something different: leverage. It gives us synthetic scale, digital superpowers and the ability to extend our runway.
That’s the bet to make right now.
