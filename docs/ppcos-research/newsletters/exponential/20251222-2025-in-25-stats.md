# 📈 2025 in 25 stats

Hi all,
Over the past year, we reviewed thousands of data points on AI and exponential technologies to identify the signals that mattered most. Across almost 50 editions of Monday Data, we shared ~450 insights to start each week.
Today, we’ve distilled them into 25 numbers that defined the year, across five themes:
The state of intelligence
The state of work
The state of infrastructure
The state of capital
The state of society
If you enjoy today’s survey, send it to a friend.
Let’s jump in!
Intelligence got cheaper, faster and more open
Margin gains. OpenAI’s compute profit margin – revenue after deducting model operation costs – reached 68% in October 2025 [ https://substack.com/redirect/53424674-92df-44fe-8756-b0ec8e049d5b?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], roughly doubling from January 2024 (36%) as efficiency improved.
Demand curve. Following the Gemini 3 release, Google has been processing more than 1 trillion tokens per day [ https://substack.com/redirect/1ce9d92d-5c0b-4f77-834b-fa9470d70410?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. Moreover, token output at both Alibaba [ https://substack.com/redirect/3fb6ea81-93b2-4fef-970e-2e9c7eaed51b?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] and OpenRouter [ https://substack.com/redirect/e9e84716-b132-4bce-acdd-b79c33cfb4db?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] has been doubling every 2-3 months.
Open beats on price. Shifting from closed models to open models was estimated to reduce average prices by over 70%, representing an estimated $24.8 billion in consumer savings [ https://substack.com/redirect/bf59b177-8489-4b68-9d9d-746bde6ddf35?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] across 2025 when extrapolated to the total addressable market.
Trust gap. Twice as many Chinese citizens (83%) trust that AI systems serve the best interests of society [ https://substack.com/redirect/35e9e105-2cbd-4650-be1f-6b145162b940?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] as Americans (37.5%).
Where it’s used most. AI usage is clustered in service-based economies [ https://substack.com/redirect/6ee4d1b9-4d3d-429c-be10-8ba0b0abb3f7?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] – with UAE (59.4%) and Singapore (58.6%) in the lead; the US ranks ~23rd.
Work reorganized around AI
Near universal adoption. Close to 90% of organizations now use AI in at least one business function, according to McKinsey [ https://substack.com/redirect/f588f9bb-13b7-4bfa-9e65-48f6ccaca553?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
Time saved. ChatGPT Enterprise users report 40-60 minutes of saved time per day [ https://substack.com/redirect/5381ab13-a192-4ebe-888b-bfde885114b5?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] thanks to AI use. Data science, engineering and communications workers save more than average at 60-80 minutes per day.
Hiring tilts to AI. Overall job postings for the whole labor market dropped ~8% YoY, but AI roles are on the rise. Machine learning engineer postings grew almost 40% [ https://substack.com/redirect/bb31897e-6457-481f-a495-959bdfb4d6ff?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
Automation pressure. In areas with autonomous taxis, human drivers’ pay has fallen. Down 6.9% in San Francisco and 4.7% in Los Angeles year-on-year [ https://substack.com/redirect/40846323-92ee-42fc-b54f-c6afbaf20c20?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
Early-career squeeze. Early-career workers between 22-25 in the most AI-exposed occupations (including software developers) saw a 16% relative decline in employment since late 2022 [ https://substack.com/redirect/cb3b1e55-4064-42e1-aaf7-72f515b60d7a?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
Compute hit the real world
Power demand. IEA projects global data center electricity demand will double to ~945 TWh by 2030 [ https://substack.com/redirect/8a67d7f1-9e1f-46de-ab92-2716958b55cb?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]; base case sees ~1,200 TWh by 2035.
Water needs. AI demand by 2027 could use as much as 1.7 trillion gallons of water [ https://substack.com/redirect/18eb90cc-e63a-4ffd-ac58-c6c2ce108274?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], nearly half of what is withdrawn in the UK annually.
Construction boom. Data center construction spending in the US has more than doubled since ChatGPT’s launch [ https://substack.com/redirect/7e057e0d-db92-4170-b76d-788a412e9edb?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
Renewables keep up. Between January and September this year, solar and wind generation have outpaced global electricity demand growth [ https://substack.com/redirect/e9ebc6e3-8281-4c5c-9e74-382c5b9f7b5d?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
Energy inequality. Over half of humanity uses less than 20% of global energy [ https://substack.com/redirect/cb2f6740-4172-4ec1-9c3d-788e2947a230?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. Qatar leads in energy consumption at 570 GJ per capita.
Capital followed the curve
Capex arms race. Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, Alphabet and Oracle) are estimated to spend ~$440 billion on capex in 2025 [ https://substack.com/redirect/eca0311d-fc70-4b0a-9c0d-758c862c1614?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], up 73% year-over-year.
YC is mostly agents. AI-agent startups [ https://substack.com/redirect/0f238cdc-32d5-49ed-8c10-18ab5f923cc1?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] made up nearly half (47%) of Y Combinator’s Spring 2025 batch.
Model makers double down. Foundation model companies raised $80 billion in 2025, more than doubling from $31 billion in 2024 [ https://substack.com/redirect/c3421442-6326-497f-8e40-aefe5a47c7a0?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
Tech capex → GDP. Capital spending by the US tech sector has accounted for more than a third of overall US GDP growth [ https://substack.com/redirect/be21e571-8966-4db7-b3e1-06ac7a598b77?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] in the past three quarters.
AI drove returns. AI-related stocks have accounted for 75% of S&P 500 returns [ https://substack.com/redirect/458d5d76-5652-45f2-b8b6-5c6af7670a93?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] and 79% of earnings growth since the launch of ChatGPT three years ago.
Society recalibrated
Social media use continued to drop. Time spent on social media peaked in 2022 [ https://substack.com/redirect/5eae97a4-954d-4f71-92ac-201526c1f8e3?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] and continued to drop, with young people cutting back first.
Digital companions rise. 72% of US teens have used AI companions, with 52% now regular users [ https://substack.com/redirect/d3855b77-1735-485a-af35-bbf3554f7b18?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] – potentially filling gaps left by declining social media engagement.
Work-life balance > pay. For the first time in 22 years, work-life balance has overtaken pay as the most important job factor, according to Randstad’s 2025 Workmonitor report [ https://substack.com/redirect/457fbdf7-c629-46b8-b904-09cfd2c02bb0?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
European procurement spending hit new highs. For the first time since WW2, Europe will spend more on defense procurement than the US [ https://substack.com/redirect/a1768922-65a1-44a4-b6c1-1ba00859554c?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
A brighter outlook. Despite mixed signals from wealthy regions, global wellbeing surveys in 2025 showed the highest levels of life satisfaction in over a decade [ https://substack.com/redirect/1116f5e7-322d-4db5-aab7-ebca6ea589ba?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
