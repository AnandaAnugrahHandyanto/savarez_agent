RIP chatbot, hello glass slipper
OpenRouter, an aggregator routing traffic across 300+ AI models for five million developers, released an analysis of 100 trillion tokens of usage [ https://substack.com/redirect/e58443b4-a6eb-46c3-8312-0f533d26430e?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. Their data offers a unique, cross-platform look at the market. It’s worth a deep read, but for now I’d highlight two directions:
First, the “glass slipper” effect – retention is driven by “first-to-solve,” not “first-to-market.” When a model is the first to unlock a specific, high-friction workload (like a complex reasoning chain), the cohort that adopts it shows effectively zero churn, even when cheaper or faster competitors emerge. This confirms my long-held view [ https://substack.com/redirect/69ac5796-6d37-4e55-a079-0463c6f69170?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]: customers don’t buy benchmarks; they buy solutions. Once a model fits the problem, like a glass slipper, switching costs become irrelevant.
Second, the shift to agentic inference is undeniable. In less than 12 months, reasoning-optimised models have surged from negligible to over 50% of all token volume. Consequently, average prompt lengths have quadrupled to over 6,000 tokens, while completion lengths have tripled. The insight here is that users aren’t becoming more verbose; the systems are. We are seeing the mechanical footprint of agentic loops iterating in the background.
China’s chip tipping point
China’s drive for semiconductor independence is accelerating faster than predicted. The recent Shanghai IPO of Moore Threads [ https://substack.com/redirect/6bc72a36-e9d5-4587-8cb7-7acfd163818c?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], a leading AI chipmaker, surged 425% on debut, signaling voracious domestic capital support for “China’s Nvidia” alternatives. This aligns with a bold forecast from Bernstein, that China is on track to produce more AI chips than it consumes by 2026 [ https://substack.com/redirect/1e38fb55-c6b2-476f-b09a-329a8af11776?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], effectively neutralizing the intended chokehold of US export controls.
Innovation is fueling this resilience. Firms are bypassing restrictions on advanced lithography by “stacking” mature 14nm chips [ https://substack.com/redirect/e9413bb8-834a-4f24-a589-f34883c7a310?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] to approximate the performance of cutting-edge processors. As companies like Cambricon triple output and Huawei closes the technology gap, the market is bifurcating. China is surviving sanctions and building a parallel, self-sustaining hardware ecosystem that is a direct threat to global incumbents.
Five years ago, I argued in Exponential View that US sanctions [ https://substack.com/redirect/9516c7ac-d4e6-425b-b9de-dca817750b44?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] would not kill China’s chip ambitions, but rather “spur increased self-reliance.” I also noted that the obsession with bleeding-edge nodes (like 5nm) missed a crucial nuance: AI architectures can often thrive on older, simpler manufacturing processes.
That thesis has now played out. By blocking access to the front door (EUV lithography), the US forced China to build a back door (advanced packaging of mature 14nm nodes). We are now seeing the solidification of the “Red Ecosystem” I described back in 2020, a parallel technology stack that, while born of necessity, is rapidly becoming self-sustaining.
The long tail of the unsolved
In the past couple of weeks, we argued that autonomous research agents could cut through the sociological barriers holding back disruptive science (see EV#552 [ https://substack.com/redirect/c50886d1-c4f4-4222-84dd-53b77ad66ab1?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] and EV#551 [ https://substack.com/redirect/e928955a-b38d-4962-9804-ce700a34b116?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]).
Mathematician Terence Tao, a Fields Medallist, now calls AI assistance on open mathematical problems “routine. [ https://substack.com/redirect/47001d36-ac2d-4a33-8130-fb9c9f41fb52?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]” He noted that a Google reasoning model successfully proof-checked a key claim tied to an “Erdős problem,” one of the hundreds of famous, unsolved conjectures posed by the prolific 20th-century mathematician Paul Erdős.
Separately, Aristotle, an AI system produced by Harmonic, autonomously solved a variant of Erdős Problem #124 in six hours [ https://substack.com/redirect/572f6536-1d56-4778-9f20-fc837579a60e?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. And physicist Steve Hsu recently published a paper in  [ https://substack.com/redirect/ddfa0c60-0394-48fc-b76f-763c0d820662?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]Physics Letters B [ https://substack.com/redirect/ddfa0c60-0394-48fc-b76f-763c0d820662?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] where the core theoretical insight was generated by an advanced OpenAI model. He proposes a new workflow of orchestrating multiple model instances to generate steps while others verify them.
These results challenge the critique that AI merely “recombines” existing text. As Tao points out, mathematics follows a “long-tail” distribution. There are thousands of tractable [ https://substack.com/redirect/bcaaa806-547c-4cf7-ae39-844ce1b860d7?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], unsolved problems that simply haven’t been touched because the world has a limited supply of expert mathematicians. AI isn’t necessarily outsmarting the geniuses yet; it is helping us scour the vast archive of neglected problems and separating the impossible from the overlooked.
Superhuman persuasion
Sam Altman predicted in 2023 that “superhuman persuasion” would arrive before superhuman general intelligence. Research released this week suggests that moment may be here.
In politics, a pair of studies in Nature [ https://substack.com/redirect/5f4badd4-0a0d-48d1-b27a-47d5d03977c2?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] and Science [ https://substack.com/redirect/40e804dc-c838-49d6-8c69-428e1c3a041e?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] found that AI chatbots are significantly more effective at changing minds than traditional campaigning. On average, chatbots shifted 1 in 25 voters away from their initial position, roughly four times as effective as TV advertising. In polarized environments like the US, the effect was a material 2-4 points; in Canada and Poland, it swung as high as 10 points.
Effectiveness didn’t come from emotional pleas, but from “fact-density.” The bots overwhelmed users with evidence – at a cost. When GPT-4o optimized for persuasion, its factual accuracy dropped from 80% to 60%. The appearance of evidence turned out to be more persuasive than truth itself.
The effect isn’t limited to politics but to ads in general. A new preprint by Meguellati [ https://substack.com/redirect/e6215259-3fd1-4b4f-85d6-98bf688039af?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] et al. reveals that LLMs have graduated from “personalization parity” to “persuasion superiority.” In a study of 800 participants, users preferred LLM-generated ads over human-written ones 59.1% of the time.
This 18-point advantage held firm even when users knew the content was AI-generated. The AI models simply crafted better visual-narrative coherence and more aspirational messaging than their human counterparts. (I’m hearing from major ad agencies that they are seeing similar results in their experiments.)
We’re seeing a change in how influence works. In traditional media, emotion lived in the broadcast message. With AI, emotion is in the interaction, the sense of being individually understood [ https://substack.com/redirect/ac65a5b5-f86f-4eee-97bf-42e950619ca5?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
