Ideas of the week
Solving gnarliness
At $200 per month, OpenAI’s new Pro tier for ChatGPT’s o1 reasoning system sets a new standard for an AI assistant. By one benchmark, which measures PhD-level science understanding, o1 pro is substantially better [ https://substack.com/redirect/a1b54b41-3533-4a09-8889-5273e1984e2f?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] than a typical human expert. I have been using it and found it impressive on harder research questions and a good companion to Claude. Would most people find it helpful? For now, likely not. In many situations, most people are likely satisfied by a cheaper, less capable model. But, I stress “for now”, because a powerful system like this can help with gnarly problems that we don’t even bother trying to address because of their gnarliness. If you are an entrepreneur or work with highly complicated issues, this more expensive tier is probably worth looking into.
This week, we looked at the evolution from AI assistants to agents [ https://substack.com/redirect/f637a3d3-8d9c-44f7-a3ac-86cd4ebb29e7?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]. We foresee that billions of agents will serve us in the background in the near future and this will change what we do and how we work.
To work effectively with an AI infrastructure we all need to become managers. Just as executives must learn to delegate and verify work, interfacing with an AI chief-of-staff will demand clear judgment about tasks, goals and overall intelligent oversight. This isn’t about outsourcing our thinking; we’ll need to elevate how we manage our AI and our human coworkers.
Putting microbes to work
The most exciting thing about synthetic biology is that it’s only just getting started. The development of an autonomous microbial sensor capable of detecting TNT in natural soil is described in a new Nature  [ https://substack.com/redirect/e51aea1b-4683-4153-a192-6795a8d8b677?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]paper [ https://substack.com/redirect/e51aea1b-4683-4153-a192-6795a8d8b677?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] this week. This innovation combines tools like computationally designed riboswitches and genetic memory circuits that enable the bacteria to detect and respond to trace explosives over extended periods of time. Unlike traditional detection methods, these biosensors are self-sustaining, scalable and adaptable. This could enable a future where biotechnological solutions dynamically adapt and mitigate negative human impact on the environment. As the technology evolves, one can imagine it being used to monitor levels of toxic chemicals in our waters and soil or to predict hazardous events such as gas or oil leaks.
Two sides of a transformation
The AI landscape is undergoing a shift that both lower barriers to entry and redefine where value lies.
On the one hand, distributed training technologies like Prime Intellect’s INTELLECT-1 project [ https://substack.com/redirect/3c964d05-b3dc-496f-8af4-bad65c4e36e0?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] – the first decentralised 10 billion parameter model – are challenging the traditional AI monopoly held by Big Tech. By leveraging distributed training, pooling disparate computing resources globally, it becomes possible for smaller players to compete on a near-level playing field. If AI development can be decoupled [ https://substack.com/redirect/c9c628ba-cda2-4aa9-8708-82791e3c585b?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] from high-end hardware monopolies, a major barrier to entry will fall.
At the same time, Amazon’s vertical integration strategy signals a commoditisation of AI models – the value is moving away from the models, with a focus on the infrastructure and ecosystems that support them. Amazon’s new offerings, Amazon Nova [ https://substack.com/redirect/72d29100-7069-4e52-bb7c-845d79dd4a7e?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] and its proprietary Trainium chips [ https://substack.com/redirect/cb2fc720-a560-4be7-b3d5-f7b654260413?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ], are cost-effective and beat OpenAI and Google in their category. Amazon is transforming AI from a premium product into an inexpensive, standardised service sold as part of a broader offering – just as it did with books, changing them from a valuable stand-alone item to something to purchase along with a garden hose and extra coffee filters. Trainium’s integration into AWS’s infrastructure (which commands 31% [ https://substack.com/redirect/57f7bec1-aa0c-4a77-98f2-0a6149820a64?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] of the cloud market) positions Amazon to deliver AI at scale with unmatched cost efficiency.
See my conversation with SemiAnalysis’s boss Dylan Patel  on all you need to know about the state of AI infrastructure and what's in store for 2025 [ https://substack.com/redirect/48430910-40cb-487d-bb4b-670ebbdb66c0?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
See also: Google DeepMind’s new AI weather tool GenCast [ https://substack.com/redirect/cb41dec4-ae5b-401f-8e15-69163ae75b76?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] shatters forecasting limits, making accurate 15-day predictions that outperform world’s best systems 97.2% of the time.
The frontier of warfare is computational
The traditional arms race of hardware dominance is being replaced by the control over algorithms and decision-making systems for strategic advantage. This has been coming for a long time – I wrote about it in my book in 2021 – but the speed and transparency with which AI is being deployed is greater than before. Just this week, defense-tech company Anduril and OpenAI signed a partnership [ https://substack.com/redirect/1c049893-7ee3-4093-88f4-6f953c8c2dcc?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] to explore how advanced AI models might be integrated into anti-drone systems. (OpenAI is apparently diversifying from LLMs to other advanced AI systems). In Ukraine, autonomous drones now lock onto targets at up to two kilometers’ range, using object-recognition software to maintain an 80% hit rate [ https://substack.com/redirect/95d28601-bc2a-4ab2-8f49-56ba7babdc85?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ] – better than human pilots achieve. Russia is demonstrating the darkest side of the technology, using drone swarms to systematically empty the Kherson of its civilians, with over 9,500 attacks since July [ https://substack.com/redirect/85113d28-914a-4a6c-a84f-7b7d73360558?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ].
TikTok on the clock
Romania’s presidential election has been reset after the [ https://substack.com/redirect/326c2ef2-7745-4bab-b3ca-896049c594a1?j=eyJ1IjoicXd0ciJ9.0tEpqnOrcfuKYRYnEOurmlOZ_pVIUnS06konjQA-bCs ]  Constitutional Court canceled the entire electoral process just days before the scheduled December 8 runoff. Romania’s swift ex post action shows quite how serious these issues are becoming. One to watch.
