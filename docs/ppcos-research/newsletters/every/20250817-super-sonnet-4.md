Knowledge base
"How to Build a Career That Thrives Alongside AI" by Ines Lee: AI is busy making job skills obsolete. But Ines Lee wants workers to become “T-shaped,” meaning developing deep expertise in one domain while expanding into adjacent skills. Her three-phase framework shows exactly where humans still add irreplaceable value—at the intersections AI can't navigate. Read this if you're tired of generic “learn to code” advice and want a strategic roadmap for when AI multiplies your value instead of replacing it.

🎧 "Best of the Pod: Vercel’s Guillermo Rauch on What Comes After Coding" by Dan Shipper/AI & I If you've used OpenAI's website or Perplexity's real-time chat, you've interacted with Guillermo Rauch's code. In this fascinating interview, Guillermo explains how AI is pushing developers to focus less on implementation details and more on delivering great products and experiences. Watch or listen to the full interview on X, YouTube, Spotify, or Apple Podcasts.

"Vibe Check: Claude Sonnet 4 Now Has a 1-million Token Context Window" by Dan Shipper/Vibe Check: Anthropic just dropped a Claude Sonnet 4 model with a 1 million-token context window, so we got to work. We buried movie scenes in 900,000 words of Sherlock Holmes, made it analyze our entire codebase, and even let it play AI Diplomacy to see if it would take over the world. Read this to see how it stacked up against the competition.

"To Improve LLMs, Coach Them Like Athletes in an Arena" by Alex Duffy: Benchmarks tell you if an AI knows facts, but games reveal how it actually behaves under pressure. Alex Duffy and his team built AI Diplomacy to discover something benchmarks miss: how to coach AI to dramatically better performance. Read this to understand why the future of AI evaluation isn't about test scores, it's about watching models play games that reveal their true character.
