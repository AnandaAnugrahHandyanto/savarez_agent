TMAI #460: IMPRESSIONS SUCK. - P3: THE SOLUTIONS!

[ Web Version [2] ]

If you total up all the Ad Impressions that Ad Platforms like Google
and Meta report delivering every day and divide that by 4 Billion
human adults, you get 10,800. That’s the number of ads Ad Platforms
claim to deliver every single day.

If you assume it takes a human adult two seconds to process a digital
ad, and they processed 10,800 ads in one continuous sitting… It
would take 21,600 Seconds/Day OR 6 HOURS/DAY.

Obviously, no human adult on the Earth is doing that.

But. This Big Lie has a real impact:

*
Marketers around Earth are PAYING FOR EVERY SINGLE ONE of those real
or unreal 10,800 “Impressions” Per Day Per Human Adult.☹️

The profit crushing result of running the Display and Video
advertising based on CPMs.

In addition to outlining why this level of insanity exists (TMAI #458
[3]), I’d shared the four Corrosive Problems this creates for
businesses (TMAI #459 [4]):

*
An activity-centric advertising world.
*
Impenetrably complex execution.
*
_Horribler_ media plans.
*
Corruption of MTA & MMMs.

In #459 [4], I’ve shared solutions to each of these Corrosive
Problems.

Today, I want to pull up the altitude and share strategic solutions
you can activate. Each solution is anchored in this unique
_impressions inflation _problem, what makes them special is that the
_think_ applies across a multitude of challenges you’ll face.

[Protesting The Obvious.]

STRATEGIC SOLUTIONS TO IMPRESSION-SKEWING.

Each solution below applies regardless of your company type, B2B, B2C
etc. Each applies regardless of your geography, China or Chile. Each
applies across Marketing type, Brand or Performance.

The problem is that your internal team, your Agency, your solutions
Vendor, is unable to or not accounting for cookie-bombing which
enables advertising channels to steal credit for outcomes from ads
that A. no one has seen and/or B. no one has seen enough and/or
C. delivered no influence.

Consider these solutions…

1. BANISH IMPRESSIONS FROM YOUR COMPANY.

A small step forward.

Just hit delete the metric Impressions (and it’s cousin Video Views)
from all your reports and dashboards. (PS: They should not have been
on your dashboards in the first place!!)

Start by replacing Impressions & Views with Spend.

So no to this: $1.2 mil Spend, 687 mil Impressions, $0.8mil Sales.

Show this: $1.2 mil Spend, $0.8mil Sales.

If Brand, replace that last number with: NUMBER OF PEOPLE LIFTED.

Next, you will notice that when Impressions and Views disappear, other
better Influencing Variables will take their place in your reports
because someone will ask: _what do you mean we spent $1.2m to earn
$0.8m?_ Smarter.

As you seek to replace Impressions and Views, seek Influencing
Variables that really matter. Ex: Try AVOC instead for your video
ads.

Let your Agency/internal Media team use them for identifying basic
media delivery bits happening or not.

[NOTE: You won’t be able to stop your Agency from buying CPMs,
trying to show their value to you by “negotiating” lower CPMs or
kickbacks. All uncool behavior. But. That is not a small step fight.]

If you find resistance to removing Impressions, from your CMO or your
Agency, start to report these two metrics: BRAND LIFT DIVIDED BY
IMPRESSIONS, CONVERSION RATE BY IMPRESSIONS. It will be such an
insignificant number, it will be a good way to let the uselessness of
Views/Impressions sink in.

2. GIVE YOUR AGENCY HARDER METRICS TO SOLVE FOR.

A medium-sized step forward.

Encourage your Agency to meet a super low bar: Switch to Viewable
Impression. Sucks less.

Better still: Ask them to use Reach with 3 Impressions Per User Per
Week.

Encourage them to use AVOC (View count for ads that were watched all
the way to the end, with sound on).

If you can find the data with scale and accuracy (vs. _we did a focus
group with 300 people_), use “Attention.” It is ambiguous, harder
to buy with accuracy, does not scale usually, but even with all that
better than just raw Impressions.

Kick things up three notches: Don’t ask for plans centered on the
_compounded crapification _of Reach x Impressions. Ask for a media
plan for a clear Primary KPI, with clear targets.

_How much budget do we need, in which channel, delivering what type of
ad formats, to which media devices, in order to lift CONSIDERATION BY
+3 over baseline? That result should lift 1.2 MILLION GEN Z at a cost
of $3.5 PER INDIVIDUAL LIFTED._

Simple. Clear. Outcome-focused.

This is much harder for Omnicom or Dentsu to deliver. But. They are
both huge, with brilliant people. Ask for outcomes. Elevate the
sophistication of thinking on all sides. You get better results your
CFO will appreciate, and that work is a lot more fun!

For your Performance Display ads, for your YouTube for Action ads, ask
for a focus on INCREMENTAL CONVERSIONS & COST PER INCREMENTAL
CONVERSION. Harder. Will distract from an obsession with
Impressions.

The barrier to the above hard ask is you, and your past. You might not
know what is an _Average Number of Individuals Lifted _or _CPIL_ for
your campaigns thus far. Realizing that is good. Incentive for your
team to work harder. 😊

_Every company deserves the Agency it gets_. Be better. Ask for
better. And… You shall receive!

[PREMIUM MEMBER BONUS: In setting higher standards of impact via
metrics, ensure you balance for effectiveness & efficiency!

3: TRUST, BUT VERIFY.

Since Impressions have such low engagement or clicks, such abysmal
conversion rates… A common tactic the ad platforms deploy is to
aggressively push the measurement of “View-Thru Conversions”
(VTC). The core idea is: _Hey, they were “exposed” to the ad, they
decided to buy 49 days later due to the influence of that
“exposure.”_

Pause for a moment and consider platforms with autoplay or infinite
scroll like TikTok, Snap, Meta. They generate a lot of Impressions,
with little depth of engagement. End result: Misleading attribution
credit.

Since the consumer journey can “expose” them to “View-Thrus”
on multiple platforms, add Over-attribution bias to the problem. (This
is why if you add up all the “View-Thru Conversions,” you end up
with like 5x the total Conversions on your site!)

Finally, every platform has their own sweet definition of what a
“View-Thru,” which can be further modified. When combined with the
viewability requirements, it is all shenanigans leading to a Big Lie.

[View-Thru Problem Landscape.]

The solution?

It is ok to trust the platforms, you remain in charge of verification.

The approach I prefer (SURPRISE!) is identifying true incrementality
via large-scale (geo) experiments. For both Brand and Performance
campaigns.

How to? Premium Subscribers: Game-Changing Analytics: GEO
Experiments. Part 1 [6]. Part 2 [7].

Suddenly, the Display ad Impressions you paid for, the Video Views you
paid for… Often, but not always, end up being worth diddly squat.
After you add in the creative costs (very high for these campaigns),
Agency fees, extra tools to target/measure/manipulate… Often, but
not always, the only entities benefiting from your Display and Video
ad spend are your Agency and the Ad Platform employees.

Trust, but verify. The recommendation applies to lots more beyond just
View-Thrus.

4. DEFAULT TO CLICKS.

If you have not followed the three recommendations above, if you have
not done deeper validation, and updated them on a quarterly basis to
account for seasonality, competitors, product launches, ecosystem
changes… Default to clicks-based assessment of effectiveness and
efficiency.

You can default to using Last Click based assessment of Marketing
effectiveness – it is less worse than the unknown impact of
cookie-bombing and other Big Lie creation tactics.

Better still, default to the CLICK BASED DDA multi-touch attribution
(MTA) model in Google Analytics 4. It accounts for the consumer
journey well beyond just Paid media by including Owned and Earned
channels as well.

For sure, defaulting to clicks will show an assessment of Marketing
driven success that is a bit conservative. We will not know if a
one-second view of one pixel of a Display ad (Meta’s definition of
an Impression) was of value. But. A click-based model is predictive as
it accounts for an active action by a human (a click). If your
analysis is sound, using the click model allows you to say with a
measure of confidence: _If we do xyz again, I will see abc results
again_.

On paper, this argument is made: _BUT ALL OF THOSE 800 MILLION
IMPRESSIONS MUST BE WORTH SOMETHING!_

My answer: Prove it.

And, if you can’t use what you can prove: Clicks to Conversions
across the consumer journey.

The best-case scenario is that you prove it, see #3 above.

BONUS NOTE: If you are measuring your Brand Marketing, the above
matters less. You don’t measure clicks anyway, as the outcome is
being collected via true test and control cells being served
surveys.

5. GET YOUR MTA & MMMS TO SPEAK THE TRUTH.

In normal circumstances, a solid MMM should be enough to allow you to
measure true incrementality – you don’t even need to follow my
advice on #3. Ditto to the brave souls out there building out an
_Impressions-based _MTA _to give fuller credit to your Marketing by
including Impressions._

Sadly, they fall victim to cookie-bombing, Impression inflation, and
the Big Lie. They chug in hundreds of millions, sometimes billions, of
Impressions and don’t know how to accommodate for that with hundreds
of thousands or a few million Clicks (and Visits).

End Result: Display and Video Brand campaigns that fail to deliver any
Brand Lift and/or those of the Performance type that fail to deliver
any discernable Conversions… Are identified in the MMM and MTA as
driving a massive amount of Revenue. A lie created by the Big Lie, in
the hands of unsophisticated Data Scientists.

I used this metaphor recently: _The Honda Civic can’t even get out
of my driveway, and you are saying it lands astronauts on the moon
routinely!_

Each Impression-MTA and MMM can be a special snowflake. I’ll share a
cluster of approaches I’ve used to mitigate the issues caused by the
Big Lie. Not all might apply to your unique approach, you can pick the
ones that’ll be the best fit.

UNSUCKING YOUR CUSTOM MTA.

MTA A: VIEWABILITY FILTERING: Leverage the MRC standards to only
include _viewable Impressions_. Better still, use tools like
DoubleVerify or Moat to track actual _viewability _and _ad exposure
quality_. For Video ads, you can go beyond viewability and consider
the duration the ad was in view. Longer view times potentially
indicate higher “engagement,” thus warrant a slightly higher
weighting (though still less than a click!).

MTA B: SITE BEHAVIOR ADDITION: We can analyze user behavior. If the
user visited the website shortly after being exposed to an ad, even
without a click, that could potentially indicate an influential
Impression. (Be vary of spurious correlations.)

MTA C: BFF FREQUENCY: It is not unusual that 80% of your audience saw
your ad once, 5% saw it 20+ times, the rest are in the middle. You can
down weight or cap the credit attributed after a certain number of
Impressions – no human loved you more because they saw your ad 20
times per week. I also throw out low frequencies – there is
extensive research that one or two impressions during the course of
the Entire Campaign does zilch for your brand.

MTA E: DECAY FUNCTIONS: You can apply temporal decay to Impressions
and Views. Reduce credit from Impressions that occurred long before
the Conversion. This limits _last touch hording _by Display/Video
channels, and reduces the over-crediting of early/passive
“touches.” (Read up on Markov Chains, Shapley Value, and friends.)

MTA F: CAUSAL INFERENCE TECHNIQUES: Use Bayesian hierarchical models
to better estimate true contributions, even with sparse data.

MTA G: GAME THEORY-BASED MTA: Assign credit fairy by evaluating all
permutations of channel combinations. Channels that always contribute
to conversions get more credit. Passive channels that rarely change
the outcome get much less. You can also adjust for impression-only
touches, so channel need to change the outcome (not just be present)
to earn credit.

MTA H: TRAINING DATA ADJUSTMENT: During model training, downweight or
exclude user journeys where the only contribution from a channel was
an impression. Focus the model on clicks or deeper interactions unless
a _verified view metric _is present (ex: viewability, time in view,
frequency).

MTA I: DATA DEDUPLICATION: For some super large advertisers… Ensure
that Impression data is not duplicated across platforms (ex: DSPs,
Publishers), which can inflate the total touchpoints. It is a pain in
the rear but you can use clean room environments (e.g., Google Ads
Data Hub, Meta's Aggregated Event Measurement) to reconcile and
deduplicate touches.

MTA J: NO TO INTEGRATING EXPERIMENTS: It feels super sexy to combine
MTA models with geo-holdout tests or randomized control trials. Then,
use the result to calibration attribution weights. I fell for this.
The problem is once you account of the number of Owned, Earned, Paid
media activity in an average company, then layer in the variables to
account for (competition, seasonality, acts of god, etc. etc.), you
would have to run hundreds of experiments to get a decent data set for
your calibration. And, you would have to keep doing them constantly
because the world is in flux. It is onerous, even impossible. Be wary
of Data Scientists who are are “calibrating your attribution
weights” based on five experiments.

UNSUCKING YOUR CUSTOM MMMS.

Media-Mix Models (MMMs) are less granular than MTA, they are still
massively vulnerable to the Big Lie. Since MMMs measure incrementality
(MTA does not), and is used to decide strategic budgets… The
consequences of an MMM that succumbs to Impression inflation can be
dire. To protect the MMM, depending on your unique approach, some
techniques above for MTA will also work. Additionally:

MMM A: IMPRESSIONS ≠ SPEND: Instead of using Impression volume as a
proxy for media activity – especially your brand marketing campaigns
– use actual cost-based spend (ex: net paid spend).

MMM B: CURATE MEDIA INPUTS: As with MTA, filter for viewable
impressions, completed views, etc. Convert to effective CPM-adjusted
weights.

MMM C: USER LAG STRUCTURES AND ADSTOCK CAREFULLY: Passive
impression-heavy channels should have a slower or flatter adstock
decay. Another idea: Instead of applying uniform rules, estimate ad
stock decay per channel by using data driven methods like grid search,
Bayesian estimation etc. Caution: Avoid assuming _instantaneous _or
_short-term _impact from high-frequency Impressions.

MMM D: INCORPORATE SATURATION AND DIM RETURNS: Use non-linear
transformation, Hill functions or Spline curves, to model how
incremental impact declines with increasing spend. This reveals if
inflated impression-heavy spend has a real cap on effectiveness. This
also prevents large volumes of low-engagement impressions from
disproportionately influencing the model.

MMM E: CONTROL CONFOUNDING WITH STRONG COVARIATES: (Always a best
practice!) Include control variables that could co-vary with
impressions delivery. Seasonality. Promotions or price changes. Brand
equity shifts. Site changes, or outages. This will help ensure the
rising Impressions aren’t simply picking up correlated demand
trends.

MMM F: USE INSTRUMENTAL VARIABLES: For passive channels, like
Display/Video, use exogenous bits like auction competitiveness, media
supply, weather triggered buys, etc. to tease apart causality from
correlation. Ex: If display impressions go up because CPMs dropped due
to low competition, but conversions don’t rise, the impression
volume isn't causal.

MMM G: REGULARIZE THE MODEL TO PENALIZE OVERFIT CHANNELS: Employ
regularization techniques (like Ridge or Lasso regression) in the MMM.
These methods can help to shrink the coefficients of less important or
highly correlated variables, potentially reducing the impact of
inflated impression counts if they don't strongly correlate with the
outcome.

MMM H: ACCOUNT FOR NON-MARKETING FACTORS: Always include relevant
control variables that can influence the outcome (e.g., seasonality,
pricing, promotions, economic indicators, competitor activity).
Properly accounting for these factors helps to isolate the true impact
of marketing spend and reduces the risk of misattributing correlation
with impressions as causation.

MMM I: First, model reach/awareness generation using impression data,
then model conversion probability conditional on awareness. This
separates impression channels' true role (building awareness) from
direct conversion impact, preventing them from claiming full
conversion credit.

MMM J:TRUST, BUT VERIFY: “Calibrating MMMs with experiments” runs
into the same problems I’ve mentioned above. BUT. I do actively use
experiments to test how truthful the MMM’s analysis and
recommendations are. The MMM says allocate $xy mil to Meta to get $abc
mil incremental revenue in return. Construct a large-scale experiment
where you do that in test, don’t in control, and see if the results
the MMM predicted actually happened. It will never be 100% right, what
you want to assess is just how wrong is the MMM – in part due to how
good it is at handling the Big Lie.

Phew. That’s it. 😊

If it feels like a lot of hard work, it is.

It is worth it because you want to ensure you are building a

economy is wobbly, and you want to defend your Marketing spend. It is
worth it because this will fundamentally transform your career.

Carpe diem.

Don’t let the Big Lie cost you profits and happiness.
