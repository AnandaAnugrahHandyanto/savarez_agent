TMAI #469: BYE SEO, HELLO AEO! -P2.

[ Web Version [2] ]

Humans are evolving from _“give me relevant data and I’ll figure
it all out”_ to _“you figure it out; just give me the answer.”_
↓
Humans are expressing this by evolving from keyword inputs that return
lists of links to what I call _RESOLUTION QUESTIONS_ — long-form,
deeply contextual questions typed with the expectation of a complete,
final answer. No more jumping from link to link.
↓
Search engines are responding to this evolution by becoming ANSWER
ENGINES (AE).

It is demonstrated by the evolved user experience: from four paid
links + ten organic links to direct answers.
↓
The _answers _are becoming more expansive, covering a greater share
of assessed human intent.

These _answers _are 100x more helpful than current search results
because you receive downloadable itineraries, teaching guides, models,
custom solutions, spoken instructions, visualizations, one-of-a-kind
mini-movies, and more.
↓
Increasingly, all of the above is delivered without leaving the Answer
Engine — a major shift from Google’s “_deliver one
hyper-relevant first link so you jump off Google ASAP_” strategy.

Incredible, no?

TMAI #468 illustrates with examples [3] that these positive shifts
for humans create, at least in the medium term, negative shifts for
businesses. Please review #468 to internalize the immense scope of
this change.

Here’s a doodle I sketched on a long flight to capture the
essence…

[SEO to AEO transformation.]

Answer Engines represent a profound change to a core reason for the
Internet's existence:

*
ORGANIZE THE WORLD’S INFORMATION AND MAKE IT UNIVERSALLY ACCESSIBLE
AND USEFUL.

TMAI #468 [3] also shared this strategic guidance:

*
Do not remove your focus on Google to protect your present — for
now, most of your traffic and revenue will continue to come from the
“old search model.”

*
Ignore Google for the future of seeking, or risk missing a revolution
against your direct competitors.

[NOTE: ChatGPT's ease of access and use is the reason I'm using it to
represent Answer Engines. Emerging Answer Engines include Claude,
Mistral, and Perplexity. Please use at least two, multiple times per
day. My daily tools are ChatGPT Plus, Gemini Pro, and Mistral.]

SEARCH ENGINE ➔ ANSWER ENGINE IMPLICATIONS

Foundational change: the shift from typing keywords to asking
_resolution questions_.

PROBLEM 1: LOSS OF TRAFFIC: Traffic to your website/mobile app will
drop dramatically. Google is already sending far less traffic to your
site. Answer Engines will never come close to _old-Google_ traffic
volume, because they don’t need to; they deliver complete answers.

PROBLEM 2: LOSS OF INFLUENCE: Third-party (3P) content has gained
extraordinary influence in Answer Engines compared with first-party
(1P) content you publish. In terms of the diversity of sites/apps/data
type being used, and their purposes (social, research, community,
blogs, etc.). Your contribution to what the human receives is
diminished.

Some of the traffic loss is not coming back.

Regaining some of the traffic we have already lost, and more we will
lose soon, requires us to retain all our SEO skills and EVOLVE TO
ANSWER ENGINE OPTIMIZATION (AEO). Keeping an SEO-only lens limits
success—we’ll miss the new things we must do to win (or survive).

I’ve organized recommendations for AEO actions into four clusters:

[Actions to win AEO | Four clusters.]

1. PRE-SEARCH. Actions to accommodate the shift to resolution
questions (in our behavior analysis/research and on our owned digital
presence).

2. SEARCH EXPERIENCE. Actions to adapt our paid ads and organic
relevance strategies (for Answer Engines, and Google Search as it
evolves).

3. PAN-SEARCH. Actions for a world where humans use AI agents to
outsource today’s “searching” and “shopping.” (The robots
are already here. Ready for ads for robots?)

4. MEASURE DIFFERENTLY. Actions to fold these shifts into what
(KPIs) and how (methodologies) we measure within our Strategic
Analytics practice.

I’ll outline my recommendations for Pre-Search and Search
Experience’s_ winning PPC_ today.

Next week I’ll focus on Search Experience’s _winning organic
influence_ (there’s so much to cover!). Two weeks from now I’ll
cover Pan-Search (agentic AI) and Measure Differently.

_Ready to rock and roll?_

1. “PRE-SEARCH” ACTIONS

Humans are moving FROM typing a few keywords to help the search engine
_guess_ what they want TO speaking/typing long, complex, nuanced
questions with all the context about the_ resolution_ they are
seeking - expecting that you (/AE) do all the work!

This change in user behavior TWO SUB-CLUSTERS OF ACTIONS we need to
take.

1a. Keyword-to-Questions Evolution Actions.

Companies, you, and I have focused on understanding keywords; now we
need to understand _resolution questions_ in all their complex
glory.

For the last 20 years there has been little need to understand how
humans seek; the Keywords report in Webmaster Tools was enough.

You must invest in research to deeply understand this change and its
implications:

*
_Primary research_
*
_Secondary research_
*
_Surveys_
*
_Industry studies_
*
_Focus groups_
*
_“Follow-me-homes.”_

If you do not understand how _seeking _is changing for your industry
and products, everything else I recommend is moot.

If you are a small business, many studies will be published that you
read. No matter your size, I encourage you to run focus groups (just
call your last five customers) and “follow-me-homes.”

Invest in a deeper understanding of this new human behavior.

1b. Owned Experiences Actions.

This behavioral shift affects Answer Engines (AEs) AND user
expectations for your owned digital experiences—your site, mobile
app, and even your metaverse presence (you have one of those, right?).

Now is a good time to start improving and get ahead.

Let's use BEST BUY, a multichannel commerce business, as an example
today.

When I typed my complex wireless-headphones question from TMAI #468
[3] on BestBuy.com [4], the results were predictably poor. The site
is optimized for keywords. Search returned a Wireless BlueParrott Mono
Headset ($139), an H2O Audio Riptide Ultra ($249), and a Garmin Dezl
201 ($399). You'll recall AEs delivered far better results.

Best Buy—and all of us—need to _skate to where the puck is going,
not where it has been._

ACTION A: ENGINE. The internal search experience on your site must
change profoundly.

There is an entire generation looking for answers, not links. Your
search needs to be... magic words... Intent-centric
_and_ Conversational.

Is your website ready to give me relevant results for _an affordable
purse for a student graduating university and heading to her first
job_? Currently it is not, because your site team solves only for
“red purse” searches.

How long will it take you to swap your internal site search to be
powered by LLMs?

Both on your ecommerce AND support sites, please.

ACTION B: CONTENT. The new LLM-powered internal site search will be
intelligent only if your product, support, about, promotions, white
papers, and all other content are more comprehensive, unique, useful,
multimodal, and, above all, expressed in human-speak.

That is the only way it will handle complex _resolution questions_,
rather than mere keywords.

_Example:_ Best Buy’s current internal search suffers from a lack of
content on wireless-headphone pages related to product usage—types
of exercise, needing super quiet in libraries, trading off sound
quality for weight, etc. Such content would let an Answer Engine,
whenever Best Buy implements it, become smarter.

_Example:_ If I am on the Sony XM5 page [5], does the page leverage
my expectation for an answer? Instead of a “Compare products”
checkbox, why not list the top three ways the XM5 beats the
competition above the fold? Why retain a data-pukey experience
reminiscent of a Google SERP? Where is the accessible multimodal
content (for me AND for the internal search engine AND the LLM
indexing your site)?

_Example: _For a B2B business, after my initial _resolution
question_, why not ask a few clarifying questions—like ChatGPT does
so well—and create a custom white paper just for my company, instead
of the horribly generic white papers dumped on the world? It is
trivial to do this with any LLM.

ACTION C: TECHNICAL AEO. Our SEO team wisely ensured Googlebot had
no technical difficulties indexing us. Great—keep that up.

But for your internal Answer Engine, you must develop new technical
AEO skills and actions. All new multimodal (audio, video, text)
content must be accessible and understandable (not just indexable).

Any new web/mobile experiences must account for technical-AEO needs.
What is the new _“don’t wrap your links in JavaScript” rule we
used to have for googlebot_?

While we’re on this topic, and while AI agents start replacing
humans in seeking and shopping, your technical AEO checklist must
address the needs of FERMAT, MINDSTUDIO, KLARNA AI ASSISTANT,
CHATGPT AGENT, and others. Is your IT team ensuring this?

Oh, and... These agents will need to log in to my Best Buy account,
access inventory levels, reviews, my preferred addresses, packaging
options, and whatever else is required to decide whether to buy the
warranty upsell you’re pitching to my agent. Your Legal and IT team
needs to work through your policies and enable this to ensure you
don't lose sales.

It’s fine to chase Gen-Z customers in your marketing, but is the
place they land—your digital existence—ready for them? Your IT
team’s answer will reveal whether the response is “no,”
“NO(!),” or “OMG, WE ARE GOING TO DIE.” Ask them.

2. SEARCH EXPERIENCE ACTIONS

The complex Eataly [3] _resolution question _for my parents’
visit and the _best wireless headphones_ scenario were clear
demonstrations of how "search" has changed to providing answers.

[Note: Some readers said, “_You don’t get it; I don’t trust
Google today or Claude tomorrow. I want to do my own research. I want
ten blue links!_” Fine. Ride your horse to work if you wish. The
rest of the world is moving to e-bikes, buses, cars, and trains. Just
appreciate the risk of building your business around that personal,
_horse_, preference could mean bankruptcy.]

The switch to answers has two implications: 1. Win Paid "Search"
Ads. 2. Win Organic Relevance.

Let's do the first one today and the second one in a long Premium
edition next week.

2a. Winning Paid-Answer Advertising.

At the moment, we have an incomplete view of paid Answer-Engine ad
options.

It is unclear whether there will be ads in ChatGPT, as monetization is
currently tied to Plus ($20) and Pro ($200) subscriptions. OpenAI ARR
as of July 2025 is $12 bil (2x in six months!).

Others lack OpenAI’s war chest, so we are seeing paid options
surface in other Answer Engines—e.g., Bing’s Copilot Search ads
[6] and in Perplexity [7]:

Google is experimenting with its old product-carousel ads in AI Mode
and has stated that many new ad options are coming. They might appear
in Gemini Free to offset costs, and fewer PPC ads on default Google.

While visibility is limited, I anticipate three likely changes to what
we call PPC:

*
FEWER AD SLOTS. Google Search can show three (or more) ads above the
fold today; that is unlikely to persist across Answer Engine
experiences.

*
HIGHER COSTS PER AD. (See A.) Hence, higher CPAs.

*
A PROFOUND SHIFT IN AD RELEVANCE AND QUALITY [8] EVALUATION.
Keyword, ad text, bid, and landing-page quality will no longer
suffice._ Resolution questions_ will require deeper, different
quality score and relevance.

With those knowns—and some known unknowns—consider these
provocations and action items to win with AE ads:

*
TRIGGER SHIFT: Paid-search ads were triggered by keywords; paid-answer
ads will be triggered by complex, nuanced questions.

Has your agency laid the groundwork to build ads from this new
expression of layered intent? (Scroll back up to Keyword to Questions
Evolution Actions.)

Does your agency know how to improve the equivalent of an Ads Quality
Score for Answer Engines?

*
BRAND-TRAFFIC MITIGATION: Google’s AI Overview/AI Mode lowers your
brand’s presence (organic links are pushed down, paid ads might not
be present at all - for now). That will translate into less brand
traffic.

My calculations suggest boosting your current Google _brand_ budget
by ~15-20 % to compensate. Have you?

Continue to use incrementality to assess brand-budget success.
Differently from today, early evidence suggests higher incrementality
in answers experiences (because without the ad, you simply won’t
appear at all).

Focus on improving the Ad Quality of your brand ads on AEs, vs. your
non-brand ads.

*
EMBRACE ADS AUTOMATION. Google's own advertising strategy is adjusting
to this new behavior by users (_resolution questions_ vs. clean
keywords). Solutions like AI Max are intelligently and automatically
assessing_ human-intent_.

Embrace AI Max (and Advantage+, and others).

For many, it feels like you are losing control, you can't micro manage
with broad and exact and 100 buttons you can press today to asset your
intelligence. You are resisting, you don't know_ how it works_.

I'm sorry: You need to grow up.

Hold your performance reports and Profit on Ad Spend (POAS)
[9] results close to you, and migrate a material chunk of your ad
spend to AI Max. Migrate all of it by Oct 01, 2025 (keeping a close
eye on POAS, and incrementality).

The sooner your Agency, internal team, learns how to profit from
advertising that uses AI for _intent-inference_, the sooner you’ll
be ready for Answer Engines paid advertising. My assessment is they
are not going to let you micro-manage ads/settings/delivery like old
Google.

*
NON-BRAND FOCUS: Shift non-brand, sometimes called _generic_, budget
from _broad informational intent _TO _more relevant non-brand
intent_.

Ex: If we were Songmont [10]: From “how to clean leather” to
“designer tote bags.”

Unlike today in default Google, I anticipate that we are unlikely to
get our ads listed for broad, informational intent questions. This is
a big problem, as it will deliver a hit to your _weak commercial
intent_ traffic. But, for the nimble companies, a decent-sized new
opportunity.

*
ALPHAS AND BETAS: Participate in every alpha/beta from every Answer
Engine to teach your organization what life will be like.

A competent agency should already have run ads on Perplexity and Bing
Copilot (at the minimum), and delivered learnings from diagnostic
metrics and success KPIs

These actions are teaching your agency and your internal teams how to
work with: New ad creatives. New modalities. New triggers. New bidding
algorithms. New measurement tactics. New landing page experiences.

Depending on your size, a few percent to 10% of your PPC budget should
already be going to these efforts. When Google whitelists you for AI
Mode ads, jump on that right away. Other Answer Engines as well.

*
CREATIVE EVOLUTION: Reflecting on quality-scoring on Meta and Google,
I expect that S/AEO content techniques will become essential in your
paid-ad copy.

Consider this...

Old Search Ad: An ad is triggered by keywords. "Best wireless
earbuds." Replying with relevant creative is not difficult with that
limited context.

AE Ad: Now consider that the trigger is a complex_ resolution
question_ looking for_ the perfect wireless earbuds for cycling that
minimize wind noise and have a fast charging feature_. What does the
creative in the ad look like now? Now imagine 80 million resolution
questions you can serve ads to each day. How do you build 80 million
hyper relevant ads?

Obviously your creatives for AE ads will be built by AI, on the fly.
Are you ready?

Did you embrace GenAI ad building features in Amazon, Meta, Google
last year? Then you are not ready. Build a plan.

*
TALENT SPARK: Change is hard for humans. Infuse your experienced
Search Team with significantly _younger in experience _AI Natives in
your, what I’m calling, Answers Team.

Infusion of _present experienced_ AI Natives will deliver acceleration
of the new _think _and _ink _you need. The_ past experienced_ team
members will bring business experience, which is critical as well.

Due to unknown unknowns in paid ads, what works today could change
dramatically—and rapidly. The competitive advantage to build:
Agility across thinking, creative, and execution. Please invest in it
while there’s still time.

BOTTOM LINE.

The crux of _The Innovator's Dilemma_ is that successful companies
often fail because they focus too much on sustaining innovations,
improving their existing products, and ignore disruptive innovations
that initially seem inferior but eventually redefine the market. These
disruptive technologies are overlooked until it's too late for
incumbents to respond. :(

My hope is you will recognize the disruptive innovation on the way,
and respond today.
