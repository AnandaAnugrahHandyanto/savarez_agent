TMAI #445: SUPER BOWL: CRITICAL MARKETING LESSONS.

[ Web Version [2] ]

The Super Bowl is _peak Americana_.

Food. Family. Passions. Corporate Parties. Millions of words of
“news.” Fighter jets flying over the stadium.

Oh, there’s also a football game.

In between all the ads! 😊

Is there a one day Marketing bonanza anywhere close to a Super Bowl?

_Nyet._

[DISCLOSURE: My beloved and I had the privilege of received a VIP
experience of Super Bowl 54. Flights, limos, hotel with celebrities,
Lady Gaga concert in a custom-built only for one night stadium (!),
police escort to the stadium, the whole shebang. Someone offered me
$200 for the used ticket as I left the stadium! Thank you FOX SPORTS
for an unforgettable experience – even if my team lost.]

As we head into this weekend in America, I want to use the Super Bowl
to reflect on four important Marketing lessons.

LESSON #1: THE REAL COST OF A SUPER BOWL AD.

The news is that a 30-second ad during the 2025 game will cost approx.
$8 million.

The first lesson: MEDIA COST ≠ TOTAL COST OF THE AD.

As we reflected in detail in TMAI #437 [3], there are Pre-Production
Costs (company, agency++), Production Costs (talent, equipment++),
Post-Production Costs (editing, music licenses++), and Additional
Costs (insurance, project mgmt.++).

The total cost of a SB ad?

For one of my clients:

$8 mil + $14 mil = $22 mil.

For someone who ran an ad shot on their phone in 20 mins at last
year’s SB:

$8 mil + $0.00001 mil = $8.00001 mil

Smart Marketers always, always, always, compute the Total Cost of an
SB ad.

If you hear someone simply using the Media Cost to justify running an
ad, or, worse, in the SB Ad ROI analysis… Be very suspicious of
them. They are not your friend.

LESSON #2: THE CONCEPT OF THE HALF-LIFE OF BRAND LIFT.

We ran a $22 mil total cost SB ad because we want some kind of
performance lift – Leads, Sales. This is effortless to measure.

I’m grossly simplifying but basically… Baselines. Ad. New
Baselines. Delta = ROI.

You want to be slicker… Leverage statistical models to assess any
additional sales impact on non-advertised other products you sell, if
there is a long-tail for Leads, Sales. It is not challenging. Just do
it.

[SPECIAL INSIGHT: Another sign you are in the process of being sold
snake oil: “We can measure the long term sales impact from our Super
Bowl ad using the MMM.” No you can’t. Altitude mismatch. Be wary.]

A vast majority of the SB ads are run for Brand Marketing Purposes.

Unaided Awareness. Consideration. Purchase Intent.

[SPECIAL INSIGHT: God forbid you are told your SB ad is to: Drive
Brand Love. Create Organic Social Buzz. _Power Water-Cooler
Conversation_. Etc. If you hear these goals for your Brand Marketing
$$$s, it might not be such a bad idea to quietly start looking for a
new role, in a different entity.]

With a gazillion people watching, SB can be a great _Brand play_.
Sincerely.

Here’s the challenge:

You run a smartly pre-tested via Human Made Machine
Creative, it passed!

You got a 4-point lift in Unaided Awareness (/whatever
your goal).

SCORE!

Day after SB question: _HOW LONG WILL THIS BRAND LIFT LAST?_

That is the key to the entire _should we run an SB ad_ question.

Years ago, for our team, I invented this computation:

_          How long does it take for us to lose half of what we
gained?_

The Half-Life of Brand Lift.

Then, of course, how long to lose all of it?

You will have your own computations of Half-Life from your Brand
Marketing campaigns – especially if you have a smart Agency.

If you don’t, just Perplexity i, and you’ll bump into numerous
Kantar and Sister studies.

In my experience, top brands, high media weight, 14 countries, around
3,000 campaigns, usually the Half-Life (IF you got any lift) is a few
days (between three and six).

By that measure, you will lose 2 of the 4-point lift in a handful of
days.

In my experience, you’ll lose all of it in seven to ten days.

I noticed the gears in your brain spinning:

IF YOU RUN A SB AD, FOR $22 MIL TOTAL COST, YOUR ENTIRE
BRAND ROI – HOWEVER YOU COMPUTE IT – HAS TO BE DELIVERED INSIDE A
WEEK AFTER THE SB.

IF THAT’S UNLIKELY… WHY ARE YOU LIGHTING $22MIL ON
FIRE?

[More on the fire bit, immediately below.]

The kicker: There is of course zero ROI in a week from Brand Marketing
– if you wanted a 7-day ROI,  you would run a Performance Marketing
Ad.

LESSON #3: THE CRITICAL ROLE OF SPIKE & SUSTAIN MARKETING.

The futile tactic outlined above: _Launch a campaign, it ends. _

AKA _spike and silence_.

In our case, we’ve applied it to the _spike_ of one SB media buy,
and then _silence_ the next day.

_Spike and silence_ is an extremely common Brand Budgeting/Execution
strategy, beyond the SB. Ex: You just launched your New Product
Campaign. It runs for a few days/weeks, then _silence_.

The problem with _spike and silence_, as your brilliant Agency or
internal Strategic Analytics team will tell you, is anything you gain
(Brand Lift, # Of People Lifted), you lose extremely rapidly – turns
out people have other things to remember than your ads!

[Spike & Silence.]

In a few days after your spike, your Brand KPI falls back to the
baseline.

That $1 mil / $10 mil / $45 mil Brand Marketing campaign impact…
Poof, gone.

Successful Brand Marketers, realizing the kiss of death above, only
execute _spike and sustain_ campaigns.

_Spike_, earn Brand Lift, _sustain_ it until the next campaign,
_spike_, _sustain_ the new incremental Lift, _spike_, …. Ad
infinitum (until you run out of headroom).

The glorious outcome…. A sexy, orgasmic, constantly elevated new
baseline!

[Spike & Sustain.]

This humble blue line ensures your Brand Marketing delivers clear,
_provable to the CFO,_ incremental financial impact.
#peakmarketingglory

[PREMIUM SUB BONUS: TMAI #386: Brand Marketing’s Secret Formula:
Spike and Sustain! [4]]

Implication for the Super Bowl Ad budget… There are only two
strategies that will deliver _provable to the CFO_ return on your $22
mil SB ad:

*
SB is a part of your _spike and sustain_ Brand Marketing investment
for the year (/three years). For $22 mil it is a pretty BIG _spike_.
The day after the SB, you plan to spend another $17 mil / $45 mil /
$100 mil in Media on your SB Creative to ensure that the Lift
sustains.

[SPECIAL INSIGHT: Actual sustain Media budget to be computed by your
smart Agency. It depends on your baseline, KPIs, headroom, channels,
audience, etc. etc.]

*
You are a new company/start up. SB is the very first time you have
ever done advertising. The SB ad will be your first spike, followed by
numerous more sustains and spikes.

Both A and B will deliver not just Brand Lift from SB, they will
sustain that Brand Lift over time, AND because of that
ensure_ PROVABLE TO THE CFO MARKETING-DRIVEN INCREMENTAL PROFIT._

If you don’t have a Media budget to _sustain_, what’s the
objective of your SB Media _spike_?

Certainly, can’t be financial business impact.

LESSON #4: THE CASE FOR A VANITY SUPER BOWL AD BUY.

You would be surprised just how many SB ads are just a vanity buy.

The CMO wants to look good in front of the CEO, prove that the
_Marketing team is cool_.

The SB ad's only purpose is to make all the employees feel good about
the company.

The CEO would like to have an SB ad so that she/he has something to
show up to her/his friends at the next golf game.

A company is trying to attract investment from a new Private Equity
firm, and an ad in the SB implies we have arrived, please value us
higher.

The SB ad is a play for the company to be considered for some upcoming
industry awards.

My favorite: _We internally at the company love this Creative. We
don’t have a campaign for it. Why not just run it in the Super Bowl
and make a big bang?_  (This has happened to me more than once!)

If you are a big enough of a company, I want to persuade you that all
of these are valid reasons for buying an SB ad. Well, maybe not that
last one.

If a CEO thinks that the _Marketing team is doing cool work_ and gives
Marketing more funding… That’s a good use of an SB ad, no?
(Hopefully, the CMO is executing incrementality-centric marketing.)

My former employer runs one or more ads every SB, they have 182, 502
full-time employees. Running a $22m SB ad is a very affordable way to
improve employee pride and engagement.

If the CEO can make her friends feel a bit jealous, why not. The CEO
is happy, that helps all employees.

If your investor will just value you at one multiple turn more, that
is worth way more than the total cost of an SB ad.

Etc.

I am FOR most vanity reason for buying an SB ad.

Just be honest about it.

Don’t fake justify it.

Don’t torture your Analytics teams to prove that there was business
impact.

Don’t lie to your stakeholders.

A non spike & sustain SB ad is a gigantic waste of money.

A vanity one often has emotional benefits, if you have the money… Be
honest, go for it!

BOTTOM LINE.

As a part of a _spike and sustain _Brand Marketing strategy, an SB ad
can contribute a big helpful spike (harvested by sustain to create new
baselines).

That ad can have a provable short-term impact (# of People Lifted,
Cost Per Individual Lifted) and provable long-term impact (Incremental
Revenue, Cost Per Incremental Sale).

It will be even more so if you use pre-test passed creative.

When you compute the ROI, use the Total Cost of the Ad.

It is ok to buy an SB ad for vanity, just be honest.

_Carpe diem._
