TMAI #456: THE WORLD’S SIMPLEST REPORT.

[ Web Version [2] ]

When dealing with the tsunami of data we all have access to, it can be
easy to make things more complicated than they need to be.

Because there is a lot to see and show.

Because it is actually really hard for Analysts to narrow and focus
– they are often removed from business context to realize what
actually matters.

Because Senior Leaders sincerely (but in a death wish) want/ask for
more data.

Because we underappreciate the value of different layers of detail for
different layers of employee altitude.

Because sometimes a report/dashboard that has just a few things does
not look worthy of an expensive analyst (and, their next promotion).

Because, occasionally, it is helpful for an Agency to have confused
clients.

☹️

Everyone is trying to do the right thing (even that last one).

This is not appreciated enough: CHOICE OVERLOADS.

There is much research around this, but even the classic Jam
Experiment [3] is enough to give us a pause. When given less choice
(6) vs. more choice of flavors, customers were _ten times more likely
to buy!_

While we are not selling Jam, in the same spirit, I want to share the
simplest possible report you can create for an Ecommerce business. I
hope to inspire you to get back to the basics, and appreciate just how
powerful it can be in driving high-impact conversations.

[PREMIUM MEMBER BONUS: A super effective way to ensure simplification
is to leverage the KPIs, Diagnostic Metrics, and Influencing Variables
framework. Please see TMAI #448 [4].]

THE WORLD’S SIMPLEST REPORT.

It is not going to blow you away.

If anything, you will be disappointed at how unsexy it is. That in
part is its power.

The world’s simplest report answers these four questions:

*
Are we getting quality traffic?
*
Is our app/site persuading people to choose us?
*
Are we effective at converting that persuasion into revenue?
*
Are we efficient in how we are spending our Marketing budget?

That’s a lot, no?

Not really.

The simplest possible view, literally the lowest bar Zeus could
create, is that the traffic does not bounce. _Stay. Make one measly
click._ Quality, simply...

[Simple Report: Quality module.]

Given many websites can have an overall Bounce Rate (a decent
Diagnostic Metric) of 30 – 45%, getting this number to be lower is a
good signal that the traffic coming to the site might be interested in
what we have to say/sell.

Any decent Ecommerce site is good at both _SHOPPING _and
_PERSUASION_. The former being the art of helping you figure out what
you want, or simply inspire you to want to want :), and the latter is
to _persuade _you to buy from them.

A simple indication of the effectiveness of_ persuasion_ is Add to
Cart Rate.

[Simple Report: Persuasion module.]

Add to Cart is the job of the site, and not the advertising platform.
Hence, with this simple Diagnostic Metric, I’m shifting
accountability from the Marketing/Media team to the site owner. How
good are your landing pages? How persuasive is your product detail
content? What about your promo strategy? Are you taking full advantage
of AI-powered behavior targeting? Among answers for which the site
experience owner is responsible for.

Different segments of traffic will Add to Cart at different rates. Not
all traffic is equally qualified. There in are crucial learnings and
insights to action.

We are on an ecommerce site, it is not all that difficult to know what
Effectiveness is. I’m going for the best of both worlds. Focus on
Last Click Conversion Rate effectiveness, it is data that is easily
accessible. I’m using Data-Driven Attribution Revenue for each
channel, to give them full credit accounting for the consumer journey.

[Simple Report: Effectiveness module.]

(For the pedantic, like me, I know that Conversion Rate is an
efficiency metric!)

Efficiency then is ROI, to more easily compare Owned, Earned, and Paid
Media.

Return on Investment = (Revenue – Costs) / (Costs)

Ad ROI = (Ad-driven Revenue – Ad Costs) / (Ad Costs)

[Simple Report: Efficiency module.]

Owned and Earned channels don’t often get credit for the ROI they
are driving for the company. Look at Organic Search or Email. Now,
they can each only do a certain job, at a certain part of the consumer
journey. But, that’s also true for Paid Search. Compare them!

Here’s the world’s simplest report:

[The World's Simplest Report.]

There are a hundred more Diagnostic Metrics you can put in there to
add value. There are a thousand Influencing Variables that can also be
added to confuse (I mean help) the audience. Why?

Two KPIs. Four Diagnostic Metrics. Simple.

Oh, and in gets a lot better. First, a critical detour.

THE TRICKY BIG: THE DAMN TARGETS!

The difficulty, and where you shine as an Analysis Ninja, is that
tricky column called Target.

Was getting 570k people from Organic Search good? NO! We were aiming
for 650k. BOOM! There’s your value, done enough then you next job
promotion.

I love Targets for understanding _is this good, is this bad_. Even
more so, Targets are a sign of a sophisticated organization. A team
that is intentionally trying to be successful.

The problem: How do you set these golden, luscious, life-changing
Targets?

My recommendations, in order of preference:

*
USE PRE-SET TARGETS.

They’ll be created by work of your cross-functional Finance,
Marketing, Strategy teams, and a sponsoring CxO. They will be tied to
pre-set company-level financial Targets.

*
USE INDUSTRY BENCHMARKS.

Often industry associations, research organizations, vendors (Meta,
Analytics, Gartner) will publish industry benchmarks. Ex: Conversion
Rate of 2% is usually the median. Good. Start setting your Targets
there. The benefits of industry level is that you get an outside-in
view, vs. the more common naval-gazing.

*
USE AVERAGES.

You can use your past performance to set some initial Targets. _On
Average your Conversion Rate is 1.6%. You’ve invested 10 people and
$8 mil in new technology to improve it. Your new Target is 1.6%*1.4._
Or similar.

*
USE LIKE-TYPE TIME PERIODS.

Target = same month last year. Or, vs. last quarter. Or, vs. same week
last quarter. Etc. If you have a bigger team, a nicer agency, more
investment in tools etc. etc., then be sure to add a multiplier to the
_like-type _time period – else you are setting low standards.
Likewise, if you cut 50% of the employees or fired your Agency etc.,
then lower.

*
USE SEGMENTS.

Visits: 1.2 mil. But. Google = 450k. Display = 300k. Email = 200k.
Direct = 100k. Context! Use those segments to set your target (at an
aggregate level or, even better, at a segment level). You can segment
by like a million different things, pick the ones more relevant. New &
Returning anyone?

BONUS: Set Targets for Effectiveness and Efficiency. Having that
balance is key.

THE WORLD’S SIMPLEST REPORT: LAYERS OF AWESOMENESS.

The beauty of the world’s simplest report is that you can play it
like an accordion – to adapt to the altitude of the people in the
room.

For a general meeting with senior leaders, to set broader context for
the business, use LAYER ONE – above.

If the altitude is just the leaders of the Paid Media team, squeeze
the accordion, and show LAYER TWO.

[World's Simplest Report: Paid Media]

The simple report is simpler, just focuses on the three Paid channels:
Google, Meta, Affiliates in this instance.

It is perfect to reflect in aggregate with your Sr. Leaders on where
the focus for the next quarter should be: Quality? Persuasion?
(always!) Effectiveness? Efficiency?

If the Paid meeting includes a group of mixed altitude, pull the
accordion outwards and use LAYER THREE…

[World's Simplest Report: Paid Media Segments..]

Often at this layer, things can get a bit messy.

Fifteen years ago, we all purchased ads for Brand and Non-Brand
Keywords. Today, often, we continue to report in the same way, though
buying advertising with Google is transformatively different.

Today, you purchase ads on Google using three approaches:

*
MANUAL.
(Don’t do this, sadly you and I are not smarter than the AI in this
application of intelligence.)

*
BROAD MATCH & SMART BIDDING.
(Do this at the very minimum. Watch the Effectiveness and Efficiency,
above.)

*
PERFORMANCEMAX.
(Exhaust every possible way to use this – for all of the headroom
that will deliver at or above pre-set Targets. Our opinions on this
topic are irrelevant, Google’s present and future is PMAX.)

Likewise, for Meta, we do an apple-to-apple comparison with Google and
use the three primary ways in which we all buy advertising on Meta:

*
AUCTION.
(Likely a big part of your current buy. But, probably should not be.)

*
RESERVATION.
(Hopefully you are only using reach and frequency campaigns for
Brand.)

*
ADVANTAGE+
(Read above re PMAX.)

You’ve dramatically improved the effectiveness of meetings by
comparing like-type, like-sized, elements across your Paid media
spend.

If, because you carefully listen to me and do everything I recommend
(!!), you are meeting with your Agency, you are also doing active
_in-flight optimization… _You can also use the world’s simplest
report to avoid data pukes that distract.

PMAX is not just Search. It is not just Keywords.

PMAX is… Discover, Gmail, Google Display Network, Google Maps,
Google Search, Search Partners, and YouTube. _OMG, I feel exhausted
writing that!_

And, Google is already providing you the data in Google Ads to help
you understand the performance.

[Google PMAX, Advertising Options.]

The challenge is: Google is showing you parts of the Acquisition view,
none of the Behavior view, and a part of the Outcomes view (credit to
them for showing a Macro Conversion above and a Micro Conversion –
as you know I love the latter!).

Pull your world’s simplest reports accordion out a bit more, and
here’s the LAYER FOUR view for discussion with your Agency and
tactical company peers to dictate _in-flight optimization_ options.

[World's Simplest Report: PMAX segmented details.]

BOOM!

Details. 😊

Running to see your CMO, squeeze the accordion, go back to LAYER
ONE.

Same metrics. Same complete view across Quality, Persuasion,
Effectiveness, and Efficiency. Same broad big picture incentives as
the focus. Magic.

BOTTOM LINE.

More is less.

It helps to look through the same KPIs, Diagnostic Metrics, and
Influencing Variables across layers. A clear line of sight.

Make things as simple as possible, but no simpler.

_Carpe diem._
