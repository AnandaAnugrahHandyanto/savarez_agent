TMAI #412: AI SEARCH: THE SKY IS FALLING!? -P1

[ Web Version [2] ]

I am the last person to freak you out.

But, when a generational change is underway, and 30% - 50% of your
current digital revenue could be _poofed_, I'll be the first to ask
you to worry.

If you are an informed Marketer, or CMO, the evolution in Search
should be a big worry.

Specifically:

Bing, Google, Baidu are rolling out (more aggressively than
anticipated) AI Search.

The underlying tech that powers this new Search is vastly different.

_Will there be a negative impact on my Paid Search traffic? How about
my Organic Search traffic?_
Paid Search: yes.

Organic Search: YES!

In previous years, we all freaked out about every "Google Update"
rolled out to Organic Search. Compared to new AI Search (LLM-sourced
results), they were puny nothings.

I’m not recommending a full-blown freak out because as yet a minimal
number of humans are using AI Search. We have a little bit of time to
learn, understand, action.

That tiny minority of humans are like me. My default Search engine for
the last year has been www.bing.com/chat [3] (and I’m very impressed
with it). I have perplexity.ai [4] as an accessible bookmark (and I
love it).

As Google has the largest market share, most humans are experiencing
AI Search as Google has been slowly rolling out AI Overviews
(currently for an unknown percentage of queries).

It is a big change, getting Bigger.

FIRST, LET'S WORRY ABOUT PAID SEARCH.

AI influenced changes to Paid Search have been happening for some time
now. Smart Bidding, Smart Creative (GenAI), Smart Bidding, to name a
few.

At the latest Google conference, they announced many more incremental
changes that you and I can take advantage of. All cute. And, there
will be more.

ACTION: Stay in the loop when Google gets excited about the *POWER
PAIR* (Search + PMax), ensure that your Agency is actively rolling out
these AI PPC features - afterall who does not want an additional 18%
conversions!

While you will be able to keep buying ads, there are two important
changes to consider:

A. How will the ads look?
The UX has to change (no more four blue links above the fold?).
Worry level: High.

B. How will the ads be triggered?
The underlying algorithm computing Quality Score, matching the intent
to you, will have to change as AI Search takes in additional context
and intelligence in return.
Worry level: Extreme.

But. There will be ads.

Ads are the bread and butter of Search Engines today, and that will
remain the case. It is not simply self-preservation, history has
repeatedly proven that subscription-based Search Engines (or Twitter
:) is a failed scale business model. (I suspect a reality-check is
coming for my fav Perplexity).

At this moment, the implications of A and B are not entirely clear. We
are seeing early _experiments _(and some throwing spaghetti on the
wall). It is important though to actively stay in the loop.

On Bing CoPilot, I end up seeing some Paid Search ads some of the
time...

I notice they are experimenting with different formats – it is not
always the hideous and early AOL-web-like ones as you see above.

On Google, I am seeing PPC ads _deprioritized _in many cases (only
for now, I'm sure).__

Here's a representative example where the PPC ads would have shown up
right under the query box, but the AI Overview takes that spot. Then
there's a what feels like an odd organic result with the same info,
then a normal Organic result, and then below the fold (!!), the normal
three PPC ads...

We all know that nearly no one scrolls below the fold. So, if this
were your PPC ads in the past... It might be natural to get a dip in
performance. (Impact of A, User Experience).

You can also see the impact of B (how ads are triggered) above, the
Oceanic Society result is a bad ad.

This will change.

Ex, at their event Google demoed this ad format, a more past-like...

I'm unable to replicate it. But, that is ok. I simply wanted you to
see examples to unlock your imagination.

I expect all kinds of new ad formats to be on their way over the next
six months. Ensure you are on active look out (though, drops in PPC
revenue will also serve as a handy reminder).

ACTION: Search Engines will be releasing loads of Alphas and Betas
(there were many in the last month). Expect your Agencies to get you
into them so you can stay ahead of the game.

PUNCHLINE: Your Paid Search strategy will have to evolve. No need for
to freak out. If you would like to freak out, do so about B and not A.
(Even more on B in Part 2 next week's Organic C2.)

LET'S (REALLY) WORRY ABOUT ORGANIC SEARCH.

When it comes to Organic results in AI Search, three new worries
arise:

1. The FOUNDATIONAL METHODOLOGY of assessing _user intent _and
_relevance of your site to that intent_ is changing . PageRank to LLM
powered, or a hybrid - the current state on Google. We can’t
remotely guess how it works.

2. The FUNDAMENTAL USER EXPERIENCE will dramatically change. We know
there will be fewer _slots _for businesses, we can anticipate and
increase in _Zero-click Searches_, but there are additional
unknowns.

3. Today we have a lot of data (ex via Webmaster Tools) re our Organic
rankings. In AI Search, atleast for now, we LACK VISIBILITY into the
black boxes. (There is a chance we might not regain anything close to
what we have today.)
To be fair, 1 and 2 are now major worries for us AND challenges for
Bing, Google, Perplexity, Baidu, as they grapple with o_rganizing the
world's information and making it universally accessible and useful_
in an AI Search world.

Some things are going well, some rather poorly.

We have seen several iterations of Google’s attempts with _Search
Generative Experience_ (RIP).

You saw the immense sadness caused by Google Gemini’s image creator
(and it is not back yet, though it has been months).

In the last few weeks, the Socials have been flooded with Gemini
offering bizarre results in AI Overviews – which was bound to happen
when you operate a Search engine at the scale of Google.

But. Let's retain some perspective. Every major swap of technology has
issues at the start.

I have ZERO doubt that Google will figure this out. And, fast. Even
for the looooong tail queries. You will not be told to put glue on
your pizza. It will improve. Then, it will be as good as PageRank
Google. Finally, it will be significantly better than PageRank Google.

Switching the focus back to us, recipients of free traffic from
Google, we have to worry about 1, 2, and 3 - rather than wait for the
changes to catch us with our pants down, and 80% drop in traffic.

I’m noticing a dangerous trend with CxOs saying:

_We are fine, look at Google, they have no idea what they are doing!_
I have lost count of SEOs whose most current advice is:

_No, nothing has to change with AI Search. Keep doing the SEO we have
been doing for a billion years. All will be fine._
I believe these views represent a big mistake.

It is easy to get paralyzed in situations where you have little
understanding of how the new thing will work (#1), little idea about
how the new experiences will look like (#2), and zero useful data that
can spark action (#3).

But, for some sites a massive amount of free traffic will disappear.
Don't get paralyzed, and certainly don't make fun of a botched feature
roll out there or there.

Over the last six months, at our Agency Croud [5], we have been
proactively anticipating, analyzing, synthesizing all we could. We are
proactively bringing our insights to our clients to ensure they are
worried.

Not worried as in _omg, let’s all freak out!!!_

Worried as in:

_Here’s what we know so far. _
_Here are the implications we can see, here’s what we can do, so
let’s start on that. _
_And, here’s everything we still don’t know._
My counsel is the same for you: Worry (and some of you freak out a
little bit).

Let that result in a small task force – or just a part of Pascal’s
time – who’ll driving changes based on what we know. Some small.
Some medium-sized.

NEXT WEEK, ORGANIC SEARCH IMPLICATIONS.

We, certainly I, can't see everything that is going to change. From
what has already changed (1 and 2 above), and the data I've gathered
from LLMs (GPT-4o, Gemini, Llama3) I've come to the conclusion that
two profound changes are upon us re Organic Search:

C1. The Switch from "Results" to "Answer."

C2. The Switch to _true Intelligence_.
The first at the Search Engine, and the second on the Search Engine by
us (humans). The implications are profound, and I fear super not good
for some (many?).

I'll cover both of these, with examples, next week.

BOTTOM LINE.

I am sorry that this has been a bit of a downer. It is a bit of a
downer for businesses.

Still, for end users, I am actually excited about the profound
changes.

Search Engines had become a little fat and too happy, innovation had
become "innovation." AI presents a massive kick in the butt to Search
Engines. That's a good thing for users.

Still, us professionals have work to do. This week you worry about
Paid Search. Next week, we'll both freak out a bit about Organic free
traffic.
