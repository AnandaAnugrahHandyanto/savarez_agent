TMAI #454: HIGH-IMPACT CREATIVE: CONCEPT & EXECUTION PRE-TESTING.

[ Web Version [2] ]

With the Creative in your ad (/email/website/app/AR glasses)
determining much of your Marketing’s impact, up to 60% - 70%... I am
irrationally obsessed with the quality of the Creative, the quantity
of the Creative, and the human-centric (vs. CMO-centric) _insights_
that power the story your Marketing is putting into the universe.

Have you sent this report today: CONVERSION RATE BY CREATIVE_ID?

How about: COST PER INDIVIDUAL LIFTED BY PASSED VS. FAILED CREATIVE?

If you have not, you are choosing to focus on delivering 100% success
by focusing on, improving, 30% of the contributing variable. Possible,
but super challenging and rare.

For Brand Creative Concepts and Executions, and Performance Creative
Concepts, Pre-Testing the Creative, with HUMAN MADE MACHINE, offers a
specific competitive advantage: _Knowing you are going to fail to
deliver a business impact, before you spend your Marketing budget!_

In TMAI #454 [3], I'd shared how you can build this sustainable
advantage by deploying the right tool, the right methodology [4],
across the Creative Go-To-Market Lifecycle:

[Creative Go-to-Market Lifecycle.]

Most frequently, Creative Executions are pre-tested, or real-world
tested. Much better than nothing! But. It is creatively significantly
effective, and financially significantly efficient, to pre-test
Creative Concepts – the second green box above.

A great Concept and often survive some poor Executions, a poor Concept
can’t be saved by great Executions (and large Marketing spend).

With HMM Pro [5], you can start pre-testing all the way back at the
Concepting stage by developing the _boardomatic_, and testing the
_storyboard _and _narrative _of the ad. This allows you to get super
rich qualitative (emotional triggers) and quantitative (relevance,
appeal) data super early.

[Storyboard Creative Pre-Testing.]

Please review TMAI #453 [3] to reflect on the details of this
journey, via the example of the creative process at Coach [6] as
they deliver profit impacting Brand Marketing.

CONCEPT TESTING STAGE 2: UNFINISHED ASSETS.

As you are heading to the exit point of Concept GTM stage (third green
box), the objective is to get critical data to inform your Creative
Executions.

The key areas of interest at Stage 2 are:

*
Brand Lift (THE campaign objective).

*
Diagnostic Metrics.

*
Audience Sentiment.

*
Message Comprehension.

*
Key Scenes Recall.

The Creative vision, the stories being told, the underlying deep
_purpose_ they are trying to connect with, all remain EMPHATICALLY
those of the Creative leadership and team. All three, deeply human and
not algorithms or AI (for now!). Pre-testing analysis helps understand
if the technical and strategic choices in _how_ the story is being
told is _making it_ to the intended campaign audience.

The first analysis you should expect is tied to your PRE-CHOSEN KPI,
and its diagnostic metrics. _Is this expression of our creative going
to deliver Brand Lift that will be profitable?_

[Unfinished Asset Creative Pre-Testing.]

Thanks to the _storyboard_ pre-testing, we have a great yield. One
star asset (76) one really star close (61), one fixable (49), and one
fell short (38).

One of HMM’s superpowers is the pre-testing is done in the _NATIVE
MEDIA ENVIRONMENTS_. Two of the ads above are unskippable (15s), and
two will be delivered in skippable environments (30s versions). Notice
the immense value of column 3: The two 30s ads in a skippable
environment are viewed for only 6.3 seconds!

If your pre-testing vendor is testing the ad in an isolated
environment or without the target audience or using some
“algorithmic” approach… You would fail to realize the challenges
you will face re delivering business results with your 30s ads.

You would also not know to give your creative team a thousand hugs
that OP 30 version 2 is so great that even with a Median view of 6.3
seconds, its Creative Effectiveness Score is so high. Cut that into a
6s non-skip execution immediately!

Once the Creative team has their to-do list for weaker unfinished
Concept versions, a deeper analysis across the _Influencing Variables_
driving the _Diagnostic Metrics_ are super helpful.

[Unfinished Assets Pre-Testing Detailed Results.]

The qualitative analysis behind this data helped identify the critical
role of the music chosen in different versions, the time on screen for
product and features, as well as the overall ad visual mood choices
that trigger emotional connections.

Super immediately actionable.

Remember… These insights are coming back from your intended Target
Audience, before really big $$$ are being spent learning all this
after the campaign is live.

In your case, if you’ve hired an expensive talent to be in your ads,
in one or more countries, you also get analysis that measure: 1.
Talent Recognition 2. Talent Named 3. Talent Favorability 4. Talent
Fit, and 5. Talent Styling.

This analysis was super helpful for a different client recently.

The Talent hired was super expensive, and a bold choice. During
pre-testing, the above five-dimensional analysis highlighted two
bits… Good: High recognition, named. Concerning: Lower favorability,
divisive with target audience.

It led to this important conversation (before the campaign was seen by
millions of people): _Is the bold choice we were going for still
something we want to choose? What if the negativity blew up when the
campaign was live?_

Not the objective: Let the data decide yes or no on the Talent based
on scores.

The objective: Understand implications. Make an informed choice. Be
ready (a _pull back and recovery_ plan).

Data is an essential dimension to influence your decision, but not the
only one.

Having used nearly every Creative Pre-Testing vendor in the market, in
the US, EU, APAC, one of my frustrations is that you get all kinds of
different scores, assessed using different methodologies [4], and yet
in the end you don’t know what to do to make the creative better.

Hence, every meeting with Charmaine or Asha at HMM, I’m on my
_Actionability High Horse_. “Does the client know what to do, after
we ship the analysis over?”

An excellent example of actionability… Scene Recall Analysis...

[Unfinished Assets Pre-Testing, Scene Recall.]

These are the Scene Recall from two different 30 second unfinished
Concepts that were pre-tested.

One leverages a storytelling element that knocks it out of the part at
the start (never ever compromise on this). The other has a slow build,
but a massive crescendo 2/3rds of what way through!

The Creative team can reflect on the role of each storytelling
element, does it make sense to combine them into one Creative, or mine
the insight from fg% emotional resonance and bring it into the second
storytelling expression, or something else.

Actionability! 😊

PRE-TESTING STAGE 3: FINAL EXECUTIONS & PRE-LAUNCH OPTIMIZATIONS.

Reflect on the partnership between your Creative Team/Agency with the
actual Target Audience thus far.

Your insights from storyboarding stage helped you create super strong
(more likely to win) unfinished assets. Big Creative and Financial
win.

Then, your assessment and reimagination of the unfinished assets power
the final Executions. Even bigger Creative and Financial win.

At this last stage, the testing focuses on:

*
Brand Lift (campaign Primary KPI).

*
Diagnostic Metrics (for Primary KPI).

*
Audience Sentiment (for Primary KPI).

Basically, you want to tie a bow on the campaign’s _true north_.

Since for us, this is a global campaign, our CMO would naturally
expect a global view for our Primary KPI, Unaided Brand Awareness
(UBA).

[Creative Execution Pre-Testing, Global Scorecard.]

In the scores, it is fascinating to see how Executions of the same
Concept are translating across cultures and environmental variables.
From time to time, one region will go against another. It is more fun,
and baffling, when you see, say, the UK and China ganging up against
all the other countries!

[Bonus Note: All of you _ANALYSIS NINJAS_ will remember from our Brand
Measurement Series that it is not possible to measure UBA on platforms
like YouTube. A real bummer. Because, UBA is a super critical business
metric, tied to long-term financial success. So, we solve for it, but
on YouTube we measure Consideration because it is all they can measure
with BLS. Don’t ever not do the right thing for the business because
of a _measurement problem._ For larger campaigns, we put in place
custom Campaign Lift Studies, which do let us measure UBA.]

Once you have the Primary KPI squared away, for the winners and the
losers, you want to dive into key details and understand the
contributions of the Influencing Variables.

For example, the scores for Brand Engagement…

[Pre-Testing Brand Engagement Report.]

One of the critical reasons you are seeing so much green at this
stage, (it is an unusually higher yield rare), is that the Pre-Testing
started at Concept Storyboarding phase. If you start to let your
target audience in early, you win a lot more in the end. You don’t
and 9 of 11 Creatives fail outright, and you have to do emergency
surgery to ensure you have enough for a five sequence VAS execution on
YouTube.

In this instance we are in good shape for Japan, we have a 30s and a
15s that score well above norms, we have another 15s that could be
fixed quickly (learn from what worked with the 30s for Enjoyment).

I’ve emphasized through this series that quantitive analysis is
helpful, and qualitative analysis is the linchpin.

With HMM Pro [5] you get an incredible amount of clustered
pattern-matching of the qualitative data. Just to give you a sense of
the types of surprises in it… Here’s reflections from the actual
Target Audience about Cinematography! It was a new one for me. 😊

[Pre-Testing Qualitative Clustered Analysis.]

At the Creative Execution stage, I pay particular attention to the
qualitative analysis because there are nuances hidden there that can
impact long-term financial impact.

Likewise, you want to understand the positives and negatives for the
core storytelling dimensions, especially for your Brand Marketing
campaigns.

Ex: Emotional Resonance…

[Creative Pre-Testing, Emotional Resonance Analysis.]

For a different client, for a recent campaign, the Negative Resonance
had qualitative data that was surprising, and on a vector that held
potential to blow up on social.

In the past, we would not have known. Now, the leadership team can
have a discussion on if they want to take an informed risk, and still
go forward with it. The decision was to do exactly that, but I
insisted that a plan be put in place between the Client, Agency, Ad
Platform, PR, to deal with the blow up, god forbid should that
occur.

The benefit of knowing things in advance.

BOTTOM LINE.

Brand Marketing is hard.

Everyone’s damn suspicious that it actually works. Especially your
CFO.

In part because IF Brand Marketing works, the financial results show
up months (and months!) later – assuming you have the sophistication
to measure them (reminder below!).

Rather than doing your best, and hoping for the best months (and
months!) later… Why not do your best, Pre-Test your Creative, pick
the winning ones to put your Brand budget on, and then just wait for
the successful results to roll in months (and months!) later?

Carpe diem.
