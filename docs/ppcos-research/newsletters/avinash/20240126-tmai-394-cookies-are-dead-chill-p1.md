TMAI #394: 🍪 COOKIES ARE DEAD!! CHILL. -P1

[ Web Version [2] ]

We heard it again two weeks ago.

_OMG, COOKIES ARE DEAD!! __
_
_Evil, soulless Google, crushing baby hearts for profits! Pitch
forks!!!__
_
The latest iteration was a 1% test of turning off third-party (3P)
cookies in Chrome – one of the browsers in the world. This 1%,
potentially increases to, perhaps, all 100% Chrome users having 3P
cookies turned off by default, maybe, by the end of 2024.

There were op-eds and breathless articles, heralding the death of the
advertising ecosystem and the collapse of the US economy. Random
TikTokers and Analytics “Gurus” jumped to the _death of digital
analytics_, _death of “personalized” advertising_ (as if that were
a bad thing), _get clean rooms, _and magical elixirs you could buy
from them to “_reverse the losses_.”

While you can’t seem my face, I suspect my words are communicating a
distaste for the entire charade.

It irritates me. It should irritate you.

In part because it is an unnecessary distraction. Ex: I'm sure your
CMO has asked you: _What is our future in a cookieless world!? _In
part because most companies barely use the extraordinary amount of
data they have, and now contracts will be commissioned, futile
development in systems, math, and more, that will barely add any
value.

[In a recent engagement, I asked five senior leaders of a 100% digital
massive business: What’s your Checkout Abandonment rate? None of
them knew. This is, sadly, normal.]

The COOKIES! freakout also represents a lack of sound understanding of
what 3P cookies do. How does 3P tracking work? Are they effective at
“personalized ads”? Where are they applied? Oh, and, what do we
measure with them?

Let's fix that today. By the end of this newsletter, I hope that
you'll be able to figure out what action you can take that will impact
your company's profits - and what NOT to do. :)

I invite your reflections, please reply.__

WHAT THE HECK ARE THIRD-PARTY (3P) COOKIES?

Simply:

When you visit NYTimes.com, they set a first-party (1P) cookie (a tiny
text file) on your browser that allows them to recognize it is you. As
in, "you" an anonymous string of characters (in that text file) to be
recognized across visits to NYT. Or, you as in Avinash is a paid
subscriber to NYT, he has logged into his account, recognize him,
don’t make him log in again and again.

During that visit, the NYT also sets like 40,000* third-party (3P)
cookies (also tiny text files). One, as an example, might be on behalf
of yahoo.com who is powering the display ad platform on NYT. This
cookie has good intent. Yahoo wants to know how many ads were served
by NYT, how many of those who watched display ads for Duracell
purchased products on Duracell.com. Or, and now you are on a slippery
slope, how many people who saw Duracell ads on NYT also purchased
something on united.com and also read the Washington Post.

In addition to making sure NYT does not make me log-in with every page
view, 1P cookies have been typically used in your digital analytics
tools. Adobe and Google Analytics, as an example, are entirely powered
by 1P cookies.

There are a number of minor and two major uses of 3P cookies.

The first major use is to observe your behavior/interests, and serve
you “personalized ads.” Yes, yes, the ads you saw for Lubricating
Grease for Toyota Prius in your face, two weeks after you purchased
the grease.

The second major use case is to prove that your 1P analytics tools are
dead wrong about Display ads having the least quality, lowest
converting traffic to your site. Yes, yes, yes, those 18 ads that
convert a 300-word article into 9 pages long.
IMPORTANT: Chrome is one of the last browsers to block 3P cookies by
default. Mozilla, Safari, others, got into this years ago.
Furthermore, people who spend a bit more time in the tech space, like
me, have blocked 3P cookies for decades as they serve nearly zero user
purpose.

[* 40k is an exaggeration. When you visit NYT only 53 different
companies [3], including some surprises, are dropping 3P cookies, and
collecting your cross-websites behavior data.]

SO, WHAT’S ACTUALLY GOING AWAY?

1P COOKIES ARE NOT GOING ANYWHERE - that would break the internet as
it currently functions.

Chrome is starting its slow walk to joining all other browsers in
TURNING 3P COOKIES OFF by default.

A sinkhole did not open up and swallow Australia when Mozilla
(first!), Safari, and others default blocked 3P cookies. It would be
unlikely to happen when Chrome joins others to default off 3P cookies.

[NOTE: The User Experience and Measurement function 1P cookies serve
is too important. It is possible 1P cookies go away some day. If they
do, you should expect something else to take its place to allow the
Internet to function. If history offers a lesson, when 1P cookies go
away it is to be expected the replacement would likely allow for even
more tracking!]

WHAT’S THE IMPACT OF 3P COOKIES DYING?

Joy.

Ok, ok, I’m teasing 3P cookies.

While the original intent was sound, 3P cookies offer nearly no User
value.

1.

On paper, “tracking of humanity” using anonymized tracking cookies
will be harder, and ad platforms will have to come up with a new way
to “understand” a browser, and convert that “understanding”
into “personalized” ads.

The team at Google has come up with numerous ideas that have received
a sound spanking by everyone else. But. They will come up with
something – too much money is riding on it.

It seems the replacement will likely move away an “individual”
tracked to “_Avinash, Pooja, Juliette, Alex, Joon, and Sandeep all
seem to love double chocolate chip cookies and Bibimbap._”  That
group/micro-segment will now, hopefully, be served a more relevant ad
– without a single ad impression knowing if that impression was to
Joon or myself.

That’s progress.

It is not finalized exactly how 3P death replacements will work,
we’ll all keep an eye on that.

2.

The second impact I’m more excited about: REALITY CHECKS FOR
DISPLAY, VIDEO ADS.

If you open your digital analytics tool, it will extremely quickly
show you that Display ads, and many Video ad formats as well, have the
lowest conversion rates. _A billion impressions turned into two
conversions!_

The Display ad serving platforms, Google, Amazon, FB, everyone else,
will quickly say _hey look at Attribution, did you not hear Avinash
say last-click reporting sucks?_

I did.

So, you look at the default MTA in your analytic tool… And, the
utterly tiny conversion of marginal business value, look a tiny scooch
better. _One billion impressions delivered two last-click conversions
and three assisted conversions!_

Still not sweet, given how much money you’ve spent on the campaign.

And, now the ad serving platforms pull their final ace:

_Ok, ok, ok, sure if you look at last-click or attribution
perspective, we look terrible. BUT, have you looked at view-through
conversions? _
_
HA! __
_
_You have no idea that the billions of Display impressions we are
serving cause a subliminal wave that tugs heartstrings in humans,
without the humans ever realizing that. When they sleep, this
subliminal wave gets converted into an urgent lust for your product.
They wake up, and rush to your site by direct typing the URL, or worse
typing your brand name into Google, and purchase the product
instantly._
_
We are being cheated! You should look at view-through conversions._
When you do look at _view-through conversions_, it is not easy, but
when you do, it will indicate that Display ads, and many Video ads,
are not terrible. They are pretty decent.

How do ad platforms pull this trick?

As humanity trawls the web, ad platforms drop trillions of cookies on
billions of browsers. So, this happens:

I went to NYT. Got my 40k 3P cookies.

Did not notice any Display ads, I’ve been patterned to have banner
blindness – just like you.

500 of those Display ads that I did not see, most of which were below
the fold which I did not scroll past, were for Expedia.

These 500 “views” are recorded by the 3P cookie for the ad
platform.

Two months later, I purchase a flight on Expedia.com. I was influenced
by their latest brand campaign on TV. [Have you seem those cute yellow
ads? I love the one with the woman and the dog looking for a sunny
vacation.]

Expedia gets sent a report:

Yes. Avinash did not click on the ad.

Yes. Avinash does not show up as our credit in the Attribution
reports.

He is in the _view-through conversion__s_ report!!

Pay us a bounty!

And, buy a crap load more of Display ads!!!

Your Agency has been dutifully obliging by spending millions more of
your budget on Display ads.

The thing practiced by the ad platforms is known as COOKIE BOMBING.
They throw so many, many cookies from everyone and all things on all
our browsers… They can always claim “view-through.”

[ADVANCED NOTE: The behavior above also majorly harms the quality of
answers you get via your Media Mix Models, unless you are taking
clever specific actions to mitigate the problem.]

On paper, this ability to cookie bomb will go away in Chrome – when,
one day, it gets to 100% 3P cookie blocking by default.

There are other smaller impacts from 3P cookies going away, a couple
minor use cases we might miss every 29th of Feb.

In large part, it is basically: Death of horrendously bad
_personalized _ads. Death of incorrect claims of _view-through
conversions_ via cookie bombing – primarily impacting Display and
some Video ads.

SMARTER ANALYTICS: _NEVER LET A GOOD CRISIS GO TO WASTE._

Intelligent Agencies and Analysts have been bursting the bubble of
view-through conversions inflating credit for Display, and some Video
ads, by using true Test/Control experiments.

[Geo, not Audience. Premium Members: TMAI #373: Game-Changing
Analytics: GEO Experiments. [4]]

These experiments will demonstrate reality clearly, and often show
that the poor conversion rates match Users ignoring most Display ads
due to their poor quality and worse UX.

If you have not done that. Do that.

Stop wasting money.

NEXT WEEK_._

3P cookies going away, the loss of some data from the Consent Mode in
the EU, the DMA coming into effect end of March, etc., are also a
wake-up call for us to continue to invest in beyond cookies solutions
to understand digital user experience and effectiveness-efficiency of
our Owned, Earned, and Paid Media spend.

We touched on using large scale geo-experiments as one approach. Next
week, we will look at FIVE MORE APPROACHES that you should invest in
so that you will be able to do intelligent analysis of your digital
performance.

BOTTOM LINE.

Do not freak out, about cookies dying.

Even if both 3P and 1P cookies are dead tomorrow, you have more than
enough data to double your conversion rates.

Reorganizing your Marketing org to ensure Marketers across
Acquisition, Behavior, Outcomes work together will deliver
significantly higher ROI than “clean rooms.”

Answering_ what can we do tomorrow to make our customers happier,
_will deliver higher ROI than the miss from _view-through
conversions._

So… Chill.
