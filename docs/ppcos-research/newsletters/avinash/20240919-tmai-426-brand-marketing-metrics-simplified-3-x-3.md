TMAI #426: BRAND MARKETING METRICS SIMPLIFIED | 3 X 3

[ Web Version [2] ]

Brand Marketing is such a fragile exercise.

Nearly all of it is disappointing (once you step out of the CMO
bubble).

If you find really good examples [3], they tend to be from small
companies (with tiny budgets, a lot to lose).

There is a lot that causes poor brand Marketing.

Start with hubris. Add a pinch of not understanding what the USP is
(for the brand or the product). Throw not pre-testing creative [4]. A
lack of understanding key executional concepts like wear-in, frequency
shaping, media minimums, etc. Wrap all of that in a bow of a culture
lacking all accountability mechanism, and you have yourself the recipe
for a consistent lack of any success.

In this reality, being able to discover that your brand Marketing is
not working (or sometimes even sucks) can be a superpower.

To earn this superpower, you have to muddle through a lot of… dirt.

When I ask PERPLEXITY _what metrics are used to measure the impact of
a brand marketing campaign_, it mostly provides dirt. The second
source it is drawing from recommends these nine:

Key Brand Awareness Metrics:

1.    Brand impressions

2.    Presence Score

3.    Website traffic

4.    The volume of mentions

5.    Brand search volume

6.    The share of voice

7.    Social media and non-social reach

8.    Social media engagement

9.    Customer feedback
A weird, weird, weird list.

I’ll pull up two crucial bits:

A. They are all activity metrics, none are outcomes.

B. Notice the conflation of brand and performance marketing - always a
kiss of death.
The end result is you are chasing ghosts.

Let’s not.

PROLOGUE.

Before I wow you with the three _jobs to be done_ by brand Marketing,
let’s appreciate a key nuance.

There are a thousand metrics for every type of measure in the
universe. You know them, you get the data pukes every day at your job.
:)

There's a simple way to solve this conundrum: Understand not all
metrics are the same.

Metrics can be classified into three types:

SUCCESS KPIS. The metrics that ultimately identify the outcome of the
Marketing initiative.

DIAGNOSTIC METRICS: The metrics analyzed to identify why our Marketing
worked (or not).

IN-FLIGHT OPTIMIZATION METRICS: Low signal sparks/metrics that help
identify tweaks to make in-flight to get even better results – while
we are still spending money.

[Premium member bonus: TMAI #172: Metrics | Let's Call Cows Cows. [5]]

Today, our focus will be on Success KPIs.

Diagnostic and In-flight metrics are helpful, but it is career
enhancing to realize that they are only measuring activity and not the
outcomes.

A second crucial bit to hold in mind:

THE PURPOSE OF A BRAND MARKETING IS TO CHANGE SOMEONE’S MIND.
In the short-term, that is the outcome you are measuring.

In the long-term, when done right, there is immense proof that brand
Marketing can build a competitive advantage, it can drive higher
review, it can cause you to charge higher prices, and it can result in
your customers becoming evangelists of your brand.

All of those bits are entirely measurable.

[Premium member bonus: TMAI #411: Proving Marketing’s
Incrementality: The Tech Stack. [6]]

Today, our focus is on the short-term (months) outcomes. This is to
ensure, if your company has a culture of accountability, you can
identify if your brand Marketing is delivering the immediate goal it
is meant to deliver (without which… obviously.... it will deliver
diddly squat in the long-term).

Ready?

BRAND MARKETING'S (ONLY!) THREE _JOBS TO BE DONE_.

I’ve divided all brand Marketing (of the paid media type) into doing
just three jobs:

JTBD 1. Brand for me.

JTBD 2. Stand out in category.

JTBD 3. Stand out product.
Life is not black or white, there are always some grays.

It is my belief that you get to the grays when you understand the
backs and whites. If you start with the grays, you get stuck, and you
never get to the blacks and whites. #careerbyebye

Each of these three jobs translates into a goal you are trying to
accomplish.

JTBD 1. PURPOSE-LED BRAND BUILDING.

Communicating the deep essence of your brand’s beliefs and intrinsic
motivations. It could be making people feel empowered, it could be to
leave the planet in a better condition than you found it, it could be
reducing the pain in the field of dentistry.

Not every brand needs _purpose-led brand building_ to be immensely
successful (think Crest, Black & Decker, Heico, Toyota, JP Morgan, the
list is endless).

In world where attention is scarce, memories even more scarce, the
pinnacle of Brand Marketing is managing to succeed at _purpose-led
brand building._  It takes time. It is very, very expensive (in both
the _talk_ and the _walk_).

JTBD 2. “YOU ARE IN THE TOP THREE BRANDS, NEXT TIME I’M IN THE
MARKET TO MAKE A PURCHASE.”

There are a hundred companies that make jackets, drill bits, shoes,
tables, wifi routers, crm software, analytics tools, paint, powder
coating machines… What makes your brand so special in its category?

If you stand out in the category, you have a big leg up on multiple
dimensions (_Think-Do-Care_ clusters). I choose Mammut [7] because in
their category they stand head and shoulders above their competition.
Lenovo, same. Chevy, ditto. Fluke [8], love 'em.

Doing brand Marketing to get into someone's top three is difficult,
and incredibly worthy.

JTBD 3. “YOU ARE MY #1 CHOICE, NEXT TIME I MAKE A PURCHASE.”

If I’m looking for a hiking shoe, I start with Oboz – fav:
Sawtooth II [9]. There are many brands I appreciate in that category
(ex: Danner [10], check out why [10]), but my number one choice is
Oboz.

I am sure, well before you are in the market for a product/service,
you have a #1 choice most of the time.

It could be that the company has done a great brand Marketing
campaign, consistently, to tell the story of that particular product.
It could be that your parents used Crest and now you do too. It could
be that your spouse keeps raving about how fantastic Capsule CRM [11],
even scrolling on the home page is a delight.

Doing brand Marketing to get to be someone's #1 _next time _choice
is freaking hard, and insanely rewarding.
Each of these jobs to be done, to achieve the goals above, has a
Success KPI associated with it - to clearly indicate success or lack
thereof:

JTBD 1. Unaided Brand Awareness.

JTBD 2. Consideration.

JTBD 3. (Purchase) Intent.
That’s it.

Three simple purposes of all of brand Marketing and three direct
Success KPIs.

Everything else masquerading as a KPI is a _diagnostic
metric _or _in-flight optimization metric - _both clusters of come
value, neither a business outcome, though.

WATCHOUT #1.

I’ve had CMO’s stuff in intermediate steps, like _there is an
Influence stage in between Unaided Brand Awareness and Consideration._

I welcome this idea just as much as hugging a pillow made of thorns.

Nearly all teams cannot even solve for Unaided Brand Awareness or
Consideration – crystal clear jobs to be done and goals to be hit.
If you think you can further sub-nuance in-between those and deliver
success… I hate to be a mean teen but _good luck with that_. (I'm
sorry!)

My advice remains to focus on KPIs, goals, jobs-to-be-done that are
separate enough, clear enough, that anyone can understand what they
are, and solve for them (from your internal teams to agencies to
creative group to media execution).

WATCHOUT #2.

Nearly all tools by default measure Aided Awareness. It is easier to
do so, and easier still to move.

20 mins after and ad impression on Facebook: _Do you recall seeing an
ad for Booking.com?_ I’ll let you reflect on everything wrong with
that.

I will share the caution that across Agencies, Universities, Research
Institutions, no one has managed to tie a change in Aided Awareness to
Financial Success.

Meanwhile, many entities have done so for Unaided Brand Awareness –
even tying it to a change in stock price!

Never measure AIDED AWARENESS (or its worse cousin: AD RECALL). Always
measure Unaided Brand Awareness.

WHY OBSESS ABOUT THE BIG 3 _JOBS TO BE DONE_?

Creative!

It is all about the Creative.

You change someone’s mind – the purpose of brand Marketing – by
telling a story. In words, images, videos, product experience, and
service.

For the first three, you need exceptional Creative.

When you give your team clear marching orders:

_The job to be done is to get someone to remember our brand to a
degree that they could recall the brand without being prompted._

Q: Which brand of handbags have you heard of? A: _Loewe._

BOOM!
They know the extraordinary challenge it presents. Mechanically, the
KPI is _Unaided Brand Awareness_, but it is clarifying the job to be
done underlines just how extraordinarily difficult the challenge is.
That helps the team build break-through Creative.

Then, when you step into top three and top one, the team understands
the challenge of being able to stand out in the category and standing
out in a cluster of similar products – and they will build
appropriately focused Creative.

It is all about getting the Creative right – the Creative is
responsible for 55% - 70% of the success of brand Marketing.

I really, _really, _want you to get the dramatic difference in
creative required for each _job to be done, _why not having the
extreme clarity I'm recommending above in your Creative Briefs can
result in _failing before the campaign starts_. Here are examples of
Creatives by each_ job to be done:_

JTBD 2: STAND OUT CATEGORY:

JBTD 3: STAND OUT PRODUCT:

SUPER BONUS BRAND METRICS LESSON #1.

I've written about this in earlier Premium editions, but quickly...
For each Success KPI, you'll measure just three things...

Points of Lift in Success KPI.

Number of Individuals Lifted. (Effectiveness)

Cost Per Individual Lifted. (Efficiency)

The same three measures, for each Success KPI.
SUPER BONUS BRAND METRICS LESSON #2.

Sharing again here for convenience... The formula to measure Number of
Individuals Lifted:

= % lift * target audience % reach * target audience size

Example: 2% lift * 20% reach * 1,000,000 target audience size = 4,000
individuals.
You are welcome! ; )

BOTTOM LINE.

Successful brand Marketing campaigns are extremely rare.

A major contributor to that universal failure is an inability to have
a clear purpose in mind. Or, worse, having multiple cross-purposes!

I hope you'll choose to simplify and focus.

Pick one, and only one, _job to be done_. Build Creative for that one
_job to be done. _Ensure that Creative (both concept and execution)
passes pre-testing. Demand that your Agency meets media sufficiency
requirements for each channel for that specific _job to be done_.

That is all it takes.

Carpe diem.
