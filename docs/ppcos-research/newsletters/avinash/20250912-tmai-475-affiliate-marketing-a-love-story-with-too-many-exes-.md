TMAI #475: AFFILIATE MARKETING:
A LOVE STORY WITH TOO MANY EXES.

[ Web Version [2] ]

The great thing about Affiliate Marketing is a lot of people are
making money – though often it is not the Brand spending money on
Affiliate Marketing.☹️

That comes as a surprise, as nothing’s more _PAY-FOR-PERFORMANCE_
than Affiliate Marketing.

_I will find you customers you would NEVER get otherwise. I will
deliver a direct conversion, in exchange for a cash kickback. _

What’s not to love about that?

Let’s find out.

WHAT IS AFFILIATE MARKETING?

A PUBLISHER (Affiliate) “talks about,” the products/services that
you the MERCHANT (Advertiser) sells. Often that “talking about”
is an ad. A human (CUSTOMER), clicks on a link or copies a coupon
code. God willing, they make a purchase on a 1P or 3P website/app.

In 2025, this “talks about” is a diverse set of activities.

*
CONTENT & EDITORIAL. Articles in Vogue or Wirecutter or BBC Sounds
podcasts.

*
INFLUENCERS & CREATORS. Short and long form videos, often reviews or
promos, on TikTok, YouTube.

*
COUPON & DEAL SITES. RetailMeNot, Slickdeals.  Delivering “cost
conscious” customers, without you needing to spread discounts far
and wide. On Paper!

*
CASH-BACK & LOYALTY PORTALS. Cousins of #3. Rakuten, Honey Gold (GPT
their recent history, #omg).

*
BROWSER EXTENSIONS. Cousins of #4. Capital One Shopping, MSN. They
“auto apply” coupon codes when they notice a “Enter Coupon
Code” box on your site.

*
COMPARISON ENGINES & SHOPPING SEARCH. Skyscanner, Google Shopping,
Trivago.

*
PAID-SEARCH AFFILIATES. Some of your “Partners” will bid on
non-brand terms (hopefully under strict rules).

*
TECH & API PARTNERS. Buy-Now-Pay-Later widgets on your site, card
linked offer networks.

It is hard enough to execute one of these in a way that drives
INCREMENTAL profits, it is incredibly challenging to do even three of
these well. You lay out the fragmentation at any large company, the
siloed execution, and lack of measurement… It is not hard to see why
the only people making money are _publishers _and middle-persons
(affiliate program management software providers).

[NOTE: I’m ignoring the impact of AI on Affiliate Marketing. From
AI Slop that not masquerades as _Content_, or bots clicking links at
ever higher rates, or the ease of _ChatGPT product reviews_ which
makes 50 different reviews by 50 different affiliates look/feel/sound
the same, AI Overviews in Google diminishing traffic to Affiliates,
etc. etc. These pressures apply to other Marketing we are doing as
well. Please ensure you are reflecting on them for Affiliates.]

On paper, Affiliate Marketing works on outcomes. The _merchant _(you
the Brand) tracks clicks, with referral information and/or the coupon
code. If there’s an outcome (a visit, a lead, a micro-outcome, a
sale, paid account opened), the _merchant _will pay the _publisher_.

If you are an intelligent merchant, you’ll pay for outcomes to the
right of the image:

[Affiliate Marketing: Commission Scenarios.]

A sign of less intelligence being applied is if you hear this language
in your company:

*
_We get so much traffic from Affiliates. _

SHOULD YOU DO AFFILIATE MARKETING?

With humility, let me challenge you with some _strategic negativity_.

This applies to many businesses…

*
Do you really want customers who only purchased from you because you
gave them a discount or cash back?

*
Do you really want customers who perceive the value of your
products/services to be so undifferentiated that they need to be
bribed to buy from you?

*
Is your business failing so much that you could use a few low to zero
margin one-time customers?

If the answer to one or more of these questions is YES, then have an
Affiliate Marketing strategy. And, I’m being serious. There are
companies in such trouble or such undifferentiated markets that this
is how they can make a little bit more money or a lot more money.

As someone who works on Strategy, I urge you to ask one more question:

*
Are you sure that your product is so magnificent, it delivers such
excess value, that the customer would not need to be bribed again?

If the answer to this prompt is YES, go.

If the answer to this prompt is NO… You’ve acquired a bad drug
habit with Affiliate Marketing.

PIERCING THE HYPE ABOUT AFFILIATE MARKETING.

Remember, we are talking about the original pay-for-performance
strategy. _I will find your customers you would NEVER get otherwise,
in exchange for a cash kickback._

Reality slaps back.

A. CANNIBALIZATION.

Recognize this: The moment a customer notices you have a “Enter
Coupon Code” box on your site, they run to these sites to deliver
this double whammy: The Brand loses 15% due to coupon from the
bottom-line instantly. They also pay a “finder’s fee” to the
affiliate “partner.”

The word cannibalism is insufficient to capture the heartache.

Coupon codes are used across multiple tactics mentioned above:
Podcasts, Affiliates, Browser Extensions, Comparison Sites, etc.

B. INCREMENTALITY.

_How many customers would you lose if they did not see you listed on
Cash Back websites?_

_What’s the loss in Profit from having one fewer, or no, BNPL
widget? _

_What if, stay with me, this one’s scary, you did not pay
Influencers to shill you?_

That’s the concept of Incrementality. [TMAI #411: Proving
Marketing’s Incrementality. [3]]

When measured, Affiliate Marketing shows low levels of incrementality.
If the incrementality is low, then your acquisition costs skyrocket.
Instead of MTA Cost Per Affiliate Sale of $18, your Cost per
Incremental Affiliate Sale is now $238 (note: real numbers from a
client).

If you have an active Affiliate Marketing program, it is critical that
you understand, appreciate, and, if possible, account for the above
two behaviors in your assessment of value to your company.

Remember, the thing we call opportunity cost is real… In a world
where new possibilities emerge every month.

AFFILIATE MARKETING: STRATEGIC ANALYTICS.

The _claimed Sales_ by your Commission Junction, Rakuten, other
reports is often significantly higher than what your Google Analytics
Last click is reporting. I’ve discussed the pernicious problem of
cookie-bombing inflating all sorts of claimed sales numbers by
multiple types of advertisers. Your MTA reports (click based only,
please) give you an initial sense of fairness re value of your
Affiliate program.

Attribution is not incrementality [4]. If you measure nothing or last
click, measure Affiliate Attributed revenue. Know, it is not enough
justification.

No matter who you are, you can measure incrementality – though if
your primary expression of Affiliate Marketing is in terms of traffic
driven, expect massive internal/Agency resistance. Neither will want
to measure incrementality.

1. ON – OFF MEASUREMENT.

Last month, Amazon pulled out of Google Shopping entirely. 100%.
Turned it off.

Guess what happens?

Amazon can measure incrementality!

On – Off measurement is a blunt force instrument. It is also not
optimal re understanding the influence of seasonality, competition, or
ecosystem forces. Ex: If during Black Friday Amazon might have seen
higher incrementality from Google Shopping, vs. in Aug. they won’t
know that.

On – Off measurement also runs into _emotional hurdles_. The
Affiliate team/Manager/VP does not want to lose power, perhaps the VP
of Digital does not want to “lose” the 6% of sales coming via
Affiliate, and there’s an _unknown unknowns_ worry and
hypotheticals.  It is enough to kill On – Off measurement.

In the present moment, there is often so much cannibalization and such
little incrementality from Affiliate Marketing that I’m an advocate
for On – Off measurement, even with all of the flaws. The upside of
knowing _let’s double down on this_ OR _jebus we need to get out of
this and put money into AEO _is super attractive.

[On – Off can also prove cannibalization. Though managing coupon
code names intelligently, pulsing them, limiting them to only New
Customers, removing the coupon field in checkout for a bit etc., are
less blunt force ways to measure coupon cannibalization.]

2. LARGE-SCALE EXPERIMENTS.

I’ve written a detailed TMAI Premium on this topic [5]. Please
review it here.

Briefly…

With erosion of identity, third-party cookies, login-states, multiple
browsers/devices… I’m wary of audience-based test-control
experiments. It is very hard to keep the groups clean. Please avoid
audience-based digital experiments.

Instead, geo-based splits allow for far better ability to match and
keep clean test-control groups.

LSE offer the cleanest methodology for measuring true incrementality.

3. SYNTHETIC CONTROLS.

For large companies, it can be difficult to experiment yourself out of
all the possible scenarios. They are simply spending too much money on
too many things, all at the same time. [Which, I do not recommend!
Marketing works best as _narrow deep vs. shallow wide_ and as _spike
and sustain_ vs. _spike and silence_.]

In such instances, we can use statistical techniques to create
artificial control groups. This also works where geographic or
temporal testing is not feasible. By modeling what performance would
have been like without Affiliate marketing (in this case) based on
historical data and external variables, you can get a _better sense_
for incrementality.

Then, you _trust, but verify_ the most compelling results from
Synthetic Controls analysis with Large-Scale Experiments.

AFFILIATE MARKETING: MARKETING ANALYTICS.

Now that we have the _higher order bits_ squared away, it is time for
the month-to-month and day-to-day exciting grind of measurement.

As you’ve come to learn, my framework is: Success KPIs, Diagnostic
Metrics, & Influencing Variables. [TMAI Premium #463. [6]]

Here are the Affiliate Marketing Success KPIs I recommend you discuss
with your CFO:

K1. AFFILIATE IROAS.

Incremental Sales / Fully Loaded Affiliate Program Costs.

I don’t need to explain why this is #1.

K2. AFFILIATE NEW TO BRAND RATIO.

% of Incremental New Customers Acquired.

If you can’t do incremental, at least do the normal ratio. You
don’t want to rent your current customers. Unless you suck at A.
delivering value via product or service or blog, and B. brand
marketing.

K3. 90-DAY REPURCHASE RATE.

% of acquired new customers buying again in 90-days.

I’ve assumed a 90-day repurchase cycle. Please customize the
duration to your top quartile repurchase rate. I deeply care about
this KPI as, to me, it is a strong indicator of the quality of the
acquisition.

Here are the Diagnostic Metrics I recommend you review inside
Marketing:

M1. EFFECTIVE COMMISSION RATE.

(Commissions + Bonuses + Network Fees) /  Net Revenue.

Great for keeping coupon leakage in check.

M2. AVERAGE ORDER VALUE.

Affiliate Revenue / Number of Orders via Affiliates.

A tactical assessment of the initial quality of the customer acquired.
Also immensely helpful in detecting margin dilution discount tactics.

M3. MTA CONVERSION RATE.

Affiliate Orders / Affiliate Visitors

Adjust to cover Leads, Email Addresses, whatever else your goal was,
if an Ecommerce sale is it. The MTA part is an initial attempt at
helping right credit Affiliate traffic.

M4. PUBLISHER DIVERSITY INDEX (PDI).

Reliance on top-5 Partners, expressed as a percentage.

I’m a tiny bit torn on it. For some businesses I’ve found it
profitable to have a high PDI, IE very few Affiliate partners (driving
lots of incremental revenue). In other cases, it has been important to
have a low PDI (hundreds of Publishers). Please adapt per your
business strategy.

M5. TIME-TO-CONVERSION LATENCY.

The distribution of time between the Affiliate click and conversion.

A latency of a few minutes is a potential big red flag. It suggests
coupon-seeking behavior from seeing Promo Code box in your checkout
process. A latency of hours or days potentially indicates the
affiliate playing a role in consideration or bringing someone new to
your franchise.

3 + 5… And entirely manageable view to power actions that ensure
your Affiliate Marketing is a strategic asset.

AFFILIATE MARKETING AS MORE THAN COUPONS & DEALS.

In the first section, _what is affiliate marketing_, you might have
noticed that I included purposes beyond _hey, here’s a coupon / 28%
discount / LIMITED TIME bundle._

Some types of Affiliate Marketing can be valuable as a partner for
other Marketing you are doing. Three of my favs:

1. AEO | ANSWER ENGINE OPTIMIZATION.

You’ve read my recent six-week series (!!) on Answer Engine
Optimization. In Part 3, I’d emphasized the insane value of 3P
distribution – because of the influence of the mid and long-tail in
influencing LLMs.

Affiliate Marketing (ex: tactic #2 above) can play a key role in 3P
content distribution – ideally solving for the key consumer
preferences vectors where you are currently scoring poorly vs.
Competition.

2. NORMAL PR & CONTENT AMPLIFICATION.

You have a PR team that’s _PRing_. Great. There is only so much they
can badger editors, content publishers, mega-influencers to
organically write about you.

A smart Affiliate Marketing program could help find scale. In a
reverse twist to AEO above, this could help you scale your Influencers
_talking about_ you – which has very little scale.

The participation could take the shape of a special
deal/discount/promo, but it could simply be paying for
posting/amplification of existing content.

3. EXPLORING ENTIRELY NEW TERRITORIES.

As my Success KPI #2 made clear above, I _really care_ about new
humans into the brand.
Let’s say a vast chunk of your current customers are 35+ years of
age. Or, they are all mostly just from Ohio. Or people who have always
mostly bought a certain product.

Use Affiliate Marketing to reach entirely new audiences you are not
sure about or would like to add to your franchise.

Ex: I am already worrying about influencing Gen Alpha for our brands.
It is going to take a few years for them to even consider being our
customer. But, I want to learn how to reach them. Experiment with some
campaigns in Fortnite with new creative. Other ideas. I could use
Affiliate Marketing to reach and experiment with these audiences –
at low cost. And, if I fail, no biggie, lessons still learned.

Ex: Your company Marketing is obsessively focused on people who order
CD-ROMS.* You know they are going to die. Yet, no one is buying your
next gen tech. And, your Marketing is gun-shy about “low ROI”. Use
Affiliate Marketing to, at low cost, low risk, reach new audiences who
are looking beyond CD-ROMs and learn conversion effectiveness.

* It is not lost on me that many of you don’t know what CD-ROMs
were! 🤭

4. CREATOR-LED STORYTELLING WITH IN-APP CHECKOUT.

Social, in-app, walled-gardens shopping has not really taken off in
America as in other places. There is some, it is not material.

As I reflect on the _young‘uns_ being more influenced by short-form
videos, vs. text, and the value of non-company creators taking the
lead in telling those short-form stories, in countries (China!) where
in-app shopping/walled gardens are the norm… There is immense value
in Affiliate Marketing playing a role in driving “mid-funnel”
intent.

I’m sure there are other smart applications of Affiliate Marketing.
Please share yours with me.

BOTTOM LINE.

I am FOR an Affiliate Marketing program as a part of a diversified
channel mix, with active, and frequent proof of low to no
cannibalization and decent incrementality.

Affiliate channels often get paid for _value captured _vs. _value
created_. Plan for incrementality. Instrument for measurement at very
step. Pay for value created.

Carpe diem!
