TMAI #483: AEO LOSS RECOVERY GROWTH MODEL. -P2

[ Web Version [2] ]

As humans, in a slow but steady trickle, adopt LLM-powered chatbots,
one key shift is the typing of context rich long questions to get to
the endpoint they are seeking.

Search Engines were _librarians_. Answer Engines are _graduate-level
research assistants_.

We used to: "best wireless headphones."

Instead, we are: "Can you recommend affordable headphones with fast
charging, for cycling on windy roads. Something around $75."

The _librarian_ brought us 14 blue links to trawl through to figure
out what we want.

The _research assistant_, Qwen, [3] instead does all the work and
gives me one answer: JBL Tune 230NC TWS. It also explains why: "_Wind
noise: Built-in mic filtering helps with call clarity in wind; decent
passive isolation. Great for active use—lightweight and stable, even
on bumpy rides._"

Sold.

Google's blue links experience would have exposed me to 15 other
brands that I would go back and forth with; they all lost out as I
directly get the JBLs.

That in a nutshell is your problem/opportunity.

model you can use to quantify the potential loss to your SEO traffic
(for a typical ecommerce B2C type US website):

In my hypothetical ecommerce company, the total SEO & PPC CY 2026
potential loss was $8m. Let's focus on how we can recover some of that
by changing our SEO, Content, UX, Digital IT, Paid Media teams, and
our one/many Agencies.

SUPERSEDING AEO RECOVERY & GROWTH CRITICAL FACTORS.

There's a lot of tactical detail below; it is necessary. These three
bits are do or die:

*
Your IT team is critical. Your site needs to be entirely indexable,
understandable completely, and restructured in dramatic ways. Then,
you need to go on the front foot with API integrations, _company out_
and _platforms in_. [TMAI #471 [5]]

Bring IT into the tent early, leverage their unique skills, ensure you
give them public credit. If you have the wrong IT leadership, I offer
that time is not your friend.

*
Content is Queen, Empress, Grand Poobah. 1P Content & 1P Distribution
is a good place to start. You'll need to reorg, hire, and build new
muscles for body parts you did not know existed. The scale at which
you need to solve this problem is scary; start now. [TMAI #470 [6]]

*
Content is Queen, Empress, Grand Poobah. 3P Content & 3P Distribution
is even more important in the AEO when compared to SEO. Quantity at
high quality. AI Resistant. Video & audio, not just text and images.
Executed in a Mega + Macro + Micro + Nano model (vs. just Mega).

LOSSES TO RECOVERY: ORGANIC TRAFFIC

Traditional SEO is still Extremely Important. Remember, Old Google is
going to continue to deliver a large volume of traffic and revenue.

Recommendations below extend that SEO work to new bits required for
AEO. Insight: 100% of everything below will help SEO. In fact, the
short-term revenue impact of below AEO work will look like SEO wins!

Our Organic Traffic recovery goal: MAKE OUR CONTENT SO CLEAR, SO
STRUCTURED, SO AUTHORITATIVE THAT AES CHOOSE TO QUOTE US AND LINK BACK
TO US FOR MORE DETAIL.

These actions will help recover some Informational and Commercial
losses ($3m).

1. Build/Reformat content for "answerability."

[Assumption A: Yours is not a site with an insignificant amount of
content on PLPs and PDPs. B: You have a rich and vibrant _content
hub_, aka old-school blog. If either assumption is wrong, fix that
first.]

Go through your top "blog" and category pages. Reformat them to be
"answer-first." The H2 should be the question (e.g., "What Are the
Best Shoes for Hiking?"). The very first paragraph must be the direct,
concise answer (e.g., "The best shoes for hiking are those with...").
The rest of the article can provide supporting detail.

Audit your PDPs (and PLPs) to ensure that they reflect the content
that can answer the now more complex User _resolution questions_ being
typed into AEs. Ex: PDP Q&As will earn AE citations.

2. Deploy comprehensive schema.

Implement detailed Product, FAQPage, and HowTo schema markup on every
relevant page. This is non-negotiable. Think of schema as "subtitles
for the AI." It explicitly tells the AI, "Here is the question," "Here
is the direct answer," "Here are the product specs," "Here is the
price."

This makes it incredibly easy for the AI to ingest and cite our
content.

Ensure your 1P reviews & UGC content (structured, deduped, licensed)
is indexable and understandable by LLMs. Ditto for interactive
fit/size, care tools, calculators, and quizzes.

3. Aggressively build E-E-A-T.

Our SEO efforts already solve for Experience, Expertise,
Authoritativeness, Trust (E-E-A-T [7]). It should be unsurprising that
LLMs powering AEs are trained to value E-E-A-T.

We must prove our authority. This means adding expert author bios
(with credentials) to every post, citing external data, and
integrating firsthand user reviews ("Experience") directly into our
content.

The following actions will help recover some Non-Brand Transactional
and Branded Navigational losses ($0.7m).

4. Build and deploy structured data & feeds.

Similar to how Google Merchant Center operates, OpenAI allows
companies to submit product data. This is especially helpful in the
emerging shopping features that ChatGPT is building. Even if you don't
use that feature for your company, if the AE accepts product/data
feeds, invest in ensuring you are doing so. Ex: AEs are already using
these feeds to populate organic product carousels inside answers.

Our feeds must be gorgeously perfect, with descriptive,
natural-language titles and high-quality images.

5. Own more branded snippets.

Ensure every branded resolution question triggers a rich answer that
we control.

This means flawless site links, integrated product carousels, and
business information.

6. Invest in creating comparisons, how-to content.

A material chunk of the Non-Brand Transactional losses will come from
comparison intent expressed by users. Ex: Per AE data, the Coach Tabby
bag (it is awesome, buy it!) is most frequently compared to the Chloe
C bag, then Gucci GG Marmont, then Chanel Flap Bags (esp. Chanel Mini
Rectangular).

Why not take control? Invest in building your own comparisons to
increase the chances that we become the source material?

Even if not direct brand comparisons, a comparison of what makes your
toothpaste, car, underwear, USB-C hub unique and special?

How-to content and guides are another rich source of value—both for
AEO and in meeting actual User needs. Ex: implementation guides,
product use case expansions, activities.

Taken together, these actions, along with others your expert team will
surely have, ensure your site is a more citable, authoritative, and
structured source to help recover some traffic/revenue losses.

Oh, and since the User has already arrived more pre-persuaded, she/he
is a lot more valuable (with a higher conversion rate!).

Here's the potential recovery you will be able to power:

[Organic Traffic AEO Recovery Actions.]

Total Organic Loss: $3.8M. Potential for Organic Recovery: $2.1M.

REMINDER: All of my recommended actions above will add additional
"SEO" value to "Old Google," which will remain for now a massive
source of traffic. Hence, additional revenue benefits should flow from
"Old Google."

ACTION: In partnership with your IT, SEO, Content, Digital UX, and
Agency teams, create a plan for how to deliver the above in a 90–120
days time horizon. If that is not possible, please update your revenue
forecasts for a loss of 30% of Organic revenue.

LOSSES TO RECOVERY: PAID TRAFFIC.

Our hypothetical company gets 7m visits from Paid Search ads annually,
for a revenue of $17.5m. Of this, I'd predicted a loss of $4.6m in
revenue from the switch from _librarian _to _research assistants_.

I'd mentioned in TMAI #482 [4] that changes in Paid Search (/Paid
Answers) advertising are a bit opaque. Most AEs don't have ads today.
Bing, Google, Baidu are still figuring out the ads strategy for their
versions of AI Mode/whatever is to come.

Hence, my recommendations are anticipatory and also focus on ensuring
you are taking every advantage of AI possibilities already available
today in "Old Google."

New mantra for your Paid Search/Answer ads: _You can no longer manage
keywords. You must feed the AI! _

1. All in on PMax.

If you are not already since early 2024, immediately begin to shift
your budget from old Search and Shopping campaigns into PMax. From
smarter bidding to smarter targeting to smarter creative to a
significantly longer _context window_, PMax is built to be way more
intelligence-powered.

Everything won't go well, in part because giving up micro-management
is hard. But you and I are not smarter than the algo. Give it time to
learn your customer & business. Teach it your POAS. Let it deliver
results.

At the moment, the new "Sponsored" slots in AI Overviews are via PMax.
Recovery!

2. Optimize product feeds as ad copy.

Since ancient times, Ad Copy has been a key part of how the quality of
your ad is computed by Google.

Your Google Merchant Center feed is your new ad copy. The AI is not
reading your text ads; it is reading your product feed. Hence,
optimize it with high-quality images and, most importantly, natural
language descriptions (not just keyword-stuffed titles) that the AI
can use to recommend your products.

This tactic in particular will help recover Commercial and Non-Brand
Transactional intent.

3. Smarten your (shrinking) Text ads.

For any text ads that your Agency is stubbornly sticking to in order
to prove their "special magic," remember: You cannot possibly guess
the 20-word, 50-word conversational prompts for AE ads; hence, use
Broad Match + Smart Bidding to match ads to User intent.

Aim Dynamic Search Ads (DSAs) at your new AEO content hub pages
(Organic recovery action #1). This creates a positive synergy: The AEO
content gets indexed, and our paid DSAs automatically use that content
to generate an ad that perfectly matches User Informational and
Commercial queries (and likely some Non-Brand Transactional as
well).

4. Experiment with new AE ads.

Microsoft has been running ads inside Copilot's chat/answer interface
for approximately two years. They continue to release interesting,
entirely new ad formats and creative possibilities.

Ensure your Microsoft account is opted into Copilot placements and
experiment with these new possibilities. Given traffic on Bing is
quite low, I'm not expecting too much recovery of the loss. My primary
interest is in the old dog (you) learning new tricks—which we can
apply to other AEs when they have ads.

[It might only take a couple percent of your Paid Search spend to
learn and experiment.]

[Paid Search AEO Recovery Actions.]

Total Paid Loss: $4.5M. Potential for Paid Recovery: $3M.

ACTION: In partnership with your internal Search team and your Agency,
create a plan for how to deliver the above in a 90–120 days time
horizon.

Due to the difficulty of seeing the evolution coming, my actions above
are limited. Hence, ensure you keep a very close eye on new
announcements, alpha and beta opportunities in-platform, and save a
small amount of your Paid Search budget to jump on them immediately to
learn and scale what is effective.

BOTTOM LINE.

It is easy to understand why uncertainty can lead to paralysis. Or,
worse, misplaced confidence that the future is going to be just like
the past... So why change?

I believe both would be mistakes.

Even this early, with ChatGPT at 2.5 billion prompts per day (Google
at 9 billion), we know enough to choose to ride the wave.
