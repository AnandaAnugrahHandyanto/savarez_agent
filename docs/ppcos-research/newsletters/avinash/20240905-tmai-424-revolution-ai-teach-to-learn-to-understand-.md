TMAI #424: REVOLUTION AI: TEACH TO LEARN TO UNDERSTAND.

[ Web Version [2] ]

There has been an explosion in knowledge about AI in the past 24
months (can you believe it has been that short?).

Yet, I find that a _philosophical_ understanding is uncommon.

To unlock our imagination – to see possibilities now, possibilities
ahead – it is super important to get that _philosophical_
understanding.

Hence, I want to revisit a framing I’d shared a couple of years ago.

ML. AI. GENAI.

Machine Learning (ML) is one way to get to Artificial Intelligence
(AI).

ML techniques include good old Regression, Classification, and
Decision Trees. The most famous one you’ve surely heard of: Neural
Networks.

Generative AI (GENAI) is a type of AI that can create a wide variety
of data, such as images, videos, audio, text, and 3D models.

GenAI has been the cool kid on the block. Cursor
[3] (coding). MidJourney [4] (images). Microsoft Copilot [5] (lots,
inc Search).

Large Language Models (LLMS) are a type of GenAI that has been
specifically architected to help generate (usually) text-based
content.

Summary:

The reason I’m so excited about AI is that it represents a superset
of possibilities for humanity. GenAI represents a subset of
possibilities.

If you hear the phrase GenAI to describe all of AI, the individual
might be early in acquiring their knowledge in the space.

Ditto for ML.

IT’S A TRANSFORMER WORLD, WE JUST LIVE IN IT.

It is crucial to get a _kinda_ technical understanding of something
fundamental to all the excitement over the last two years: TRANSFORMER
MODEL.

It was developed at Google, in 2017 [6].

Generative AI exists because of the transformer.

The Financial Times did a magnificent job of visually explaining what
a transformer [7] is, how it works, and teaching us all new
foundational words like tokens, self-attention, encoding, probability
score, etc.

Please take 30 undistracted minutes and go through the page.

No matter you are a new Engineer, Finance Analyst, CMO… Gaining a
solid understanding will be helpful in your role.

An additional benefit is that when self-described AI Gurus will come
to peddle their wares, you’ll see through them more efficiently.

Read the FT article. [7]

I’m going to take five steps back, and share the philosophical shift
that has dramatically accelerated the AI revolution over the last 15
years.

From _dumb_ terminals to I to AI, a shift from TEACH to LEARN to
UNDERSTAND.

TRADITIONAL PROGRAMMING: TEACH.

Here’s how traditional programming works, Say you want to write a
program to be able to recognize that this is a table…

A brilliant Engineer will write something akin to…  If you see a
rectangle shape with four legs, it is a table, _recognize _it as
such.

You give the program the above picture, and it says _table_.

Sweet.

This is _EXPLICIT PROGRAMMING_. I call it, _Teach_.

So, what happens when the program gets these two pictures...

It can recognize the first one, but not the second one. Because the
programmer did not explicitly program that structure.No worries.

The Engineer teaches the program with some explicit instructions. The
update is something akin to… _If you see a rectangle shape, or a
circle, with four legs, it is a table, recognize it as such._

Next time, this explicit programming will work flawlessly every time.

Until it sees this…

The program _conks _out.

To you and me, this is an easy problem. To a child of three, it is
likely easy to recognize all of these variations are tables. But, not
to a program that has been _taught _(aka, explicitly programmed).

Try to imagine all the code that would have to be handcrafted to
recognize every variation of the table you see above (and the
restrictions in the code to ensure that a chair does not get
recognized as a table!).

This is why your flights don’t land on time or your phone can’t
complete seemingly simple tasks or nearly all Tech Support Chatbots
suck or almost nothing seems to work in any government around the
world.

The world is so full of variations, _teach_ only works until the next
variation pops up – then comes lots and lots of pain to reprogram
the software.

LIMITATIONS OF TEACH EXECUTION:

The absolute best-case scenario is that the _teach _approach is only
as smart as the human / human imagination. Big limitation.

The simplest deviation, and it fails.

SHIFT | DEEP NEURAL NETWORKS: LEARN.

Machine Learning is the ability to learn WITHOUT BEING EXPLICITLY
PROGRAMMED.

The specific Machine Learning technique that just started practically
useful AI was _deep learning_. These models process information in
ways similar to the human brain, and hence they are often called deep
neural networks.

[Though, I rush to add that it is imprecise to think of neural
networks as matching the human brain’s ability, with the latter
being infinitely more complex.]

Deep neural networks, to simplify something incredibly complex, are
made up of a massive quantity of processing layers, each made up of
nodes. Their skill at learning was dramatically accelerated by massive
amounts of data and powerful graphics processing units (GPUs).

Each layer can perform complex operations such as representation and
abstraction that make sense of images, sound, and text. The signal
travels between notes and assigns corresponding weights. A heavier
weighted node will exert more effect on the next layer of nodes
(visually represented below).

Deep Learning required massive amounts of labeled training data to,
well, learn. Say, for example, lots and lots of pictures of different
types of tables with their parts identified and noted, along with the
fact that it is a table.

When you input the image in one end, the image is broken down into
microscopic pieces, pixels, with each node learning to do one very
specific job – across millions upon millions of nodes in millions of
layers.

Your hope is that when the output layer compiles all the disconnected
information, it does not deliver the output CAT! :)

If it does, the neural network makes a note of the error – and the
errant nodes! — and adjusts the weightings.

You keep feeding images of tables over and over again and the
weightings get adjusted, and through this supervised learning, the
neural network outputs _table _when the input is a table.

One layer creating an output which in turn becomes the input for the
next layer, over and over again, is the magic.

For our table, one layer might encode the edges of the table, compose
the pixels. The next layer might compose an arrangement of edges. The
next layer encodes the legs, again and again.

An element that takes some getting used to is that while we understand
the input and recognize the output, we really don’t know how these
networks learn. Like, we can understand at a simple level – as above
– how they work, but exactly how do they give us the answer that
they do? We don’t know for the most part - though, scientists are
trying.

Self-driving cars, cancer detection, pattern matching for law
enforcement, fraud detection, trading of stocks, translation, are
among the million applications of Deep Learning.

LIMITATIONS OF LEARN EXECUTION:

While we now also have unsupervised learning approaches, deep learning
relies on availability of lots of labeled data, and that in of itself
poses a limitation.

Our data is also a reflection of us, and in that it contains our
beliefs, our biases, and our bounded imagination. [If you think about
it, not such a bad thing [8].]

SHIFT | LARGE LANGUAGE MODELS/GENAI: UNDERSTAND.

The learning process with large language models is way, way, simpler
in some ways (yes, yes, it is computationally intensive if you want to
be pedantic).

Large language models use deep learning (see above) to produce
human-like text.

I’m simplifying a lot of complexity (see the FT link in section
two), but essentially, you tell the model to read a massively massive
pile of books, or the entire internet (millions of terabytes of data),
and learn concepts.

Large language models learn about patterns in language. Then, given
some text, they predict what comes next.

Basically, auto-complete on steroids.

Except, and here’s the exciting part, these models can converse on
any topic!

And, they can be quite brilliant.

In 2021, when I first used an early LLM (much, much less powerful than
today's LLMs), I asked it: _W__hat the meaning of life?_

It answered (it ordered by its assessment of quality):

_the journey not the destination.
to give life meaning.
to find happiness.
to love and be loved.
42._
Honestly, it blew my mind.

I thought it was incredible that you send a “thing” to just go
read a whole lot of stuff, and it can make that much sense!

I love the second one (and, of course, 42).

Since then, there has been dramatic progress. Consider a lame prompt
I'd used in June 2022, _kungfu mouse ninja, _to create an image, and
redoing it again in Aug 2024...

I remember saying in 2022... _Look at the promise this early attempt
demonstrates_. :)

Checkout how powerful Llama  3.1 405B by Facebook [9] is.

Play with it here: meta.ai [10]. An extraordinary amount of
intelligence and compute, free for us.

unlocking, read this post [11] by Andy Jassy, Amazon CEO. Average time
to upgrade an application to Java 17 plummeted from a typical 50
developer-days to a few hours - saving Amazon a mind-boggling 4,500
developer-years!
