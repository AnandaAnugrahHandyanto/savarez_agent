TMAI #490: VIDEO ADS: BIG WIN CREATIVE TACTICS.

[ Web Version [2] ]

Effective Brand Marketing is critical to growing Market Share. In our
multi-modal world, video ads play a critical role in powering success.

The problem?

No one is watching your video ads.

Well, at least not beyond the first handful of seconds.

Your Creative Director and CMO are the only people seeing the
“payoff” that comes from patiently waiting for the 27th second of
a 30s ad or the 55th second of a 60s ad.

Here’s the reality of attention availability:

[Seconds of available attention: Video ads.]

[Please see deeper details on attention data in TMAI #489, table one
[3].]

Sobering, no?

The TikTok (/Shorts/Reels) data demonstrates that whenever humans have
a choice, they’ll choose to swipe away, or hit skip, at the first
moment possible. Data identified causal factors:

*
Most video ads are unappealing, interruptive, self-indulgent, complex
“brand stories.”

*
Today, human attention is so incredibly fragmented: Devices. Browsers.
Apps. Channels. Work. Life. School.

*
Atrocious media plans. Ex A: Stuffing most of the video ads to be
served on low-CPM, horrendously poor attention and viewability mobile
device screens.

*
Ex B: Poor understanding of the magic of Frequency x Min Duration.

*
[IMHO, the biggest one:] The refusal to accept that while storytelling
remains essential, how stories are told has changed.

Bam. Poof. Kapow. Zing!

Consistently across companies, CMOs are unable to prove any business
value to their CFO peers – in terms of consistent Brand Lift (UBA,
C, PI) with high Number of People Lifted, and revenue impact from
brand marketing.

WINNERS WANNA WIN.

You are paying $100/yr for this newsletter because you refuse to
accept status quo. Your actions demonstrate you are willing to seek
new information, and WIN!

Let’s win.

On Social video channels, humans pay only 12 SECONDS OF ACTIVE
ATTENTION FOR EVERY HOUR WATCHED. That has three implications. We have
2s to grab attention AND tell the story to most people. For some, we
have 2s to earn permission to watch 6s. Occasionally, we have 5s to
grab attention, earn permission, to get them to watch 15s.

In TMAI #489 [3] I’d shared five universal creative effectiveness
strategies to win the 0 to 2s > 2s to 6s > 6s to 15s challenge:

*
Brand in 3.
*
Frame-One Impact.
*
Sound as Lead, Not Support.
*
The "Why Now?".
*
Pre-test Creative.

Today, Imdouble-click and share winning creative tactics across
different ads platforms. My learnings are sourced from approximately
$10 bil in brand marketing analyzed across companies, countries, with
a vast majority of it video ads (on TV, CTV, Digital). A ton of ideas
I’ve tested come from peers, ad platforms, deep user research (ex:
follow me homes). They are validated with real-world experiments.

If you’ve not yet done this work, embrace my guidance immediately to
start winning. If you’ve done deep meta-analysis and
experimentation, compare notes below (and let me know if you’ve
learned tactics not below!). With the passage of time, user behavior
will evolve, platforms will change, we will always be testing!

CREATIVE EFFECTIVENESS TACTICS | AD PLATFORMS

Every channel has its nuances. What works on TV rarely works on
YouTube. What works on Reels often does not work for Facebook.

Let’s win everywhere, by respecting a human’s engagement on each
platform.

Reminder: Regardless of ad length, the first two seconds are The Most
Important.

HOW TO READ:

*
My recommendations are ordered by size of impact:

H = high impact. M = medium impact. L = lower impact.

*
My focus re impact is on two big Brand Marketing KPIs:

UBA = unaided brand awareness. C = Consideration.

I'll share data-identified tactics on: YouTube, TikTok (inc. Shorts,
Reels), CTV (+Linear TV), Meta, Snap, and Programmatic OLV

Ready to rock?

WINNING YOUTUBE.

Please anchor your creative meetings on this critical fact: YouTube
and TikTok are _pull _mediums. That means, very high resentment risk
if you don’t respect the user and the context they are in. Creative
directors should incorporate the psychological implications.

If you are in China, my recommendations also apply on iQiyi, Bilibili,
others.

1. JUMP IN IMMEDIATELY. [H]

Drop humans into the story with tight framing and engaging pacing.
(UBA, C)

_How_: Flip the traditional arc. Punchline/climax first (0s-2s), then
how you got there. I know, sounds insane. Works like a charm for ads.

2. BRAND EARLY, OFTEN. [H]

Use multiple brand cues: Product, colors, sonic signatures, packaging,
logo. Do not rely just on the end-card (though you should have one).
(UBA)

3. EMPHASIZE KEY POINTS WITH AUDIO & TEXT. [H]

A big Gen Z adaptation. Ensure audio and text support (not compete
with) the creative’s story. Design for comprehension on small
screens. (UBA, C)

_Bonus_: DO NOT assume people are watching your ad. The voiceover must
state what the viewer will learn or get by watching.

4. BUILD “CONNECTION” FAST! [H]

Aim for attention to persist past the first (skip, swipe) moment.
Focus on relatability, and, as has been true for 100,000 years,
emotion to carry through. If relevant to your story, use unexpected
movement. (C)

5. LEVERAGE BRIGHT, HIGH-CONTRAST VISUALS. [M]

If it works with your story, this approach _reads instantly_ –
especially on mobile. (UBA)

6. EMPHATICALLY CLEAR NEXT STEP. [M]

Be explicit about the _reason to believe / big takeaway /
call-to-action_. The ad’s job is to convert attention into action.
(C)

7. FOCUS. [M]

The job of the first 2s is to earn permission to watch 6s. The job of
the 6s is to earn 15s of attention. 2s and 6s are very little time.
For the sake of all that is holy, please focus. One clear point. One
product feature. One brand value. One. Just one thing.

Because your ads go from 60s to 6s is not a reason for you to cram 60s
of things into 6s. (UBA, C)

WINNING TIKTOK.

These tactics will also deliver superior results on YT Shorts, Reels,
Xiaohongshu, Kuaishou, and, of course, Douyin.

1. DON’T CREATE ADS, CREATE TIKTOKS. [H]

If TikTokers smell an ad in 0.25s, SWIPE! Embrace TT’s native
shooting style, text overlays (yes, those ugly ones!), voiceover,
green screen/duet norms (where relevant). Your ad has to feel
non-interruptive. Ex: Embrace the lo-fi authenticity, vs. high
production value which signals in 0.2s: AD!! (UBA, C)

2. FOLLOW _HOOK-BODY-CLOSE _STRUCTURE. [H]

TikTok’s massive meta-analysis recommends explicitly communicating
value early, because nearly all the value occurs in 2s. (UBA, C)

3. LEVERAGE SURPRISE, SUSPENSE. [H]

As hooks, they are effective at sustaining attention. This does not
mean “loud” openers (_DON’T SWIPE!_). (C)

3. SHOW THE PRODUCT. [H]

Your ad is a TikTok, but it is also an ad. Show the product, if the
story is about the product. Don’t be shy. Build additive branding
cues through the body. (UBA, C)

4. TRENDS AS STORYTELLING TEMPLATE. [H]

Cultural relevance is particularly powerful on TT. IF it is something
your team knows how to do well, leverage this superpower. If you
don’t, please skip it because there is nothing more _cringey _than
a grandpa emulating teens at a party. (UBA)

5. SOUNDTRACK MATTERS. [H]

Audio is a primary attention and emotion driver. Voice. Music. Sound.
(UBA, C)

6. EDITING _SIMULATION_. [M]

Transitions, motion, faster scene changes, on-screen text, emojis –
use with a measure of control. (I am so old, reminds me of early MTV
days. 😊) (UBA, C)

7. CLOSE WITH A DECISIVE PAYOFF. [M]

CTA cards, additional creative tactics to ensure the ending feels like
a resolution. (C).

~ ~

_Bonus_: TikTok’s ad platform will only show a person the same
creative two or three times. Hence, if you are doing video advertising
on TikTok, you need 20, 30, 50, more variations of your creative
execution (with minor changes is ok). The exact number will depend on
the frequency and duration you are aiming for.

To _win before you spend,_ pre-test the CREATIVE CONCEPT you will use
on TikTok using HMM Pro [4], then test the tons of CONCEPT VARIATIONS
using HMM AI [4].

WINNING CTV. (+ LINEAR TV).

Some misunderstand CTV as limited to Hulu, Disney, etc.

CTV stands for Connected TV. Any ad platform that can deliver your ads
on a television screen is CTV. Ex: A massive amount of YouTube and
Netflix are consumed on TV screens, both CTV. Peacock, Roku, ditto. If
you are in China: Tencent Video.

CTV is a _push _medium as we discussed, hence skipping is less of an
issue… Attention drift, tune-out is a massive challenge.

1. TELL YOUR EMOTIONAL STORIES! [H]

About time! 😀 On CTV you have stronger, passive attention. You
also get stronger memory encoding. Use the bigger screen for emotional
storytelling (but don’t forget it is still an ad for a brand purpose
or company product). (UBA, C)

2. GOAL: 0-5S “MOVIE TRAILER”. [H]

Treat the opening as a trailer for the ad itself! High drama,
curiosity, or humor – you know, all the things that would work on
you as a human. (UBA)

_Bonus_: Another super effective creative tactic identified in BLS
results: _Start in the middle_. For your non-stick pan ad, start with
the chef mid-sizzle. For your tires, start with the car already
drifting.

3. [IF YOU DO A 30S] SONIC & VISUAL REFRESH AT 15S. [H]

Create a beat change, a new visual motif, as a question at the 15s
mark. A fantastic tactic to re-engage the second-screeners (all of
us!). (UBA, C)

4. [IF YOU DO A 30S] END IN THE MIDDLE. [H]

Deliver a branded payoff at the mid-point. Vs. the typical _wait all
the way to the last 2 seconds for the “payoff.”_ Now, if people
leave at 15s, they still got the core message. (C)

5. DELIVER HIGH TECHNICAL EXCELLENCE. [M]

Audio mixing. Resolution. Latency (none). These creative elements help
retain attention, and prevent experience-driven drop-off. (UBA)

6. LENGTH IS YOUR FRIEND. [L]

Sadly, nothing beyond 30s. But, CTV is the place for your 15s and 30s
ads. Catch: Ensure the story advances every 2-3 seconds. (C)

[Please see the media plan recommendation in TMAI #489 [3] to identify
how much budget you should spend on 15s and 30s ads.]

~ ~

_Media Bonus_: Frequency and Pod management are super important on CTV
– primarily because most CTV ad platforms don’t offer both. Please
check, and worry. Reco: Prioritize your spend on ad platforms that let
you shape frequency (including pod management).

WINNING META/FEED.

Remember the big challenge for effectiveness in the Meta feed: You
have less than 2s. The sound’s off.

Creative recommendations below apply for any _feed-type _ad formats.
Ex: Insta feed.

1. “THUMB-STOPPING” FIRST MOMENT. [H]

A very strong first frame, fast clarity on what’s happening, why it
matters. Ensure front-loaded branding and message. (UBA, C)

2. DESIGN FOR SOUND OFF. [H]

Captions. Text. Extreme simplicity in the story. Solve for
comprehension and memory. (UBA, C)

3. TIGHT FRAMING IS YOUR FRIEND. [M]

People. Faces. Hands, Product. When you reduce cognitive load,
you’ll increase likelihood of watching. (UBA, C)

4. BREAK PLATFORM-NORMS. [M]

Interrupt the patterns the user expects. Use 9:16 vertical video in
feed, vs. using square. Or, a static image that _becomes _a video on
scroll. (C)

5. AVOID SLOW-BUILD INTROS [M]

Keep the narrative modular. Every 1-2s, advance the story. (C)

6. THUMBNAIL / OPENING FRAME OPTIMIZATION. [L]

Where surfaced, it improves starts. (UBA)

7. EMBRACE NATIVE FONTS. [L]

Use native fonts, regardless of your “brand guidelines.” And,
look, I realize how “ugly” some native fonts are. It is a part of
committing to the context the user is in.

WINNING SNAP.

Snap ads, Story ads, Spotlight/Creator placements. (By now, I hope you
are starting to sense patterns.)

1. BRANDING FROM SECOND ZERO. [H]

Don’t wait for the reveal. Early branding, in vertical full-screen
formats. (UBA)

2.  LEAD WITH CLEAREST VISUAL IDEA IN THE FIRST FRAME. [H]

Aim for instantly legible benefit, with fast payoff. (UBA, C)

3. USE SOUND INTENTIONALLY. [H]

Snap is largely sound-on. Leverage sound as a strong emotional and
attention cue. (UBA, C)

Note: Leveraging sound on TikTok and Snap is a different art,
important in both.

4. LO-FI, CREATOR-LIKE AESTHETIC. [M]

_Native-feeling _camera language is very effective at reducing the
interruption perception. (C)

5. FREQUENT CREATIVE REFRESH. [L]

TikTok needs multiple creative variations, Snap’s audience requires
multiple creative executions, to prevent fatigue. Depending on your
campaign’s frequency and duration, you will need multiple variations
of each creative execution (and many executions, immensely
_complexifying _your life!) (UBA)

WINNING PROGRAMMATIC OLV.

Goes by many names: Premium Online Video. Publisher Pre-Roll. In-App
Video. All in skippable and non-skippable formats (which matter a ton,
when you have a choice, please default to short format non-skippables
– thus reduce resentment rate and increase brand lift).

1. FRONT-LOAD THE ADS. [H]

Data overwhelmingly indicates attention decision is made immediately.
Thus, front-load value proposition + branding cues. (UBA, C)

2. CHOOSE EMOTION OR UTILITY. [H]

When _front-loading_, choose emotion or utility. One of those two must
arrive quickly, to sustain attention long enough for any shot at brand
lift. (UBA, C)

3. REDUCE “INTERRUPTION TAX.” [H]

You get access to creative tactics like pacing, framing, and UI
conventions native to the programmatic OLV environment. Leverage
‘em. AKA, your standard YT or TV ad might not be effective. (UBA,
C)

4. BUILD FOR SOUND OFF. [M]

Captions. Supers. Visual clarity. Very helpful on feed and in-app
placements. (UBA, C)

5. END-CARDS HELP. [L]

Final-frame branding as a clear ending payoff can be helpful in
nailing brand lift, and deliver a CTA (which does not have to be a
click). Do remember that early & throughout branding is even more
important. (UBA)

BOTTOM LINE.

An ad’s length on its own is almost never the hero.

Length doesn’t build brands – emotional resonance in the attention
window does.

Today, that attention window for an ad is <5s.

You can stick to the _way it used to be, _90s to ∞s. I offer it is
time to build ads for the world that is – not the world we wish
still existed.

The average human attention span has stabilized at roughly 8.25
seconds (under 2s on mobile). The data-identified tactics above will
deliver unimaginably positive results on your career and your CMO’s
career by delivering long-term business profits.
