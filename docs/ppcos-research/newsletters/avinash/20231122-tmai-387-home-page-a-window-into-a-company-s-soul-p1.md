TMAI #387: HOME PAGE: A WINDOW INTO A COMPANY’S SOUL – P1

[ Web Version [2] ]

I am obsessed with website home pages.

Not because they are the most important thing for a site (/mobile
app). No. Typically, your home page will be like your third or fifth
most popular landing page.

I am obsessed about home pages because, above the fold, they give me
such a great view into how sophisticated the company’s thinking is
(at least the Marketing team), the complexity of the organization, its
ability to _think smart, move fast_, and their inability to recognize
bad.

This is particularly so because there is a lot of focus on a
company’s home page. People at the very top care, frequently there
is even a small group of tech, UX, analytics, promos, assigned to the
home page. It is often the first thing most Usability Study
participants see. It is the first thing that gets redesigned by the
new Agency.

Hence, when I visit the home page… It tells me A LOT. Not
everything. :) Just a lot. If a company can’t even build a good home
page (a score of 7 out of 10 on my scale), then I know that everything
else will be way, way, way harder to get done (buy a new platform,
change the email campaigns, a new org design, cut waste, etc.).

It is also why my discovery questions start with: _Why does x look
like that on your home page?_ Or, _what would it take for our home
page to y?_ In my experience, very often, pulling that thread unravels
so much that is valuable to know if you are an agent of change like
me.

Your home page, is a peek into your company’s soul.

It is betraying a lot more than you realize.

It is not everything, of course. It is a place to start with a very
high payoff re insights.

And, if you can fix the home page, the benefits permeate a vast
quantity of what it takes to be in business!

WHAT CONTRIBUTES TO A MAGNIFICENT HOME PAGE?

In our CRO practice at Croud, I’ve distilled six factors that
influence home page quality. Noting that every business is a special
snowflake, these six are influential enough to apply to most websites.
They also cover solving for now and solving for the long term.

Once we’ve covered the _think_, I’ll share six examples, and we
can reflect on the _ink_. My hope is that reflecting on real-world
examples will allow you to better internalize the lessons, and
ACTIVATE CHANGE at your company.

So, what contributes to a magnificent home page?

1. GOOD TASTE.

The older I get, the more upset I get when companies don’t treat
every single gift of attention from a User as an opportunity to make a
good impression about the brand.

I visited a real-world store of a major retailer in London. It was a
delightful experience. Great merchandising. A lovely lady standing at
the door directing me to the right floor. The products were
beautifully organized. I happily paid them more for the product
because the experience was so good.

A few days later, I went to their site. It was the equivalent of me
going to their real-world store and discovering that three trucks had
dumped trash all over the store. The digital experience was awful.
Cheap. Slow. Images that cut off. Every bit of text/images was very
much how can we make a quick buck. Not only did I not buy, as a
protest, I will never go back to the real-world store.

Good taste matters.

It cost this retail company a lot of money to wow me in the physical
store. It would be 10x more affordable to do it on their desktop and
mobile websites. Yet.

This might feel odd coming from the author of two best-selling books
on analytics, in 14 languages... You don’t have to A/B test good
taste. Else, you get sucked into the quicksand of what might work for
the 1.3% of the Users who convert, while setting aside the 98.7% who
do not convert immediately.

Build with good taste, because… Good taste is who you are.

When done well, GOOD TASTE PERMEATES EVERY INTERACTION your brand has
with a human. The site is one expression along that journey.

Oh, and when you do things above and beyond, you can earn gold stars
from me!

GOLD STAR: A great brand experience can lower CAC and improve CR. If I
sense you are doing this, you get a gold star from me.

GOLD STAR: B2B entities, Agencies, Vendors: You know who you are, I
don’t. Please, please, above the fold, in a simple headline, jargon
free, tell me what is it that you do. Bonus: What makes you special?
140 characters.

2. SELF-SEGMENTATION CAPABILITIES.

It is very difficult to guess why Users show up on your home page.
Even after lots of usability studies. The awful 35% Bounce Rate is
showing that.

My approach to dealing with this problem is simple: Design home pages
that allow for self-segmentation. Have a balance of _what we think you
want_, and _what we want you to want_, _please choose your
adventure_.

It manifests itself in the, hopefully, _skinny nav _on the site, the
hero image, the options next to it, the balance between text and
images, promos and in demand low profit products, and so much more.
Oh, and in solving for Macro and Micro Conversions – because Macro
is just 2% of the site traffic!

For the Very Best Home Pages, the self-segmentation options are living
breathing choices. By using behavior targeting (anonymous is ok), 40%
of what I get on the home page chnages on my second visit (because you
understand me more), 60% of what I see changes on my third visit (you
know me even more), so on and so forth as I give you more data
illustrating my interests.

[Even when using behavior targeting/smart algorithms, 40%
self-segmentation choices can remain selfish for the company, driving
sales/profits/leads/whatever.]

GOLD STAR: Delivering smart self-segmentation on mobile is hard, so
you win my love if you can do this well. Ideally, now powered by
machine learning – so you have to think less and the algo thinks
more!

PLATINUM STAR: 80% of the Users can find everything compelling without
scrolling. Because, a vast majority of the Users don’t scroll!

3. KILL USELESS LINKS.

Why are you showing me Chat when your chat team is sleeping, and I
can’t chat?

Why are you showing me your social links on every single darn page –
adding weight – when the last time you made a Social post was in
1993? Why confirm to me that your Marketing team is clueless?

No, I do not want to learn more about your green commitment that will
be delivered in the lifetime of my great-great-great grand kids.

Why do I need to listen to your CEO’s speech at the last event, when
it has only 11 views on YouTube and you know it is boring as heck?

Why are there layers and layers of menus and items in your mobile
site’s hamburger menu?

Why do you have irrelevant promoted links on top of your internal site
search results page?

Why are there five different types calls-to-action above the fold on
your home page?

Why does your linking scheme feel like another representation of your
org structure?

GOLD STAR: Your site has context-sensitive links!

4. ZERO POP-UPS/BEGGING.

If you visit my blog, I’ve done my best to configure the TMAI email
sign up to show up on your third page view. You have to respect the
first date is the first date.

No DOWNLOAD OUR (useless) MOBILE APP, GIVE YOUR EMAIL FOR 15% OFF,
FILL OUT OUR SITE SURVEY, ALLOW LOCATION PERMISSION, FOLLOW US ON
FACEBOOK, and a thousand other things you are popping. I just arrived.
Give me a chance to get to know you. Let me at least see if you have
anything worth buying, before you give me 15% off.

Let the only pop-up/banner be the legally mandated cookie thing. And,
please make it as simple and user-first as you can.

If I’ve seen five pages, sure, pop your site survey. If this is my
third visit, sure, pop your mobile app download. So on, and so forth.

GOLD STAR: Be so good at product and merchandising that I go hunt for
your email signup, your mobile app, your site survey, and more. A good
example for me is Rapha [3]. I LOVE them so much, I’ve done all that
for them – without being asked. PS: Their gear is awesome.

5. SPEED!

Countless home pages are bloated with images, scripts, carousals,
videos, and just the worst kind of stuff – usually invisible to the
User. I care about Page Speed first from the lens of the User
Experience, #slowsucks, and second from the lens of harmed SEO
rankings. Use the PageSpeed Insights [4] tool to quantify if you rock
or suck.

Here’s the report for a premium product company with loads of
Marketing budget:

Their performance score was a 28. Out of 100. They are paying for a
world-class ecommerce platform. They have very tool and vendor you can
imagine. Just... 28. Hearbreaking.

There is a ton of tech that can speed up your site, ex: async loading
of _material _unnecessary for the User. Use it. Incessantly bug your
tech team, with love, to get this fixed.

Also, don’t take their word for the fact that our tools are saying
the site is fast. Trust PageSpeed above.

If you have to choose, prioritize the mobile site – more users on
it, on often flaky connections.

GOLD STAR: Above the fold fully loaded in less than one second for
mobile. Three scrolls down, fully loaded in two seconds. I love you.
Your Users love you more.

6. MEETS BASIC QUALITY STANDARDS.

In addition to speed as a measure of technical quality, my assumption
is you are monitoring and ensure none of these basic issues arise:

Errors.

Broken links.

Missing content.

Missing title / meta tags.

Inconsistent styles.

Grammatical errors.

Buttons and links are clickable (on most browsers).

Compliance with ADA, and other, standards.

Complete and accurate implementation of digital analytics tags using a
tag manager.
GOLD STAR: You meet all quality standards. :)

BOTTOM LINE.

Home pages underdeliver when they are run by committees, when there is
no leader with a clear vision for what great looks like, when few in
the company actually see the home page and experience it – all they
see are the metrics puked out of Google Analytics about the home page.

To me, home pages are a way to see a company’s soul. If the home
page is broken, it leads me to finding elements (people, process,
structure) that end up being 50x more impactful than any update to the
home page.

In that, a home page remains unique.*

Starting today, I hope you can also unlock some of that power for your
clients/company.
