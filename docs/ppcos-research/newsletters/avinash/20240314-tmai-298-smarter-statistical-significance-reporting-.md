TMAI #298: SMARTER STATISTICAL SIGNIFICANCE REPORTING.

[ Web Version [2] ]

Is there anything more overused, and abused, in the practice of
Analytics than statistical significance [3]?

I think not.

At the very core of statistical significance is something beautiful.
It says: _Hey, in the recommendations based on this analysis, we are
not reporting feelings! IF you act on our recommendations, we can say
with confidence positive business results will be delivered. _

In our everyday use, when done right, statistical significance implies
that we feel confident that the result is real and not because we got
lucky (or we are seeing a false positive).

When I started in my last analytical role, this just about captures
the reality…

[Significant! Significant.]

There were people parading 80% significance.

There were people sticking to 90%.

There were meetings where the HiPPO would say: _74% is so close to
80%, let’s just call it “directionally significant.”_ 26 billion
cells in my body died.

On some level, I get that p-values are not the easiest things in the
world to internalize. I get that for lay people, 74% just seems so
close to 80%. It is hard for them to understand that in this context,
74% is a coin toss.

Yet, because saying _statistical significance of xy% _is implying
guaranteed business results, it is crucial that there be crystal clear
standards to follow across your internal teams, your agencies, your
vendors/partners, everyone.

To help you create a uniform standard to be applied globally, with a
degree of prescribed precision (standard colors!), I want to share
the _one pager _that we use at our agency to deliver higher quality
work to our clients.

My objective in sharing this, making it our agency standard, is to
also encourage recipients to bring more sophisticated thinking.
Rather than just _xx significance is great_, create a focus on
p-values, and to shine a light on sample sizes.

To ensure zero abuse of the phrase _statistical significance,_ improve
the integrity of results, and over time enhance your personal
influence... Copy/paste the below page into your own template and make
it a standard in your practice!

This one-pager is to provide guidance for reporting of all hypothesis
tests, company campaigns, including user surveys, creative testing and
meta analysis.

Success or failure based on significance tests are represented by
p-values, i.e., THE PROBABILITY OF RESULTS OCCURRING BY CHANCE. Use
verbiages like results “suggest/indicate/illustrate”... and
interpret the results dispassionately.

SUCCESS IS DEFINED AS STATISTICALLY SIGNIFICANT  WHEN

* Tier 1 campaigns (super high media spend):   P <0.05 (report as
Strong Statistical Significance, include 95% confidence interval,
color code GREEN)

* Tier 2 campaigns (high media spend): P <0.1 (report as Statistical
Significance, include 90% confidence interval, color code PURPLE)

* User surveys (Creative tests, Digital Brand Lift Studies, etc.): P
<0.1 (report as Statistical Significance, include 90% confidence
interval, color code PURPLE)

REPORT “PRACTICAL SIGNIFICANCE” ALONG WITH “STRONG/STATISTICAL
SIGNIFICANCE.”

* Always report the size of the effect or lift. P-values are in
general not measures of the size of any underlying effect.

* Report headroom lift for statistically significant lift to account
for different baseline

* Report cost per point lift with statistically significant lift

ACTUAL P VALUES ARE IMPORTANT!

* P values should be expressed explicitly for significant results
(P=.04, unless P<.001).

* P values of 0.051 and 0.049 should be interpreted similarly,
despite the fact that the 0.051 is greater than 0.05 and is therefore
not "significant" and that the 0.049 is less than 0.05 and thus is
"significant."

NO DIRECTIONAL LIFT.

* P values near 0.10 shouldn’t be taken either as weak evidence for
an effect or as evidence of a weak effect. There is no 80% confidence
level when P< 0.2.

SUFFICIENT SPEND/SAMPLE.

* For most medium-sized and small campaigns, robust estimation
approaches tailored for small sample sizes are needed.

* Adopt BOOTSTRAP and JACKKNIFE sampling approach for small sample
estimation to be able to measure the performance of small campaigns.

ADJUST P-VALUE FOR MULTIPLE COMPARISON PROCEDURE (MCP) TESTS.

* If four hypotheses were tested using the same data, apply
BONFERRONI adjustment with adjusted P value (.05/4= .0125 per test is
equivalent to 95% confidence in the case of a single test).

FUTURE PLAN

The results of statistical analyses should be used to substantiate
your findings and help objectively identify the outcome of the study
when results are significant. It’s important to make sure the sample
are representative of your target population and the results are truly
replicable.

The measurement team’s efforts will be focused on moving beyond
statistical significance to identifying repeated measures,
meta-analysis, and an emphasis on other strategies that’ll help more
directly predict future performance.

BOTTOM LINE.

It can be difficult as an Analytics team to take something so very
nuanced and get lots of non-analytical peers to understand it
optimally. It can be hard to explain to someone _what do you mean 89%
is crap and 90% is acceptable, IT IS JUST ONE PERCENT!!_

Yet, the burden is yours and mine.

If compromises are made, the outcomes we predict/approve will not
occur. Then, the pain will also be ours to shoulder. Hence, better to
have data, analysis, be used optimally and the results interpreted
optimally.
