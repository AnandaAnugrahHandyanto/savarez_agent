TMAI #434: ATTRIBUTION TO NMMMS: PATH TO GLORY!

[ Web Version [2] ]

Even when things are utterly obvious, it can be challenging to take
the path to glory. At times, the root cause is not having a simple way
to paint the stark reality.

A few weeks ago, for one of the largest advertisers on the planet, I
had the opportunity to solve such a problem. I thought I would share
it with you.

It was about activating a better methodology to prove impact to the
CFO: INCREMENTALITY.

You would be surprised that such a big company, still was not on the
incrementality rocket or that they were having such trouble trying to
get to a starting point.

The secret to increasing velocity: A. Don't try to solve the whole
thing, all at once. Big mistake. B. Create desirable hunger for the
ultimate destination.

Ready?

The default measurement methodology in digital analytics tools, in
digital advertising platforms, is _Last-Click Credit. _

[Last-Click measurement]

No matter how long the consumer journey, as long as the last click was
from Facebook, all 100,000 Conversions would be credited to Facebook.

Nearly all companies use multiple channels in their Paid Media
execution, and additionally do loads of Owned and Earned Marketing
activities.

It is not difficult to see why Last-Click attribution is bonkers.

But.

It persists because it is easy to understand, and since the universe
started with Last-Click attribution, today that is considered
"predictable" - as in _that is what all our financial models are
based on_.

[Implication of Last-Click measurement.]

Last-Click attribution breeds short-term thinking and execution, it
completely hoses Brand Marketing (because no short-term results from
it), and since the entire intellectual exercise is lazy, there is
little incentive to innovate.

If your Finance team builds its quarterly, monthly, and annual
forecasts based on Last-Click results, if your Marketing Scorecards
are based on Last-Click attribution, it would be unusual that your
Marketing is delivering causally provable profits.

You probably struggle to get Marketing budgets needed, it is also
likely Marketing is the first budget to be cut when times are even
slightly tough.

Optimal attribution modeling methodologies begin to resolve this
problem.

They help you see more of the actual consumer journey.

[Attribution measurement vs. Last-Click.]

You spent money on Facebook, Spotify, and Google during that time.

Attribution modeling helps you identify the impact of all three in
driving 100,000 conversions.

Yea!

My picture above is simplistic. Good attribution modeling will show
the _credit _across Paid Search AND Organic YouTube AND Email AND
Billboards AND Magazines AND Affiliates AND... Everything else you
partake in.

Attribution modeling sucks less than Last-Click - this is a big
positive.

[Implication of Attribution measurement.]

A medium-sized problem with most attribution modeling solutions is
that they use poor modeling methodologies - just please use
Data-Driven Attribution, machine learning is the way to go when you
have such immense complexity in the data (due to consumer behavior).

A large-sized problem with activating the value of attribution
modeling is that its results are complex to internalize, and nearly
always kick off a food fight between the teams - none of whom are
incentivized to look at their true value, and all of them worry their
budget will be taken away.

Politics gets in the way of better decisions with machine learning
based attribution modeling.

Hence, at Croud, for our AttributionGeni product [3], we have chosen
to build automation integrated with ad platforms!

[Automating decisions with Attribution.]

The platform analyzes the consumer journey to a
lead/purchase/long-term value. It does so using a sophisticated
machine learning based attribution modeling. At this point, just like
all the other solutions in market, our solution produces more reports
than you shake a stick at. BUT. For our Media clients, the automation
built into AttributionGeni also automatically changes bids, budgets,
etc., DIRECTLY into ad platforms.

Humans can still be informed, there are numerous reports and a spiffy
Scenario Modeling product for them to play with.

We don't need them to. We can automate the best decisions for the
client.

This sucks a lot less! :)

At my ripe old age, I do not recommend engaging in the vanity project
of buying an attribution modeling project that is Very Very Good, but
does not have automated actioning built into it. Such projects solve
for happiness in knowing we are doing something important, but have
zero impact on the business.

My mantra: _SUCK LESS, EVERYDAY._

Hence, attribution modeling is important, it helps you tactically
optimize your budgets, it allows you to make decisions reflecting the
complex consumer journey.

Do it.

Realize that when it comes to the CFO, she / he isn't concerned with
everything above.

Her question: _What would happen if we spend no money on Marketing?__
_
AKA Incrementality

[Marketing's Claimed vs. Actual impact.]

What would happen, if we spent Zero dollars/yen/rupees on Marketing?

In the simplistic example above, Incrementality assessment identified
that 70,000 of the 100,000 Conversions would have happened any way.

That identifies 30,000 as truly Incremental - driven exclusively by
Marketing.

100,000 Conversions, Last-Click or Attribution based: Claimed Impact.

30,000 Conversions, Incrementality based: Actual Impact.

Where did those 30,000 come from? All from Spotify? All from Facebook?
100k each from FB, G, S? Something else?

See why Incrementality measurement is so crucial?

[Comparing attribution and incrementality.]

See why CFOs love Incrementality?

Incrementality measurement forces Marketing organizations to assess
their mortality.

Process, Org, Media, People, Incentives, Local vs. Global Control,
Agencies (in or out), Dashboards... Everything else... Challenged,
evaluated, addressed.

This is why Incrementality measurement breeds innovation and reduces
mediocrity.

It powers a modern way to assess Marketing's impact.

[Incremental measurement visualization.]

The big challenge with Incrementality is that literally No One in
Marketing wants to measure it.

Why would you want to be “credited” with $50m of impact when you
can get away with claiming $180m?

It is extremely understandable. It is a very human reaction.

Hence, the barrier to measuring and activating Incrementality is not
metrics or methodologies (though, they are a bit [4]). It is culture.

The responsibility for this cultural change rests with Finance.

We need our CFO Richard to start the chain of events that will embed
Incrementality powered innovation and excitement in Marketing.

The first step?

Setting Marketing Incrementality based Targets, for the top 3 of our
Big 6 KPIs [5].

That'll get the ball rolling.

It will take a lot of work, yes. But. Your value to your employer will
never be greater!

If you require help, call me for a Strategic Consulting project.

[Move from less actionable Last-Click and Attribution to
Incrementality.]

BOTTOM LINE.

Many of us are still using Last-Click reporting to our CxOs. Share the
implications with them. Move on. We should have a decade ago.

Many of us primarily focused on Attribution Modeling to achieve the
goal of reporting. Move on. What good is it to know if you cannot do?

Insist your Attribution Modeling solution have built in automation.
Insist your Agency/Provider implement immediately. Jump over all the
political problems instantly.

If you want to do radically better Marketing, embrace Incrementality -
by first bringing Finance into the Marketing tent as a culture change
partner. All the Marketer, Ad Platforms, Agencies, will deeply resist.
It is human. Finance will help you overcome.

Carpe diem.
