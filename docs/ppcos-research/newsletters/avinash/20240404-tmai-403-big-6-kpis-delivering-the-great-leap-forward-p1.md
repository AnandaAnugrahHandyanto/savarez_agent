TMAI #403: BIG 6 KPIS: DELIVERING THE GREAT LEAP FORWARD. -P1

[ Web Version [2] ]

I never tire of using KPIs as a lever to drive immense change.

Simply because: KPIs = Incentives.

Yes, there is a lot to think carefully, and action: Picking KPIs. The
difference between a KPI and a Metric. Not being distracted by vanity
metrics. The role of methodologies in hurting or elevating KPIs.
Success KPIs vs. Diagnostic Metrics. Conversation Rate vs. Conversion
Rate (that vs. Assisted Conversions). Do This! Don’t Do That!

Still, this deep into my career, I find I can deliver non-normal
results by continuously refining my KPI recommendations based on a
continuous close observation of CFOs, CMOs and Marketing
organizations. I OBSESS about separating symptoms that look like root
causes from actual root causes. I’m still looking for pathways to
simplicity – clutter is the enemy.

I also, _still_, look for magic bullets – the one strategy, one
framework, that will fix everything!

Given humans in our ecosystem find such innovative ways to avoid the
right path, I keep trying to adapt, uplevel, close loopholes!

As an example: For a while, the metric Bounce Rate was a good magic
bullet. A terrible 50% Bounce Rate forced a closer look at 1.
Incentives set for each team, 2. Org design, and 3. A/B & MTV testing.

Fixing any one of those three things, led to outsized progress. Fixing
all three was magical.
Even if, Bounce Rate by itself was not all that gloriously important.
It is, after all, a diagnostic metric and not a success KPI. But it
got results like: Getting the site owner to stop saying _oh, it’s
Marketing sending us crap traffic_ and Marketers to stop saying_ hey,
my job is just driving clicks!_ It delivered such a great,
long-lasting, benefit for the business that improved so much more than
Bounce Rate.

Today, another _magic bullet.*_

A super, incredibly simple KPIs-based lens, that’ll change your life
– if you embrace it.

It holds the potential to start a needed analytics revolution.

[*there are no magic bullets, just a lot of work, ideally smart work!]

CONTEXT.

I was reflecting on a typical setup of the _business of
analytics _at a large company.

The organization as a whole, their challenges. All dimensions of data
collection, ETL, warehousing, reporting, methodologies, tools, AI,
budget setting, reporting paid media success, and businesses across a
large multi planetary existence.

A normal day for me. ; )

The challenge for any company with such an awesome reality, is that it
is extraordinarily difficult to figure out how to deliver the next
generation of innovation (or, fix current entrenched problems). Every
approach you can think about will quickly devolve into differences
across teams/countries/business types, the distracting minutiae, a
multitude of marketing tactics, and the constant stating of _omg it
is not going to work for us, we are a special snowflake _by different
teams involved.

This can be, is, intensely frustrating because every new idea to
improve anything, by making a small change, is dead on arrival.

Worth acknowledging that there is no malice. She/he/they/them are not
coming to the table with malice, and there is more than a grain of
truth in their minutiae (and, resistance).

With that level of immense complexity, often created due to past
business success and scale, comes this very unique problem to deal
with:

_How do you find a common lens to look through, when there is little
in common to look through? _
Or, even harder:

_How do you help everyone to find the right answers for them, across
25 different existing dimensions, using an approach they can all agree
on?_
Ponder that for a moment.

Ponder the paragraph after _a normal day for me_.

You are to give them the answer to fix operational and innovation
challenges. How do you approach that across, say, 20 countries and six
divisions?

I would love to know what you would do, please reply. I’m sure there
are a hundred ways to do this, and I want to benefit from knowing
yours.

Finished pondering?

THE BIG 6 MAGIC BULLET.

Reminder: You have to fix:

1. data infrastructure,
2. reports,
3. create a culture of analysis,
4. recreate decision-making steps,
5. drive actions for brand and performance marketing, close the loop
with
6. post-spend justification to Finance, and, looking into the future,
upfront decisions to be made around
7. budgets,
8. media/channel allocations, and, super inanely crucial,
9. targets to hold the whole machinery
10. accountable.
That is what it takes to shift from being in the _business of
analytics _to being in the_ analytics of business. _

Daunting, no?

Yet, I suspect, a common challenge – your current challenge.

My approach was simple:

Use six KPIs to prompt high-quality reflections globally (across the
10 items above).
My super-duper secret:

IT’S NOT THE KPI, IT’S THE METHODOLOGY BEHIND THE KPI!
My 2024 higher order bit:

Give me incrementality.
Or, accept that there is still low quality across all 10 bits.
You put those three things together… BOOM! Magic.

Let me unpack it for you.

MAGIC BULLET APPLICATION STEP 1: METHODOLOGY.

Incrementality, in our context, is the answer to this question:

_If we fire everyone in Marketing tomorrow, and put the Marketing
budget in the company Savings account, what results would occur?_
[Vital Nuance: In the short-term, _sadly_, not only will there be no
harm, in most cases, the company’s bottom-line will actually
dramatically improve! In the long-term, for the few companies
executing incrementality-centric Marketing, the bottom-line will
suffer.]

Incrementality hence provides the perfect common lens to understand if
our data infrastructure is sophisticated. Or, if our Marketing leaders
are making high-quality decisions. Or, if your Owned, Earned, Paid
media is being optimally allocated across teams/channels. Or, how
delightful is this, ANY other vital question you can ask! Especially
the one above.

First ask: _Are we making decisions based on incrementality?_

[Premium Member Bonus: Remember: Attribution is not Incrementality,
TMAI #327 [3].]

If the business outcomes you are reporting, to Marketers, Finance,
Agency Account Manager, CMO, SVP of User Experience, are not on the
basis of incrementality… You are not answering the question: _What
would happen if we stop spending Marketing budgets on campaigns,
buying “magic tools”, agency fees?_

If you stop spending money on your Marketing agency, employees,
creative directors, tools, in your non-incremental reports/KPIs it
will look like something terrible is happening. Your last-click
conversions, your industry brand tracker lifts, Markov Chain blah
blah.

But.

In aggregate for the company, all those non-incremental Conversions
and Revenue will still show up – and your Marketing salary and my
Agency fees being cut will improve the bottom-line!

Or, at least you’ve not proven otherwise.

EX: If all you report is Paid Search Performance, and you report it
based on last-click, or even DDA, basis… If I cut your budget to
Zero, and layoff your entire team, sure in your existing narrow view
Paid Search Performance report the Clicks, Add To Carts, Conversions,
Profit will all go down. BUT. All I have to do is look at the SEO
Performance reports, and it would show a spike [because you are no
longer cannibalizing SEO, because buying brand keywords rarely
withstands the scrutiny of incrementality conversion lift studies
(CLS) etc. etc.].

Apply the above example’s philosophy of _we have to prove
incrementality (!)_ to your retail marketing, your coupons, your TV
ads, Email, etc.

Prove that it will hurt the company’s bottom-line to cut any of
those. If you do, your CFO will love you [and become your champion].

Doing this requires courage.

Solving for this will demand overcoming deep, entrenched powers (and
politics of influence).
It also delivers Marketing that will be missed if it is cut.

Nirvana.

MAGIC BULLET APPLICATION STEP 2: BIG 6 KPIS.

Robust incrementality methodologies, check.

Most Marketers are not entirely familiar with incrementality, or have
different definitions for what it is. The other day a Senior Leader
vehemently tried to persuade me that that Pre-Post is a fantastic way
to measure incrementality, and one _did not even need a clean Pre, the
last Post measure would suffice_. #omg

[Premium Member Bonus: In this high stakes conversation, how you
measure incrementality takes on outsized importance. My favorite
methodologies, TMAI 233 [4]. And, no, I don’t like Pre-Post. I
approve two: A. True in-market test  control. B. Advanced ML
models, that are _trust, but verified_.]

Now, we require a simple way to look at every dimension of the
_business of analytics_, and assess if incrementality is present.

This, _hallelujah!_, is rather easy. We are going to use KPIs.

When assessing the strategic impact of Marketing, regardless of what
type of Marketing initiative, only six KPIs matter:

1.    iSales
2.    CPiS
3.    iProfit
4.    Points of Brand Lift (in Target KPI)
5.    Individuals Lifted
6.    CPIL
All you have to do, across all 10 dimensions I mentioned at the start,
is ask:

_DO YOU HAVE THE BIG 6 KPIS ON YOUR REPORT/DASHBOARD/ANALYSIS?_
If the answer is yes, you are golden.

[BONUS TIP: Double-check the methodology being used, frequency of
measurement, and then you are platinum!]

The more common answer, if my experience is reflective of the norm,
will be _no_.

While it will feel like your heart is being broken, welcome _no_ as an
answer. The first step to a better, rosy, future is knowing today is a
problem.

With that honest assessment completed, _our CMO or CFO do not have the
Big 6 in their dashboards_, we can start to do the exciting rebuilding
to move from heartbreak to golden!

It will take two steps: 1: Internalize deeply each Big 6 KPI. 2: Use
the optimal methodology (above) to start measuring it.

Let’s understand deeply, along with my _spent loads of sweat and
blood _lessons, why each Big 6 KPI is magical.

BIG 6 #1: CAUSALLY DRIVEN INCREMENTAL SALES BY MARKETING. [ISALES]

TACTICAL: _How many Incremental Sales did your Marketing initiative
deliver?_

STRATEGIC:_ How many Incremental Sales did your CMO deliver?_

iSales are typically reported at three altitudes:

LEVEL 3: Channel-Silo level (for Marketing Directors, and in-flight
optimization),

LEVEL 2: xStack level (for Marketing VPs, and channel
allocation/cutting), and

LEVEL 1: Portfolio level (for your CFO, and proving the CMO’s glory
/ getting a higher Marketing budget)

For Levels 3 and 2, you’ll use a combination of true test <> control
experiments. On many ad platforms, you’ll get L3 using Conversion
Lift Studies [CLS]– for free! For L2, you’ll typically set up
large scale matched-market tests – not free.

For Level 1, you’ll leverage super advanced modeling (ideally using
machine learning) – backed up with _trust, but verify_ proof.

Level 3, Channel-Silo incremental Sales is the minimum acceptable
state. In part because it is so easy to get to.

Realize that the level 3 iSales number you’ll report is inflated
(often, vastly). That is ok. It is a million times better to be at L3
when compared to low levels of Last-Click or even data-driven
Attribution.

L2 removes overlaps and inefficiencies hidden in siloed views, hence
the incremental impact from Marketing will be lower.

At L1, you not only remove overlaps and inefficiencies, you are also
including the impact of Owned and Earned marketing initiatives. You
also include answering delightful questions like:

Was it Marketing or just Product Quality?
Was it Marketing or our spectacular Customer Service driving Word of
Mouth?
Was it Marketing or just three of our Competitors going bust?
Was it all because of our incredibly innovative operating model?
Hence, L1 is the truest incremental impact of Marketing – the only
one a CFO will care about.

Start with level 3. Don’t stop until level 1.

Some of you might be wondering, wait, why was the KPI “magical”?

Fair question.

Because this is what’s happening behind the scenes, when you ask for
iSales…

[Big 6 KPIs | Cascading positive impact.]

You are going to have to figure out a way to put the I into your
current Marketing Claimed Sales measurement.

That will force you to move up the sexy methodologies totem pole.

Which will in turn require your Analysis Ninjas needing to develop
sophisticated _trust, but verify_ approaches.

Oh, and you are not going to stop until you are measuring iSales at
scale across everything you are doing.

Finally, because just reporting Sales will feel dirty by then, you are
going to eliminate all the false gods from your reports and
dashboards.

In the quest for this one KPI, every single dimension of your
existence will have to change.

Magic. : )

Do the Marketing impact scorecard you send to your CMO & CFO have
iSales?

Level 3? Level 2? Level 1?

I want you to know that I know that this is all hard.

There are times when I know that I can get away with reporting opaque

I don’t because I know that that is not going to prevent Marketing

I don’t because my job is to make the CMO bulletproof to the CFO,
