It is time for humans to hand Digital/Web Analytics over to AI &
Algorithms.

At a deep level this pains me.

You'll remember I originated the 10/90 rule of Analytics [3] 20+ years
ago.

"_If you have $100 to invest in smart decisions, invest $10 in tools
and implementation and invest $90 in humans who will analyze the
data!_"

The new 10/90 rule:

"_If you have $100 to invest in smart decisions, invest $10 in
brilliant human analytical strategists, invest $90 in AI activation._"

In fact, over time the $100 is likely to reduce to $80, then $70, and
maybe less... While the quality of decisions, the scale of
intelligence and automation, will exponentially increase.

Incredible, no?

I'd laid out my thinking powering this revolution in TMAI #492 [4].
Here's the (non-ironically) AI-generated visual summary:

[Shift from human-powered to AI-powered analytics.]

We are at a seminal moment in analytics, the transition from mostly
_human-powered _reporting and analytics to _AI-powered_ reporting,
analytics, and automated decisions.

This will DRAMATICALLY reduce the _INSIGHTS LATENCY_ that bedevils
_human-powered _analytics. The upside is unimaginably immense.

The first three real-world use cases that demonstrate this were in
TMAI #492:

*
Predictive Analytics via Propensity Modeling

*
Advanced Customer Segmentation.

*
Voice-of-Customer Integration with Behavioral Analytics.

In each instance, you are shifting FROM _rear-view mirror,
data-spewing of reports, dashboards, and hoping for the best_ TO_
predictive causal insights, and automation of actions!_

Let’s learn three more joy-inducing profit-accelerating
possibilities available today - one of them has AI running the
equivalent of 5,000 tests Per Week. 🤯

4. BEHAVIOR TARGETING & INTELLIGENCE (BTI).

IMPACT POTENTIAL: Transformational.

Traditional segmentation and A/B testing treat users as large
ambiguous globs or static cohorts, serving content based on broad
demographics or simplistic rules. This creates a frustrating,
one-size-fits-all experience that ignores individual behavior
patterns, real-time intent signals, and evolving preferences. Result:
A sad failure to recognize and respond to the user's immediate
journey, resulting in frustration and abandoned intent.

[Premium Subscribers, a challenge: TMAI #442:  Stop A/B Testing [5].
[5]]

BTI moves beyond simple "testing" into the realm of CONTINUOUS
INTELLIGENCE. AI excels here because it can ingest and analyze
individual-level behavior signals in real-time to determine intent.
Instead of just picking a winning headline or offer or image, the AI
identifies macro and hidden patterns across the dataset and builds the
most relevant experience. BTI creates an enviable flywheel effect: As
the AI learns from the relevance and meaningfulness delivered to one
user, it instantly improves the predictive model for every subsequent
user!

Reinforcement learning approaches in particular are exceptional at
solving what humans are poor at: Balancing _exploration _(learning new
intent-response mappings) and _exploitation _(delivering the _known,
more meaningful _experience). Producing a vast library of assets
takes a lot of work, but the result is magical: Joy for users, profits
for the company.

AI APPROACHES AND ALGORITHMS to explore, stress test, and embrace:

*
CONTEXTUAL & BEHAVIORAL BANDITS. Your core engine. They personalize
the entire experience (content, offers, layout) by continuously
evaluating the context (device, source, time) against the user's
behavioral history and real-time actions to infer intent and maximize
long-term value (you have to be able to identify what “long-term
value” means – not as easy as you think).

*
DEEP REINFORCEMENT LEARNING: If you know AI, you know this one. It
models the entire experience journey as a sequence of decisions. It
rewards the system not just for a click, but for generating “joy”
(which we define) and downstream profit. It does so while learning
complex, non-linear relationships between user signals, inferred
intent, and business outcomes. To see it working is mind-blowing.

*
COLLABORATIVE FILTERING FOR BEHAVIOR. Clusters users not by profile,
but by dynamic behavioral similarity ("users who did this, also
responded well to that"), enabling intent-based prediction for
individuals. At a nano-size level, we humans can do this; the scale at
which Collaborative Filtering works is Milky Way-size level.

*
NATURAL LANGUAGE GENERATION (NLG) & DYNAMIC ASSEMBLY. Dynamically
constructs copy and offers that resonate with the specific emotional
state or intent phase of the user (e.g., urgency vs. reassurance)
rather than using static templates. The depth and breadth varies by
your ambition, start with deploying for Paid Media (say, Search).

*
SEQUENCE & PATTERN RECOGNITION MODELS. Identify macro and micro
behavioral patterns across your entire user base. It uncovers hidden
intent pathways and predicts critical next actions (which your tech
stack responds to, if it is modern).

One of these is not the answer. It is our ability to assemble the
right combo – based on the company’s strategic objectives _for its
human-company interaction layers._

PRACTICAL EXAMPLE.

Many companies are attempting the “BT” part. The rare one is
attempting the “I” part at any scale. In this rarest instance a
media company implemented a true BT & I system across their digital
property. It customized content and experience. Results:

*
In under 100 milliseconds, the system assembled an experience from a
stack of 50+ headlines, 15+ media formats, and 10+ layout structures
– to match inferred intent.

*
The system learned that users demonstrating _scanning behavior
_(rapid, shallow scroll – hopefully not what you are doing as you
read this newsletter!). It responded by creating a _key fact summary,_
which increased article completion by 45%.

*
(Reverse of above.) It identified users exhibiting _lingering
behavior_ on specific paragraphs. It responded by dynamically
generating _deep dive _related content modules, which increased
engagement time by 70% (and boosted NPS).

POTENTIAL OUTCOMES FOR YOU.

Looking across my work dataset and the limited examples in the
industry, the potential outcomes include:

*
Intent-responsive experiences drive an approx. 45% increase in NPS.

*
60% reduction in bounce rates for high-intent segments (think paid
media, brand or performance) for ecommerce. 30-80% improvement in
engagement metrics compared to static content for publishers.

*
(Only one instance of this in my personal experience, at a large
company:) The system eventually ran autonomously, conducting the
equivalent of 5,000+ traditional A/B tests per week – maximizing
this outcome: Profit.

*
Approx. 25% uplift in LTV, for users who initially converted via a
majority optimized BTI experience.

The thing that attracts me to BTI over and above all else is the
discovery of non-intuitive winning combinations of experiences (and
the scale at which it can be done). It’s the _omg, look, that works_
or _omg, I would NEVER have thought of that_. It’s being humbled.

5. NATURAL LANGUAGE PROCESSING (NLP) FOR UNSTRUCTURED DATA.

IMPACT POTENTIAL: High.

We’ve been waiting for useful NLP for a hundred years! Finally…
more useful _why_, at scale.

Web analytics has traditionally focused on the _what_, quantitative
data (clicks, pageviews, conversions). Without the _why_ from the vast
qualitative data from surveys, chat transcripts, support tickets, and
social media, all in silos, it was challenging to convert _what _into
actionable insights. The limited reading of qual data meant any
“insights” were subjective.

NLP algorithms can process thousands of text documents in minutes,
extracting themes, sentiment, and, my fav, intent at scale. Recent
advances in transformer models (like BERT and GPT architectures) have
dramatically improved machines' ability to understand context and
nuance in human language – with even more improvements on the
horizon.

AI APPROACHES AND ALGORITHMS to explore, stress test, and embrace:

*
TOPIC MODELING: LATENT DIRICHLET ALLOCATION (LDA), BERTOPIC. Start
with _generative probabilistic modeling _with LDA to uncover hidden
thematic structures in large text collections. Graduate to a _modern,
transformer-based _topic modeling framework with BERTopic to generate
semantically meaningful themes in unstructured data.

*
INTENT CLASSIFICATION MODELS. Really great at identifying what users
want to accomplish based on their language, words, and layers within.
DistilBERT is a transformer encoder. MiniLM is great at intent routing
and clustering. FastText is exceptional for short-text. NLI prompts
are great for long-tail intents.

*
NAMED ENTITY RECOGNITION. Fantastic at extracting specific mentions of
products, features, offers, or competitors, and you can link entities
across channels and campaigns. BiLSTM-CRF, RoBERTa, and SpaCy for a
named entity recognition pipeline.

*
ADVANCED TRANSFORMER MODELS. Fine-tuned models that understand
domain-specific language and context (ex: financial language, medical,
contracts, error codes, and workflows).

PRACTICAL EXAMPLE.

One of my personal favs was for an online retailer. Integrated NLP
analysis of customer service chats (_why_) with traditional behavioral
data (web analytics). Insights:

*
47% higher cart abandonment for users mentioning “size
uncertainty” in chats – intervene!

*
30% higher lifetime value when positive sentiment was expressed about
“easy returns.” (Feels like a _doh!_ But, need the hard number to
get leadership to move/invest.)

*
Identified specific product confusion patterns that predicted negative
reviews before they happen.

[BONUS: I can’t vouch for 100% accuracy, but in an interview a
candidate shared an example of their NLP work: _To identify which user
was most likely to directly email Tim Cook with a complaint!_ I was
impressed. 😊]

POTENTIAL OUTCOMES FOR YOU.

See the 47% and 30% above – entirely possible for your B2B,
Canadian, business. Also:

*
+15 point improvement in Employee NPS from automating what previously
required immense boring manual analysis of thousands/tens of thousands
of qual data.

*
15%-20%  improvement in Conversion Rate from identification of
specific language that resonates (or confuses) different customer
segments & their intent.

*
Not easily quantified impact from a holistic customer understanding
(what+why).

Effective use of NLP knows no bounds across a modern corporation.
(More: Early warning systems to detect emerging issues weeks before
your tech team realizes / an employee likely to quit / a customer
unlikely to renew!)

6. ANOMALY DETECTION AND AUTOMATED INSIGHT GENERATION.

IMPACT POTENTIAL: High.

Most analytics teams rely on threshold-based alerts. Ex: _alert me if
daily conversions drop 10%_. I have advocated for this [6]! Sadly,
this creates either alert fatigue (too many false positives) or missed
important changes (false negatives). Alerts also do not come with the
IAbI [7], hence when anomalies occur, Analysts spend hours diagnosing
the root cause across dozens of potential dimensions.

Anomaly detection algorithms solve three problems: 1. They learn the
normal patterns across multiple metrics simultaneously – awesome. 2.
They can detect deviations that would be invisible to human threshold
alerts which are usually monitored individually - fabulous. 3.
Advanced implementations can then automatically correlate anomalies
with potential causes, significantly reducing diagnosis time – BOOM!

AI APPROACHES AND ALGORITHMS to explore, stress test, and embrace:

*
ISOLATION FORESTS. Super effective for high-dimensional messy data,
identifying anomalies by isolating outliers in feature space. The
model isolates points by splitting data with random rules.

*
AUTOENCODERS. They are neural networks that learn to reconstruct
normal patterns, and flag anomalies when something no longer fits that
pattern. Very good at handling complex relationships between metrics,
and adapting as behavior shifts over time.

*
CAUSAL INFERENCE MODELS: Correlation is not causality. 😊 These
models explain why something changed, not just that it changed. Common
model types include _difference-in-differences, propensity score
matching, _and_ uplift models_. The insights are stronger as the
approach prevents false wins with random noise.

*
BAYESIAN STRUCTURAL TIME SERIES. BSTS models explain changes in
time-based data. The model learns normal trends, seasonality, noise.
It builds a counterfactual baseline. Observed data is compared to that
baseline. The gap estimates causal impact. Particularly helpful when
randomized tests are not possible.

(Note: BSTS is often the engine behind tools called “causal
impact.”)

PRACTICAL EXAMPLE.

A fun project. Approximately 130 KPIs, metrics, and influencing
variables, across the company’s mobile app and website data, were
_thrown _into an AI monitoring system. Instead of separate alerts for
each metric, it understands the whole. Results:

*
Anomaly detection time reduced from hours to minutes due to a switch
from single threshold to an Ensemble Anomaly Detection system.

*
Higher quality root-cause rankings helped isolate _why_ when cart to
checkout funnel abandonment increased. Conversion losses in millions
prevented.

*
In the first 30 days, reduced false positives by 70%, improving
employees’ lives and reducing MTTR.

I’m not including our first embarrassment: Drop in mobile
conversions causally tied to deployment of the new analytics tag! Hard
to admit that one. 😊

POTENTIAL OUTCOMES FOR YOU.

Looking across my work in three continents:

*
65-90% reduction in false positives, compared to threshold-based
systems.

*
Revenue loss due to technical incidents reduced by 20-40%.

*
Not easily quantified impact from proactive issue detection – often
identifying problems before the users notice them.

AI takes the practice of causal root-cause analysis for anomalies to a
whole new level. But, even with earlier algorithms, the possibilities
were immense. My example above I would not call pure AI. Please push
your Data Science team to embrace this approach – and if they
don’t, question the incentives your organization has set for them.

BOTTOM LINE.

The integration of AI into Analytics represents the most significant
shift in our field since its birth as a science.

The organizations that will thrive in the coming years aren't those
with the most data, most Analysts, most spending on Analytics. They
will be the ones who can extract the most insight from their data with
the greatest speed. i.e., reduce _insights latency _and increase
automation.

AI and advanced algorithms provide the tools to make this possible,
transforming analytics from a practice of historical reporting to one
of predictive intelligence and prescriptive optimization.
