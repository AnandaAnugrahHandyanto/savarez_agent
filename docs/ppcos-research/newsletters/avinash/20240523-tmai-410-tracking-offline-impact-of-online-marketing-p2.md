TMAI #410: TRACKING OFFLINE IMPACT OF ONLINE MARKETING – P2

[ Web Version [2] ]

With most commerce happening offline, Multichannel Analytics remains
the white whale to your Mr. Ahab.

That’s not just true for your regular _joes _Macys, Marks & Spencer,
and Mannings. Even the very best DTC companies, think Caspers, Warby
Parker, Allbirds, have to grow beyond their initial spurt with
intelligent 1P and 3P real world retail strategies.

In TMAI #409 [3], I’d help illuminate the two central challenges:

A. Access to offline data.

B. Missing “primary keys.”
Everything multichannel analytics comes down to A and B.

Once we’ve sailed past them, we can begin our Multichannel Analytics
journey.

We covered the following in TMAI #409:

LEVEL 1. Track the online impact of online Marketing.

LEVEL 2. Track offline impact of online Marketing – Simple.
And, it is so exciting to start to take control of your destiny.

You are almost half way there by getting to Level 2. Your boss is
excited. Your peers are envious of your hotness.

It is time to kick things up five notches!

LEVEL 3. TRACK OFFLINE IMPACT OF ONLINE MARKETING – COMPLEX.

Complex as in, super smart Math.

A little harder to do. You’ll need more data to separate the signal
from the noise.

Do this if you have a couple of hundred retail stores [1P or 3P].

The simplest start is to execute LARGE-SCALE EXPERIMENTS. Pause online
Marketing activity in California, continue it in New York [assuming
they are _like-type_ and _like-sized _for your business]. Measure the
delta in offline sales. You can dial up the precision to the extent
the sophistication of your online Marketing allows:

Only turn on/off Marketing for one product, or just the latest product
launch.

Or, stop all Facebook advertising west of mississippi river.

Or, all initiatives targeting GenZ across two or three test/control
markets. [Though don’t be entirely shocked when you can’t measure
any lift – because, as a client found, all their advertising
targeting GenZ was doing nothing for the brand!]

Or, pick the weakest offline sales products, across test/control
markets use online Marketing to boost sales – see if online works. :
)

Or, cut all the Display ad budget, pump it all into CTV across
test/control markets, and measure the impact on offline Conversions.
[Tip: If your experiment will be long enough in duration – use these
experiments to measure impact on Brand KPIs like Unaided Brand
Awareness, Consideration, and (Purchase) Intent.]

Or, if you are a multi-product and multi-VP-Marketing org… And, you
have a big enough Marketing budget… Set up A-B-C-D cells to vary
spend by VP1, VP2, VP3, and measure impact on offline Conversions by
effectiveness of VP1, VP2, VP3!

Or, stop 100% of Organic Social activity for two months globally, see
if you see any impact on offline Sales. [I’m teasing you, you’ve
read TMAIs on Organic Social impact. Unless you have no choice,
don’t use Pre-Post as a way to do experimentation – causality is
extremely difficult to prove.]

Or, this is cheeky, do nothing to online Marketing, but pump up
offline Marketing by 3x in a test market. Measure offline Sales lift.
Compare Incremental Sales & Cost Per Incremental Sales to online
Marketing. Maybe, just maybe, a better use of Marketing budget is
moving it offline.

The possibilities are endless.

Most experiments fail. Be sure to have someone in the team, or Agency,
with expertise in Design of Experiments, AND your business.

[Premium member bonus: TMAI #373 [4], and #374 [5]: Game-Changing
Analytics: GEO Experiments.]

[AND: Premium member bonus: NEVER below 90% stat sig [6] for
experiments. Ideally, 95%!]

An advanced variation is SUPERPANEL EXPERIMENTS.

You work with someone like Beat Grid [7]. You get to build custom
test/control cells for your ads, and for that custom Superpanel, track
their exposure to ads and offline Conversions. A bit more work, but
for the very largest companies, a neat option.

My next recommendation is our trusty friend: MODELING.

If you can collect the right sets of Marketing activity data, and
atleast 1P offline sales [or 1P + 3P offline sales data] for a
sufficient period of time [six months at the very minimum, ideally two
or three years], then you can use statistical methodologies from OLD
SCHOOL REGRESSION ANALYSIS to the super sophisticated MEDIA MIX MODELS
based on machine learning to identify the causal impact of any
Marketing [or just online Marketing] on sales anywhere [including just
offline].

If you are working with a smart MULTI-TOUCH ATTRIBUTION vendor, like
Croud [8] :), you can use spiritually the same approach that you know
as MTA analysis to assess the impact of all your online Marketing on
your offline Conversions. I say smart because typically MTA will not
measure incrementality. If you have a smart vendor, they can use the
underlying approach to help you intelligently assess offline impact.

LIFE LESSON: Insist on showing the confidence intervals of any results
being presented – how much signal quality is in the noise.

Modeling, whichever solution you use, will provide the desirable
narrow confidence interval answers at higher altitudes. Hence, stick
to that.

Ex: High altitude: Does my Google Paid Search budget drive offline
sales? Low altitude: Do my Google Paid Search ads for keywords_
rubharbh-taco ice cream, durian candy_, and _brown sugar boba_, drive
offline sales?

Lower altitude – super granular dimensions – simply means less
data, so significantly longer duration experiments, massive chance of
uncontrolled variables influencing the experiment, and, of course,
wide confidence intervals [_incremental sales are in the range of 600k
and 4,700k_ – essentially useless].

LEVEL 4: REALITY-CHECKING QUALITATIVE ANALYSIS.

Surveys, Primary and Secondary Research, are your BFFs.

Use your POST-PURCHASE SURVEYS to assess what influenced the offline
purchase [or offline and online purchases, if you have both].

Conduct CUSTOM RESEARCH across your current customers – atleast
quarterly – to assess the influence of various Marketing activities
on their past or future purchases [segment the question!]. Be ready
for _word of mouth_ to be ranked #1, and question why you spend all
that money on Paid Media.

Conduct PRIMARY RESEARCH across your prospective customers.
Same/similar set of questions.

Deploy an ON-EXIT SURVEY on your website – capturing both Task
Completion Rate [to judge if the site/app did its job of persuasion]
and Intention to Buy Offline.
You are leveraging qualitative analysis for two reasons:

A. Your quant methods will not capture everything perfectly – even
the complex ones. Qual is a nice attach to see if anything big might
be missed.

B. Trust, but Verify your quant analysis. If your MMM is saying 79% of
offline Conversions are driven by online Marketing, but your qual
analysis says 19%, your MMM is for sure wrong. The goal is not for
them to match, but they can’t be that out of whack.
LIFE LESSON: With qualitative analysis, Do NOT react to small or
short-term movements in the data. The signal quality will never be
that good [wide confidence intervals]. Look at the qual analysis over
longer periods, look at the trends and patterns in the answers –
they will more closely reflect reality.

ALL FOR ONE, ONE FOR ALL.

Long-time Subscribers will know that I’m a fan of using the metaphor
ladder of awesomeness [LoA].

As in Marketing’s LoA, Attribution Modeling’s LoA [9].

That implies a one way, going up to a destination.

For measuring the offline impact of online Marketing, you’ll have
all four levels in play at the same time. Each will solve a different
problem.

Level 1 for in-flight optimization.

Level 2 for making quarterly budget decisions across online
initiatives.

Level 3 every six or nine months for VP/CMO level budget decisions,
with the CFO.

Level 4 basically always on.
It feels like a lot of work, but if you recall, most ecommerce impact
is offline – what else could be more important for your CFO to
measure?

BOTTOM LINE.

You will never get to the perfect answer.

That is simply not the point.

You can start today, literally today, with Level 1. Get additional
signal with Level 2. For larger companies, you will be very close to
reality with Level 3 – especially if you _trust, but verify_ with
Level 4.

At every level, your CFO will buy more into Marketing’s true
incrementality in offline [B2C or B2B] impact. Your CMO might even be
made CEO – which might have a cascading positive impact on you as
well!

Carpe Diem.
