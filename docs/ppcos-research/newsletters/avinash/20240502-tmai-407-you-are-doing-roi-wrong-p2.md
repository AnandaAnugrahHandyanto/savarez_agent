Does your CFO _buy _your assessment of Marketing’s ROI?

Not entirely be your fault as the VP of Growth or the Planetary Chief

Marketing’s ROI can be insanely difficult to measure (as we covered

What you end up showing as Marketing’s ROI is usually the ROI of a
part of Marketing, for only a part of the business outcomes, often
only super-short-term, using methodologies covering vastly different
intelligence.

4. CHANNEL SILO INCREMENTALITY SPECIAL CASE: BRAND LIFT STUDIES.

When this methodology is used, your smart Agency, you’ll hear:

_The ROI of our brand campaign was a 12% Lift in BLS_.

Nearly all digital ad platforms provide Brand Lift Studies (BLS) for
free when you spend your Brand dollars with them [above a small
threshold].

Ad platforms like Facebook, Google, TikTok, others, create true test
(exposed) and control (no ad exposure) audience groups. Over the
course of the campaign, they’ll report your lift in metrics like
Unaided Awareness, Consideration, and [Purchase] Intent. They also
measure one thousand five hundred diagnostic metrics like Ad Recall,
Brand Familiarity – be wary, these are mostly distractions.

Offline ad platforms, TV, Radio, Billboards, etc., usually require you
to set up your own BLS, usually with a third part measurement
platform.

[PREMIUM MEMBER BONUS: Please see TMAI #323 [4] for the best brand
measurement KPIs, just three, and for each KPI the three metrics you
need to report for high-impact insights.]

Unlike easily discredited methodologies like Pre-Post studies, BLS
provides you with the first level assessment of Brand ROI using a
methodology built on incrementality.

WHAT BLS ROI INCLUDES:

Brand lift delivered in individual channels [/channel silo].

Only paid Brand Marketing initiatives. [No Owned, Earned, Brand
activities.]
WHAT BLS ROI DOES NOT INCLUDE:

Any implication of impact on Performance [aka, Sales, Conversions –
online or offline].

Also, does not imply any implication on medium or long-term impact on
Performance.

Delivering +5 Lift in Consideration does not imply a x% increase in
Sales in three months or six or nine. [Ex: Spike and silence will
deliver zero impact from that five-point lift!]
WHAT'S BLS ROI'S BIGGEST CHALLENGE:

If you are advertising on multiple channels, BLS can't account for
channel interactions, hence might over/under value its measured
channel.
WHAT IS BLS BEST FOR:

If your Brand budget is large enough: In-flight optimization across
creative, targeting, audience, frequency, etc., in the BLS channel.

Identifying incremental impact of a Brand campaign for the
campaign’s duration.  [It decays really, really fast, hence your
Brand Marketing investment strategy HAS TO BE spike and sustain –
see TMAI #386 [5].]

Investing future Brand budgets in channels with lower Success KPI
baselines where you see the channel delivering lift, for desired
audiences.
In April 2024, it is impossible to call an Analytics team good if they
are at the minimum not measuring BLS using true and balanced test and
control clusters.

BONUS FOR ANALYSIS NINJAS: Worth noting that Brand Lift Studies are
conducted radically differently by each ad platform, [even when they
use true test/control methodology].   Here are BLS questions across
platforms to give you a hint at how messy it can get if you just take
the results and run to your CMO without digging into exact questions
and answer choices…

[Brand Lift Studies | FB, YT, TT]

So, you should use Intent (fb), Purchase Intent (YT), and Action

5. XSTACK INCREMENTALITY.

When this methodology is used, you'll hear:

_Our Channel Silo ROI on Google Search, DV, and YouTube is 22%, 3%,
and 18% respectively. Our xStack Incrementality ROI on Google
platforms is 13%._
When you aggregate ROI across channels (say, FB and Instagram or NBC
and Peacock), ROI often goes down on an incrementality basis.

[NOTE: It typically does not when you look at attribution! Hence,
important not to confuse those two methodologies.]

Put another way… If you spend $2m on Google Search, $1m on DV360 and
$1m on YouTube… Should you spend $4m on the Google Stack? The
incrementality answer is almost always: No. Across brand or
performance. Identifying that answer leverages xStack incrementality
ROI.

Should you spend money on Facebook, Instagram, and TV to drive +3
points of lift in Consideration? How about to sell 80,000 Coach Tabby
purses in the month of May? xStack Incrementality.

Unlike CLS, no one will give you xStack incrementality measurement for
free.

For xStack incrementality measurement, your wonderful internal
team/Agency will set up large scale controlled experiments/super
panels to measure xStack incrementality.

It is critical to have a systematic process to follow, a genetic
algorithm to pick test/control cells, have a proper cool down period,
_yada, yada, yada_. Otherwise, the failure rate of experiments can be
as high as 100%, and very painful.

WHAT XSTACK INCREMENTALITY ROI INCLUDES:

You can set up measurement to focus on campaign level brand lift –
across channels.

You can set up measurement to focus on Performance outcomes online,
offline, or both!

And, this is the first methodology allowing you to set up measurement
to focus on Brand outcomes AND Performance outcomes at the same time.

[TIP: Just be careful to ensure you get the Creative right. Getting
one Creative to do both requires your Creative Agency/VP of Global
Creative to have God-like skills.]
WHAT XSTACK INCREMENTALITY ROI DOES NOT INCLUDE:

xStack incrementality won’t deliver insights re tactics (like
creative, niche targeting options).

It won’t deliver answers for 15 different micro-hypotheses.
[You will be pressured by Marketers, your VP, to go for these. Know
that that is the kiss of death for any large-scale experiment.]
WHAT'S XSTACK INCREMENTALITY ROI'S BIGGEST CHALLENGE:

Honestly, not a lot. Maybe, if something weird's going on with Owned
and Earned initiatives, they might pollute your experiment.
WHAT IS XSTACK INCREMENTALITY ROI BEST FOR:

Quarterly budget, and budget reallocation, decisions.

Stack level budget optimizations (say, across Search, YT, DV360).

For the first time, truly understanding multichannel impact of
Marketing, limited only by availability of data. You’ll make
intelligent decisions across Marketing VPs (which they may or may not
enjoy).
I cannot emphasize my pal Harry’s advice enough:

_For the sake of Thor, keep your hypothesis as simple and as clean as
possible._
Most experiments do not fail due to metrics, tools, methodologies, or
the Analyst. They failed because the hypothesis was unclean.

BONUS FOR ANALYSIS NINJAS: It is not unusual to re-run business
crucial xStack experiments across seasons, products, platforms – the
meta-analysis will help you capture important variables changing
outside the experiment period.

6. NIRVANA: PORTFOLIO INCREMENTALITY.

Our destination.

When this methodology is used, your CFO will hear the words:

_Marketing’s ROI is an incremental 18% of all Sales anywhere. At a
Cost Per Incremental Sale of $41.
_
Portfolio incrementality!

Finally, coming very close to answering the question:

_What would happen if we shut down all of Marketing?_

It is challenging to get to the ROI of _all of Marketing_, it is
nearly impossible to get all the necessary data. There's also the
reality that some Marketing remains _faith-based _(and that is
totally ok). But. You can get close. Excellent news: You can for sure
account for the largest chunk of cash being spent by Marketing,
like…

[Portfolio Incrementality]

The most common approach to measure Portfolio Incrementality will be
to use a next-gen Mix Modeling [nMMM] solution. The old school Mix
Modeling [oMMM] solutions are also often referred to as “econometric
models.”

[Pattern: A heartbreaking number of CMOs lave long disliked  oMMMs.
When I speak to them, there are two reasons: A. They don't like the
methodology, often find it challenging to understand it. B. They don't
like the answers the model give - this is difficult to overcome.]

THIS IS KEY: It does not matter if you are using oMMMs as your
methodology or nMMMs as your methodology. Invest in Portfolio
Incrementality based ROI.

At the conclusion of the exercise, you will be able to report
incremental Marketing-Driven Sales and Cost Per Incremental
Marketing-Driven Sale. You will send your CFO to _joy overload_ when
you segment the two KPIs by marketing initiatives, quarters, geo, and
other slices – as long as you can stay inside narrow confidence
intervals.

Over time, with partners, by yourself, you will get a signal quality
boost by moving from oMMMs to nMMMs. As you do, consider the following
simple requirements:

oMMMs assess impact only inside channel silos - ok in the past as we
invested in so few channels. nMMMs assess channel interactions -
critical now as we invest in so many, many, channels all at the same
time.

The best nMMMs can identify the ground truth inside the data, vs.
having human bias - aka  “expert judgement” - hand coded into the
models – as is common with oMMMs.

In addition to % or raw incremental impact, demand confidence
intervals in both approaches. You’ll be surprised, sometimes
shocked.

Every high quality nMMM will come with a Scenario Modeling layer for
optimal future budget allocations. These models use the channel
interactions, diminishing return curves, etc., to recommend:

A. overall Marketing budget,
B. allocate it across channels,
C. quarterize the allocations accounting for seasonality and
competitive patterns, and, crucially,
D. predict INCREMENTAL_ _bottom-line outcomes.**

Your Agency will take it from here re trafficking and media delivery.

No MMM measurement, o or n, should exist without an active _Trust, but
Verify_  (TbV) program.  Without TbV, you will fail - no matter how
smart your MMM. See note on pattern above.  Hence, at Croud, our MMMs
are bundled with TbV - using a combination of Causal AI (for scale)
and experimentation (for precision).
One last enticement: oMMMs have typically required large budgets (to
deliver narrow confidence intervals), they can be expensive, and they
often arrive six to nine months later. nMMMs typically deliver the
same or higher signal quality for lower budgets, they can be
expensive, and with a smart team/agency they arrive three months
later.

End of dropping complicated advanced reflections on you. : )

WHAT PORTFOLIO INCREMENTALITY ROI INCLUDES:

Nearly all of your large Marketing budgets. For sure 100% of your Paid
Media budget. I love including Retail Marketing spend (including via
third-party resellers).

Incremental impact as you put together, say TV + FB + G + Catalogs +
CTV + Retail Media + Coop Dollars + etc. etc.

It will compute impact across online, offline, and any other line you
can find.

It is very close to: _What Performance value did your CMO add to your
company?_
WHAT PORTFOLIO INCREMENTALITY ROI DOES NOT INCLUDE:

It does not include Brand data (surveys), Brand KPIs (UBA, C, PI
etc.).** Some will say that _oh for sure, our MMM also includes brand
metrics_, be careful. Very careful. Ask hard questions.

The concept of “_we can rerun your MMM every single day or on demand
to power your daily Marketing decisions_” is also immensely sketchy.
It is promising: _Yes, the plane we’ve built to fly you from Chicago
to New York can also be used to commute in downtown Manhattan!_ Yes,
physically possible, but if you don’t want lots of destruction, a
Citibike is better.

MMMs, even nMMMs, cannot account for the impact of Creative (or nearly
all tactics and sub-tactics). It is also not their job because of the
altitude they are operating at. And, we have other causal options for
tactics.

WHAT IS PORTFOLIO INCREMENTALITY ROI'S BIGGEST CHALLENGE:

Data. There is never enough. It is a massive pain to gather and
clean. This causes delays in doing analysis, and limitations in
questions that can be answered.
WHAT IS PORTFOLIO INCREMENTALITY ROI BEST FOR:

Quarterly budget, and budget reallocation, decisions.

Stack level budget optimizations (say, across Search, YT, DV360).

For the first time, truly understanding multichannel impact of
Marketing, limited only by the availability of data. You’ll make
intelligent decisions across Marketing VPs (which they may or may not
enjoy).
[** nMMMs cannot include Brand survey results – a mismatch in size
of data – but, if your Analytics team/Agency is excellent at
meta-analysis, and has a handful of years of data, they can compute
the _predicted impact on brand KPIs, based on nMMM recommended channel
allocations_. Super advanced, but so freaking magical because you get
both Brand and Performance future – and this is the only place
they’ll co-exist across everything we’ve discussed.]
