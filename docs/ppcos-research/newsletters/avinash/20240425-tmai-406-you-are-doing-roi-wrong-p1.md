TMAI #406: YOU ARE DOING ROI WRONG -P1.

[ Web Version [3] ]

Marketing measurement has always been complicated.

That complexity results in the CFO, CEO, CSO rarely having meaningful
confidence in the “ROI” the Marketing team reports. [You finally
get why Marketing’s the first budget to be cut in hard times.]

The explosion of everything digital has only made Marketing
measurement insanely complicated.

The differing mix of Creative, Audience, Targeting, Impact Horizon,
and causality discernment required for Brand and Performance Marketing
result in you being in the_ it's a riddle wrapped in a mystery inside
an enigma measurement_ territory.

Finally, if you pause to consider that the outcomes are multi-modal
multi-channel….

🤯

It is very hard to figure out Marketing’s ROI – or even what it is
we are saying when we say ROI.

Now you understand why there was a massive freak-out a few weeks ago
about COOKIES ARE DYING, OMG THE COOKIES ARE DYING, MARKETING IS
DOOMED!

If Marketing measurement was better understood, it would be easy to
see that only 3P cookies are dying (and even that is a maybe), and the
staggering uselessness of 3P cookies should cause us all to throw a
party to celebrate their impending death!

[PREMIUM MEMBERS: Please see TMAI #394 and #395, on our exciting
cookieless future, please email me if you can’t find them.]

Another example: An advertising entity spent half of their precious 60
minutes extolling their glorious glory re _1P data this_ and _1P data
that_ and _unlock blah, blah, blah_.

If Marketing measurement was better understood, just a bit of poking
into the size of opportunity quickly translates into realizing that
unless you are willing to violate major laws on privacy and
security…. There are only very small-sized outcomes from
Unreasonably Massive Very Expensive 1P Agency/Internally Projects.

Yes, long term profitable employment and fees for the advertising
entity from scaring/exciting CxOs re “1P data,” but next to
nothing for the company paying them.

It is rare that malice is involved.

The people worried out about the cookies, the people pushing their 1P
Data low return projects, had simply incompletely processed available
measurement knowledge – in large part because of just how incredibly
complicated it all is.

As we do each week with TMAI Premium newsletters, let’s unpack the
available knowledge into bite size chunks. Let’s focus on ROI,
unpacking the term and all the confusing ways it is expressed... To
get to a better destination. Today’s about _uncomplexifying_.

1. LAST CLICK.

If you are normal, you hear this everyday: _We are reporting last
click ROI._

Last Click simply refers to the habit of Digital Analytics tools
(on-site, ad platforms) to CREDIT THE SALE TO THE REFERRER OF THE
SESSION IN WHICH THE CONVERSION OCCURS.

[Confusing, but reality: In GA when the converting session’s
referrer is Direct, if you have a previous cookie value from a
campaign, organic search, affiliate, etc., then credit goes to that
referrer and not Direct!]

I used Bing AI Chat, visited davidyurman.com [4]. A couple of days
later, got an email from DY, visited the site. 27 days later, see a DY
ad on YouTube, clicked to site, purchased a bracelet.

Last Click ROI would say:

Converting Channel = YouTube.

All previous interactions = Useless.
Obviously, uncool. But. Now you know.

WHAT LAST CLICK ROI INCLUDES:

Only digital outcomes. Site. Mobile App.

Only covers digital Owned, Earned, and Paid (OEP) initiatives.

Often, due to mistakes by Analysts, can be limited to only covering
Performance ads.
WHAT LAST CLICK ROI EXCLUDES:

Online impact of offline Marketing.

Offline outcomes (retail stores, 3P resellers, call centers, etc.).

Nearly the entirety of the impact of Brand Marketing (online or
offline).
WHAT'S LAST CLICK ROI'S BIGGEST CHALLENGE:

It overvalues short-term performance marketing, vastly
inflating/misstating its value.
WHAT IS LAST CLICK ROI BEST FOR:

It is the fastest available online conversion/lead gen data. It is
everywhere. It can be very useful for in-flight optimization – IF
your Analysis Ninjas have done meta-analysis to understand past
performance to identify leading indicators of incremental outcomes.

Use LC data to smartly catch Marketing activities most likely to fail,
the ones throwing hints of higher success, and intervene before all
the Marketing budget is gone!

Do not use LC ROI to decide which Online Marketing initiatives to
invest more or less in, or to give your Performance agency more money,
or do more email or less SEO.
In April 2024, Last Click data should not be on any reports going to
anyone in the company expected to make any material decisions.

No dashboards with LC ROI. Certainly, never to Finance.

2. "MULTI-TOUCH" ATTRIBUTION.

More and more companies have shifted to reporting _ROI based on
attribution._

Often also referred to as Multi-Touch Attribution (MTA).

A popular, free (!), approach to MTA is Data-Driven Attribution (DDA)
which is based on large-scale ML assessment across behavior of tens of
millions of digital footprints of your Users across your site and
mobile app.

Remember my DAVID YURMAN experience: All digital. Bing > Email >
YouTube Ad > Bracelet.

The objective of Attribution is to assess if Bing and Email should get
some credit for the Bracelet conversion.

If the assessment across tens of millions of digital footprints
indicates credit is deserved… YouTube would be the Last Converting
Channel, with Bing and Email being Assisted Conversions.

Beyond DDA, there are other MTA approaches that use smart algorithms
and/or advanced machine learning to figure out how to distribute
credit, across what time periods, etc. If you want to move beyond DDA,
call Croud! [5]

ROI based on Attribution is such a step-up over Last Click ROI’s
royal sucking.

WHAT MTA ROI INCLUDES:

Only digital outcomes. Site. Mobile App.

Useful MTA analysis SHOULD include digital Owned, Earned, and Paid
(OEP) initiatives.

Analysis Ninjas earn faster salary increased by MTAing Macro & Micro
Conversions!
WHAT MTA ROI EXCLUDES:

Offline outcomes. [Retail stores, 3P resellers, call centers, etc.]

Nearly the entirety of the impact of Brand Marketing [online or
offline].

[Unless you insist, especially if you go down hyper-custom route] All
of Owned and Earned media initiatives, including digital.
WHAT'S MTA ROI'S BIGGEST CHALLENGE:

It overvalues paid advertising, specifically short-term paid
performance marketing
WHAT IS MTA ROI BEST FOR:
Identifying holistic contribution of all Digital initiatives. Paid
media for sure, and [if you ensure inclusion] Owned and Earned.

They offer actionable clues where your Paid Marketing is cannibalizing
your free, nearly free, Marketing initiatives.

You will use algorithmic, human-untouched, MTA to reallocate budget,
resources, prioritize Digital initiatives, definitely your Performance
ads.
Reports going to your Digital Marketers should now be based on MTA,
all the way up to VPs.*

[* Assuming it is impossible for you to currently leap to
Incrementality – our destination.]

[NOTE: Please, please, please at this point in history, do not build
your own customized set of "attribution rules," artisanal crafted.
First-click, even distribution, middle-preferred etc., or custom
look back windows of 7 days for TV and 16 hours for Facebook etc.
Digital user experience - see picture above - is so deeply
complicated, it is arrogant to believe you, human, can understand it,
and build "attribution rules." Let machine learning do it, you'll
thank me when higher business profits - attributed to Marketing -
flow.]

3. CHANNEL SILO INCREMENTALITY.

There’s a very high chance that if I hear you say _here’s a report
with Marketing ROI based on Channel Silo incrementality_, I might jump
up and give you a big hug!

Often, Channel Silo incrementality ROI is identified via Conversion
Lift Studies [CLS], which are powered by true test  control
experiments [geo or audience].

So, why the hug?

CFOs love incrementality. And, solving for the CFO is the greatest
give you can give a CMO.

Incrementality:

_What would happen if we decided to invest $0 in Marketing, and
redeployed 100% of the Marketing employees to Product, Customer
Service, Retail, Operations and other teams?_

It is the hardest possible question to answer in Marketing
[/business].

Long time TMAI Premium subscribers will recognize that Channel Silo
incrementality is a narrower version of the above question. At Level 1
of incrementality measurement, it answers the question:

_We invest $12 mil buying Brand Keywords on Bing, and received 12,000
Conversions. __
__
Would we be minus 12,000 Conversions, if we cut the Bing budget to
$0?_
No, of course not.

A vast majority of those Bing claimed Conversions will still happen
[after all, in 2024, you were still only bidding on Brand terms that
indicate a high-intent User who would buy anyway!].

Will you get 11,000 with Bing cut to $0 budget? Or 11,900? Or 5,386?
That’s getting to ROI based on CS incrementality.

You get why this answer is magnificently powerful, right?

It solves for the trifecta of:

MORE Effectiveness. MORE Efficiency. MORE budget for other Marketing
activities!
Important Clarification: Attribution is NOT Incrementality. [See TMAI
Premium #327]

This is a common occurrence:

Bing PPC Last Click Conversions: 12,000

Bing PPC MTA’ed Conversions: 12,000, credit also spread across FB,
Email, SEO Assists.

Bing PPC Channel Silo Incremental Conversions: 1,900
When you have Attribution, you DO NOT have incrementality. You have
Attribution.

Your CMO and CFO aren't concerned with LC or Attribution, they care
about Incrementality. That 1,900 out of 12,000 will be missed if PPC
budget it cut to zero. Or, 5,000. Or, whatever.

Bing/Search is the most obvious example. Analysis Ninjas measure
Channel Silo incrementality of YouTube budget, Facebook budget, TikTok
budget, TV budget, everything.

Fantastic News: It is totally tree to measure Channel Silo
Incrementality for digital channels, just ask the Ad platform to
conduct a Conversion Lift Study for you. Free!

WHAT CHANNEL SILO INCREMENTALITY ROI INCLUDES:

Only digital outcomes. Site. Mobile App.

Only Paid Advertising initiatives.
WHAT CHANNEL SILO INCREMENTALITY ROI EXCLUDES:

Offline outcomes (retail stores, 3P resellers, call centers, etc.).

Zero impact of Brand Marketing on performance (online or offline).
WHAT'S CHANNEL SILO INCREMENTALITY ROI'S BIGGEST CHALLENGE:

Culture. Having gotten used to taking credit for vastly inflated
Claimed Conversions, you'll face a repressive revolt, especially from
Performance Marketers, when you start measuring incrementality.

They will throw so much negativity at you, I wrote three Premium
editions outlining practical solutions for dealing with it, and making
it through! [TMAI #347, #348, #349.]

WHAT IS CHANNEL SILO INCREMENTALITY ROI BEST FOR:

Cutting wasteful advertising, lazy advertising, and funding
hard-working advertising. [Even if the incrementality conversions cost
a bit more, remember they are incremental!]

In quarter, across quarters, reallocating budgets within channel
strategies [say Google Brand KWs to Non-Brand KWs], and across
channels [cut Facebook due to low/no incrementality, put into YouTube
due to high incrementality].
In April 2024, it is impossible to call an Analytics team good if they
are not measuring Channel Silo incrementality for all Digital ad spend
- and using the results big boost Marketing’s ROI.

SPECIAL NOTE: It is totally possible to measure Channel Silo
Incrementality for offline advertising channels on online outcomes or
online and offline outcomes combined. It requires leveraging
Experimentation. More work, more intelligence needed, but entirely
possible.
