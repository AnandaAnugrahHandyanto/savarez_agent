TMAI #484: 🪄 AEO LOSS RECOVERY GROWTH MODEL. -P3

[ Web Version [2] ]

The ecommerce company we have been following receives 5m Organic
visits and 7m Paid Search visits. On our website, we monetize this
traffic at the value of $2.5 Per Visit.

Humans (48% of GenZ!) actively use LLMs like Qwen, Claude, ChatGPT,
Gemini, for a multitude of purposes, which includes, at the moment, a
couple billion queries Per Day (!). Many of these queries are for
tasks that we would default to "Old Google."

Downstream traffic from seeking answers is dropping due to two
reasons:

*
For a whole lot of queries, the LLM-powered Answer Engine (AE)
provides a complete answer itself.

*
If you were looking for a product, service, or solution, the AE will
give you a near-final recommendation, which means instead of visiting
3, 5, 10 sites, you only need to visit one or two.

For our ecommerce type site, depending on how fast/slow users change
their behavior, in CY 2026, there's potential to LOSE $3.8M in Organic
Revenue and $4.6M in Paid Revenue.

(!)

In TMAI #483 [3], I'd outlined the six actions you can take to RECOVER
$2.1M of your Organic loss and $3M of your Paid loss.

Notice that is smaller than your losses -- some of your lost traffic
is never coming back because those queries are now resolved at the
Answer Engine.

[AEO Impact Visual.]

Good news: We can lean into this revolution! Change in user behavior,
the change in the user experience in LLMs, is also an opportunity to
GROW TRAFFIC.

Let's go. 😊

AEO RELATED GROWTH: ORGANIC TRAFFIC.

There was a measure of the user's intent contained in two, three, five
keywords. Now, we have significantly richer twenty-, thirty-,
fifty-word highly specific, nearly complete intent prompts -- I call
these _resolution questions_.

OLD: _Running shoes._

NEW: _I'm a new runner with flat feet training for a half-marathon.
What shoes, socks, and gear do I need? My budget is $200._

"Old Google" cannot handle the new query. ChatGPT, AI Mode in Google
will happily deliver a perfect final answer. And send you a
_pre-persuaded_ human who is likely to convert at a significantly
higher rate.

[Old Search vs. New Answer Engine Experience.]

Qwen3 does all the work for me and helpfully tells me that it saved me
$21 to put towards the race entry fees or buy a foam roller to use
after the race (I'll need it!).

You can review the full helpful answer from Qwen3 here [4]. It
provides a lot more than the table above. [HOMEWORK: Use Google AI
Mode to run the _resolution question_ above. Contrast it with Qwen.
Form your opinions.]

This is an exciting opportunity for us to move faster than our
competitors and smartly add traffic and revenue. Here are techniques
to activate:

1. BUILD _SOLUTION HUBS_, NOT BLOG POSTS.

This work will contribute to delivering _long-tail conversational_
traffic.

A solid AEO tool (see here for example analysis) will help you
undertake analysis like Consumer Preferences -- which assesses along
six or eight vectors your company's strengths and weaknesses vs.
competition. You can leverage the Words Association and Sentiment
analysis report to identify patterns in _resolution queries_.

The combination of these two helps you understand solution-based
prompts for your business.

NOTE: As of now, no AEO tool has access to the actual prompts being
typed into LLMs -- as they are not providing that data. Please read
TMAI PART 5 to understand how AEO tools collect data -- avoid being
tricked by an AEO tool vendor's jazz hands exercise.

Our "blog posts" and other 1P content tend to focus on a product and
its use cases. To earn _long-tail conversational_ traffic, focus on
creating single, comprehensive landing pages that provide a solution
vs. a product. Ex: It should recommend a shoe, a type of sock, a
running belt, and anything else that creates a complete solution.

Our objective is to create definitive answers that have a higher
chance of being cited by AEs.

For some businesses, _long-tail conversational_ queries might cover
life stages, sustainability, an expansive covering of user challenges,
how-tos, and more.

The solutions content clusters will answer specific intent,
positioning us as expert guides.

2. CREATE "SHOP BY USE-CASE" CONTENT.

Another few steps above and adjacent to products.

Build content in the spirit of "shop for your first half-marathon,"
"shop for hiking in the rain," and equivalent relevant for your
business.

These pages bundle products into solutions and align more clearly with
user intent.

This content will also include_ x vs. y _comparisons, outfits by
occasions, care by materials, etc., with crisp summaries.

If relevant for your business: Interactive experiences that cover size
finders, compatibility checkers, ROI calculators are elements AI can
link to but not easily replicate, and hence helpful to grow traffic.

3. SCALE 3P: PR, CREATORS, REVIEWS, SOLUTIONS, USER CASES.

LLMs are immensely influenced by 3P content that is spread around the
web.

Don't follow some of our peer lemmings by whipping up some Reddit
pages (Reddit's temporarily enhanced influence is already dying, per
recent data).

Instead, what you want to do is encourage your customers, magazines,
industry associations, Mega -- Macro -- Micro -- Nano influencers, PR
entities, to share experiences with your products/services. These will
do the same job as reviews on your site; they'll just be at a
_massiver_ scale and credibility.

Initiatives under this recommendation will include Creator / Affiliate
Q&A covering structured answers citing your facts/specs.

4. BUILD "SHOPPABLE" VIDEO & AUDIO.

"Old Google" also indexed videos, but analysis we have thus far
indicates that video and audio play a significant role in LLMs
learning (which may or may not result in a citation, but if you can
teach the LLM what it needs to learn about your company/products, grab
that opportunity!).

Invest in 1P and 3P videos and 3P audio content. Ensure that the
scripts cover the solutions spirit expressed above, they include
reviews, and use cases that specifically solve for data you'll pull
out of solutions like Evertune [5].

Technically, ensure your video and audio are accompanied by clean
transcripts with timestamps and anchors.

5. INTEGRATE REVIEWS AS AN EXPERIENCE.

LLMs value 3P first-hand accounts.

In addition to reviews on our product pages, I recommend integrating
them into category pages to provide additional _proof_ of our
products' quality/relevance/use cases (including some inventive ones
that only your users will think about).

6. PREPARE FOR AGENTIC COMMERCE.

The other day, I wanted to gift a Coach handbag. My day was busy, and
hence I sent my ChatGPT Agent to research which one is hot (it said
Brooklyn Shoulder Bag 39) and buy it for me. It sorted out from its
intelligence which handbag would be the best but then failed because
Coach.com [6] blocks the ChatGPT Agent.

Agentic commerce is in the very, very early stages. It is going to be
_a lot_ of work.

_Very Little Pain Action:_ You can gain a competitive advantage by not
blocking known reputable Agents (unless Google, OpenAI, etc. violate
your TOS).

_More Pain, Rush To Start, Will Take Time Action_: Start activity
required to ensure your IT Tech Stack is ready for Agentic Commerce.
"Shop via API" endpoint. "Headless Commerce" pivot. Details in TMAI
#471 [7].

_Wait and Watch_: I am recommending against the
mostly-for-publicity's-sake rush to: "Let's add our products to
ChatGPT's buy now feature!"

AEO RELATED GROWTH: PAID SEARCH TRAFFIC.

Our hypothetical company gets 7m visits and $17.5m in revenue from
Paid Search ads today. This is also under threat.

Our loss model predicts losses of approx. $4.6m in revenue. Our
recover model predicted a possibility of $2.2m.

There is immense uncertainty re what type of Paid Ads are ahead in
Answer Engines. The latest rumors are ChatGPT's free version will have
ads in early 2026, with _Sam-mind-readers_ intimating the ads will be
closer to affiliate than PPC. AI Mode is Google's AE interface; we
know there will be ads in there, but only speculation re what the ads
might look like and how bidding, targeting, and creative will work.

With that Mt. Everest-sized caveat, let me share actions that hold
potential to grow Paid traffic -- many of which I've envisioned in
context of Google but will work as paid options arise in AEs.

1. USE AEO CONTENT AS AUDIENCE SIGNALS.

In Recovery and Growth for Organic, we have built out a ton of content
(#1, #2 above). Take the URLs of our new AEO Solution Hubs, Shop-by
content, and feed them directly into PMax campaigns as "Audience
Signals."

Your ads can qualify for an entirely new set of audience intent and a
wider array of touchpoints with you, as your AEO content is about so
much more than just BUY NOW.

2. CREATE AN AI-TO-AI FEEDBACK LOOP.

[Note: I'm assuming you are already taking your 1P data lists (_past
purchasers, email subscribers_, etc.) and feeding them into
PMax/equivalent on other platforms.]

The _feedback loop_ strategy tells the AE Ads AI: _Do you see this
list of people who bought from us?_ And, _do you see this other list
of people who read our expert guides/solutions hub content?_ Please
_go find more people just like them._

This provides the AI a _cheat sheet_ for our perfect customer. With
the Ad AI's ability to match patterns at unimaginable scale (combined
with the thousands more signals available only to the Ads platform),
we target significantly more and with surgical precision.

3. EVOLVE YOUR AD COPY TO BE CONVERSATIONAL.

Recall (TMAI #482 [8]) we lost big when it came to Informational and
Commercial queries, and medium-sized losses for Non-Brand
Transactional queries. A material causal factor: They were moving to
be paragraph-long, context-rich, nuance-laden, conversational queries.

Hence, to grow, we need our ad copy to be conversational.

Ex: For our shoe example... We will stop writing copy that says _Buy
Shoes Now_; instead, we will write copy that _answers the prompt_,
such as _A great choice for flat-footed runners is..._ Or, _As seen in
our expert guide..._ Etc.

I anticipate that AEs with ad options will provide the ability to use
Gen AI to build our dynamic ad copy. Leveraging this capability would
be super smart.

BONUS BENEFIT: This new copy makes our ads more _contextually
relevant_ to the AI Overview -- increasing chances of being featured
_within_ the AI's answer, making it look less like an ad and more like
a helpful recommendation. That should deliver more traffic (&
revenue).

MUST DO: 1, 2, 3 will result in more traffic coming to your website. I
CANNOT emphasize enough how critical it is that your landing pages
evolve accordingly. Ensure there is scent, ensure they are solving for
conversational conversations, and they are optimized via Behavior
Targeting & Intelligence.

4. BUY "FOLLOW UP" SLOTS (+CHAT-CAPABLE AD UNITS).

Perplexity, Google, and Bing Copilot have all announced or provided ad
options in "follow-up" slots. As an active user of LLMs, you'll
recognize the "follow up" suggestions at the end of the answer to your
_resolution query_ -- that is a very relevant place for an ad.

Various _Chat-capable_ ad units which answer the user's questions
instantly have also been demoed. When available in your space,
consider buying them and learning if there is a profitable return from
them.
