TMAI #459: IMPRESSIONS SUCK. - P2.

[ Web Version [2] ]

Does this seem plausible:

*
4 billion people on Earth, above the age of 16, spend 6 HOURS PER DAY
dedicated solely to viewing digital ad impressions.

I see you yelling _OF COURSE NOT! _

Yet… That Big Lie exactly what Ad Platforms like Meta, Tiktok,
Google want you to believe - as represented by their official counts,
in their official documents, of Ad Impressions they are delivering.

[PREMIUM SUBSCRIBERS: See TMAI #458 for the math [3], and links to
platform documentation.]

What transforms this _oh, just another lie on the internet_ into
_large negative business pain_ is that the Big Lie is the fundamental
basis for buying digital Display and Video ads – in the form of CPMs
(_Cost Per Thousand Impressions_).

You are paying for an entirely made up 6 Hours Per Day of Impressions
counted for your ads being viewed by almost no one. And, you report it
to your CMO!

😒

With the Marketing strategy built on a Big Lie… None of us should be
surprised that few CFOs believe anything a CMO claims.

TMAI #458 [3] lays out the history of impressions. What they are,
where they came from, why do they dominate mind share despite the Big
Lie. I’ve also shared the first of four corrosive problems created:
An activity-centric advertising world – an escape hatch for internal
teams and external agencies from much (if any) accountability.

Today, let’s cover three more corrosive problems, and a set of
invaluable solutions to move beyond the Big Lie.

CORROSION #2: IMPENETRABLY COMPLEX EXECUTION.

Impressions are the currency, despite obvious flaws, because it is
frustratingly impenetrable to unpack that is really going on in that
world.

This diagram, a collaboration between me and Claude 4, illustrates the
nightmare complexity of programmatic display advertising:

[The complex Display advertising ecosystem.]

[Right click, Open in New Tab, for higher resolution version.]

The Technology Stack Problem:

*
Demand Side: DSP, Trading Desk, Ad Server, DMP, CDP
*
Data Layer: Verification, Attribution, Measurement, Fraud Detection
*
Supply Side: SSP, Ad Networks, Publisher Ad Servers
*
Exchange Layer: Real-time bidding with multiple simultaneous auctions

The result is a system where advertisers have minimal control,
publishers receive a fraction of the spend, and users experience
privacy violations and irrelevant ads. It's a classic case of
technology creating complexity rather than solving problems.

How can one penetrate this from an Agency (actual example):

_Our proprietary Cross Channel Optimiser leverages diverse datasets
(Touchpoints, ComScore, and direct media owner APIs) to optimize video
reach across 50+ audience demographics, any month, and numerous
broadcast and online video channels_.

We tolerate that only $30k from a $100k budget actually reaches the
publisher – the rest disappears in fees, commissions, and “tech
costs.” And more.

One of the reasons Display Advertising, largely proven to have very
low incrementality via statistical modeling or true test  control
experiments, still persists as a fat line item in budgets is that
nearly no one understands how it works.

That in large chunks has been inherited by Video advertising as well.

Together… They contribute to the challenge of delivering business
impact via Brand Advertising.

CORROSION #3: HORRIBLER MEDIA PLANS.

If I look at the media plans of a random company, the chances are
nearly 100% that the plans will have this compound crapification:
REACH X IMPRESSIONS.

_With your Brand budget of $450k (or $45 mil), we will Reach of 89% of
the audience, and deliver 180 billion Impressions. We will do so on 6
channels during the 8 weeks of the campaign._

No sign of business outcomes – short or long-term. ☹

You want efficiency, the focus is on trading CPMs up or down (usually
sans any assessment or audit of the lowering of quality).

It is a corrosive problem created by Impressions being the trading
currency of media planning.

Marketing winners recognize the need for media plans that put outcomes
first, and other small delivery variables 19th.

_To deliver a target lift of +4 over baseline in the KPI of Purchase
Intent, you will need a frequency of 3/User/Week, over 12 weeks. With
your 450k budget, we can deliver 50k relevant Individuals Lifted, at a
Cost Per Individual Lifted of $9._

The primary focus is outcomes. To achieve that, the secondary focus is
on persuasion. From there, it will translate into building better
creative. The Agency will worry about how to identify audience intent,
so that better creative is delivered in a more relevant moment. Which
will mean more effective and efficient targeting. And… Closing the
circle… Extremely high probability of business impact.

BOOM!!

Yes, yes, that advertising will _Reach_ humans, and
_Impressions _will be delivered. But those two things are tertiary.
That will prevent sucking and still celebrating.

An efficiency-focus discussion should not be on _how can I get lower
CPMs_, it should be on _how can I get a lower Cost Per Individual
Lifted. _

If the focus is effectiveness, the discussion needs to be on _how can
I get a higher Number of People Lifted_.

Or, both… _What does our media plan look like if we improve the
efficiency of my CPIL by 20%, how much effectiveness will I lose?_

Yet, those are not the discussions you are having with your Agency. A
corrosive problem created by Impression-worshiping.

WHOSE FAULT IS THIS? VPs and SVPs of Marketing functions. A lack of
understanding the mechanics of how media is planned, bought, executed,
prevents them from ensuring a focus on Persuasion.

[Reach vs. Frequency-centric Media Planning.]

CORROSION #4: CORRUPTION OF MTA & MMMS.

Few things make me angry as much as this corrosive problem. It really
winds me up.

CONTEXT #1: Display advertising falls short at a heartbreaking scale
unmatched by anything else. Most Display advertising, if not all of
it, has poor creative (not from the lack of a bazillion creative
formats). It is sprayed unintelligently all over. The user experience
(ad sizes, placements, quantity) is atrocious. Hence, it is rare that
a human engages with them. Don’t take my word for it, just
Bing/Perplexity studies. Even easier, log into your Google Analytics
account.

CONTEXT #2: There is little to no short-term impact of Brand
advertising. Perfectly normal. Persuasion takes time, effort,
creativity, and more. All good. Short-term Brand advertising impact is
measured using a few hundred survey responses. They are sadly not a
useful input into MMMs due to the asymmetry in data. Poor MMMs end up
using the hundreds of billions of Brand ad impressions (Display,
Video, TV, Radio, Magazines), as a variable to compute impact of Brand
ads on delivering long-term outcomes.

Here’s the common thread between both bits of context above for
digital Brand initiatives: COOKIE-BOMBING BY AD PLATFORMS.

This behavior, processed unintelligently, steals credit by massively
over-inflating the value of your Impressions-centric ad buys.

You see, even if you did not see that Display ad or Video View, the Ad
Platforms will drop cookies on your browser. Both first-party (when
you are on their platforms) and third-party (when you are on other
sites/apps using their ad platforms).

Since all of us have visited a website, used an app, likely have an
account with Ad Platforms, our browsers have been bombed by thousands
of cookies claiming we have been “exposed” to this advertising.

All of humanity is in Google’s claim of “ad exposure.” All of
humanity is in Facebook’s claim of “ad exposure.” All of hu….
You catch my drift.

The corrosive problem: When you buy something from any website…
Google steps in and claims a “View-Thru Conversion.” _Hey, my
Google cookie is in the browser, my Google tag on your website shows
you converted 29 days later, that cookie indicates the ad view I
delivered also delivered the conversion!_

It is as insane as that sounds.

Remember, you don’t have seen the ad on LinkedIn, because only 1% of
it was above the fold. The cookie bombing provides LinkedIn a way to
claim credit. Ditto for all other Ad Platforms.
It is almost comical that if you add up all the View-Thru Conversions
claimed by each website… You end up with 3x to 10x revenue you
actually made.

MTA CORRUPTION: Display and Video advertising, of the Brand or
Performance type, delivers such poor clicks (or has no clicks
involved), and even worse Last Click Conversion performance.

On paper this theory holds some water: _Wait, all those impressions,
all those “engagements” with ads like mouse hovers, completions,
must have done something “upper funnel.” We have all these cookies
proving that, they are tied to those impressions! Include them in your
analysis. _

And, MTA providers do. Now, suddenly, a vast mass of humans converting
on your site/app have all those bombed cookies. In the MTA, a Brand
and Performance campaign that did not deliver any Brand Lift and a
tiny number of Clicks are given the credit for the Conversion!

If you got no Brand Lift from all those Display and Video ad
Impressions, your campaign failed to persuade. That is literally what
no lift means. Now your MTA thanks to cookie bombing wants you to
believe that campaign is responsible for 20%, 40%, 50% of the total
Conversions, and you should spend even more on Meta.

MMM CORRUPTION: Take everything above, and pass it through even more
complicated algorithms trying to prove Display and Video advertising
works. You have 2.6 billion Display impressions, 280 million Video
Views, which have proven cookie bombs for most humans who purchased.
BOOM! _High incremental impact of you, and you, and you and you all
cookie bombers!_

See the corrosion?

There are ways to pierce the global cookie bombing approach to see the
truth.

Don’t accept the idea of View-Thru Conversions. [Meta counts
view-through conversions for display ads even if just 1 pixel of the
ad is shown!] Run your own true test and control experiments to prove
that without that Display impression on Meta 29 days ago, you would
not have gotten that conversion.

It can’t be a one-time thing. You have to _trust, but verify _for
yourself by building experiments that will account for seasonality,
products and promotions, audience types, and more. This takes time and
analytics savvy. Hence, it is rarely done. Companies instead, often
blindly, accept the Ad Platform or their Agency saying _we have
validated this with a meta-analysis of x over y. _😔

For your MMMs, at the very minimum, you have to take the
recommendations for actions (ex: budget allocation changes), run true
test and control experiments to validate that your MMM modeling
choices were able to overcome cookie bombing and data corruption by Ad
Platforms.

This takes time and effort to do for every client. Hence, companies
accept the MMM provider, saying _we have validated this with x
clients with y experiments_.

EMPATHY: Look. Being able to pierce through the baloney that is an
entire system of cookie bombing and impression counting is a very hard
problem. Being able to measure the long-term impact of Brand Marketing
is a very hard problem, even if the world is prefect.

I am simply saying: Don’t forget common sense. Don’t trust
fantastical predictions blindly. Do, feel the challenge and pain of
your MTA and MMM Analysts. Then, realizing that all models, MTA & MMM,
are imprecise, actively seek ways to reduce the imprecision (going
from 50% accurate to 70% accurate is a massive leap towards being less
wrong).

STRATEGIC SOLUTIONS FOR THE BIG LIE.

For each Impressions created corrosive problem I’ve shared, I’ve
included solutions.
To close out this conversation, next week, I’ll to share a set of
strategic solutions with high probability of creating incentives for
good behavior.

From defaulting to clicks (WHAT!!!) to technical solutions to make
your MTA and MMM solutions more immune to cookie bombing by
Impressions.

BOTTOM LINE.

This is truly a problem where Occam’s Razor thinking is truly
effective.

This is truly a problem where the solution sits with you, and relies
on your critical thinking skills. Your Agency will not save you (no
incentive). Your Ad Platforms have other incentives.

Good news: The problem is solvable.

Carpe diem.
