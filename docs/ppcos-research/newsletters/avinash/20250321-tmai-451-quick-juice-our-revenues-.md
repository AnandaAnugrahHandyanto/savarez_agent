TMAI #451: QUICK! JUICE OUR REVENUES!!

[ Web Version [2] ]

Every once in a while, we find ourselves in a pickle.

Downwards results for a couple of months, more. Change in competition.
Drastic change market. New CEO. Suddenly, you need to deliver a 20%
boost in Revenue overnight. Or something like that.

In a previous role, we were seeking new investment in the company,
guess what we needed to get a high valuation? Quick juicing of our
Revenues. 😊

We all find ourselves in such situations: What would you do?

CREATE A CLEAR UNDERSTANDING.

If you just opened a restaurant, and it is in trouble after three
months, EVERYONE recognizes it. The imminent death due to cash flow is
extremely clear.

In larger companies, this clarity won’t come naturally. There are
too many layers and multi-matrixed teams.

Hence, good leaders ensure there is a clear understanding of the
reality.

The best leaders do so in a way that does not create panic, rather
creates a spirit of _we are in this together, we can DO
THIS! _Companies are filled with good people who need clarity,
purpose, and direction.

The second critical leadership trait: _Unwire _important life
lessons.

A good organization is taught: _Don’t make decisions for the
short-term, test everything you do _(this generally is a bad idea),
_don’t push for effectiveness at the cost of efficiency_ etc.
(Premium Members: TMAI #421: Mistake #1: Efficiency Without
Effectiveness! [3]).

The best leaders quickly and thoughtfully _unwire _this by
underlining that short-term boost will ensure there is a chance to
survive for the long-term.

THE PROBLEM SPACE.

I do Marketing (Owned, Earned, Paid). I do digital experiences (with
multi-channel tentacles).
To stay in my lane, I’ll share guidance on what any one of can do
for a company that needs a quick hit of Revenue, over 30 to 90 days,
to deal with circumstances?

A small warning: My advice below also applies to creating long-term
revenue positive outcomes. But, please know that in a number of places
I will be making aggressively short-term recommendations as this
situation calls for that. I trust your judgment to see through them.

SITE OWNER ACTIONS.

The fastest, most effective way to juice Revenues?

Take money from people who already showed up to give it to you.

Fix your digital experience.

What tends to happen when Revenues are short, the focus shifts to
Marketing instantly. Paid Marketing, specifically.

The logic is understandable: _Let’s just get more people into the
shop, we will make more money._

The problem is not the logic, the problem is ignoring the Giant Vat of
Opportunity that is your mobile site, your desktop site, and your
mobile app.

If you are a global standard quality ecommerce site, your Conversion
Rate is 2.5% on Desktop, and 2% on Mobile. That’s normal.

A 100k, 1,000k, 17,000k people are coming to your digital presence
each month. 97.5% of them are not converting. How about focusing
there?

Move your Conversion Rate from 2.5% to 2.6%. _Cha-ching._

Your Bounce Rate is 61%, how about getting five more points of people
to stay and give you one more click, the lowest bar God could create.
Your 56% Bounce Rate gives you a fighting chance with an additional
mass of people WHO ALREADY DECIDED TO MEET YOU AND SHOWED
UP! _Cha-ching._

Your Top 10 Landing Pages for your Paid initiatives are still the
general pages on your site. Why not increase _scent_ between the ad
and the landing page by investing in $1,000 code to deliver higher
relevance? _Cha-ching._

Your Checkout Abandonment Rate is like 75%. These people opened their
wallets, stood in line at the counter with their products in a
shopping cart, and then decided to walk out as soon as their turn came
up at the cashier. Crazy. Keep 5% more of them. _Cha-ching._

The Japanese like to shop with 70 photos of the product on their site
for consumer goods. Why does yours have 4? In a world where AI now
makes creating the missing 66 trivial? _Cha-ching._

The product pages on the site have no videos, fix it. There are barely
any reviews. The content is too esoteric and inaccessible to humans.
It is hard to find the price or type in the discount. The product
pages make comparison harder. The recommendation engine barely works
for x-sells and upsells. _Cha-ching. Cha-ching. Cha-ching. Cha-ching.
Cha-ching. Cha-ching._

Why can’t I sort the internal site search results by color? Getting
parametric search right is a consistent conversion
booster._ Cha-ching._

Why is your site still trying to test itself into one right version,
why is not already doing behavior targeting at scale using machine
learning? _CHA-CHING!_

I have 45,000 more ideas. So many sites suck so much.

I admit, not all of my specific ideas above can be done quickly, but
your top ten landing pages, your broken site search…. So many can be
addressed quickly. You have the people, imagination, and technology.
You just need to light a fire, the company is on trouble.
Don’t start your Revenue quest with Marketing.

Start your juicing by focusing on the site, and creating an incentive
– positive or otherwise – for people who build your digital
experiences.

The Cost is relatively lower, the Margin is relatively higher, and the
impact can last well beyond the current financial troubles.

The one mental _unwinding _you’ll have to accomplish: Don’t try
for perfection. Good enough is good enough (for the short-term).

MARKETERS ACTIONS.

There are three primary kinds of Marketing.

OWNED: Email, Organic Social, Blogs, Physical stores.

EARNED: SEO, PR, User Generated Content, Word of Mouth, Partnerships
(renting attention).

PAID: PPC, Video ads, Display (god forbid), Affiliates, Billboards,
Radio, Sponsorships, Etc.

There is one more lever we have at our disposal, in partnership with
Finance.

PRICING, PROMOTIONS: We can up and down prices, to juice sales. We can
up the promotions and discounts to up to juice sales.

It is important to trawl through that landscape very quickly and
understand two facts:

*
If you want to rapidly drive revenue, you only have two options:
Email, Paid Spend. That’s it. Unless you are missing skills in the
company or have not done a good job with them thus far… You are in
complete control of those two levers, you can deploy them rapidly.

*
While Promotions will for sure bring some _not-right for the
long-term_ customers, while costing Margin… Actively explore with
your Finance team how much headroom is there, if any, to selectively
use promotions, sales, to juice Revenue.

SEO? No, too long to see results.

Brand Marketing? No, if we survive (or all Paid/Performance funding is
maxed out).

Organic Social? No (it is likely ineffective today [4]).

MARKETING ACTIONS ACTIVATION PRIORITIES.

Two sets of actions in parallel.

_Owned Activation:_

I pray you have been investing in building an effective Email
Marketing program. If you have, starting there first will help you
save Costs of Acquisition at the very minimum, but can also help you
retain higher Margins.

Your tendency will be to spam your entire mailing list with BUY NOW. I
have done that once in 25 years. It was a deeply desperate time (and
the company still went bust). If you are that desperate, do it.
Ideally, don’t.

FIRST, focus on trigger emails. _You did this, we predict this action
is what you want next_. Simple example: Abandoned Carts. Even better:
_You bought x, 69% of our customers also buy y_.

SECOND, focus just on valuable customer segments. You need to
challenge your Analytics team to pour over the current customer
segments and narrow down to just those that are most likely to convert
quickly (time since last purchase, proximity to store location,
lifetime history, number of tech support calls, potential interest in
new products launched, cross-sells appeal, etc., etc.).

THIRD, unleash AI! 😊 Ok… Propensity Modeling. If you have
invested in advanced algorithms and models when the sun was shining,
then bringing them to bear when the clouds are out is an excellent
idea.

The most obvious use is for recommendation #2 (above), segmenting
audiences for targeted campaigns. Additional invaluable uses include
tactical benefits like optimizing send timing, early warnings for
Churn, to strategic benefits like hyper-personalized product
recommendations (_likely to buy maple 26” handbags, but not shoes_),
to discounting strategies (_Scott does not need a discount, but
Avinash can be enticed by a discount of 12%_), predicting email
engagement (_likelihood to click, and on context abc_), and, in good
times or bad, refining your loyalty program.

If you are hearing phrases like good old _logistic regression _(ex:
click probabilities) and _K-Means clustering _(ex:
segmentation)_, _or the newer _neural networks _(product reco,
dynamic content)_, gradient boosting models _(churn
predictions)_, _or _reinforcement learning _(great for optimizing
email sequences), then you are on the right track to juice Revenues
quickly.

You are reading a newsletter that successfully raised approx. $200,000
for charity last year. Email Marketing works. If it does not work for
you, it is unlikely that the problem is channel.

Reflect inward, juice Revenues successfully.

Before you move on to Paid – the Cost & Margin implications of Email
are insanely attractive.

_Paid Activation:_

Focus on Paid Search FIRST. It is the only channel on the planet with
_explicitly expressed commercial intent_.

If you have a ranking weakness in Brand Keywords, spend there first
(these people are looking for you explicitly). Remember, Brand
Keywords will have lower incrementality. Then, quickly focus on
non-Brand Keywords (aka. Generic). These are people who want a
product/service, but have not decided on which a specific brand they
want. Slightly longer sales cycle (review your _Days to Purchase _and
_Visits to Purchase _reports in GA), but high incrementality.

SECOND, ensure that when you are spending on Search (or honestly any
modern digital platform), for the sake of all that is holy, please
adopt all the AI features immediately (TMAI #420: Unlock AI-Powered
PPC! [5]). Your Pmax, your AI-Targeting, your Smart Creative features
optimized based on ROI (TMAI #436: Creative Strategy: Performance vs.
Brand Ads. [6]), etc. This is already costing you in efficiency and
effectiveness, so _never let a crisis go to waste_.

Then, focus on whatever is the next best scale channel for your
company were intent-exists.
Ex: YouTube’s new Demand Gen product that is focused on Performance
juicing. Any other platform that has an ad product explicitly for
Performance marketing (if you need a definition: Ad to Sales driven in
1 to 3 weeks, depending on the business).

THIRD, in normal times I worry about the annoyance and cannibalization
of re-targeting ads. That goes out of the door in trouble times. Use
retargeting. If you have time, please A. make it as smart and relevant
as you can, and B. user frequency capping actively (under 2 please,
there is a lot of cross-industry data to suggest beyond 1.2 there is
little incrementality – but we are in trouble, use 2).

FOURTH, as you prioritize my recommendations, choose the ad types that
are _pay for performance_. So, no CPM type crap. We don’t care about
impressions. We pay for Conversions, Revenue, Profit, etc. Google,
Facebook, others, have this option available – usually the barrier
to using them is two fold: It feels good to report to C-suite your
advertising delivered 1.7 billion impressions, and your site/dev team
can be uncooperative in sending a basic success signal of success to
the ad platform (in which case that explains why you are having
Revenue problems in the first place).

FIFTH, this is a double-edge sword: if Finance says there is Margin
headroom… Release the Kraken! Sorry, discounts, bundles, other
promotions. Look, we all know that people love a deal – be it in B2B
enterprise software, cars, or toothpaste. It can be an effective
short-term Revenue booster for anyone. You can also try non-expensive
options. Just remember, it is hard to stay in business by juicing
Revenue with discounts. So, if you do it, ensure it is a short-term
_unwindable _tactic. Oh, and if you can be creative. Ex: We did
factory tours for one of my clients – not just cost to us but the
clients were super jazzed.

SIXTH, another were I abandon my deep reluctance… Leverage your
Affiliate program. The large scale experiments I’ve used in multiple
countries, the MTA, MMMs run, rarely indicate much incrementality from
Affiliate marketing in the long run. But. Boy, done with modest
smarts, can they deliver Revenue. If there is Margin headroom, using
Affiliates might be a short-term helpful tactic. Create the right
incentives for your Affiliate network by setting the right payouts,
over the right time, to get to the right kind of customers.

Systematically, galvanize your teams to go after this list.

BOTTOM LINE.

I am not implying this is easy. I'm nudging you to consider that the
alternatives are immensely undesirable.

It will take a measure of shared passion, and a commitment where
everyone is rowing, together, in the same direction. For that, it will
take informed and inspirational leadership.

It is absolutely possible.

_Carpe diem._
