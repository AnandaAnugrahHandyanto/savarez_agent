TMAI #431: WINNING WITH VIDEO ADS: GOT MONEY?

[ Web Version [2] ]

Converting strategic guidance into tactical execution can be complex.
Tackling that with a simple table can yield magical results - both on
tactics and the strategy! A demonstration of this magic today.

If you've not already, please read _TMAI #430: Win Before You Spend_
[3] - it contains the strategic guidance, and below's impacting
tactics with a simple table.

PROLOGUE.

I recently delivered a keynote to the top 100 Advertisers for a major
tech platform.

Right after me, a VP for Digital Creative Innovation delivered a
30-minute talk to extort the audience to:

_Make looooooong creatives for online platforms. __

Looooooong creatives are revolutionarily impactful.
__
Don’t be a kitten, and just use short ad formats._
Ok, fine, they did not use the exact words in the last line. They
STRONGLY implied them.

To give them due credit… Two clarifications:

A. They did not say ads. I brought that lens (for why, see below).

B. They shared good examples from top brands. Like a seven-minute
mini-movie by Nike. A 30-minute DEI tome by a B2B company. That
famous four-hour Nissan creative [4], that lasts as long as the
battery in a Nissan electric car - such great work by The Mayda
Creative Co./Titmouse.
As a representative of an online ad platform, they could afford to
ignore the realities that I simply cannot afford to, as a Strategic
Consultant expected to deliver incremental results:

1. Creativity applied to advertising by top brands is as difficult to
find as snow in the forests of Indonesia. Implying otherwise is
unwise.

2. There is an extraordinary amount of data available from Kantar,
Moat, Facebook, Your Favorite Auntie, that attention availability for
ads is extremely scarce online.

Two data points: * Just 1% of the audience will watch your video ad
all the way through (that number drops precipitously as you go from
15s to 60s to XYs). * Best case, approx. 30% will watch the first 5
seconds.

3. Looooooong creatives DO NOT have to be ads. But. It is career
limiting not to realize that posting looooooong creatives on YT, FB,
WeChat will fall into a vacuum.

You need to spend *a lot* of money to tell people HEY COME LOOK AT
THIS MOVIE WE MADE AT SALESFORCE/FORD/PFIZER.

You are back to ads, even if you did not start there.
My sense was that asking a hundred brands to focus on looooooong
creatives felt cruel.

Beyond a certain point in their career, every Brand Marketer wants to
be a movie maker, aka looooooong creatives.

I can sincerely see the appeal. Intent/Purchase Ads are considered
_icky_. Job promotions in Marketing are achieved by delivering shiny
objects. A Brand Marketer wants their friends to envy them. A Cannes
award can do wonders for internal company publicity, a context where
actual results from the creative are irrelevant. This is all done with
good intent.

Yet, as a responsible SVP of Marketing you have to figure out how to
find an outlet for these realities AND you cannot afford for them to
drive all of your Brand Marketing.

I have a solution - a financial solution.

I’ll repeat this again at the end:

The world does need looooooong creative.

It is why we go to movies.

It is why we watch channels like Kurzgesagt [5] or TabiEats [6].

The question to ask: _Does the world require your brand to fulfill
that need?_

THE COST OF ATTENTION + MEDIA = SURPRISE!

: )

In context of looooooong creatives, let's identify how to incent
intelligent choices.

A big assumption: Regardless of Ad Length, you’ve pre-tested your
creative with a superior solution like Human Made Machine [7]. (Note:
I’m an Advisor.) It uses an in-stiu true test and control media
target audience splits with large samples to predict if the creative
will deliver Brand Lift for your pre-identified Success KPI (say,
Unaided Brand Awareness). From this point on, we will assume that your
ads have been pre-tested, and they passed with flying colors.

Let’s pull the magical media delivery and effectiveness thread.

One thing that might surprise you is that the amount of attention a
human pays to an ad does not really change for different ad lengths.

According to data from, who else, our BFFs at Kantar, here are
percentages of audience who’ll pay attention to an ad based on ad
length…

[Audience attention by Ad Length.]

Attention goes up slightly by the length of the video ad.

[KNOW THIS, BUT SET IT ASIDE FOR NOW: When the Creative
Agency/Imagineer-in-chief are given the choice of longer ad length,
the result is very understandable and human: The creative nearly
always drags on and/or becomes more layered/complex. Hence, in a world
where most people only see the first 2.5 to 5 seconds of an ad, longer
ads fail at a higher rate for both effectiveness and efficiency.]

To get the 30ish percentage of the people who are paying attention,
what’s crucial to understand is that you would need the same amount
of persuasion to win them over. Persuasion? Yes, regardless of ad
length, the same amount of Frequency and Duration to get creative
wear-in.

At Croud [8] we have Media Minimums needed to _near guarantee_ that
the campaign will deliver target Brand Lift in the Success KPI. Media
Minimums can be split by channel, by ad format, and Success KPI (UBA,
C, PI).

In the simple case we are discussing today, here’s what that
computation would look like…

[Ad Length's role with media minimums for success.]

A minimum frequency of three, and for a minimum of six weeks to
deliver a point of Lift in Unaided Brand Awareness.

Creative teams/agencies believe that longer ads (stories) are better,
will need to “_work less hard_” to deliver success. There is no
evidence to support this in the data. Please check in with your
internal Strategic Analytics team or your external super smart Agency.

The 3/6 numbers above are not Optimum – that would be a higher point
at which you can be more efficient and more effective.

The 3/6 numbers above are not Maximum – that would be a much higher
point, further up the diminishing returns curve.

Key point: Wear-in is not wear-out.

Next, you already know this one: In most countries in the world, on
most ad platforms, it costs more Media $$$ to run longer ads.

Two reasonable contributors:

1. A 60 takes up more ad slots than a 15, hence pricier.

2. Especially online, but also on TV, ad platforms realize that 60s
are not as effective; hence they don’t have as much inventory, hence
pricier.
[EXCEPTION: China is an exception where on WeChat, Tmall, other
platforms, longer video ad lengths cost the same as shorter ad
lengths. Please account for this.]

Since the costs vary by a multitude of factors, for our purposes
today, I’ve simplified to a factor, that changes on what I’ve
approximately.

Costs go up dramatically as ad length goes up.

Please change the numbers in red based on the rates you actually get
from your Agency.

In step four, we do some simple math to compute the Budget required to
deliver success.

LIFE ALTERNATING REALIZATION: If your Agency does Media Planning based
on Activity (delivering media, aka Reach / Impressions), they will
come back to you asking for significantly less budget.
Reach/Impressions are cheap, and solving for just interruptions. Media
Planning to ensure delivery of Brand/Conversion Lift (outcomes), will
be more expensive.

When our Agency models based on Media Minimums, we are modeling based
on Outcomes.

At the end of this step, you feel the nuclear level pain when your VP
of Global Creative or Agency’s Creative Strategist or the ad
platform VP of Digital Creative Innovation is pushing you to
looooooong Creatives as the starting point.

It costs a LOT more money to deliver longer ads.

So… Sure… Build looooooong ads. BUT also ask for three to five
times more budget to ensure your campaign will deliver Business
Outcomes. (Brand Lift in the short term, Revenue Lift in the medium
term, and Profits Lift in the long term.)

You would need a budget of, say, $18 million if you’d chosen to use
15s ads.

You require a budget of $90 million when you select the 60s ad format.

And, here’s the thermonuclear insight… At the end, you don’t get
anything more in terms of business results…

The Media Minimums were computed to deliver one point of Lift in
Unaided Brand Awareness.

Since the attention you get from the audience is basically the same,
and the ad costs scale up dramatically, you end up paying a lot more
to get the same result.

Humbling, no?

I’m often not in the role to influence the Creative vision or
strategy for a client. Perhaps many of you are in the same position.
That is ok.

My role, our role, is to ensure our Creative team and the CMO are
aware of the Media implications based on creative choices (ex: ad
length). That might end up being crucial data for the CMO to consider
as they approve the Creative strategy and expression.

MEDIA PLAN AS KEY INPUT TO CREATIVE TEAM/AGENCY.

While I’ve used the video ad format to tell the Media implications
story, this mindset applies across formats. It is why, my very best
Media plans optimize across all ad formats. How to use a combination
of 6s and non-skip 15s and 18,000 billboards and two YT Mastheads and
two months of on-open ads on TikTok to deliver two points of Lift in
UBA for $18 million.

Typically, the Media team is the recipient of the creative. Creative
teams are unburdened by the ad formats the Media plan calls for.

This is a mistake.

When I make the above Media plan based on outcomes, 6s, 15s skips,
mastheads, billboards, sharing that with the Creative team UPFRONT is
crucial. They will now know where and how their creative vision will
be expressed. And, if they disagree... UPFRONT, before the creative
worked upon, is a good time to discuss where to get the 2x, 5x, 8x
extra budget to express the creative vision your Creative Agency/VP
want to deliver.

SPECIAL NOTE: One place looooooong ads and mini-movies can be very
effective: Captive audiences. Internal employee All Hands meetings. To
show off company creativity at Cannes/Award shows. To play before your
CMO gives a major keynote at a big event to show their creativity. To
play at gatherings of your Top 15 customers. When you have a captive
audience,  the high Fully Loaded Costs to build looooooong creatives
might be justified.

BOTTOM LINE.

See… So simple… And, yet so complicated.

In life, as here, painting the complete picture of decisions helps
illuminate choices with enhanced clarity.

For advertising, challenge your creative team to 1. Express their
creativity in 6s or less - because it is all you'll get from your
audience. 2. Express it across multiple _surfaces - _video, display,
billboards, text, radio, etc.

You will need a lot more human ingenuity, get a lot more creative, at
less cost. That leaves you will sufficient funds to ensure your
creative gets wear-in, across a _spike and sustain _plan.

Carpe diem!
