TMAI #427: INCREASE VELOCITY: CONVERT “THINGS” INTO METRICS .

[ Web Version [2] ]

I adore, and author, loads of frameworks (they help solve for the
_think_).

When you operate at that high level, many concepts get aggregated, and
you can lose track of some important threads.

One common example: On measurement frameworks, I’ll see a column
called Metrics but because the view is so aggregated, what I see
listed underneath it are “things.”

Resulting common problem: The Non-Analysts (Marketers, Leaders, Agency
peers) for whom this measurement framework is supposed to bring focus
and priority…. They all end up “interpreting” the things…
Which results in using whatever metric they think the thing is…
Resulting in… Chaos.

It is a difficult issue to avoid. It really is.

When you are operating at a very high-level, say “Upper Funnel,”
or “Engage,” there are so many metrics that could fit in there,
choosing one is akin to picking your favorite child. Or, you are
afraid to leave something out.

You can see why we end up with “things.”

For example: In “Upper Funnel” of your measurement framework, in
the Metrics column it says “Emotional Associations.”

That is not a metric. It is a thing.

I could read it one way.

Kevin could read it another way.

Justina could read it yet another way.

Glory could read it a fourth way.

We would all be confident we are measuring “Emotional
Associations” and thus following the guidance in the framework.
Except… Chaos.

Don’t pick “things.”

Pick metrics.

EFFECTIVENESS AND EFFICIENCY.

When you pick metrics for each stage of your measurement framework,
you HAVE to pick an efficiency metric for every Effectiveness metric.

So, so, so sorry. I know you had such limited choices in the first
place, and I’ve just made it even harder.

It is important.

Unconstrained effectiveness (go get me lots of Revenue!) can come at
the cost of explosively bad efficiency (CPA goes from $45 to $280!).

Likewise unconstrained efficiency (get my CPA from $45 to $35!) can
come at the cost of quickly shrinking effectiveness (Paid Search
Revenue goes from $18m to $8m).

CONVERT THINGS INTO METRICS.

Let’s look at an actual measurement framework, and practice
converting things into metrics. The thought behind the framework was
excellent, a lot of work went into customizing it for the company.
I’ve changed some bits to protect privacy.

UPPERMOST FUNNEL STAGE OF THE FRAMEWORK.

Currently listed under Metrics column:

Awareness.

Share of Voice.

Emotional Associations.
That’s one metric and two things.

What would you to do convert them into precise metrics?

Allow me to share my thinking,  and see if we can squeeze in the
missing effectiveness or efficiency metric.

Awareness > Unaided Brand Awareness | CPIL
Awareness is a metric, that’s good. But Awareness can be of two
types. Aided Awareness is effortless to move, and has never been shown
to tie to any long-term financial success. Unaided Awareness is very
hard to move, when it moves it can be very resilient, and multiple
entities have tied it to long-term financial success.

Now, our internal teams, our Senior Leaders, our Agency peers, our
Platform partners are clear on exactly what we want to move – the
hard stuff.

Unaided Awareness is an effectiveness metric, so we pair it up with
Cost Per Individual Lifted to ensure our Agency, our Platform partners
know that we don’t want Unaided Awareness to improve at any cost!

Share of Voice > Marketing Spend Indexed Against Direct Competitor.
Share of Voice is a thing in that it has a dozen different meanings.
It can mean things as different as how many GRPs are we buying on TV
vs. our nemesis Macy’s and what how many people search of us on
Google as a percent of the total industry queries. See? A thing.

I choose to show the detailed name, to ensure people understand
exactly what it is. I don’t like Share of Voice as a KPI or a metric
because it is kind of saying let us spend more because our competition
is – with no sense of if it is effective OR efficient for them.
Hence, no second metric above. (I would just remove SoV, if this was
my framework.)

Emotional Association > Purchase Intent | CPIL
You could pick from a thousand different metrics what “Emotional
Association” is. Emotional association with product? Brand? Service?
Feelings about the company mascot? The newly launched font? Likelihood
to Recommend?

My choice is Purchase Intent. I want a positive emotional association
with our excellent customer service, which I hope to convert into
higher purchase intent for our banking products. I pair it with CPIL
for efficiency.

You could have picked an entirely different dimension of Emotional
Association, totally ok. Just be specific, and tie it to the current
Marketing strategy/budget.

ENGAGE STAGE OF THE FRAMEWORK.

Currently listed in the Metrics column:

Brand Consideration.

Perception Metrics.

Share of Google Search.

Website & App Metrics.

Social Media Conversation.
Metric, thing, metric, thing, thing.

We’ll go a little faster now, let’s convert them all into metrics
and ensure ef + ei.

Brand Consideration > Consideration | CPIL
I would add a clear definition of Consideration (“_next time I’m
in the market for a shoe, you’ll be in the top three brands I’ll
consider"_).

Perception Metrics

> Brand Advocacy | % of Current Customers

> Innovation | % Shift Driven by Investment
I’ve just chosen a couple from many, many possibilities. Please
select one relevant for your Marketing Strategy.

Share of Google Search > Nothing.
In 2024, this metric is value deficient. I do not recommend it. Not
for “early impact of brand advertising,” not for Organic or any
search strategy effectiveness, or anything else.

[Premium Members, please see: TMAI #398: Uncool Metrics: “Share of
Search. [4]” [4]]

Up next, one of our favs.

Website & App Metrics

> Paid Media Bounce Rate | Paid Media Email Signups.

> Internal Site Search | Micro Conversion Economic Value.

> App Usage Frequency | Net Promoter Score.
Obviously, you can have one of a thousand metrics that fall in
“website and app metrics.” I’ve just chosen some that are
relevant for this company. I’ve also been careful to account for the
fact that this is a “mid-funnel stage” (a phrase I’m an unfan
of) because I don’t want to hold this stage directly to account for
Profits or LTV etc.

Social Media Conversation

> % Organic Positive Sentiment on Twitter | # of People Talking.
Again, “social media conversation” could mean so many things.
I’ve just picked something specific, Positive Sentiment, emphasized
the Channel, so we know what we are looking at, and clarified that it
should be words typed by our customers and not us or our paid
influencers. The constraint anchor is to quantify the People Talking
(you’ll realize it is 0.0000001% of humanity, giving important
context as to what impact to expect from the Positive Sentiment).

LOWER FUNNEL STAGE OF THE FRAMEWORK.

Currently listed in the Metrics column:

Customer Engagement & new acquisition contact volumes.
Journey metrics.
Both are things. Actually, make that: All three are things.

Customer Engagement

> Depth of Visit | % of Visits over 18 Pages.

> % of Sessions Sharing Content on Social | Assisted Conversions.

> Recency (gap between two visits) | Customer Lifetime Value.
Engagement is not a metric, it’s an excuse [5]. I’ve attempted to
share three different types of engagement that might be of value in
the Conversion stage of the framework. Obviously, for your company,
there might be additional choices. [Can I just say, sure I wrote them,
but I love every one of the constraining choices above!]

New Acquisition Contact Volumes.

> Net New Customers Acquired | Cost Per Acquisition.

> Number of Support Calls from New Customers | Average Hold Time.

> First Time App Installs by Customers | Repurchase Rate.
Again, the thing could mean so many things. I’ve picked three
entirely different metrics to spark your curiosity.

Journey Metrics > ?
Ok. This one is a challenge. I have no idea what they could possibly
mean by journey metrics in the lower funnel conversion stage.

Do you want to help me out with suggestions? Please reply.

I was a bit surprised that the _mid-funnel Engage_ stage of the
measurement framework had five things, and _lower-funnel Conversion
_stage had two. All of us can add so many more because this is one
place we are spoiled for choice. I would add attribution methodology
metrics, there’s checkout and cart, there’s incrementality, on and
on.

CARE STAGE OF THE FRAMEWORK.

This is the stage that focuses on current customers.

Currently listed in the Metrics column:

Jobs to be done.

App downloads metrics.
A thing, and a metric that we can sharpen a bit more.

Jobs to be done

> # of Customer Referrals | % Customers Referring.

> Incremental Revenue from Upsells | Cost Per $ Earned.

> Likelihood to Recommend | Average Customer Age.*

> Weekly Active Users | Daily Savings Rate.
* How long they’ve been a customer (not their birth age!).

Again, the thing represents many dimension of the business to explore.
I’m sharing four examples of what the thing could be. You should not
choose more than two. At a framework level, select just one if you
can. Bring super sharp focus and clarity. This does not mean deeper
into the org, the subteams could not pick something more relevant to
them inspired by your one specific choice.

App downloads metrics

> # of App Downloads | % New Accounts Opened

> # of App Downloads | % Uplift in Revenue for Customers visiting
Retail and using App

Couple of choices for you to show the fun we could have with
efficiency metrics.

As in all the cases above, my hope is to use metrics (ef or ei) to set
significantly higher standards for the Analytics team and the
Marketing teams. When we identify that the hard stuff is important,
the hard stuff gets done. And, that is so fun (besides being
profitable).

IMPORTANT REMINDER: METHODOLOGIES MATTER MORE THAN METRICS!

In context of measurement frameworks, you should worry about the
metrics you choose. As above. It is far, far, far more essential to
get a common understanding of the methodology used to measure that
metric.

Conversions can be measured using Last Click methodology.

Conversions can be measured using Multi-Touch Attribution
methodologies. (Yes, more than one!)

Conversions can be measured using Incrementality methodologies. (OMG,
not multiple again!!)
So, your framework can say Metric =  Conversions. Target =
87,134/week. Good. But. Using what methodology?

Sweat this part.

Unaided Awareness can be measured using Pre – Post methodology.

Unaided Awareness can be measured using true audience Test Control
methodology.

One of the above methodology is really, really, not good. The other
one does not have that problem. Which one are you using? : )

BOTTOM LINE.

It made me so happy to see that the company above had a measurement
framework. That is such a good sign. It is a pre-condition to getting
a medium or large company to shift from the _analytics of the
business_ to the_ business of analytics._

With a little tweaking of the Metrics column… We accelerated the
progress that was happening. Things that bring me joy.

Earn some yourself.

Carpe diem.
