TMAI #436: CREATIVE STRATEGY: PERFORMANCE VS. BRAND ADS.

[ Web Version [2] ]

Between 55% to 70% of all success of a Marketing campaign – Owned,
Earned, or Paid – is down to the Creative.

The video of an ad. The images in your Email. The story on your
website. The windows display of your retail stores.

It is the most important thing you will do to deliver success.
Literally.

Hence, you can imagine why I weep when so little attention is being
paid to the Creative in campaign planning meetings, in analysis being
presented to SVPs post-campaign, and in choices being made around
trafficking media.

This is not all due to ignorance.

I need to give my Media, Analytics, Marketing peers a little love. An
extremely common pattern is that between the CMO, SVP of Global
Creative, Creative Agency, Insights & Research on Audience Behavior
Team, it takes so damn long to get to a Creative concept, and the
Creative executions only arrive 4 hours before the Media is turned on.
No wonder the Creative is not a part of all these discussions.

CALL TO ARMS: Please, please, please focus on the process it takes to
get Creative to market. It needs to be ready to go, final ship-shape,
three weeks before the campaign starts.

Why?

Before we start spending money, we need to figure out if the Creative:

A. Works only for the CMO & SVP of Creative.

B. Works only for the CMO & SVP of Creative and Agency EVP of Creative
Innovation.

C. Works for Target Audience, but not for the CMO, SVP, and EVP.

D. (1% of the time) Works for CMO, SVP, EVP, and the Target Audience.
You want to get to C or D.

Both ensure profits.

Both are rare.

It is why most Marketing campaigns have such poor Incrementality
(Channel-Silo, xStack, or Portfolio [3]).

Being able to know, BEFORE YOU SPEND your Marketing budget, that the
Creative asset falls in C or D categories is: _Winning before you
spend._

You go to marketing without knowing if A, B, C, D is true… You are
banking on 4.7% chance of success.

DEFINITION: BRAND & PERFORMANCE MARKETING.

With Brand Marketing, we are typically solving one of three important
opportunities at a one time:

BRAND FOR ME. (aka Purposeful Brand, aka Unaided Brand Awareness.)

STAND OUT IN CATEGORY. (aka Consideration.)

STAND OUT PRODUCT. (aka Purchase Intent.)
There are a million different slices of the above three, there are
distracting _unprovables _like “Brand Love,” there are sub layers
(understood via diagnostic metrics, and key drivers analysis), etc.
But. Big picture: Just three foundational opportunities.

[Premium members, bonus read: TMAI #426: Brand Marketing Metrics
Simplified [4]]

[Bumble, Calvin Klein Billboard Ads]

The Calvin Klein ad is an obvious example of Brand Marketing, I love
the Bumble ad on the side (solves for stand out product, aka Purchase
Intent).

With Performance Marketing, we are typically solving for just one
important opportunity:

PROFITABLE REVENUE (PR).
Ideally, Incrementally Profitable Revenue (iPR) – especially for
Paid Media.

We want Profitable Revenue to arrive overnight. If not, arrive over
the next week. If not, the lag could be three weeks or so. Basically,
relatively quickly – short gap between spend and iPR.

This varies a bit – industry, company, nuance – but, you drift
beyond three weeks or so, and you are in Purchase Intent (aka Brand
Marketing) land. Different strategy, different media spending pattern,
different creative, different a lot.

[Performance Ad - Rotunda.]

With Brand Marketing, you are targeting behavior with _no current
commercial intent._

With Performance Marketing, you are targeting_ current weak or strong
commercial intent._

What Performance Marketing, you are solving to _protect _your current
Market Share or grow it one percent or not lose more than one percent.

With Brand Marketing, you are solving to _steal _four, five, seven
points of Market Share over six to 18 months. A massive game-changing
exercise.

Two very different, symbiotic, opportunities to solve for. Both, very,
very exciting.

It is important to understand this difference, with all the greys that
might be present because:

The approach to solving for winning Creative is profoundly different
for Brand and Performance Marketing.

WINNING CREATIVE STRATEGY: BRAND MARKETING.

Creative Pre-Testing.

That’s how you win before you spend when it comes to brand creative
- to figure out if your Creative falls into clusters A, B, C, and/or
D.

Your Agency/Creative Team builds the Concepts, and subsequent Creative
Executions. Same as now.

Get the Creative well in advance. Use Human Made Machine [5] (because
I’m an Advisor!) to:

UNO. Use the True Test Control Real Audience tests for Concepts, and
heavy media weight (HMW) Executions.

DOS. Use their AI-based approach for low media weight (LMW) Executions
(tip: never AI Concepts, for now, it rarely passes _trust, but verify_
vs. _real-world results_).

The same advice applies if you use a different Creative Pre-Testing
solution.

Make sure:

1. They are using large enough samples (n=400, or sometimes more).

2. They are predicting Brand Lift in pre-identified success KPI (vs.
“feelings” or “future something”).

3. They back their predictions up with provable trust, but verify vs.
real-world results.
I like HMM because they do all three out-of-the-box.

[Premium member, bonus read: TMAI #416: Rock Creative Pre-Testing -
The Right Way [6]. [6]]

Creative Pre-Testing is the right answer for Brand ads because
currently, perhaps for another two, three years, solving for brand for
me, stand out in category, stand out product remains a very
human-needing opportunity. You are tugging memory, you are tugging
emotion.

Getting a quality prediction in potential Brand Lift upfront (in UBA,
C, PI) remains a problem we can mostly only solve with humans (True
Test Control cells).

If your Brand ads (Paid, Organic, Social, Video) are not being
Pre-Tested, you could be solving for only A or B…. And, by the time,
you find out that was true…. It would be three months after you
spent all the money on the campaign, only to realize your answer to
the question of _what did we get for all that money is… Zilch_.

WINNING CREATIVE STRATEGY: PERFORMANCE.

It differs by medium.

For OFFLINE Performance ads, billboards, TV, magazines,_ yada, yada_:
Use the exact same approach as outlined for Brand ads above with HMM.
Success signal is harder to accumulate in the real world.

For high media weight ONLINE CREATIVE CONCEPTS of Performance ads, use
the same approach above with HMM. Creative concepts are notoriously
difficult to get right – and you get the concept wrong all… All
the Creative Executions will suck – No amount of AI can salvage a
failed Concept.

For all Creative Executions, for low media weight, ONLINE Performance
ads, Facebook, TikTok, YouTube, _yada, yada_: Don’t build ads.
Don’t Pre-Test.

Yes.

You don’t need to.
