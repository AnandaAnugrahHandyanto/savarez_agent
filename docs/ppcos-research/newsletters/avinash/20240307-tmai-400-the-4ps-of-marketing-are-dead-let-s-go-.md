TMAI #400: THE 4PS OF MARKETING ARE DEAD. LET'S GO!

[ Web Version [2] ]

[400 of anything is a lot. I'm proud of all the learning and
discipline it took to get to this milestone. I'm beyond grateful to
you for being on this journey with me. Together, we've raised $588,000
for charity!]

A challenge to the orthodoxy that's drilled into us. An attempt to
liberate our minds.

If you open any Marketing textbook, across the 300 pages, in
exhausting detail, it will teach you that there are 4Ps of Marketing:

Product
Price
Placement
Promotions
The PRODUCT matters. OMG, yes!!

[There are many genius Marketers at Apple. Usually though, EVERY
Marketer at Apple is automatically assessed as a genius, and every
campaign they run is an extraordinary success! If you apply the
lightest critical thinking, the realization dawns… _It’s not the
Marketing at Apple, it’s the product._]

The PRICE matters. Slay!

[Towards the end of the quarter/fiscal year, when short of the target,
no Marketer says: _Crap, we are going to miss the targets, quick,
let’s build a better creative / do better targeting of our media
campaigns / update our website with a more appealing message!!_ No.
They say: _R__un the ads with discounts!_]

The PLACEMENT matters. TOTALLY!

[I find the word _distribution_ more applicable than placement. But.
Same concept. The number of retail stores matters – yours or those
of others. Localization of your website/app into languages matters.
Reflect with humility just how few DTC brands exist that are at any
level of scale in America, or dare I say world. Bonobos? DTC to
stores. Dollar Shave? Same. Casper? It’s at Costco now!]

The PROMOTIONS matter. Absolutely.

[Many humans confuse promotion with discount. _Get more customers with
10% off._ I believe the correct interpretation of the word: _Let’s
celebrate and promote our product_. Even better: _Let’s celebrate
the solutions we create for our customers_. Or, my fav: _Let’s tell
the stories at the intersection of our products and customers_.]

4Ps continue to be just as crucial as ever for a business.

SHOCKER: Three of the four Ps have nothing to do with the Marketing
department/CMO.

REALIZATION: Creating the illusion, striving to build a Marketing
department to solve for 4Ps, hiring people in Marketing to worry about
the 4Ps, is responsible for CMOs retaining the title of: Shortest
Executive Tenure!

[Why CMOs Never Last.]

HBR identified a number of insightful reasons for CMOs getting
_released _from their roles the fastest in a modern American
corporation.

They include this big one: Hiring the wrong person because in this
specific role charisma is frequently valued higher than competence.

The one with a higher coefficient: Poor job description. [Leading to
hiring the wrong person.]

There is no universal understanding of what a CMO does/owns, so
companies make up their own variation of the 4Ps, set up an
organization with various direct powers and mostly indirect (or no)
influence on others, and expect the CMO to deliver the impossible:
_Use your charisma to deliver a transformative existence for our
company, after all if Nike can do it… Anyone can!_

An ill-defined role also directs leads to an underappreciated problem:
W_hat should you hold the CMO accountable for? What are their KPIs and
Targets?_

Every CMO then fills the vacuum with 1. vanity metrics and 2.
non-causal measurement of impact.

At the first sign of company trouble, guess what? Neither 1 nor 2 can
save the Marketing budget or the CMO’s job. And. If you think about
it. It is not the CMO’s fault. They were always set up for failure
due to this simple formula: _Ill Defined Role + Ill Defined Metrics to
Drive Focus = Cut._

THE 4PS ARE DEAD (FOR MARKETING).

My life revolves around _incredible incremental marketing_, and I'm
passionate about contributing to longer CMO tenures!

[Desperately…] I want Marketing teams to have a clear purpose,
supported by a clearly understood True North -which is translated into
causal Metrics and Targets to incentivize focus and accountability.

I’ve come to believe that it is hyper-crucial to realize that the
4Ps no longer apply to Marketing – if they ever did.

The 4Ps are dead.

More accurate: One P remains, but it's not what you think.

[The old 4Ps of Marketing.]

Except in the smallest of companies – where everyone is everything
-, the Modern (the year 2000 onwards) CMO has no say in anything
related to the Product.

Companies have a powerful and influential Engineering department,
staffed with Primary and User Research teams, or simply loads of
strong opinions. They drive Product.

I’m sure you’ve heard of the Chief Product Officer role. And,
noticed it does not belong to the CMO.

Sure. Marketing (and arrogant people like me) believe that we in
Marketing are doing a ton of research on audiences, their needs, their
behavior, to build genius-level creative. Ergo, we are SO CLOSE to
customers and what they need!!

Sure. We even often impose on the Product organization – or the
small team working on feature m – and excitedly share our learnings.
We do get thanked politely for it, and then see it all ignored.

Ok. So. Product influence, bare.

Price?

You’ve heard of the Finance organization, right?

The Chief Commercial Officer? Yes, increasingly common.

Corporate Strategy and Operations? You have! CSO controls every
material dimension, including…. Pricing.

Marketing, depending on the distribution of Performance : Brand, has a
measure of influence on the role of discounts, bundles, how large the
font of those elements should be.

If the P:B is 99:1, they have more influence on discounts, if the P:B
is 30:70 (much, much, much better for Marketing’s long-term causal
impact on the business) then they have nearly zero impact on discounts
(and less than zero on price – as I dare say it should be).

Ok. So. Price influence, bare.

When was the last time you heard of a Modern CMO’s incremental role
in anything related to distribution [/placement]?

You’ve heard of the Chief Sales Officer? No?

[CAVEAT: I am a massive advocate of CMO’s influencing the retail
USER EXPERIENCE – digital, non-digital, anything in between. Even
the Service experience. I’ve learned from watching brilliant CMOs
what a massive impact their assessment, their taste, their eye, their
high standards, can deliver.]

No one is asking the CMO to have even a minor role in where to open
stores next or if it is time (in 2024!) to launch a mobile app or stop
being dogmatic about only having own stores and buy distribution from
Pep Boys or Macys or WalMart.

No one.

Ok. So. Placement influence, bare.

3Ps… In reality… Dead.

The only place they are alive for the CMO? In old Marketing textbooks.

Now… Don’t get me wrong… Are some of your Marketing VPs, and
your CMO, being invited to be present in some of the above 3P
meetings? Sure.

Are some VPs forcing themselves into those meetings because they can
convert that presence into stories they tell back in Marketing about
their personal growing "influence" and accelerate their next job
promotion? Of course.

The cold reality remains: Marketing has no influence on 3 of the 4Ps.

The larger the organization, the more true that is.

The faster Universities, CEOs, Boards, CMOs understand this reality,
the faster we can craft a purpose for the Marketing function that will
result in 1. a causal business impact and 2. longer-tenured CMOs!

The result we all want.

FANTASTIC NEWS: THE 1P IS ALL WE NEED!

The textbooks, and old thinking, also conflated Promotions with _let's
tell people about the product and its price/current discount, in two
magazines and one of three TV channels._

This last P, Promotions, is SO MUCH bigger! So much more exciting. So
much more complicated. So many, many, many new possibilities arising
every six months.

I see this: _Let’s tell the stories at the intersection of our
products and customers._

As being defined as…

[The new awesome 1P of Marketing!]

SO AWESOME?

Yes?

It fills my head and heart with joy.

Such exciting problems to solve!!

When the old Marketing textbooks were written, or traditional
marketing consulting companies were started, the nine words above were
maybe two or three.

Starting the year 2000, give or take a year, there was a massive
fragmentation in how content was consumed. Online. Offline. Nonline.
That necessitated Marketing having to rapidly adapt to an explosion of
Channels, necessary changes to the expression of our stories
(Creative), understanding the role of Media (owned, earned, paid), a
million new ways to measure (from HITS – to Incrementality), and so
much more.

In the last decade, with the rapid shoving of AI into our faces, the
massive complication of ad platforms, the possibilities of actually
optimizing experience/ads/creative/delivery in-flight has been an
incredible boon, and a sophisticated intelligent Marketing strategy is
no longer a nice to have. Oh, and we don’t have to be satiated by
the _generic slather_ of “ages 18 -24” or “women ages 24-34, who
earn more than 20k”, to a richer understanding of an audience’s
_INTENT _via their expressed behavior!

[Premium Member Bonus: The Future of Analytics: Intelligent
Automation, Automated Intelligence [3].]

The most fun part? We need a richer set of skills in Marketing, from a
diverse set of people [education, backgrounds, geographies, creative,
science, and strategic!]. An environment for more fun work, with more
fun people, solving complex challenges that nurture your head and
heart - and clear and measurable impact from our collective work.

This makes me so, so, so, happy.

Ending exactly where I started… Guess the most impactful outcome, a
life centered on “Promotions”? A CLEAR PURPOSE FOR MARKETING, a
True North that can be translated into specific business KPIs and
Targets, which drive focus, accountability, and success.

BOOM!

BOTTOM LINE.

Look. I’m not saying that if you are given true influence on the 4Ps
as a Marketer that you should not grab it with both hands and legs.
Good for you.

I’m saying the reality is that you won't.

The good news is that the job you are supposed to be doing is
exciting, expansive, complex, and can drive a massive causal impact on
your business. [Up to a total of an INCREMENTAL 22% of all sales!]

So. Embrace the 1P.

Ride it to a deeply satisfying role, and business glory!
