TMAI #310: FAITH: GOOD > < PROVING FAITH: PRICELESS.

[ Web Version [2] ]

24 hours into your career in Data, I'm confident you bumped into a
company ESL (Extremely Senior Leaders), or self-declared Gurus on
Planet Social who say:

MEASUREMENT IS NOT EVERYTHING.

YOU CAN’T TRACK EVERYTHING.

YOU CAN’T IDENTIFY EVERYTHING THAT DRIVES SUCCESS.

Entirely reasonable. I'm aligned. I'm happy to shout _Hallelujah!
Preach, my Sister, PREACH!_

This indicates the first hint of dissonance:

BEING ABLE TO TRACK A MARKETING TACTIC, A SALES STRATEGY, OR
CONVERSIONS DOES NOT MEAN THE OVERALL EFFORT ACTUALLY WORKS!

Wait. What?

IN FACT, THE REVERSE IS OFTEN TRUE.

WHAT THE…?!

I KNOW FOR A FACT THAT THE _UNTRACKABLE _TACTICS I EXECUTE ARE
EFFECTIVE. I KNOW THEY WORK. THEY DELIVER IMMENSE BUSINESS IMPACT.

The best marketing strategies are those that can’t be tracked.

🤯 [3]

It can be infuriating to hear the above… well… let’s call it
“_point of view_”.

I am, you should be, open to the idea that not everything can be
tracked, or even tracked with desired accuracy. Absolutely. For sure.
100%.

This next part feels challenging (and, admittedly, frustrating):

NOT EVERYTHING CAN BE TRACKED, AND MEASURED.

What cannot be tracked is "working."

How do you know it is effective?

Because I _FEEL_ IT HAS TO BE WORKING.

Let's focus on it, spend money on it, over what we have data for.

It takes extraordinary courage to push back against these Leaders,
they are nearly always very senior, and the power imbalance is
non-trivial. The end result can be a mix of continued budget spend on
areas that have never demonstrated any impact, continuation of_ pet
projects_, and continued resistance to new ideas.

I approach situations with humility.

Lack of data simply implies one thing: YOU DON’T KNOW.

Then... All a lack of data sparks is the desire to get data. Even with
all the imperfections in the world, you can often get _some _data.
Qualitative. Small sample. Research. Something to spark a more
intelligent discussion in situations that are _trust me, I know
without data this is working, I've spent a 27-year career in this
field_.

My objective: Get to _waterfall planning_ when it comes to budget
allocation. (More below.)

ACKNOWLEDGEMENT: WE LIVE IN AN IMPERFECT WORLD.

As you would expect from a human who’s written two best-selling
books on analytics, published in a dozen languages… Someone who has
written nearly two million words on their blog and newsletters over
the years… I get that data and tracking of success are incomplete.

Let me paint you a picture.

The most prevalent discussions about analytics tend to be about
digital (marketing, sales, user experience, qual, quant) and cover
these strategies pretty well…

[Trackable Conversions]

You can use one of many free digital analytics solutions to track
nearly all dimensions of everything above. (It is unwise to spend
money on internal projects to replicate functionalities of an Adoble
Analtyics, Google Analtyics, with internal _warehousy data
puking_ solutions.)

You can be very year 2010 and use last-click based reports to get your
answers, or you can be very year 2023 and use data-driven attribution
to get even smarter answers. (Bonus read: TMAI #48: Smart
Attribution: Solve for the Global Maxima [4]. [4]) You can be very
2027 and use True Incrementality based methodologies for
assessing_ acquisition, behavior,_ and_ outcomes._

Lots of data, lots of insights, lots to translate into effective
business actions.

And, yes, there are still some gaps. There are cookies and bad tagging
and what not. (Though, if you are getting hung up on these, you are
missing the forest for the trees.) You have far more data than god
intended anyone to have. Use it to deliver customer delight.

But.

Digital is not everything. (Well, not yet. This metaverse thing
might_ still_ be a thing!)

Most companies engage in online and offline marketing strategies…

[All Trackable Marketing]

So, do the reports in a Google Analytics – last click, data-driven
attribution, incrementality – contain the full marketing picture?

As is clear above: NO.

You would have built a proper business analytics platform that would
provide you a Marketing 360 picture. This will power both strategic
and tactical decision-making.

But.

This is still not everything.

And, it is important to understand that to deal with the specious
argument at the start of this newsletter.

Marketing does not drive all the success that a company has. I know, I
know, I just crushed your heart. I’m sorry.

If you are an established medium-sized company, and definitely if you
are an established large-sized company, the TRULY INCREMENTAL IMPACT
driven by Marketing is likely to be between 0% to 25% (from Owned,
Earned, AND Paid Marketing). Perhaps you are an extreme outlier for a
quarter or two, and you might see 30%. It would be rare, and fall back
to somewhere in that range.

This means when we look at the full business success, for example via
Media-Mix Models (MMMs), you might see something like this…

[Business Success Drivers]

Marketing will be between 0% and 25%, and the other unmarked boxes
will depend on your company / industry / history / product but will be
drivers of business success like: Customer Loyalty, Seasonality,
Retail Stores, Customer Reviews, Competitive Halo Impact, Serendipity,
Word of Mouth (aka Customer Referrals), One-time Shooting Star Events,
etc., etc., etc.

If you have a great data science team, you can explain a bunch of
those boxes above Marketing in the left stacked bar chart. In the case
of our team’s efforts, we can explain almost 60% of that stacked bar
chart (which is to the team’s immense credit in both data collection
and machine-learning algorithms).

The 40% we can’t explain is technically called: THE BASE.

For you, the base might be 80%. It depends on your ability to capture
data connected with the above-mentioned eight causal factors (or more
factors like them).

Let me restate what’s in _the base_: Business results unexplained by
the data you have captured and analyzed.

Let me further emphasize something crucial: Your base will never be
0%. It is impossible on Planet Earth to explain all reasons
contributing to business performance.

I hope I've emphatically made this case:

*
We can track and measure a lot of what the company spends money on to
be successful.

*
We cannot track and measure lot of what the company does not spend
money on to be successful (product innovation, etc.).

*
Life is imperfect. This is ok.

LET'S MEASURE THE UNMEASURABLE!

Almost all the colorful boxes above can be measured. Sometimes you can
measure them super well, other times you can measure them with 90%
statistical significance and narrow confidence intervals [5], and
occasionally, you can measure them partially.

Either way, some measurement.

For other difficult to measure or completely unmeasurable activities,
you can follow one of these strategies to get at least a better idea
to support your wild claims of effectiveness:

1. PROACTIVELY TRACK COMPANY INITIATIVES.

We can ensure all our PR campaigns with digital components have
company branded short URLs with individual UTM parameters coded onto
the landing page links. Not perfect tracking, but something good.

Do the same for your TV campaigns.

Make sure your sites, mobile apps, social links, and amplified shares,
are all tagged correctly and completely.

It allows you to have a better – even if not perfect – sense of
reality.

2. TURN THINGS ON AND OFF.

Say you believe your C-Suite regularly posting videos on YouTube or
constantly being interviewed on podcasts is driving 90% of the
business success… Stop doing it for, say, 60 days. See what happens
to the base.

You believe, like I used to, that giving speeches and the main Keynote
in a conference will have a transformative impact on you/your
business? Stop them for six months, or don’t do them in the EU, or
only do small private events for a while. See what happens.

(The probability is very high that you’ll discover, as I did, that
there is almost no impact on your business – and if there is any, it
is extremely transient. Perhaps you’ll discover it is insanely huge
for you, and aren't you glad it is measurable?)

3. CONDUCT MATCHED MARKET TESTS.

You believe advertising three of your company’s products drives a
brand halo impact on other products of your company? Pick test-control
markets, do the advertising in test markets, do nothing in control
markets, see what happens.

You believe word of mouth is driving 20% of the base. Ok. Do an
experiment. Creative incentives for customers of certain products or a
certain customer segment or certain geographic locations to contribute
word of mouth, do nothing in control markets, see what happens.

You believe chasing Public Relations activities via expensive PR
Agencies is the reason your company has sold the last $98 mil of
products. Great. Do Matched Market Tests [6].

So on, and so forth.

Now, MMTs are not easy. We had to invent an entirely new synthetic
algorithm to help us pick optimal markets. But. MMTs are possible. You
don’t need to accept some loud ESL’s faith-based assertion.

4. BUILD ADVANCED STATISTICAL MODELS.

Maybe you are right. All Digital is doing with its stupid data-driven
attribution models is stealing credit from Television. No problem.
Build a modern MMM.  (You don’t even need to pay some expensive
Agency/Consultant to build you something custom, just use
Facebook’s Robyn [7], it is free and good!).

Maybe all your brand campaigns that look awful in driving short or
medium-term results are actually the reason your DR campaigns are a
success. No problem. It takes some effort to collect all the relevant
data, but all work done by 100 other companies every day. Build
statistical models, use machine learning, it is what we do.

Maybe you believe all your “upper funnel organic social activity”
is stuck in the base and you are not getting due credit. No problem.
Almost all data about this activity can be pulled from the Social
platform, or if you spend money on Paid Ads with them, then they will
likely ship you more data to help you with this assessment.

Will the above recommendations be able to explain your entire base
(the current unknowable contributors to business success)?

NO.

Will you know a lot more than you know today?

Heck YES!

Do it.

Do not let an ESL (many ESLs) spend money purely based on their "gut"
/ "27-year career". Help them see everything we possibly can, and
clearly acknowledge parts that we cannot. I know this will demand a
lot of courage.

THE WATERFALL BUDGET ALLOCATION MODEL.

Advocate a simpler solution that will drive customer happiness and
business profits. Instead of_ faith_ to_ data_ allocation, switch
to a_ data_ to_ faith_ allocation.

If you have $1,000 (/$200m) to invest in driving business results:

Invest first in all initiatives with proven business results –
because, well, they are proven to work!

Invest next in all initiatives with partially proven business
results.

Invest whatever is left over after that in imperfectly proven business
results.

Now, if you have any money left, invest that money in the four
strategies above to make the untrackable more trackable.

Finally, if you still have money left, invest it in _faith-based
initiatives_.  (You don't even have to measure them. After all,
measurement is not free.)

You will have successfully executed your fiduciary duty.

BOTTOM LINE.

NEVER accept the assertion that what cannot be _tracked is actually
driving business success_.

An accurate answer is: _We simply don't know. _

If the ESL/Guru is not a bully, share that you will put in best-faith
intelligent effort using techniques to identify the impact – even if
partial.

And, don't forget to use the Waterfall Budget Allocation model – for
optimum business success. There is room in it for _faith-based
initiatives_.

_Carpe diem_.
