TMAI #417: 2X ROI, WITH ZERO INCREASE IN MARKETING SPEND!

[ Web Version [2] ]

A common mistake:

Judging Paid Media’s business impact by only focusing on Paid Spend
and Paid Revenue.
Yes, that is something to keep an eye on for sure.

But, when you only focus Revenue/Spend, you get brainwashed into this
unwise culture:

_To get more I have to spend more, how much more should I spend?_
Or, something even more close minded:

_Our Revenue/Spend is not looking so good, our Paid Media team stinks.
/
Our Revenue/Spend is not looking so good, our Agency stinks, let’s
go get another one._
It is possible your team/Agency stink. It is possible you can get a
bit more Revenue by spending more budget. It is possible all faults of
you having missed your Marketing quarterly targets are because of
how/why/where you spend your Paid budget.

Still.

Only focusing on those two bits, especially at the VP/CxO level, is
equivalent to covering your eyes to see the world more clearly. Sure,
there’s a little peek through gaps in your fingers, but it is
limited.

Here’s a simple way to underline the extremely common flawed
approach.

Acquisition represents everything you do to earn attention, and get
someone to engage with your digital presence (site, apps, whatever).
Paid Media is one way to earn attention.

Outcomes represent the result of that earned attention - a Lead, a
Sale, an activated Account, a profitable business impact.

[Acquisition, Outcomes - missing Behavior!]

In this instance, when  you only look at Outcomes and Acquisition
(1000/10) the picture does look bleak.

OMG. Perhaps our Paid Media team/Agency/Facebook Ads/VP of Social do
royally stink!

That would lead to a cascading set of urgent and large-sized actions.

Before we throw out a perfectly good calzone, why not widen the
aperture?

Let’s look at Acquisition, let’s look at Outcomes, and let’s
include Behavior – _what happens when people whose attention we’ve
earned arrive at our digital existence?_

[Acquisition Behavior Outcomes.]

It is still possible that your Paid Media team/Agency stink. But, the
_burning platform_ that needs urgent and large-sized actions is your
digital presence (site/app/whatever).

The experience is so heartbreaking, only 12 of 1,000 actually made it
to the start of the conversion process. Once there, nearly everyone
made it through – fantastic.

Without widening the aperture to include Behavior:

1. You look at 1,000 and 10 and fire your Agency. You _at least_ think
they stink.

2. You send your Agency/internal Paid team, on wild goose chases to
“fix” things.

3. To deliver higher Outcomes, you massively increase your Paid Media
budget.
Each scenario places limits your career for sure, and, perhaps worse,
delivers limited value to your company.

I cannot tell you just how common it is. I saw it everywhere in my
previous job at a large company. I see it with most clients today.

[REVELATION: I recently saw a MUCH WORSE expression of only A and O.
The company judged the Paid Media team/Agency using A and, the outcome
the Agency has no control over, Outcome = lifetime value.]

THE VALUE OF AN END-TO-END VIEW.

As obvious as the error is of only judging based on A and O,
Revenue/Spend… I’ll bet you a dollar that it is precisely what
your company/department/team is doing at this very moment.

At Croud, I am a massive advocate of showing the complete view of
reality. A. B. O.

I have an additional hidden agenda:

I WANT CMOS TO KNOW THAT YOU CAN IMPROVE PAID MEDIA ROI, WITHOUT
SPENDING MORE BUDGET ON PAID MEDIA.
I use a simple table.

I know your data team, your Super Advanced Agency, is focused on
_clean rooming first-part data in a unified single source of truth
with AI APIed LLMs statistically sampling consumer journeys to
multitouch attribute lifetime value…_ But, try this simple table…
It will take 15 minutes, cost no more money, and deliver an immediate
impact on focus, strategy, and talent.

This example is for a SAAS B2B company. The outcomes take two steps.
On the digital experience, prospects will submit Leads and start a
30-day trial. During the trial, the lead converts into a New Paying
Account – this can happen online or offline.

[INSIGHTFUL CAUTION: While we will judge the Paid Media team on the
basis of post-trial converted New Paying Accounts (NPA), it is worth
appreciating that the Paid team is just responsible for the Leads.
Between Leads and NPA, your Product, Pricing, ease of use, etc., have
immense influence – none of those elements are controlled by the
Paid Media team.]

The first row of the table shows the current reality across A, B, and
O.

[ABO | End to end view.]

2.3 million into 20,400 is not so good.

2,900 from 20,400 is not too bad, though we can sense scope for
improvement.

The second row of the table shows holding Paid Media spend exactly the
same, but obsessing about improving the Bounce Rate on the top 20 Paid
Media landing pages.

All else being equal, what would reducing the Bounce Rate from 32% to
16%s deliver?

[ABO | Improved Bounce Rate.]

With no more increase in Paid Media budget, you get a 24% improvement
in Paid Media ROI!

Obviously, this was not entirely free. The employees you already have
in the ecommerce team, your UX team, will have to reprioritize their
current loooong to-do list, and focus on this simple task. There is a
cost to it. Though, I offer there might be little on their to-do list
with that giant of a short-term impact.

We get more prospects delivered by Paid Media to stay. Hurray!

Let’s now focus the same employees, using the same platform, tools,
processes we already have, to focus on experience that delivers the
Lead.

What would a humble 25% improvement in Leads submitted deliver – all
else held equal?

[ABO | Higher Conversion Rate]

WHAT!

Yes.

With zero increase in your Paid Media budget, your Paid Media
team/Agency, delivered a combined 1.5x improvement in ROI.

: )

When was the last time you managed to do anything that would improve
ROI from your spend on Facebook, Google, TV, Catalogs?

Oh, and was this free? No. There is some internal cost, and in some
companies, not all, the courage to deal with the internal politics and
priorities that prevent this type of super sharp focus on the _job to
be done_.

One last step left.

Remember, converting trails into paying customers is a matter of the
onboarding experience, Product, Pricing, customer-centricity.

What if we take our Product team, get them to obsess about focusing on
just one of those things: Improving the onboarding experience? Always
such a disappointment in SAAS.

What would a small 25% improvement in Leads to NPA Conversion Rate
deliver – all else being equal?

[ABO | Improving NPA]

_Hallelujah!__
_
A combined nearly 2X improvement in the Paid Media ROI!

With zero increase in Paid Media budget.

The most disappointing part of focusing just on Paid Media
Revenue/Paid Media Spend?

You end up giving a _free pass_ to the large teams responsible for the
digital experience.

Create the simple table above with your actual numbers.
You are welcome to add other Behavior metrics that will allow you to
see the next layer of  insights. [TMAI #405: A Simple Dashboard. [3]]

The results will help you have a critical conversation with your CMO.

It will transform ROI.

With less budget.

CAN I DOUBLE-CHECK IF MY AGENCY STINKS?

YES!

Of course.

Widen the aperture being applied to Acquisition.

How much _expressed intent_ is out there for you to capture?

Add that column.

If this were true for your Agency, there is an opportunity for your
Agency to be better. So, push them to work smarter for you.

Or, if this has been a pattern for some time, maybe it is time to seek
a different Agency.

You can, of course, make it more complicated. Consider that optional.

Take this simple table to your senior leaders, and start a necessary
hard conversion with a simple table.

BOTTOM LINE.

I suspect you've noticed my hidden agenda: The table above is an
attempt to break team silos, and incentivize them to work together.
