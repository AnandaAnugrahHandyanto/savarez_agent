TMAI #432: AI UNLOCK: VALUE-BASED BIDDING.

[ Web Version [2] ]

My hypothesis is that when your internal team, or Agency, is placing
bids for your ads on platforms like Google (for key phrases) or Meta
(for video ads), they are placing the same bids for all humans in your
target audience.

_We are Lloyds Bank, we would like to sell more mortgages, let’s buy
all the keywords related to Mortgages, and given historical
assessment, let’s bid 100 to win the competitive auction.
_
We all have done this for centuries.

Even though we know that not everyone who sees the ad might buy a
mortgage or buy a mortgage from us (Lloyds Bank).

At some point, the crack team at the Lloyds Agency will undertake a
meta-analysis covering the journey across Owned, Earned, Paid media
ads, focus on people who come to the Lloyds site, zero in on those who
submitted a mortgage lead, magnify those who completed the application
and were approved.

That smart meta-analysis will illuminate something like this…

[Bids to Value delta.]

Not every customer, who saw the SAME AD for the same keyword query,
was worth the SAME VALUE to Lloyds when the mortgage was finally
funded.

Pooja is worth a lot more, we lost money on Carolyn.

It seems sensible then that our bidding strategy should reflect this
reality. We should bid 10 for Carolyn and bid 150 for Pooja, if that
is what it takes to get our ad in front of her!

The problem?

How do we know the value of the individual, when we deliver the ad...
Well before the individual came to our site, applied, went through a
long process, and converted?

That would be black magic, no?

A PROBLEM WORTH SOLVING.

At Croud [3], we’ve worked smart to solve this gap for our clients,
delivering remarkable results across a range of measures.

For a global client, 80_ish_ countries, we’ve delivered a 38% lift
in Revenue, using a machine learning based Propensity model – it
used ad platform and first-party data to score leads (and predict the
Poojas, Mei Lings, and Carolyns).

Oh, oh, I loved this recent one, for a retail client (real-world
stores), we crafted a privacy-safe solution that calculates Profit at
the point of sale without divulging any margin data publicly! Being
able to _bid to Profit_, is delivering an incremental 400k in profit
every day – I wish we were being paid on a cut of that. :)

For yet another client, the combination of the above two scenarios
meant our bidding signals fed into SA360, delivered a 58% lift in
Profit.

All of these scenarios are to entice you to solve this problem for
your company, with your teams and Agencies. It demands clever people
– thank goodness for our INTELLIGENCE & AUTOMATION team -, clever
Data Engineering, and smart algorithms. But. You can see clearly above
just how absolutely delightful the outcomes are.

THE SOLUTION WORTH BUILDING.

In building these solutions for our Performance Marketing clients,
there are two parts to an impactful solution.

The first part is the intense hard work you can see above, but I’ll
simplify to:

_What behavior from millions of rows of the consumer journey, hundreds
of thousands of successful on-line, off-line, B2B, B2C, Conversions,
can we identify as predictive of the ultimate value to our client?_
We leverage AI for the intelligence piece, and inventive Data
Engineering because the key to intelligence... Good clean data and
APIs. :)

The second part, we are fortunate, is done by the seventy-five
thousand engineers at Meta and Google. I’ll simplify it to:

_Given the propensity scores from Croud, let’s ensure the
Value-Based Bidding (VBB) algorithm, in real-time, sets the optimal
bid for Pooja vs. Carolyn vs. Mei Ling._
The ad platforms do what they do best: Use their AI and unique data to
convert our signal into smart bids.

A lot of your Performance Marketing (but not all) should already be
leveraging this unique combination of intelligence and automation tied
to your bottom line.

If you do everything in-house, is your internal P team leveraging
Value-Based Bidding? If you use a WPP or Publicis agency, are they
bidding to your Profit?

[LTV predicting, VBB Propensity Model.]

Today's emphasis is on unlocking the value of VBB to solve for higher
Performance Marketing Profits, but propensity modeling can be
leveraged to solve additional fascinating problems.

(Against my advice!) You are choosing Influencers for your next
campaign. How much should you pay each Influencer – assuming you
would rather not do it based on vanity metrics like how many Followers
or Likes they have?

We use propensity models to predict a quantified _likely business
impact_ by each influencer – using different algorithms than you see
above, but a similar spiritual approach. Now you can pay each
Influencer different amount based on _predicted outcomes_ vs._
potential activity_.

Perhaps you are in a large company with 18 brands and 72 sub-cultures,
each sending “helpful” emails to customers – not realizing that
getting 21 emails a day from any one company is deeply upsetting. Our
solution is to use multiple sources of the client’s first-party data
to predict: _Should Avinash get an email from us today?_ The model
instantly cuts down on email spam.

Phase two of a project like this is to extend the propensity model to
do something harder: _What should be in Avinash’s email (if he
should get an email today)?_ See content? Think? Care?

Fantmagical, no?
