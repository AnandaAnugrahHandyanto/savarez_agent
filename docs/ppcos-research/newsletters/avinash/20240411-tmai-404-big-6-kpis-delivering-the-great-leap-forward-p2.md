[ Web Version [2] ]

When looking to transform the impact of data in complex ecosystems,
I’ve built a simple framework, BIG 6, that delivers magical results.

I’d shared in TMAI #403 [3], that the source of the magic is
applying the duality of a super sharp focus on a bottom-line KPI,
combined with bleeding-edge methodologies.

The methodology was pivoting to truly INCREMENTAL measurement for all
KPIs, Brand or Performance.

To ensure we are making decisions across all layers of the
organizational needs, I’d recommended measuring _Channel Silo_
(level 3), _xStack _(level 2), and _portfolio _(level 1)
incrementality – using true test <> control experiments, and
advanced modeling using ML which is _trust, but verified._

[NOTE: I’m comfortable trusting black boxes, as long as I retain
control over verifying the truth they deliver!]

The first Big 6 KPI we dived deep into was _Causally Driven
Incremental Sales by Marketing_ [iSales].

Each Big 6 KPI triggers this magical virtuous cycle…

[Big 6 KPIs Performance | Cascading positive impact.]

Are you reporting iSales to your CFO?

Using a blessed incrementality-centric methodology?

Do you have a robust practice to _trust, but verify_ the causality
being implied?

Are you doing it at scale across a vast majority of our Marketing
spend?

If you are, have you eliminated the false gods (like last-click and
attributed Sales)?

My goal with the Big 6 framework:

Build a simple set of lenses that any company, any part of Marketing,
in any country, can apply to understand its sophistication when
practicing the _analytics of the business._

MAGIC BULLET APPLICATION: BIG 6 KPIS.

The full set of Big 6 KPIs are:

1.    iSales
2.    CPiS
3.    iProfit
4.    Points of Brand Lift (in Target KPI)
5.    Individuals Lifted
6.    CPIL
Let’s reflect on KPIs two through five today.

BIG 6 #2: COST PER INCREMENTAL SALE.

TACTICAL: _Fully Loaded Marketing Campaign Costs / iSales by that
Marketing Campaign_

STRATEGIC_: Fully Loaded Marketing Costs / iSales_
_
_Simple. No?

Everything said in context of iSales applies. Obviously. : )

The bit to be careful about: _Is it CPiS at levels 3, 2, or 1?_

Why?

You’ll remember, from TMAI #233 [5], that Marketing’s identified
incrementality reduces as your assessment of it goes from level 3 to 2
to 1.

The cascading impact: Your CPiS will increase as you report it from
levels 3 to 2 to 1.

Your identified incrementality reduces, your Marketing budget stays
the same, CPiS goes up. Simple.

The Level 3 CPiS for your Google Paid Search is $72.

Once you assess across Google Paid Search, YouTube, and DV360, your
Level 2 CPiS will be $138 [because for some, a lot, of the audience,
you did not have to use PPC, YT AND DV360, they were already sold].

Then, when you assess across Google Paid Search, YouTube, DV360, TV,
Billboards, everything else, the Level 3 CPiS will rest at
$220 [because put together, all those channels are not incremental,
and should not have been in your budget].

Over time, after you hire me, and you practice incrementality-centric
Marketing, this disappointing initial reality can, and will be,
reversed!

[LIFE-ALTERING REFLECTION: There is a fear that speaking the truth
about the reality of escalating CPiS, from the silly CPM ($0.05) and
CPC ($1.5) that crowd your current dashboards, will mean your CFO will
be super mad and cut your budget. I’ve observed two things
consistently: 1. CFO’s almost always celebrate that you can measure
incrementality of Marketing - as they currently believe all Marketing
is a _hobby._ It comes as a relief that there is some incrementality!
2. If Level 1 CPiS $220 means there is no Margin, they will give you a
new CPiS target – a fantastic outcome.]

[NOTE: Worth emphasizing, 72, 138, 220 are illustrative numbers from
my experience. YMMV.]

BIG 6 #3: CAUSALLY DRIVEN INCREMENTAL PROFIT BY MARKETING. [IPROFIT]

_iRevenue - Fully Loaded Costs = iProfit.__
_
You identify the Incremental Sales driven, you take out from that the
costs that went in, and you have profits! Or, margin or other Finance
BFFs.

The MISTAKE made here is to only compute Marketing’s iProfit using
just the campaign costs / media costs / cost of clicks / cost of the
GRPs purchased.

You want fully loaded costs [at Levels 3, 2, 1, respectively].

Profit =
[Revenue] -
[(Creative Costs) + (Campaign Media Cost + Humans + Fees) + (Cost of
Goods Sold)]

Please see TMAI #247 [6] for a detailed explanation, including, for
example, the four costs to include in Creative Costs, and other
helpful glorious goodness you need to know.

LAME:

Facebook Cost Per Click = $2.
100 Clicks = $200 Cost.

Claimed Revenue = $400.
Marketing "ROI" = 400/200 = 2X!
BETTER:

Facebook Cost Per Click = $2.
100 Clicks = $200 Cost.
Creative + Humans + Fees = $100 Cost.
CoGS = $150.
Claimed Revenue = $400.
Marketing Profit = 400-200-100-150 = -$50

BEST:

Facebook Cost Per Click = $2.
100 Clicks = $200 Cost.
Creative + Humans + Fees = $100 Cost.
CoGS = $150.
Claimed Revenue = $400.
Incremental Revenue = $100
Marketing Incremental Profit = 100-200-100-150 = -$350
All numbers illustrative, please compute your own using the
methodology above. And, please do so starting Level 3 as it will be
the simplest. Then L2 [every quarter], and finally L1 [every six
months / annually].

[LIFE-ALTERING REFLECTION: Yes. We both know that very few Marketing
teams can survive looking at both Revenue and campaign costs – the
lame ROI measurement. A tiny subset will survive the truth-light of
Marketing’s Claimed Revenue and Fully Loaded Marketing Costs. A
fraction of that tiny subset can withstand Marketing’s Incremental
Revenue and Fully Loaded Marketing Costs. Lesson: The sooner you get
to this, the better – certainly before your CFO figures out this
reality. Gives you time to fix it all!]

A small, important, detour, and then back to Big 6s.

Profitability of Incrementality-Centric Marketing.

All campaigns, Brand or Performance, need to deliver iSales, CPiS, and
iProfits.

For PERFORMANCE campaigns, also sometimes known as Demand Capture,
you’ll be able to measure iSales, CPiS, and iProfits relatively
quickly  (three, four weeks), then you can start optimizing your
campaign execution (and your Agency should be doing all that work
already). You can also measure it over medium (quarter), and long-term
(quarters).

For BRAND campaigns, also sometimes known as Demand Creation, you’ll
measure iSales, CPiS, and iProfits over the medium (quarter), if you
spend material amounts on Brand Marketing consistently. You can
measure it over the long-term (quarters), if you spend material
amounts on Brand Marketing (even if, sadly, inconsistently).

If you are a small company, or super inconsistent with your Brand
spend, you cannot measure iSales, CPiS, and iProfits, over any period.
Simply not enough signal to tie (real) Brand spend to incremental
Sales impact.

[CRITICAL: Worth pondering, at any company size, if you have small
Brand budgets, below Media Minimums, or inconsistent Brand spend…
Why are you doing Brand Marketing in the first place? All that money,
little provable impact. Confident, you want to put just faith behind
those decisions? Especially when you likely have massive headroom on
the Demand Capture side?]

If you are spending material sums on Brand Marketing, and doing so
consistently, you’ll notice above that there is no short-term
incrementality measurement!

Fear not, but first a tiny scooch of context.

WHY (SUFFICIENCY-LEVEL) BRAND MARKETING?

For a company to survive / thrive for the long-term (decades), it has
to be exceptional at Brand Marketing.

No company survives (decades) just being good at Performance
Marketing. You can’t think of one.

You can think of 950 companies who over indexed their Marketing
budgets on Performance Marketing, had a great, and I do mean
incrementally great, couple years, and then realized they were in the
death grip of a shrinking mind share, first, and then, eroding market
share.

Given the colossal importance, intelligently building a _spike and
sustain _Brand Marketing strategy (/investment) is incredibly wise.
To support that, you want causal measurement of Brand Marketing’s
incrementality. [Premium Members, see TMAI #386 [7].]

That last bit will happen via two analytics approaches:

A. [Depending on budget size] The medium and long-term impact of Brand
Marketing will be measured via iSales, CPiS, and iProfit - as above.

B. [Regardless of budget size] The short, medium, and long-term impact
of Brand Marketing will be measured incrementally using Brand KPIs.

Let’s look at the three KPIs that fit into item B now…

Big 6 #4: Points of Brand Lift Delivered.

A typical Brand impact report will share the _percentage of Lift
delivered._

Ex: _Your $14mil Douyin Video campaign targeting GenZ delivered a 90%
Lift in Aided Awareness._

[NOTE: Yes, I’m teasing you a little bit. 90% is highly suspicious.
You also should never measure Aided Awareness, it is proven to deliver
no business impact, just like other _never-use_ like Ad Recall,
“Perception Shift,” etc.]

I want to _de_influence you from reporting the percentage of lift:

Percentages hide what’s essential, and revel in distractions.
Ex: 90% of what?

I recommend reporting Points of Lift delivered.

Ex: _Your $14mil Douyin Video campaign targeting GenZ delivered a +4
Lift in Unaided Awareness._

For my fellow Brand measurement nerds: There is one spectacular
benefit of reporting points of lift. You’ll ask: _+4 on what
baseline?_

If Douyin/any ad platform tells you this, it is such a treat.

You’ll discover if +4 was going from 54 (baseline) to 58 (new
baseline) or going from 2 to 6.

One of those is an _oh, that's nice, _and the other is _omg, we are
Gods of Marketing_!

Asking that of all the other platforms will help you see this often
hidden super precious insight: Your baselines for the same KPI (UBA
above) will be different on every ad platform.

It is, say, 54 on Douyin, it may be 22 on YT, 6 on FB, and 70 on TV!

Realizing this will dramatically impact your media plans going
forward, depending on strategic business priorities.

It makes me sad that most companies, and their agencies, have no idea
what the baselines are for their Target KPI on each platform. They
then peanut-butter media, or, worse, didn't realize the impact on
media minimums!

Your new agency (Croud? :), will now report:

_The baseline for Consideration on Douyin is 28.__

Our target for the campaign was +2.
__
Our causal reporting indicates a lift of +3 on Douyin at the
campaign’s end. _
Simple. Insightful.

As you’ll recall from my recommendation on how to report Incremental
Sales, Points of Brand lift are reported across three levels – all
using true test  control methodologies only:

LEVEL 3: Channel-Silo level (for Marketing Directors, and in-flight
optimization),

LEVEL 2: Campaign level (for Marketing VPs, and channel
allocation/cutting), and

LEVEL 1: Portfolio level (for your CFO, and proving the CMO’s glory
/ getting Marketing budget)

L3 is our short-term measure for tactical decisions. It uses platform
provided (free!) Brand Lift Studies (BLS) that leverage a true test  control setup to ensure causality.

L2 is our medium-term measure for strategic decisions. It uses (not
free) Campaign Lift Studies (CLS), that leverage custom super panels
to set up a true test  control across all Brand spend in the
campaign, across all channels.

L1 is our long-term measure for annual budgets. It uses, and this is
good news, the same L1 measurement you’ve set up for iSales! Super
advanced modeling, ideally using ML, backed up with _trust, but
verify_.

On your CMO scorecard, do you report Points of Lift (with baselines)?

If yes, at Level 3 or Level 2, or both?

BIG 6 #5: NUMBER OF INDIVIDUALS LIFTED.

Did you know that +3 Points on Lift on Facebook could be just 52
people? Or, 5mil!

Kinda important to know, right?

Oh, and get this, for the exact same campaign Creative, duration,
media audience, a +2 Points of Lift on YouTube could be 19mil people!

Even with knowing the baseline, and Points of Lift delivered, it is
insanely crucial to know just how many individuals you lifted Unaided
Brand Awareness for! [Or, Consideration, or Purchase Intent.]

You realize now why when you run around telling people _hey, hey, hey
our brand campaign delivered lift! our brand campaign was a massive
success, 3 points baby!!_ no one seems excited. Least of all your CFO.
They have no idea what these points mean [even if you include the
baseline!].

Going back to our example above, your new agency (Croud? :), will now
report:

_The baseline for Consideration on Douyin is 28. _
_
Our target for the campaign was +2. _
_
Our causal reporting indicates a lift of +3 on Douyin at the
campaign’s end. _
_
That +3 translated into 1.2 million GenZ Individuals lifted._

BOOM!

Your CFO does not know how that 1.2 million translates into sales or
when, but they can appreciate 1.2 million. Their heart will go _pitter
patter._

That’s your goal. Heart palpations.

You will measure Number of Individuals Lifted in both L3 (Marketer
use) and L2 (CMO use).

Do your Agency or Ad Platform provided reports contain the Number of
Individuals Lifted?

Why not?

BIG 6 #6: COST PER INDIVIDUAL LIFTED. [CPIL]

_Fully Loaded Brand Marketing Campaign Cost / # of Individuals
Lifted__
_
[See Big 6 #3 for how to compute fully loaded costs.]

Number of Individuals lifted will resonate with Finance because it is
an effectiveness metric.

In the same spirit, Cost Per Individual Lifted (CPIL) will resonate
because it is an efficiency metric.

You’ll see why CMOs appreciate the work we do: We obsess about the
CFO!

Going back to our example from earlier, the complete report:

_The baseline for Consideration on Douyin is 28. __

Our target for the campaign was +2.

Our causal reporting indicates a lift of +3 on Douyin at the
campaign’s end.

That +3 translated into 12 MILLION GENZ INDIVIDUALS LIFTED.
__
The COST PER GENZ LIFTED WAS $25 ($30mil/1.2mil)._

At the end of this exercise, you are reporting Brand (Demand Creation)
with the same rigor that typically comes with Performance (Demand
Capture).

Perhaps $25 is crazy for a $5 product. Perhaps not so crazy for a
$1,400 product.

The good news is, you get to have an intelligent conversation with
your CMO and your CFO – vs. no conversation or distrust with the
usual Brand campaign data pukes.

[Big 6 KPIs Brand | Cascading positive impact.]

[FOR PEER BRAND MEASUREMENT NERDS: Points of lift, especially across
different BLS, cannot be compared. Each channel, be it TV or YT or
Hulu or OOH, will use their own sweet methodology, and have KPI
limitations. Hence, +3 on FB might be smaller than +2 on TV! You
cannot compare Points of Lift. BUT. You can compare Number of
Individuals Lifted. You can compare Cost Per Individual Lifted.
Fireworks! Joy! You can use both to drive in-flight optimization, or
mid-campaign budget reallocation etc.]

BIG 6 ACTIVATION.

STEP 1: Ensure that a core group of one to four people with expertise
inside your company understand the approved methodologies to use for
each of the Big 6 KPIs.

STEP 2: Give the entire transformational exercise a cool project name.
Orion.

STEP 3: The Orion team, sets up deep dive exercises with the current
Performance and Brand reporting infrastructure to understand the KPIs
being reported to the CMO, the KPIs being used by Marketers to deliver
impact, the diagnostic metrics being used by internal teams or
external agency to do in-flight optimization.

STEP 4: Celebrate any Big 6 being already used by the
divisional/product/country teams – after checking that they are
using approved methodologies.

STEP 5: Work with divisional/product/country teams to drive
initiatives across people, process, politics, to activate remaining
Big 6 KPIs based on true incrementality as outlined above.

STEP 6: Once again celebrate.

It might take you a quarter to get to Step 4 celebrations, and,
depending on company size, six months to never to get to Step 6.
