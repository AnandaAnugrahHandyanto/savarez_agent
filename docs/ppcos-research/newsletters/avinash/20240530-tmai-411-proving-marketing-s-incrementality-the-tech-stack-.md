TMAI #411: PROVING MARKETING’S INCREMENTALITY: THE TECH STACK.

[ Web Version [3] ]

I’m sure you’ve noticed that over the last seven years, as the
Brits put it, I’ve been _banging on_ about proving to your CFO what
Marketing’s True Incrementality is.

Having solved this problem across companies, and countries, I’ve
come to believe that if I can make the CMO _bullet-proof_ to the
CFO… All other problems take care of themselves. More budget?
Absolutely CMO sitting on the company Board? Done. Attracting the very
best talent in the work? YES! Employee motivation? 11/10.

True Incrementality measurement is a strategic superpower. For a
company. For an Agency. For ad platforms. For every Marketing
employee.

Achieving that superpower requires knowledge, patience, tools,
_Analysis Ninjas_ (in-house or outsourced), and persuasion.

That last skill is less appreciated than I would like it to be.

1. YOU’LL NEED PERSUASION TO EVEN GET GOING ON THE JOURNEY.

Your less-knowledgeable leaders will tell you: _Hey, don’t we
already measure attribution_ (attribution is important, but it is NOT
incrementality!). Your less-knowledgeable Agency or in-house Analysts
will tell you: _Hey, we did a Pre-Post experiment in July 2022 it
proved 90% incrementality_ (1. Pre-Post is an awful methodology,
non-causal. 2. July 2022, once!).

2. YOU’LL NEED PERSUASION (FUELING A ROCKET TO MARS AMOUNT) TO GET
EVERYONE TO EXECUTE every complex element of Incrementality
measurement – online, offline, nonline, channel-silo, x-Stack,
portfolio.

3. YOU’LL NEED (NEARLY GOD-LIKE) PERSUASION AFTER THE INCREMENTALITY
RESULTS START ROLLING IN.

Chances are, if you’ve never measured Incrementality, it will be
astonishingly low and scare the _bejesus _out of everyone. You’ll
need to persuade everyone (in-house team, Agency, creative shop, user
experience) on a new and exciting way to do their jobs – because
they want to do what matters (!), and they want their CFO to value
Marketing (!!).

Because my mantra is _Incrementality, or (professional) death!,_ I’m
doing all of the above, every day. It is an incredible competitive
advantage for Croud as an Agency [4].

Recently, for an internal opportunity and an external conference
keynote (GPeC, Romania), I made two sketches for persuasion
opportunity #1.

I thought I would share them with you. I hope they’ll help you more
simply make the case for Incrementality, and how to measure it (the
_Incrementality tech stack_).

[Premium member bonus: If you would like the Slides versions, just
reply.]

What is Incrementality?

Simple definition you should use:

Marketing’s Incrementality:
_Business results from marketing tactics that would not have occurred
otherwise._
To bring it home in a more colloquial manner, I also phrase it as:

_What do you lose in terms of company profits if you fire everyone in
Marketing?_
This seems to clearly communicate what Incrementality is, instantly.

And, everyone is for it. Instantly.

[NOTE: Obviously, the answer is more complex at that super-high level.
Typically, but not always, if you do fire everyone in Marketing
tomorrow… You will lose very little in terms of company profits.
They might even increase for a handful of quarters as the _fully
loaded costs_ are gone. In the long-term, more than a handful of
quarters, the company is likely to lose Market Share – assuming the
Marketing was incremental.]

Let’s say you are a little scared of my colloquial definition.

I want to help.

I had to recently create a super simple sketch for an audience that
would not be very savvy about Marketing, much less a concept like
Incrementality.

Here it is…

[Incrementality Definition]

Another thing you know about me: I like bar charts. They are very
accessible to humans.

On the left, how Marketing is currently measured at most companies.
_We spent $10mil in Paid Media in 2024, here’s the Google Analytics
report that shows we drove revenue of $180mil._

AKA Claimed Revenue.

[BONUS: Analysis Ninjas will notice that the Paid Media team is
claiming credit for All Revenue, even though much of it came from non
Paid Media initiatives like SEO, Email etc.]

Claimed Revenue is the reason your CFO does not believe anything you
say.

Claimed Revenue also fails all common-sense tests – all the Gods in
the Greek pantheon would have to work together to make $10mil into
$180mil.

Claimed Revenue smells bad because Claimed Revenue smells bad.

On the right, how Marketing should be measured at all companies. _We
spent $10mil in Paid Media in 2024, the Incremental Revenue driven was
$60mil._

Just the green bar.

The rest of the yellow bar was mis-claimed credit [colloquially, a
lie].

You can see how this simple visual is persuasive, every human at the
other end now wants to know how big their green is.

This was all that was needed.

I’ve found when you present something this challenging (making
$180mil claimed by the CFO into $60mil), you require a bit more
because the first suspicious question will be: _Wait, how did you
identify the $60mil? _

I added a little bit of text, deliberately small to make it difficult
to read if presented (but easy to read as a print-out later).

[Incrementality Definition | Details]

I wanted to plant the seeds to solve persuasion challenge #2.

I also wanted to teach that Incrementality is measured at three
altitudes.

Finally, since the audience is not super savvy about Marketing, I knew
that Channel Silo Incrementality might be too complicated for them to
remember. Hence, I added a question that would make that measurement
accessible to them:

_How many Google PPC sales would disappear if I cut the Google budget
to zero?_
Most senior leaders wrongly believe that they are _paying the Google
tax_, that _Google is undeservedly taking my budget!_ The question is
phrased that way to play into their phobia, to get them to remember
and understand Incrementality.

When we at Croud, or at your Agency, or your Company, start measuring
Channel Silo Incrementality, we will clearly identify Google’s
incremental impact. Thus, creating more informed senior leaders.

Storytelling is also an underappreciated skill in Marketing (ironic).
I hope you find the sketch helpful in persuading your
leadership/clients, even more I hope you’ve picked up a tip or five
about how to tell simpler stories.

TECH STACK FOR PROVING MARKETING’S INCREMENTALITY.

It is exciting when I get to join Conference Organizers who want to
deliver non-normal value to the attendees. I love creating a
collection of highly impactful short stories. The Conference gets to
stand out from delivering the boring _same old, same old_, and the
attendees get actionable knowledge from the very edge of what’s
possible.

It is also an insane amount of pressure on me.

Audiences at conferences – 60 or 6,000 (my max) – are invariably
at different levels of knowledge, they are going to hear 4 to 20 other
speakers on a wide array of topics, and it can all get very
confusing.

For someone who lives the mantra _it’s not the ink, it’s the
think_ [5], my biggest objective is to share a new way to _think
_(usually via a framework). What makes my stories special is that I
back up the _think _with the _ink_, specific actionable
steps/tactics/immediate focus areas.

It is how I learn. So… It is how I teach.

At GPeC in Romania, my keynote was simply titled:
Incrementality-centric Marketing.

My scope was exceptional brand marketing, performance marketing,
powered by AI (where relevant), and… All of it focused on delivering
incremental results.

After the initial _slice of the pie_ vs. _size of the pie_ purposes of
Marketing, I shared six short stories about the type of incremental
Marketing and Analytics you should be solving for.

Since all this gets very complex, very quickly…. In Hansel and
Gretel style, I laid out _breadcrumbs _that allowed them to keep track
of the stories. Each _breadcrumb_ also had the dual purpose of serving
as a memory aid.

Unbeknown to them, this one magical slide, also solved for other
hidden agendas.

In most companies, Brand Marketing and Performance Marketing are silos
(so, so, silly, and counterproductive!), and I wanted to bring them
together. In many Agencies/companies brand is measured with the same
intelligence applied when _a fish is judged by its ability to climb a
tree_, I wanted to fix this. Finally, a super sophisticated advanced
item: If you do _Sustain Brand Marketing_, MMMs – _o_ or _n_ –
can’t measure its impact and I wanted to underline that.

That’s a lot, no?

Hard to do simply.

Here’s my attempt at creating a _frameworky _slide that brought
everyone along the journey (in under an hour, solving the equivalent
of world hunger in a Marketing context!).

At the end of short story one… Channel Silo Incremental Marketing,
and the tech stack required to measure it for Performance…

Continuing the journey…

At the end of short story two, Channel Silo Incremental Brand
Marketing, and the tech stack required to measure it…

Forty-five minutes, and six inspiring short stories, later….

One _magical _slide…

A book’s worth of lessons, from painful failures and joyous
victories.

It is unlikely that the audience will remember most of the details in
my stories, that is not the point. The details are there to earn their
confidence that I’ve actually done all this work, and they can do it
all as well. What the _magical _framework slide does is lay it all out
in a way that there are threads they can pull when they get back to
the office.

Clean stacks, that fit together, to create a better future.
