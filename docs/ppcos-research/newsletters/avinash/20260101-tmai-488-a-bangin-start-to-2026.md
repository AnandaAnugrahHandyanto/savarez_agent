TMAI #488: A BANGIN’ START TO 2026!

[ Web Version [2] ]

If experience has taught me anything, it’s this:

*
The distance between _we have data, _and _we have profitable growth_
is still mostly… humans making disciplined choices.

It is Jan 1.

Here are my recommendations for the highest impact moves to put into
motion (immediately!).

#1. MAKE INCREMENTALITY YOUR NORTH STAR.

RECOMMENDATION:

Stop treating attribution as “truth.” You must move to
Channel-Silo and Portfolio Incrementality. Optimize media _based on
incremental profit_, not (MTA) claimed conversions. Start with
Conversion Lift Studies (for Channel-Silo), add/remove/spend to
diminishing _incremental profit_ (DIP)_._ Step up to MMMs,
add/remove/spend to DIP.

2026 RELEVANCE:

Privacy regulations, 3P cookie deprecation, multi-device &
multi-browser usage, have deeply eroded multi-touch attribution’s
value. Incrementality is the _only_ methodology that Finance trusts
because it measures CAUSALITY, not correlation.

BUSINESS IMPACT:

You will likely discover that 40% of your budget is driving ZERO
INCREMENTAL REVENUE. Cutting that waste goes straight to the bottom
line (Profit!). In the 60%, you will find low returns, medium returns,
high returns. More winning.

[BONUS ADVICE: Brand Marketing is about high CAC, high LTV, and
long-term memory structures. Performance Marketing is about low CAC,
low LTV, and capturing existing demand. Don’t judge a fish by its
ability to climb a tree. AKA, do not judge Brand by CPA, and do not
judge Performance by Brand Lift.]

#2. EMBRACE AEO NOW.

RECOMMENDATION:

You will lose approx. 30% of Organic and 15% of Paid Traffic in 2026
as users continue to shift from keyword searching to asking complex
questions. Build an Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) program: category
narratives, product truths, comparative claims, and distribution that
trains LLMs to recommend your brand. Then instrument Answer Engine
Analytics (AEA) [3]: track where you show up, what “performance
vectors” the LLM associates with you, and how that changes over
time.

2026 RELEVANCE:

You don’t want to be Blackberry after the launch of the iPhone.

If you drive $20m annual revenue from Organic, you're facing $6m in
losses. AEO recovery captures $3m back. That's $3m your CFO wasn't
expecting. Career rocket fuel.

BUSINESS IMPACT:

Recover 40-50% of lost traffic if you execute AEO strategies. (The
other 50%? Gone permanently.) Even the for now small volume of AE
traffic is converting at 2x to 4x higher - take it!

#3. WIN BEFORE YOU SPEND: PRE-TEST PASSED CREATIVE + MEDIA
SUFFICIENCY!

RECOMMENDATION:

Stop launching campaigns and _hoping_ they work. Your creative is
responsible for 60-70% of your success. Before a single dollar leaves
your bank account, you must rigorously pre-test your creative (with
HMM Pro! [4]). Then, ensure you fund your campaigns to meet _spike and
sustain frequency-first _media optimums.

2026 RELEVANCE:

The AI era increases content volume and competitive noise;
insufficient weight and mediocre creative will disappear into the void
faster. Also: organizations still silo creative and media, which
equals “kiss of death.”

BUSINESS IMPACT:

Eliminate 40-60% of campaign failures before they happen. Every
failed campaign costs you 1x your spend (the wasted money) plus
opportunity cost.

Improve campaign success rate from industry average of 30-40% to
80-90%. When you only launch Pre-Tested Creative with Sufficient
Media, you almost cannot fail. (Almost!)

#4. FOR BRAND MARKETING, FOCUS ON THE BIG 3 KPIS.

RECOMMENDATION:

Doing brand marketing right means focusing on one of three valuable
purposes: UNAIDED BRAND AWARENESS, CONSIDERATION, (PURCHASE)
INTENT. And, only accept true test-control lift measurement.

2026 RELEVANCE:

Brand budgets will be attacked when CFOs can’t see impact. Commonly
pushed useless metrics like Ad Recall, Aided Awareness, Brand Love,
etc. will show zero impact/long-term profits. With your brand being
kicked out of generic queries, and increasingly out of influencing
steps by AI/LLMs… Effective branding is critical insurance (not just
a growth strategy).

BUSINESS IMPACT:

You protect investment in long-term growth while still being
accountable. This improves portfolio balance (short + long), reduces
over-reliance on lower-funnel channels, and strengthens pricing power
through brand preference.
#5. OBSESS OVER METHODOLOGIES, NOT JUST METRICS.

RECOMMENDATION:

For every KPI that matters, ask: _how exactly is it measured?_ Ex: For
Attribution: Demand Data-Driven Attribution, not Last-Click or
First-Click nonsense. For Brand Lift: Demand true Test & Control Brand
Lift Surveys, not Brand Tracker garbage. For ROAS: Demand Marginal
ROAS, not Average ROAS. Ask for the formula. Torture it until you
understand the _HOW _of measurement, not just the score.

2026 RELEVANCE:

As “measurement” gets easier to produce and harder to trust
(privacy limits, modeled conversions, AI intermediaries), methodology
becomes the integrity layer. Two teams can report the same KPI and be
living in different realities.

BUSINESS IMPACT:

Correct 30-50% of misattributed budget decisions. When you use
Incrementality vs. Attribution, you discover half your "winning"
channels are actually losing money.

Improve forecast accuracy by 25-35%. Better methodologies = better
predictions = better planning.

#6. KILL DATA PUKES: SPLIT KPIS, DIAGNOSTICS, AND INFLUENCING
VARIABLES.

RECOMMENDATION:

Movement in a KPI directly impacts your bottom line. Diagnostic
Metrics explain why KPIs move. Influencing Variables explain why
Diagnostic Metrics move. For each KPI, map the 4-6 Diagnostic Metrics
that explain it. For each Metric, identify the 5 - 25 Influencing
Variables underneath. Then... Ruthlessly simplify. CxO dashboards:
KPIs only. Director reports: KPIs + Metrics. Agency reports: All the
IVs they want.

2026 RELEVANCE:

Your org was already traumatized by the data pukes you send out each
week. In 2026, the org is about to drown in automated charts and AI
summaries. Scaled uselessness! Companies that simplify complexity will
win. The ones drowning in their own data will die.

BUSINESS IMPACT:

15-25% reduction in decision-making time. When your CMO can look at 4
KPIs instead of 47 metrics, decisions happen faster.

20-30% improvement in marketing effectiveness. When everyone knows
what actually matters, they optimize for the right things.
#7. PREPARE FOR AGENTIC COMMERCE (YES, IT’S EARLY; NO, YOU CAN’T
IGNORE IT).

RECOMMENDATION:

Get your Technical AEO in order. Agents (like ChatGPT's agent or
others) are beginning to shop _for_ humans. Ensure your site doesn't
block reputable agents. Build "Shop via AP" endpoints and ensure your
inventory and pricing data are accessible to these bots. IMPORTANT:
Upgrade your tech stack to ensure it is _a collection of individual,
independent API-first microservices _– the future of ecommerce.

2026 RELEVANCE:

We are moving from humans searching to Agents executing tasks. 66% of
shoppers are hesitant now, but that will change fast. This is the
“Pan Search” future where the customer _is_ a bot.

BUSINESS IMPACT:

In 2026 it’s a strategic option; beyond that, it can become
existential. If agents can’t transact with you, you risk being
silently removed from consideration.

#8: APPLY THE "MONEYBALL" PRINCIPLE TO MARKETING TALENT.

RECOMMENDATION:

Hire and promote analysts and marketers not based on familiarity with
specific tools (which change), but on my fav core competencies:
intellectual curiosity, business acumen, and a love for the "_so
what?"_. Seek _analytical storytellers_ who can find the story in the
data and communicate it powerfully.

2026 RELEVANCE:

The tool set of 2026 will be different from 2024. AI will handle more
report generation, it will automagically build more creative, and it
will identify relevance at scale. You need humans who can think
critically, ask the right questions, and drive strategy—not just
operate a platform.

BUSINESS IMPACT:

Build future-proof, high-performance teams that can drive edge
innovation. You’ll also reduce tool-dependent silos and create a
culture of strategic problem-solving, accelerating the impact of all
my recommendations today.

#9. ATLEAST BE A CATEGORY B HUMAN.

Everything above is something you would do for your company. This one
you would do for you.

RECOMMENDATION:

Three types of humans occupy the planet today: Category A: Using
current AI solutions to the max. Category B: Using AI a few times a
day. Category C: Use AI once in a while, or not at all. Be in B. No
matter what you are trying to do, try to do it with AI first. Writing
an email to your boss or a poem for your beloved. Analyze a
spreadsheet. Spot a flaw in a PhD thesis. Looking for a new planet. It
will teach you the jagged edge of AI effectiveness, help you identify
where you can use AI as an effective collaborator / outsourcer.

2026 RELEVANCE:

AI started to take away parts of our job in 2025, I anticipate the
trend to accelerate. The productivity gap between Category B and C is
a chasm now, and between B and A is widening.

BUSINESS IMPACT:

[I am so sorry to be blunt…] If you don't shift to Category A/B, you
will be left behind. If your company doesn't, it will be outpaced by
competitors running at 100x speed.

BOTTOM LINE.

More than ever, I hope you sense that my recommendations are not about
tools or tactics. They are about courage.

Courage to kill vanity metrics. Courage to speak profit, not
potential. Courage to prioritize _economic value_ over activity.
Courage to challenge the status quo. Courage to befriend a technology
that holds potential to do what you do today – faster, better.
Courage to learn. Courage. Courage. Courage.

That to me is 2026 – the start of a once in a generation shift.

Carpe diem!
