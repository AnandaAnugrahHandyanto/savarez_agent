TMAI #414: THE CLIENT  AGENCY  PLATFORM FLYWHEEL!

[ Web Version [2] ]

In the _game _of delivering _provable to the CFO incremental
outcomes _to the business, typically three players are involved:

THE CLIENT (/BRAND).

Let’s say, Zappos. The Client will have numerous internal teams,
spanning Owned, Earned, Paid Marketing clusters, a Creative team,
obviously, the real change drivers, an Analytics team, UX experts, a
hidden Project management team who actually make sure stuff gets
delivered (I see you, Bernadette!), at times some _GTM Experts, _and,
finally, the two people responsible for 100% of Strategic Innovation.
THE AGENCY.

Let’s say, Publicis. It contains numerous internal teams, spanning
Owned, Earned, Paid Marketing clusters, a Creative team, an Analytics
team (I see you, Michaela!), UX experts, obviously, a Sales team, a
Client Services team with immense AM and AE talent who make sure stuff
gets delivered, and, finally, often someone responsible for
innovation.
THE PLATFORM.

Let’s say, Google. It contains numerous internal teams, spanning
Paid Marketing clusters (and in the case of Google: Organic, AI and
ancillary teams like Creative),  an Analytics team, massive Client
teams with awesome HOIs (I see you Ames!) and AE talent who make sure
stuff gets delivered, and, finally, often a team responsible for
_let's keep innovating on the ad products people can buy from us!_
A happy threesome: Client + Agency + Platform.

THE CHALLENGE.

I’m sure you noticed that there are basically the same sets of
talent across all three entities.

So…

How do you make sure each entity is doing what it’s supposed to?

While the clusters of skills seem similar, which entity should lead
and which one should follow when it comes to the work that needs to
get done?

How can you recognize one link in that chain is weak and needs to be
asked to move on? (Sadly, the candidates for this are Never the
client, and always the Platform and/or the Agency.)

It is not easy.

Often there is one dominant party in that threesome, and they drive
the agenda.

Most often this tends to be the CLIENT – because it is their money
that’s being spent - and the other two, Agency, Platform, have to
bend to their will.

This is not always a great idea because it is not written in stone
that the Client is the smartest, most knowledgeable, in the threesome.
It is heartbreaking how frequently I see the Client acting on their
unjustified sense of competence. The outcomes are terrible in the
short-term and catastrophic in the long-term.

[Resulting in my fav quote in this context: _Every Brand gets the
Agency they deserve!_]

Occasionally, there is humility at the Client end, and the AGENCY
becomes the dominant parties. I’ve seen so much good come from this,
and lots of ugly come from this. The causal factor for ugly outcomes
is the current Marketing savvy of the Agency C-Suite, and their
real-world experience executing campaigns. If those conditions are not
true, an Agency dominant in the flywheel is not pretty.

It is rare that the PLATFORM is the dominant party in the threesome
(though lord knows they try and try and try!! – Tiffany and I tried
and tried! :)). The reason that Marketing Platforms have to follow is
that they only know their little piece of the puzzle. They are Very
Very Good at that piece, but it is career limiting for Platform
employees to forget that they are a little piece in a Brand’s
Marketing universe.

So… Back to the three questions above.

How do you make sure this threesome is a harmonious flywheel - where
each entity is doing what it's supposed to, letting others do what
they are good at?

How does that flywheel operate at Max Q, delivering insanely
incremental results?

FLYWHEEL PRE-REQUIREMENT: A FOUNDATIONAL PHILOSOPHY.

Each entity in this threesome has strengths and weaknesses.

In part sourced in the reality that each has such a different set of
responsibilities, and access to a different set of specialized
knowledge.

In my Strategic Consulting engagements [3], after a more in-depth
study of the +/- across each entity, I construct a custom operating
model (aka flywheel) for the client. The recommendation considers
where intelligence, experience, competence, and resilience is across
layers of work. It accounts for the incentive structures in place to
drive optimal behavior. Finally, it accounts for the audacity of the
expected outcomes.

It is hard work. : )

Recently, I had the opportunity to recommend an operating model in
aggregate. Let me share a version of it with you today, in the hope
that it will inspire you to make your flywheel operate with higher
velocity.

Ready?

I find that jumping to roles, responsibilities, accountability without
a foundational philosophy is a mistake. You are operating in a vacuum.
I don’t like that (or recommend it).

Hence, the first challenge is to have a foundational Marketing
philosophy that will power the operating model.

Guess what’s my preferred choice?

Yes, incrementality!

[Types of Incrementality.]

The objective of the operating model (aka flywheel) will be to focus
on delivering highly incremental results across three layers:

CHANNEL SILO INCREMENTALITY. Requiring deep tactical expertise, for
decisions that’ll happen in seconds, in hours, weekly and bi-weekly.
Altitude: Marketers.

XSTACK INCREMENTALITY. Requiring deep expertise in both execution
strategy and tactics, for decisions that’ll span monthly, quarterly.
Altitude: CMO.

PORTFOLIO INCREMENTALITY. Requiring deep business and Marketing
expertise, in strategy and meta-patterns, for decisions that’ll span
quarterly, and six monthly. Altitude: CFO.
[Premium Members: If the three terms above, the colors, are new to
you, please see TMAI #233: Solving Incrementality | Marketing's Holy
Grail [4]]

You can see above the Analytics lens applied to each element of the
philosophy. I could just as well have written approaches for Creative.

Creative Concept, blue.

Creative Executions, green.

An explosion of Creative Executions customized for segments of humans
AND based on performance, powered by GenAI… yellow.
It is not that blue cannot do what I’ve noted for yellow – but it
will take them 10x longer, require them to pay for things that are
free, and the optimization will be 50x slower.

It is a test of a resilience that we can look at all kinds of
incremental Marketing bits through the philosophy above.

Ready for the big reveal?

THE CLIENT  AGENCY  PLATFORM FLYWHEEL.

First, the Client.

Here's the optimal distribution of _roles, responsibilities,
accountability_ that typically rest with the Client (/Brand) to
ensure that the flywheel is operating at_ global maxima_…

[Client responsibility & accountability.]

When the Client has the right set of skills and experiences in their
senior most Marketing leaders, their optimal role is that of
“business owners.”

They own Marketing Strategy, Budget, and Execution Models that span
all three entities.

In context of the real impact of Marketing, the Client also owns and
executes the super critical _LEARNING FROM LEARNINGS_ (aka
Meta-Analysis). Looking across Marketing functions, teams,
initiatives, time horizons, no other party will be as unbiased, as
plugged into business challenges, like the Client. Done well, this
will play an Out Sized in feeding a Marketing strategy that becomes a
competitive advantage.

Likewise, a lot of smoke will be blown up the pipe, a lot of vendors
will sell crap wrapped in stardust, occasionally the Agency and
Platforms will make fantastical claims about impact or pre-testing
results or incrementality or machine learning or… a lot. It is the
Brand’s job to run an effective _TRUST, BUT VERIFY_ program.

[The first target of _Trust, but Verify_ will be internal and all the
VPs, CxO, claiming _I know exactly how this is supposed to be done,
you don’t need data_.]

The Client (and Agencies and Platforms) can have a hard time focusing
because there are always new shiny objects to chase (ORGANIC SOCIAL!
AI! INFLUENCERS!).

It is the Brand’s job, the CMO specifically, to drive _RIDICULOUS
FOCUS_.

Here are some ways I do it: 8_0% of the budget on just three channels.
No Creative in-market without Pre-Testing passed score. No budgets
funded below media minimums. Enough weight to get to wear-in _(please
for the sake of Jebus, atleast get to wear-in, ideally to wear-out).
_No, every team does not need Paid Media._ I have, literally, 150 of
these proven results - from past successes and mistakes, trust, but
verified!

This only applies to large and larger companies: When you have a dozen
divisions, each with a dozen teams, each doing a dozen different
things, each thing being measured a dozen different ways… You get
zero incrementality (it might even be negative). The biggest gift a
Client can give an Agency, the Platforms, is to figure out how to
effectively deal with INTERNAL POLITICS, the conflicting agendas, the
allure of ineffective execution ideas. This. Is. Hard.

I place an extraordinary set of difficult responsibilities on the
Client (and carried them when I was Client in the past) because at the
end of the day, it is their money. No one else will care about their
money as much as them. That is not to say the Agency and Platforms are
not committed, they are. But. It is not their money.

Instead of the CMO ensuring that the above Big 5 recommendations are
getting a bulk of their attention, intelligence, and influence... Some
get focused on tactical distractions. Then, even if the Agency and the
Platforms rock… The Client can’t be saved.

When the CMO has the Big 5 assigned to her most competent and
courageous leaders… Even if the Agency and Platforms suck… The
Client will win. (In part because competence and courage will ensure
they are fired in a timely manner. You’ll be surprised how long past
the expiry date Agencies manage to survive at a distracted Client.)

So what about the _roles, responsibilities, accountability_ for the
Agency?

I thought you would never ask...

[Agency responsibility & accountability.]

[NOTE: I have deep details to share, as in the case of Client above.
But, this newsletter is getting too long. I'll talk fast the rest of
the way.]

The Agency's obsession: Next level down details, pan-channel and
pan-purpose in-flight optimizations, bringing intelligence up to the
Brand and down to the Agency from learnings, data, and executions that
they have – across industry and account norms – as the client will
never see this.

What about the Platform’s_ roles, responsibilities, accountability?_

They are both easier and much harder...

I spent a big chunk of my life at a platform. I could write a small
book on their role and transformative impact.

Briefly, work to ensure that their platform, say Facebook, is the
greatest thing since sliced bread for the client. They do that by
focusing on their Big 5 above.

If you need an image for your slide decks… Here’s the full
picture…

[The Client + Agency + Platform Flywheel.]

Regardless of which pink (?) cluster you sit in today, I hope you find
the _role, responsibilities, accountability_ of the other two, as
applied to an incrementality-centric Marketing philosophy, to be
helpful.

Use this to redo your current operating model with the other two.

BOTTOM LINE.

Regardless of the cluster you are in, the flywheel consistently hits
_global maxima _when:

1. You do your job.

2. Lead with trust in the other two entities.

3. Send knowledge up.

4. Solve for (one) Success KPI and pre-set Target.

5. Innovate with intelligence, but focus it in your area of focus and
altitude.

6. As much as possible, focus on making the other two look good via
delivered results (and not words spoken).
_Carpe diem._
