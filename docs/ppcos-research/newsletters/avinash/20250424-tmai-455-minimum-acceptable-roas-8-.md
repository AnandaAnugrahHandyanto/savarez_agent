TMAI #455: MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE ROAS? 8.

[ Web Version [2] ]

I am deeply unfond of the metric ROAS.

ROAS = REVENUE FROM ADS / COST OF ADS.

If you spent $1,000 on ads and generated $3,000 in sales from those
ads:

*
ROAS = 3,000/1,000 = 3

The implication is, you earned $3 for every $1 you spent — a ROAS of
3x.

Of all the metrics that measure advertising’s outcomes, ROAS creates
the least incentive for good financial behavior.

At the very minimum, I prefer ROI.

MARKETING ROI = (REVENUE FROM MARKETING -
MARKETING COST)/(MARKETING COST)

If you spent $1,000 on ads and generated $3,000 in sales from those
ads:

*
ROI = (3,000-1,000)/(1,000) = 2

A little better, a little more responsibility for _what the company
made_ by accounting for some related costs.

BUT.

ROAS is everywhere.

So, I’ll get off my high horse.

I want to incentivize good financial behavior by Marketing. (My
mantra: _Solve for the CFO, her standards, and your CMO will be
golden._).

One effective strategy: PRE-IDENTIFY WHAT’S _GOOD FOR THE COMPANY_.

Some people call this Targets. 😁

If I can set a target for ROAS that clearly identifies what’s _good
for the company_, good Marketers will work to solve for that target,
and everyone wins.

But, the problem is, how do you come up with a good target?

The discussion quickly gets bogged down on reality, politics, past,
present, change, challenges, and goes nowhere.

I’ve come up with an approach I use with my clients. I use it to
create the first Targets for ROAS for them (though you can use this
approach for many Outcome KPIs).

Let me share it with you today, please adapt to suit your company's
financials/reality.

THE CAMPAIGN.

Here’s a campaign from Europe for an old client... Traditional ROAS
is still Revenue from Ads / Cost of Ads...

The ROAS exceeds 1, so... Success?

Two problems:

*
The company’s success is not Revenue, it is something closer to
Profit – if not Profit.

*
If I were the CFO: Is the best use of $1.1 mil in my company, to go
out and earn $1,8 mil? What if I put it into Product Development? What
if I put it in coffee machines for employees? What if I hired three
more people in Customer Service?

A common standard for ROAS tends to be: _Just be positive, come in
above 1_. Sadly, it fails both these tests, spectacularly so. Then, in
good times, and definitely bad, Marketing is easily the first budget
cut.

So let’s create high standards, by presetting higher targets.

INCREMENTALITY-CENTRIC ROAS.

A simple incentive for good behavior: Deliver incremental Revenue, in
exchange for Marketing spend.

Definition:

INCREMENTALITY: BUSINESS RESULTS FROM MARKETING TACTICS THAT WOULD NOT
HAVE OCCURRED OTHERWISE

Translation:

WHAT HAPPENS TO MARKETING’S CLAIMED BUSINESS IMPACT, IF YOU DO NO
MARKETING?

In our use case above:

IF WE DID NOT SPEND $1.1 MIL, WOULD WE HAVE LOST ALL OF THE $1.8 MIL
IN REVENUE?

The answer, unless you are in an odd market, and your Marketer team is
the personification of God, of course, is no.

Many of the people in that $1.8 mil in revenue would have converted
anyway. They might be your existing customers. They might already have
made up their mind before walking into your retail store. They might
have had an influential friend called Simon, and they buy everything
Simon says. They might have purchased your product on a whim. You sell
cheap diamonds, and the Buyer only cares about price. A hundred
reasons.

Each company’s incrementality is unique to them, you measure it
using a mix of sophisticated long-term Mix Models and _trust, but
verified _large-scale experiments.

In a very large study in the US I was a part of, the range for
Marketing’s incrementality for large companies was in the range
of 0% TO 22%. As someone who works on Marketing Strategy, my heart
wept when I noticed the median was 4.7%.

That range is in aggregate. You will some campaigns that will have
slightly higher incrementality – especially small hyper focused ones
– at 26% and a lot of campaigns – say your Brand keyword campaigns
– will have very low incrementality closer to the median.
Since I don’t know your Marketing’s incrementality, I’m going to
assume that you are a cross between Krishna and Jesus, and your
incrementality is off the charts at 25%.

What happens to the ROAS Meta provided?

[Incremental ROAS calculation.]

You lost the company a ton of money.

And, I’m not even accounting for Profit!

If I include the cost of advertising ($1.1mil), cost of goods sold
(approx.. $0.7mil in this case), etc., you did not just lose a ton of
money doing advertising… You lit a gigantic money bonfire.

With sincerity, we forgive our Marketing co-worker Emma, all of this
is new news.

We can help Emma in the future.

We can set a Traditional ROAS target upfront, our co-workers in
advertising will know clearly, they need to be super brilliant with
their ad choices.

Let’s set the Incremental ROAS target as 3, on a Revenue basis. That
number is high enough that even if we accounted for the cost of
advertising, cost of goods sold, agency fees, creative production
costs, and other costs… We are likely to break even. Repeat, just
break even.
So, what should the Traditional ROAS target be for our Marketer Emma?

Emma’s new ROAS Target = 12.

(As I’m sure you noticed, that number is a function of
incrementality, you can ignore all the revenue, ad costs etc. It is
always 12. 😊)

If Emma cannot get 12 ROAS, we are better off using the money to give
every employee free lunch on Fridays, hire two sales reps, seven
product engineers, etc. etc.

Maybe you are like… _Come on man, 12 is impossible! _It is not.
But. Fine.

We can lower the Incremental ROAS target to 2.

Your advertising has to deliver a Traditional ROAS of 8.

In our use case, at a ROAS of 8, $1mil in advertising will have to
earn non-incremental $8.8 mil in Revenue.

That will deliver an Incremental Revenue of $2.2 mil.

Which after taking out the cost of advertising (minus $1.1 mil), and
accounting for a Margin of 35%, is a measly $0.7 MIL IN INCREMENTAL
GROSS PROFIT FROM ADVERTISING.

In most financial models… It is difficult to justify investing in
advertising to acquire such little Profit. Hence you probably want to
be safe and shoot for an Incremental ROAS of 3.

How much of your current Paid Advertising ROAS survive if your target
for Traditional ROAS is 8?

OMG, nothing?

😊

Time for a smarter advertising strategy!!!

CREATE YOUR OWN INCREMENTAL ROAS TARGET.

Play with your own numbers.

Perhaps you have compelling evidence that your advertising is godlier
than god, hence your incrementality is 35%. Great. Go punch it into
the sheet. Recompute.

(Your new ROAS Target is 6. How much of your current advertising
survives?)

Perhaps your Margin is 95%. OMG, so great. Go punch it into the sheet.
Recompute.

(You added $0.3 MIL to your incremental advertising Profit, maybe
get a small relief on Traditional ROAS target.)

This should be crystal clear:

*
FOR TRADITIONAL ROAS BELOW 8, YOU CAN BE ALMOST CERTAIN ADVERTISING IS
LOSING YOUR COMPANY MONEY.

In fact, if you did not spend any money on advertising, your company
would make atleast 75% of the Revenue claimed to be driven by
advertising…. And, that 75% would arrive WITHOUT DOING ANY
ADVERTISING!

BOTTOM LINE.

For your Performance Marketing ROAS reported by Google, Meta, TikTok,
WeChat… The minimum Traditional ROAS should be higher than 8 (to get
an Incremental ROAS of 2).

Anything less, and your CFO is better off investing your advertising
budget in customer service / engineering / free lunches / her personal
Swiss bank account.

Create higher standards for your Marketing teams. Inspire them to ask
more of the ad platforms.

Carpe diem.

-Avinash.

PS: In certain edge cases, I have considered 6 as the Traditional ROAS
target – where incremental Profit ROAS math worked.

PPS: What to do if your Traditional ROAS is 2? First, accept that
there is no incremental Revenue, certainly no incremental Profit being
delivered by ads. Second, ensure the team, agencies, and ad platforms
to emphatically know there is no incremental anything. Finally,
challenge the ecosystem to do smarter advertising. It is hard, that is
why it is a competitive advantage.
