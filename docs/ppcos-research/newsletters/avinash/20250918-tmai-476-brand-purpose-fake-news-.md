TMAI #476: BRAND PURPOSE: FAKE NEWS?

[ Web Version [2] ]

A super strategic discussion today.

Nike’s _brand purpose_ is “promotes diversity and inclusion in
sport.”

Nike has spent billions on Marketing activities. Per EB Institute:
_32% of the people surveyed in the UK and 52% of the people surveyed
in the US were aware of it._

Here’s a table from the EB study, for 14 brands:

[EB Study Results, Brand Purpose.]

As a believer in _brand purpose _(more on this below), you can
imagine how immediately heartbroken I was at reading the study’s
results [3].

Oh, and above is the BEST CASE SCENARIO. 🤨

The numbers above include guessing!

Ex: The study authors isolated a participant guessing by giving them
dummy purpose attributes. 9% of the people in the US agreed Patagonia
“_advocates for neuroscience led literacy_”. That’s a false
positive.

Recognition of _brand purpose _is often near the 10% mark.

The results are also the best case for another reason: The surveys
were _prompted recall_ – a tactic that aids recall and hence
inflates.

In the study’s conclusion, the authors, very politely, say (lightly
paraphrased):
_Brands adopting a purpose in the hope of driving significant sales
growth may be disappointed, as many consumers remain largely unaware
of brands’ purpose per study. Marketers need to be wary of claims
the success of a brand to its purpose, without acknowledging other
factors that may have contributed, for example, increasing advertising
budget to reach more potential buyers and/or building Physical
Availability._

And…

_For brands that don’t have a purpose, adopting one is not
imperative for brand success. For brands that already have a brand
purpose, we recommend re-evaluating the level of investment in this
strategy._

_T_he punchline…

_“Brand purpose” is not necessarily a bad thing, the research
simply documents the shortcomings of brand purpose as a marketing
strategy, noting its commercial impact is often overstated and lacks
support from empirical data._

Pretty cut and dry, no?

No.

EB STUDY AGREEMENT.

The premise EB is exploring is sound.

Do I want my toothbrush to declare their _brand purpose _is to save
the world or feed hungry hippos in Brazil? Or… I just want Phillips
to make a good toothbrush?

The latter.

Numerous companies have been caught with their pants down when it
comes to _brand purpose_ (Unilever, Volkswagen, HSBC, Keruig). In each
case all it was… An exercise in _purpose washing._ While, forgetting
they are just a soap seller, a car company, a bank, and makers of poor
coffee in the morning.

Below, you’ll see that _brand purpose _can be a force for good.
But. I completely understand why EB wanted to spend time getting 3,000
human responses to assess the value of _brand purpose. _

The way _brand purpose _is being deployed in many companies, needs an
empirical reality check. It is of value to know, why _brand purpose_
results in unmatchable magic, when it does.

EB STUDY DISAGREEMENT.

As an Analyst and Brand Strategist, I sense a flaw in the study’s
foundational assumption:

*
ASKING CONSUMERS ABOUT _BRAND PURPOSE._

Let me explain using three terms all of you in Marketing will be aware
of.

A MISSION STATEMENT is _what do we do. _

*
Nike: _To bring inspiration and innovation to every athlete in the
world_.
*
McDonald’s: _Make delicious feel-good moments easy for everyone._

A BRAND PURPOSE is _why do we exist_.

*
Nike:_ Promote diversity and inclusion in sports_.
*
McDonald’s: _Feed and foster communities._

An AD TAGLINE is _how do we cue the brand memorably_.

*
Nike: _Just do it_.
*
McDonald’s: _I’m lovin’ it._

When we study brand purpose, there’s potential to mix these three
entirely different things, or to use them interchangeably.

Here’s a helpful table to illuminate the differences.

[Mission Statement, Brand Purpose, Ad Tagline.]

A tiny, insignificant, number of your potential customers care about
your mission statement or have heard it. And, honestly, why should
they? See above “where it lives.”

A few more than tiny, insignificant number of your potential customers
care about your _brand purpose _or have heard it. And, why should
they?

A much larger number of your potential customers might care about your
_ad tagline_ as it might communicate an important value or emotion.
Even if they don’t care for it (like Geico, Toyota, Verizon, etc
taglines for me), but they might be aware of it because they are being
blasted with it day and night.

This reality highlights a mismatch in the EB study:

IT ASKED 3,000 PEOPLE ABOUT SOMETHING THEY ARE NOT EXPECTED TO KNOW.

I’m surprised 10% did!

I was unaware of _promote diversity and inclusion in sports _(Nike)
and _feed and foster communities _(McD).

I’ve certainly heard of _just do it _and _I’m lovin’
it. _I’ve heard them a million times over the years, because
Marketers at both companies have resisted the temptation to earn their
job promotions by “adding value to the brand’s positioning” by
changing it.

EB study’s [3] title: _Brand Purpose… Are consumers aware to
care?_

For _brand purpose_, that the customers are not aware… Has no
implication on if they care (or not). They are not supposed to be
aware.

Hence the conclusion, warning about the non-empirical obsession in the
value of _brand purpose_, feels flawed.

AN EXCEPTION.

There might be companies where the _ad tagline_ is tied to the _brand
purpose_ AND constantly blasted to customers. Perhaps EB could have
selected just those companies, and that would be a decent empirical
starting point.

Ex: Airbnb.

*
Mission: _Create a world where anyone can belong anywhere._
*
Brand Purpose: _Belonging – helping people feel they can belong
anywhere._
*
Ad Tagline: _Belong Anywhere._

Belong. Belong. Belong.

Good candidate for EB’s starting assumption: “_Brand Purpose…
Are consumers aware to care?_”

The 14 companies EB used don’t seem to have Airbnb’s clear tie
between the three dimensions.

That makes this leap by EB worrisome: _Because people outside the
companies can’t recall your brand purpose… Brand purpose is
something you should be wary of._

EB STUDY ALTERNATIVES.

Now… Plenty of companies have _brand purpose_, and often it is worth
as much as _a dead fish nailed to the wall_ to them.

I am FOR a study that identifies empirical evidence for this _brand
purpose _obsession. I hope that EB will collect data and, in their
standard high quality way, assess reality.

Here are alternative approaches:

A. If they want to retain their methodology, I hope they will switch
their survey to _ad taglines _– something the customers would have
heard of (or have plenty of chances to hear).
The _Awareness Rate_ will speak volumes.

B. There are plenty of CMOs who have declared they worship at the
altar of _brand purpose_, and shared how it _infuses every dimension
of their Marketing_.

These companies tend to be public. There should be plenty of publicly
available Revenue, Profits, Market Share, other data to establish
causality.

This could be combined with the type of survey research to collect
customer perception shifts – especially over time.

C. If EB wanted to stick to _brand purpose _and the same survey…
Since _brand purpose_ is often an internal facing concept (see table
above), an optimal approach would be to test the value of _brand
purpose _by bypassing consumers all together and survey company
employees and executives.

The survey would detect if a clearly defined _brand purpose_ improves
internal decision-making, innovation, and operational efficiency,
which could then lead to better products and services that drive
growth.

The first one’s likely easy, the second would require significant
analytical effort, the third likely not as difficult to do (but likely
need long-term observations).

THE AVINASH EDITORIAL ON _BRAND PURPOSE._

Disclosure: This section has the empirical “evidence” of one
person. 😊

I hold two contradicting thoughts in my head.

You’ve noticed my deep suspicion of _brand purpose _declarations by
companies. My experience is that all it is is _power washing_.

I am also a sucker for companies that truly believe in and walk their
_brand purpose _talk.
I buy brands EB mentions Ben & Jerry’s, Patagonia, Seventh
Generation, Newman’s Own, because of their _brand purpose._

Each has a competitor that is cheaper/bigger/more assortment. I still,
easily, choose purposeful brands.

In each case, the thing that connects with me is not their latest
advertising campaign or posters on their stores (which DO NOT brag
about their _brand purpose_).

It is this: DECADES OF WALKING THE TALK.

I can see them living their purpose in how they behave re products,
sourcing vendors, founders protesting (when relevant), aligned
charities (if relevant), and how they behave when no one is looking
(ex: the emails they send, the balance of company selfishness vs.
content value for me).

And, a brand’s purpose does not have to be altruistic / social
goody.

From my first Walkman (kids, look it up), I’ve believed
SONY’S _brand purpose_ with exceptional quality and reliability. I
have decades of proof. Sony walks the talk. I am a GIANT Sony
loyalist.

Ditto re DANNER [4]. I love their commitment to the craft of making
exceptional shoes. It eeks out of all dimensions I’ve interacted
with them – starting with the product.

Whenever I can find it, I choose TRADER JOE’S. Their brand purpose:
_The best quality products, at the best everyday prices_.

Did I know that was their _brand purpose_ from their Marketing?
Absolutely not. I Binged it five seconds ago.

I just knew it in my bones what it is.

They walk the talk that much.

EB’s data emphatically proves one thing:

*
If _brand purpose _is just a Marketing shtick… A tiny number of
_customers care to be aware_.

Because every part of your company, every part of your experience,
every dimension of the thoughtfulness of your product or service,
every pore in your business body is NOT oozing that _purpose_. It is
just some Marketers _BSing._ It is just their ads trying to trick
potential customers.
That is what gets you an EB around 90% _who gives a crap_ score from
customers.

You want to do _brand purpose_?

Make it a company existential thing.

Be Sony. Be Costco. Be Traders Joe. Be Patagonia. Be Newman’s.

Walk the talk.

BOTTOM LINE.

Before you react to study results, methodologies are worth obsessing
about.

Mission statements, brand purpose, and ad taglines each play a
different role, for different stakeholders.

If your company wants to _lead with brand purpose_, make sure it is
not just a Marketing shtick.

If that is all it is, Marketing’s time, money, employees, agencies,
offsites, might be profitably directed at creating new _points of
entry_, reducing _deselection barriers_, or Victoria’s suggestion of
_increasing mental and physical availability_.

Carpe diem.
