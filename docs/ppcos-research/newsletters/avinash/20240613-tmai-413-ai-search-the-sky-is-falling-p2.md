TMAI #413: AI SEARCH: THE SKY IS FALLING!? -P2

[ Web Version [2] ]

In trying to create a sense of urgency for action, I used this
slightly edited version of a Tom Fishburne cartoon in a recent
presentation...

[Tom's original version [3], had the word COVID-19 on the wrecking
ball.]
As I write this edition of TMAI, the edited cartoon feels only
slightly hyperbolic.

In TMAI #412 [4], I'd shared the changes Search Engines are
implementing as they embrace AI to power their Search platforms.

At one end of this spectrum is_ _old PageRank Google_ _- with its blue
links and an operating model of providing results you can use to
figure out answers. This dominates Planet Earth today.

At the other end of the spectrum is Preplexity - with its default
choice of as much as possible providing one answer to the user intent
and an operating model of directly answering the question.

Somewhere in the middle is the new Hybrid Google using a mix of the
new LLM power of Gemini AND the freshness and factuality of old
PageRank Google - to provide a mix of an answer, PPC ads and organic
Search links.

We covered PPC implications in detail in TMAI #412 [4] - change in
the User Experience of ads, and how ads will be triggered (Quality
Score next gen).

I'd also shared three Organic Search challenges:

O1. Change in methodology (tech powering Search).
O2. Change to the UX (the purpose of Search).
O3. Change in User Behavior.
[There was a fourth hidden one, but let me make it explicit: O4. Data
that provides visibility about what's going on, what does
the _intelligent "agent"__ _understand about us (company)?]

We don't know enough about O1 apart from surface-level stuff, _it's
using LLMs _or _it is using GenAI_. I'm going to leave that alone
today - I dislike unintelligent speculation.

AI Search presents a potential grave threat to your company's Organic
traffic > revenue > profits. It is optimal, for now, to worry and
focus on O2 and O3.__

O2: CHANGE TO THE UX | THE SWITCH FROM "RESULTS" TO "ANSWER."

If you pause and think about it, for 20 years, little has changed in
terms of WHAT Search Engines provide.

You and I type in keywords, and we are delivered a combination of
three to four ads and, the famous, _ten blue links._

Yes, the _one box _would often appear on the right side with a
direct answer. Sometimes, questions you can click on appear under the
fist Organic result. But. The underlying purpose of the Engine
remained:

_Here are the most relevant OTHER places for your query, please go
there._
In an underappreciated irony, _searching _for Users started after they
had done the search and received the results.

This is not to imply that Search Engines have been twiddling their
thumbs for 20 years. No. Every single month, Google, Yandex, Seznam
improved the quality of the Search results with a focus on relevance.
Their goal was for us to click less back and forth.

I'm simply saying, the foundational business was the same:_ Provide
Search Results._

Companies pushing the edge on AI Search are in a different
business: _Provide an answer._

Not answer_s_. Answer. One.

Their goal: Eliminate the need for _searching. _(The back and forth
_ten blue links - _AKA you figure it out).

Perplexity [5] is at the forefront of this _answer _model.

The other day, on LinkedIn [6], on my post on AI Search, someone
shared a query they had just done: "_What is the best way to get from
Bristol to Clarkson's Farm_" As a non-native to the UK, I did not
understand what this was.

This real query turns out to be a great way to illustrate challenge
O2, and the foundational shift in Search,
from _results _to _answer.  _Old Bing on the left, Perplexity on
the right...

[All images are high resolution. Right-mouse click, choose Open Image
in a New Tab, review all the details.]

How bad is the old _results__ _Search model!

Especially contrasted with Perplexity's _answer _model!!

They actually had so much space left after giving the answer, they
answered questions I would have _later_:_ _When to Visit, What to
Expect, etc.

Irony:  Perplexity has some custom LLMs, but it is mostly using other
people’s LLMs (!). Simply building a new User Experience is such a
lovely way to be valued at $3 billion dollars. :) I sincerely
congratulate the team at Perplexity.

I’m getting side tracked.

Back to the story.

I'll plant a worry:

What happens to your company if all Engines switch to AI Search's
_answer _model?
The _answer _model does not always mean just one result from one
company. The UX can take different shape based on expressed intent.

As an example, if someone did an _old school keywords _search without
clear intent, you might see _answers_, but in a different
experience...

Searching for _best cross body bag _in the old model gives me what I
expected: Around 10 ads at the top (which I know is not the literal
answer to my query _best_), and, above the fold, five or seven more
links to do my _searching._

In the _answer _model, I get answers. Ranked. My _searching _could
be done on the results page, I can accept the first recommendation, or
scroll and read the others. This is the important part: There is one
answer at the top, and I could be done. [Even in scenarios
of _zero-click searches, _common on Google today, this query would
not end on Google with _zero-clicks._]

If Search Engines are switching from _results _to _answer_…. What
are the chances that you’ll be THE _answer_?

Worrying, no?

We can’t “hack” with “10 Proven SEO Shortcuts,” get to the
fourth Organic link (for a few days/weeks) and pray some Users will
come to us.

What now?

Action: I suppose we will just have to build really great products and
services that people love and talk about. That’ll result in the
LLMs choosing us as the _answer_.

Action: For my clients, we are also solving for the _talk
about _part with specific tactics based on the _social built-in_
[7] philosophy, and solving for _content _through the full lens of
See AND Think AND Do AND Care (vs. the common tactic of just the Do
lens).

Action: Of course, also wait and see how Paid Search options evolve
and leverage them (aggressively if we won't do the two actions above -
both hard).

There are other search results page problems to deal with.

In early iterations of AI Overviews, Google has put the AI Overviews
answer first, then a PPC ad. Often, it is AI Overview, then some
Organic answer, then, below the fold (!) PPC ads. [See TMAI #412.]

I so, so, so, worry that numerous companies will lose lots of their
PPC traffic at the same time an _answer _model will cause them to
substantially lose their Organic traffic.

We can't predict how fast this model will shift, only Google, Bing,
Seznam know. My belief is that it is coming, and coming soon.

NOTE: For those of you who hate getting an _answer _and want to do
all the _searching _yourself because you'll _never trust Google_...
I believe you'll likely always have that choice, especially if you are
not a cohort of just 50k people. My prediction is that the trust model
in place today will change profoundly in the next five years, and
shift to the intelligent machine. If you are a business relying on
most of the Users to be _give me ten links, I'll help myself because
I don't trust Google... _I worry you'll likely be querying Chapter
11.

O3. CHANGE IN USER BEHAVIOR. | THE SWITCH TO _TRUE INTELLIGENCE_.

If you pause and think about it, for 20 years little has changed about
HOW humans search.

We type in two or five or six _keywords_ into a box, the Search Engine
delivers a page full of links.

The base assumption since Alta Vista, for me, for nearly all of us,
has been:

_There’s a kind of smart thing at the other end._
We learned clues to make the two or five or six keywords better, to
make the smart thing give us relevant Results.

[True story: In Verona last week, my wife gets on her phone to find us
a good restaurant, but after a few minutes nothing popped. I then
tried and got the perfect restaurant in 30 seconds. The difference?
She typed in “restaurants,” and I’d learned that Google Maps
works better when you type in “restaurant.” How insane? Oh, and
she had just typed “res” and “restaurants” was the first
suggestion! I’d learned to ignore it. That, in a nutshell, is how
humans learned to work with _a kind of smart thing at the other end_.]

Search Engines HAVE gotten better and better at
interpretability: _What might the human actually mean with these two
or four or six words?_

Outcome: A. Humans stayed rudimentary (keyword-land). B. Engines
improved at interpretation, delivering ever more relevant _ten blue
links._

With AI Search, something deep is changing regarding A and B.

AI Search is being powered by something far, far, far more intelligent
than the “PageRank Tech.” It is intelligent LLMs for now (and in a
few years AGI).

Realizing that, the small number of humans using AI Search are
building a new habit:

Unshackling themselves from the _keyword keyword keyword
keyword _yoke, and asking more complex, multi-layered, _end
point _questions.

[NOTE: I’m not implying Old Search lacks intelligence. It does. I'm
using the word _interpretability _to sharpen the difference with AI
Search, where the multi-modal _intelligence _at play is a whole new
ball game.]

As the right side of the picture above goes from
_interpretability _to _intelligence_, my behavior as a human changes
in fundamental ways. And, it presents a small threat to you or HP or
Michael Kors or Nespresso today, and a significant threat ahead.

Let me illustrate using an example.

Instead of my typical _kw kw kw kw _query _BEST LEATHER CROSS BODY
BAG_, which would yield this very normal Old Search set of results
with relevant _ten blue links _telling me to go help myself…

I know that with AI Search there is an _intelligent __agent_ at the
other end. So, I can throw at it the real problem I need solved, a
present for my daughter. I type something far more complex:

_WHAT’S THE BEST CROSS-BODY BAG FOR A MASTERS STUDENT AT NYU, WITH
AN UNDERSTATED SENSE OF STYLE?_
I expect the _intelligent __agent _to understand the complex clues in
“Masters student,” in “NYU,” and in “an understated sense of
style.” [Ponder that last one.]

It does, giving me an _answer _vs. _search results..._

BOOM!

An answer. One.

New user behavior, using new search, powered by _intelligence._

I'll plant a worry:

If you were showing up in the _ten blue links _plus _four or ten PPC
ads... _What happens to your traffic > revenue > profits now?
Bing CoPilot has also been on the innovation train over the last year
(and hence my default Search Engine). Their UX is on a slightly
different path compared to Perplexity. But, Bing is also firmly in
the _answer _operating model...

Unlike in the _results _operating model. I search, and then there is
no more _searching _by me. There's an answer, an _intelligent
_answer.

I would have never typed my more complex real question into Old
Search. Because something built for _interpretability_ cannot
understand what I'm saying, and will fail me.

See below, see above...

See what I mean? Compare it to how Bing or Perplexity handled my
complex question.

Does this mean Google sucks? No, no, no. It is working exactly as
designed. It is built for _keyword keyword keyword keyword, _and uses
_interpretability _to provide _results _so that I can continue
_searching_. It is working exactly as designed for that human.

This also explains why humanity's default for searching is _keyword
keyword keyword keyword._

It simply won't be true, slowly, from now on. Humans will understand
there's _true intelligence _at the other end and change their
behavior - which is good for them and a threat to your profits.

IMPORTANT: If I use the _intelligent _version of Google vs. the
_interpretability _version of Google, I get search results similar to
Bing CoPilot. If I use Old Bing for my complex_ _query, I get results
very similar to the sub-optimal ones as Old Google immediately above.

In my experience, the _intelligent _version of Google (Gemini) is
still early in evolution. At the moment it often delivers results of
inferior quality...

It would be a stretch for anyone to consider the Bellroy as a
_cross-body bag, _it is a waist pouch. Also, the "eco conscious,"
"organized," "stylish" feel odd and irrelevant, when I was clear my
priority was _an understated sense of style_.

Gemini is new. You can count on the sun to rise tomorrow, with just as
much certainty you can count on Gemini to become better and better and
better.

When I use AI Search, there’s more expectation re the complexity
stuffed into my questions.

With AI Search, there’s a mental reset.

I really want you to get that.

Stay with me.

I anticipate humans will not go to Search and type more keywords to
do _searching. _They will go to Search, type in
complex _end-point _questions, and expect an _answer__._

The evolution will NOT be from _oh, I'm typing some keywords
_to _let me type more keywords._

So. No more _best running shoes for high arches_.

Not even this version of that Search to keep on _searching_...

Because that only leads to this... More _searching_...

Instead of _HOW TO PICK, _realizing that they have an intelligent
_agent _at the other end, they can simply do an _end-point _search:

_WHICH is the best men's running shoe for beginners with high arches__
_

This shift from _how _to _which_ is key to internalize, because for
the user that leads to the end of _search _and to an _end-point..._

The above question's result and experience is even better on
Perplexity. Try it.

Prediction to worry you: Today, we may trust word of mouth, or we may
trust ourselves to come to an answer after 8 minutes or 8 hours of
research. As _intelligence _improves (LLMs > AGI), I believe there
will be a _transfer of trust _(to the _intelligent agent_). Because,
it is so good already, and will be mind-blowingly so soon enough.

Ponder that for the longer term, re implications.

Back to today. In the short-term, this _transfer of trust _manifest
itself in even more _end-point _search queries, and an increased
preference for an _answer _vs. _results._

I cannot emphasize this enough:

This is good for Users. I've been So Excited about the better that's
already here.

This is also a profound challenge for companies - you see there are
17,000 shoe companies, how do you get into the top result (Perplexity
model) or the top four (Bing CoPilot, Gemini, model)?
Action: 1. Inside your company, you have to have a swat team that is
immediately assessing the implications of the shift in User behavior
that O3 implies above. 2. Scroll back up, read the actions recommended
for O2, they will help you tremendously for O3 (for now).

BOTTOM LINE.

My hope with these two TMAI editions is to illuminate just how
profound of a change AI Search is bringing, create excitement in you
as a User, and a minor freak out that yours could be among many
companies likely to lose a lot of traffic, revenue, and profit.

As you reflect on the challenges, Organic or PPC, do so in three
clusters: Methodology. User Experience. User Behavior. This simple
framing will help you see with clarity.

Then, get your Agency & company to activate accelerated action.
