TMAI #393: ORGANIC SOCIAL | SURPRISING SOURCES OF VALUE -P2

[ Web Version [2] ]

Traditional Organic Social only seems effective in isolation of all
other Marketing activity.

In TMAI #392 [3], we did an in-depth reflection on Organic Social
dynamics, starting with a reflection on non-vanity metrics.

Like so, for one of my fav British brands Marks & Spencer...

Thought narrowly from the lens of the impact of _traditional Organic
Social_ on _Organic Social KPIs_, the scale of the impact,
performance (brand lift, sales), shrinks to negligible.

[NOTE: A lot of your Social Media performance data is public. It will
take you five minutes to replicate my analysis above for your
company's Organic Social activity. I recommend taking the five
minutes, I promise illumination.]

In #392, you discovered that the problem in part is the Social Media
(SM) platforms restricting reach – to all those Followers you spend
cash accumulating -, and the monumental shift from the Social Graph to
the Content Graph.

[Premium members: TMAI #344: Bye Bye Social Graph [4]. [4]]

The problem in, larger, part is the deeply unappealing content
companies share: Product news, sales/promotions, “gen Z tone
content,” attempts at humor, videos, and generic replies to negative
comments or frenetic requests for tech support.

When you get 27 Likes, it is one of many signs that not even your
company employees are willing to make you feel better about your
Organic Social content.

BUT!

If you, your Agency, your CMO, is willing to think differently...
There are use cases that hold immense promise in delivering
incremental business value – by impacting the entirety of the rest
of your marketing portfolio.

That sounds exciting, no?

These use cases take advantage of the unique combination of the size
of the audience on SM, their unique content consumption pattern, and
how influence flows.

In TMAI #392 [3], I’d shared the first use case, using Organic
Social to as an experimentation engine for your internal team. Please
review, if you've not had an opportunity.

Today, let’s look at two additional major use cases for Organic
Social investment and five slightly smaller ones. Each of the 2 +5,
done well, provide significantly higher value than siloed traditional
Organic Social activity.

All you need... Thinking differently, thinking portfolio impact.

UC #2. EXPERIMENTATION ENGINE FOR CREATIVE, INFLUENCERS.

Nearly always, the only person who gets rich off Influencer Marketing
[5] is the Influencer.

You don’t have to take my word for it. The data is public. The KPIs
are the same as the ones shared above: Amplification Rate,
Conversation Rate, Applause Rate, Economic Value.

Find your posts by the last Influencer you gave money to. Note the
data. Do the computation above for Marks & Spencer. I’ll wait.

…

See what I mean?

Little impact of note, apart from possibly a vanity boost.

Sad face.

Like me, you’ll discover two exclusions to the _OMG Influencer
Marketing delivers no ROI at scale_:

E1. Small businesses. Sometimes Influencer Marketing “works” for
them – because on a small business scale, some impact can still be
material. Your problem is, you are a medium or large company.

E2. Outliers. The Kardashians are outliers. There are others. Chances
of you being an outlier? Zero. I'm sorry.

It is super crucial, as an Influencer, I emphasize this:

INFLUENCERS ARE GREAT.
They have built their brands by activating innovative, creative
tactics, consistently. They are incredibly in tune with their
audiences, their content is super smart hard work, and a lot of them a
deeply analytical. I have unabashed admiration for them. Every single
Influencer, micro or macro, richly deserves their success!

Traditional Influencer Marketing of the _let me pay you to make one
Instagram post or five Tiktok posts next month _variety does not
work for two reasons practitioners of Strategic Analytics are familiar
with:

A. The Organic reach of Influencers is also limited by the SM
platform, just as it is for your corporate page. Except, high-follower
count Influencers might be at 7% and you at 0.7% (or less).

B. The execution model for your Influencer Marketing strategy is Spike
and Silence - a recipe for failure across all media channels on planet
Earth.

[Premium members: TMAI #386: Brand Marketing’s Secret Formula:
Spike and Sustain! [6]]

But. Wait.

There is one incredibly impactful use of Influencers:

Get them to tell your stories, in a way that you never can or will.

Your aim is to solve for building impactful Creative across all media
channels!

Hand your Creative concept, your brand marketing campaign's purpose,
your product, your competitive insights. Set them free. [Sure, give
them a little list of your brand safe guidelines.]

Measure results.

On the Influencer’s channel/s.

Unless you are doing something wrong, the Influencers will invariably
come back with an expression of your brand, that will be new, novel,
and, often, more effective (than yours).

For a simple reason:

They do not need to think _out of the box_ like your Creative team…
The Influencer/s are already outside the box.

Influencers frequently come back with product attributes we never
highlighted, or cared for.

They will routinely come back with telling my brand’s story in a way
that I would not have thought of.

They will use imagery / video in ways that often we might not [because
we don’t want to, or we might not be aware of].

They will routinely find ways to connect to an audience in a way that
we can’t because every Influencer worth anything invests so much in
staying insanely in tune with their audience [for them, it is a matter
of social media survival, or irrelevance!].

It is worth appreciating that while this feels risky, it really is
not.

All this new expression of your creative voice will be in a limited
channel, with low organic reach. [It is significantly bigger than the
focus group of 18 your corporate Creative agency/team are using.]

The upside is immense.

When results show that Influencer built Creative concepts or
executions are effective [hey, maybe one even goes viral!], ship them
over to the teams executing your digital media first. Measure results.
Then, ship them over to the teams executing your offline media
strategies. Measure results.

For brands who want to stay relevant, who wish to infuse their comms
strategy, their brand values, with outside-in thinking at scale…
Influencer Marketing is a high-value choice.

Oh, and there will, of course, be some minimal impact from this
Influencer activity on Organic Social… But, it will be 500x to 1000x
less than the value the Influencer will deliver with impactful
creative you can use as a part of your Paid Media strategy on Hulu,
Facebook, Television, Billboards, Radio, etc.

Please keep in mind, 60% of the success of *all* campaigns is the
Creative. Give Influencers the power to help you move that 60% -
everywhere.

[BONUS RECOMMENDATION: An additional strategy to increase ROI from
Influencer Marketing budgets is to incorporate Influencers into all
other Marketing strategies. Your TV ads. Your Events. Your billboards.
Your webinars. Your YouTube video ads. Your landing pages. Your site
product demos. Your top 1% customer engagements. You catch my drift.
The shift is from Influencer in a bubble, to (proven) Influencers
across the entire portfolio can deliver magical profits. Another
example of shifting from _spike and silence_ to _spike and sustain_.]

UC #3. ORGANIC SOCIAL TO POWER PAID BRAND SOCIAL.

It is relatively straightforward to assess if your YouTube, Facebook,
TikTok performance marketing campaigns are delivering incremental
results. Do Conversion Lift Studies, all those channels offer them –
for free.

If this is the first time you’ve looked at Performance Social from
an incrementality results lens, it will turn out the results are
rather poor (less than 5% incrementality). I’ll join you in crying
a little, 5% does hurt my feelings too. Then, we’ll go fix it. Call
Croud [7], if you need help.

The bottom line is that it is not difficult to know your Performance
Social is sucking royally.

[PERFORMANCE SOCIAL: Advertising expected to deliver in-month Revenue,
Leads, Profit.]

[PAID BRAND SOCIAL: Marketing expected to deliver medium to long-term
lifts in KPIs such as Unaided Brand Awareness (unprompted, _I’ve
heard of you_), Consideration (y_ou are in my top three choices_),
Intent (_y__ou are my #1 choice_).]

It is takes effort to realize your Paid Brand Social is sucking
royally.

Surveys take a long time to arrive. Often you refuse to measure my
three standard metrics above and cook up metrics like “_Brand
Love,_” which are impossible to measure cleanly. It takes so much
effort to tie anything Brand to business results. _Yada, Yada, Yada._

But.

When you do measure your Paid Brand Social spend cleanly, using KPIs I
recommend above due to their Financial impact, you’ll discover that
your Paid Brand Social marketing does indeed fall well, well, short of
delivering results.

A causal factor will turn out to be that your Paid Brand Social
consists of “FAKE PERFORMANCE ADS.” They are “buy now” vs.
“BUY NOW!!!!!” Still, they are performance ads. I can’t save you
from this. _Fish don’t climb trees._

It also ties to the guidance the TikTok sales team gives:_ Make
TikToks, not ads_. It is very, very, very hard for companies to do
this.

So.

Use Organic Social – on your own corporate pages/channels or those
of your Paid Influencers – to experiment and figure out how to make
_TikToks_ that achieve superior results.

Then. Spend Paid Brand Social budgets to amplify them to Mount Everest
levels!

This will transform your Organic Social Agency/team’s reason for
existence: Not Organic Social Success. Rather, _superpowering _Paid
Social results!

From 27 Likes per Post and 15 Reshares, to +3 Lift in Consideration,
1.2 million Individuals Lifted, at a Cost Per Individual Lifted of
$2.5. Magnificent!

From 2000 Likes per Post and 150 Reshares, to $12 million in
Incremental Revenue and $3 mil in Profit. OMG!

The Organic Social team/Agency's job will change. They will now obsess
about figuring out audience, targeting, creative (tone, values,
expression, more), frequency, patterns in comments, insights from
reshares, and a million more things to transform media planning,
delivery, and creative for Paid Brand Social.

Measure results.

Rinse, repeat.

In my experience, the impact is nothing short of transformative.

The Paid Brand Social team gets a real_ come to Jesus_, as does the
Brand Agency executing your considerable brand budget [in complete
isolation from your Organic Social Agency/team]. They learn invaluable
new tricks about how to _make TikToks_, not ads. They also spend money
amplifying tactics already proven at a smaller scale on Organic Social
– higher guarantee they’ll show results when scaled.

Delivering this will require change to your business existence. Some
of this will be challenging. You need to end up with:

One internal Social team. With Organic Social leading, responsible and
accountable for the Paid Social budget. (WHAT! Yes.)

One Social Agency. The Organic Social one, assuming they have
capabilities and intelligence to execute Paid campaigns. (HONESTLY?
Yes.)
There are at least two more parties involved today. They leave the
scene.

The path to glorious Social glory.

UC #4. ORGANIC SOCIAL: MINOR USE CASES, FOR MORE THAN MINOR IMPACT.

If for some business, or company culture, reason you are unable to
global maxima your Organic Social time/people/budget/agency to use
cases #1, #2, #3, I wanted to share a handful of smaller
recommendations.

Each of these narrow the use of Organic Social (often, reducing the
time/people/budget). But in each case the shift in strategy will
deliver provably higher ROI from Organic Social – and just might
teach the company, _an old dog, new tricks_.

4A. ORGANIC SOCIAL AS A PRACTICE FIELD FOR INTERNS.

This is simply saying:

If nearly no one – in context of all other Marketing activity reach
– is going to experience anything we post, why not do it at the
lowest possible cost AND with employees who through freedom of
experimentation might just teach us a few small things.
Turn your Organic Social feeds to Interns or employees in entry-level
roles.

Humans in those two categories are likely _young in age_ (Interns)
and/or any age but _young in experience_ of your company (new
entry-level employees). I’ve come to value the freshness of the
perspective they bring, the fact that they are not brainwashed yet by
company culture and norms.

Through their Organic Social activity, you just might learn how to
_make TikToks, not ads_. [A concept that applies to all Social
channels, not just TT.]

You might learn about frequency.

You might learn how to respond humanly to haters on your
channel/page.

You might learn how to express a corporate sense of humor – among
other dimensions of creativity.

And, more.

Depending on your company culture’s ability to listen and learn, the
benefits will atleast be felt in Marketing tactics, with the potential
to be medium-sized impact on outcomes.

4B. ORGANIC SOCIAL AS TECH SUPPORT.

You only have to go to a random Facebook page of a brand to recognize
that, regardless of their wishes, their customers are looking for tech
support on Facebook.

Most companies post extremely generic replies, like: _Please reach out
to our team at 1-800-confusing-phone-trees.__
_
Over the last five years, there have been dramatic improvements in
chat software that allow for productive conversations. Another
evolution is that there are a raft of employees at our companies who
are Discord, iMessage, Whatsapp first (unlike old people like me).

The combination of those two means that Social platforms could be a
viable mechanism to provide tech support to your customers, in their
first moment of truth.

The challenge will be your SVP for Customer Support, which is likely
an old person like me. She/he might need to be persuaded (nudged?).

I do need to persuade you on the impact on the LTV and Advocacy KPIs,
if you get this right.

4C. ORGANIC SOCIAL AS A WAY TO BUILD A LOYAL COMMUNITY OF LOVERS.

Speaking of LTV… One of the flaws with Organic Social execution is
that your company is trying to get to the entire generic slather of
people on the platform/s.

That makes it extraordinarily difficult to know what content to post,
what one or two segments are we talking to, where to source stories
and tactics that might stick. Our Organic Social often ends up being
generic slather.

For many companies, their existing customers are on Social as well. In
those cases, why not narrowly focus the entirety of the Organic Social
efforts on just the extra-loyal customers?

[DEFINITION: Extra-loyal customers: Those who have purchased from us
two or more times.]

With that narrow lens, content, frequency, level of personalization,
engagement in comments, everything gets simplified.

In these cases, from my experience, the following works:

1. INFORM ME. 2. ENTERTAIN ME. 3. PROVIDE UTILITY.
You’ll still have to break through the limitations of organic reach,
but as the community builds around you, as the platform notices higher
engagement, you will break through those shackles (just as it happens
for effective Influencers).

I recommend the ownership of this stay in the Marketing team (vs.
sometimes Support or Customer Care).

4D. ORGANIC SOCIAL AS A WAY TO PRE-TEST EMAIL BRAND STORIES.

Email Marketing is the most underappreciated strategy for brand
building.

It has been so profoundly taken over by I need to hit my revenue
targets, all companies can do is spam me to death.

[AN EXPRESSION OF ANGST: Look, there are always 0.002% of your mailing
list who can be badgered into placing an order because you sent them 8
emails per day for 15 days straight, and threw in a 20% coupon and
finally free shipping too. It is unlikely that you are earning any
loyalty, you shot to death your profit margin, and you are teaching
your customers to wait 15 days and a coupon will show up. Still, as
your SVP for Digital will remind me: _More revenue_. My plea to him:
_Yes, at what cost?_]

Since companies are going to send multiple emails per week… Why not
leverage them for brand marketing?

Top half: BUY OUT LATEST PRODUCT NOW! Hopefully said with taste.

Bottom half of the email: A story about our brand.

I have so many ideas from success with clients: Our latest brand
anthem. A story about a customer. A novel use of our product. A
helpful article about how to solve problem x. We donate 1% of sales to
charity, talk about the impact. A video of our CEO talking about the
hard decision to increase prices. Partnerships with other companies.
Ideation behind the latest concept of our creative.

And, where to practice, to check, which of these ideas are likely to
resonate? Organic Social. :)

And, if you do 4a and 4c, the learnings and impact will be
turbocharged!

4E. ORGANIC SOCIAL AS THE _SUSTAIN COMPONENT_ OF YOUR _SPIKES._

An advanced strategy.

If you are willing to spend money on amplifying your better performing
Organic Social posts AND plot that strategy with your Paid Media
plans… You can use Organic Social amplification as a way to build
frequency during the Sustain component of your media plans.

Allow me to explain.

It is Valentine’s day. I’m the jeweler David Yurman. [I love the
Crossover Cuff bracelet [8]!]

Leading up to v-day and closing out on the 14th, I run a Marketing
campaign to drive sales. A classic SPIKE. A large media spend, to
drive brand KPIs and Sales.

If I go silent on the 14th, any brand KPI gains I’ve made will
disappear in less than a week.  People have lives to live. My KPIs
will fall back to baselines.

To prevent this, we do a SUSTAIN phase of the campaign. Much less
money. Lower frequency. Narrower audience (just the media audience for
the spike). But. Consistent drumbeat to retain brand gains, and then
build on top with the next spike.

Organic Social could play an effective role in the sustain phase of
the campaign, to supplement the frequency your sustain paid media is
trying to build. The only requirement is that your media targeted
audience is also on Social (often, highly likely) AND you need to
allocate some of that paid budget to your Organic Social team.

The immense benefit is you are building frequency not just using your
paid assets, rather using signals for Organic Social content that is
resonating – improving effectiveness and efficiency!

[NOTE: Call me if this feels a bit complex. It is. But. Not more than
other bits you are doing. And. Highly profitable.]

BOTTOM LINE.

Seemingly every day, there is fresh data [9] that identifies how
little traditional Organic Social matters.

I now don't care. And, you should not.

Within the limitations the platforms and their ever-changing
algorithms impose, we can execute successful strategies to convert
Organic Social into a competitive advantage. It only requires letting
go of "traditional," and boldly execute use cases 1, 2, 3, and 4 a -
e.

Profits await.

Carpe diem!
