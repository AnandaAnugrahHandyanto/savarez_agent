TMAI #428:  DON’T SLEEP ON TIKTOK.

[ Web Version [2] ]

An SVP of Marketing wrote:

_Any brands still sleeping on TikTok need to wake up._
_
"This is the first time we've experienced something like this" -
Iceland as they run out of cucumbers._
_
I'll admit it, I've made the TikTok cucumber salad._
_
TikTok's global reach and ability to hold attention and change
behaviour is only growing. Especially as we get better at using it._
_
Sure, marketers can still just use other channels. But on TikTok, the
only barrier to entry is creativity. The right talent and strategy can
be a game changer._
_
That's why it's a brilliant channel - it makes us value creativity. A
channel where brands must entertain for commercial gain, exactly like
effective TV advertising._
They included this image:

[BBC Iceland Cucumber Story.]

This type of advice is amplified all the time on the socials, in
conference keynotes, and trade publications.

Before you jump to action, allow me to share a simple thinking model
that will help you save a ton of grief (and help you save profits).

NEW IDEAS. BE OPEN TO THEM.

Let me acknowledge the macro message from Mx. SVP:

You should be open to new and emerging ideas.

You should evaluate those ideas to see if they could be opportunities
for your business.
This is good. Even holy.

Don’t just talk, walk the talk.

Consider having a healthy 5%, 10%, of your Marketing budget dedicated
to _trying things._

The ONLY requirement I impose: Ensure as much signal instrumentation
as possible.

This simple action will help you learn with purposeful clarity if
something worked or it did not. Without signal instrumentation, having
10% of your Marketing budget dedicated to _trying things_ will at some
point blow up in your face if the choice is _we’ll figure out after
the fact what the point was and if we learned anything_. Not a good
thing.

I am FOR _let’s experiment_, knowing in advance _what we are trying
to learn._

OUTLIERS ARE RARELY THE NORM.

You all know the normal distribution [3].

Broadly, most data points in the middle, some right next to that on
the left and on the right, and a data point or two on the far-left and
far-right – these are the outliers [4].

Outliers tend to be “weird,” in that they are one time shooting
stars. Unnatural occurrences. Difficult to predict. And, often, even
difficult to understand.

Ex: There are humans who can free dive (single breath) to 132 meters
[5]! An outlier. They are absolutely worth celebrating, their rarity
to be awed by. But. For the rest of us, the norm, there is little to
learn from them or activate because 7 billion of us will fail to free
dive two meters!

That is the problem to appreciate about the cucumbers.

It is an outlier, but it is being framed as a norm.

If you act, the predicted outcome is unlikely to occur - and deliver
sadness and loss of profit instead.

How could you have figured out you were being misdirected via this
switcheroo?

Consider that it was only in Iceland. If it were the norm, cucumbers
would have run out in 20 out of 200 countries. (Even then, honestly,
not a norm.)

Consider that it was just cucumber. If there is something to emulate
here, you would have examples that created runs on tomatoes, peas,
sweet potatoes etc. Even stronger _norm signal: _You notice that some
countries are similarly running out of Chicken or cordless
screwdrivers, and you would go… _Wait! Something’s going on here
that we can apply to our business of selling Car Insurance / shoes /
MMMs!_

Consider that of hundreds of thousands of creators
(sorry, _influencers_) are hawking all kinds of products and
services… Just this Creator, Logan Moffitt, who absolutely needs to
be admired and praised, is selling out cucumbers in one country,
but… It is just him. No one else. That’s a tell.

One last giveaway of the _outlier being presented as a norm_: Mx. SVP
did not link to it, but your curiosity would take you to the story on
the BBC [6]. There, you would have read Icelandic Supermarket food
product manager, Vignir Þór Birgisson, share that at this time of
the year, there is usually a cucumber shortage in Iceland!

See. That was not as hard.

Well-meaning _experts _can end up causing us to take unhealthy
actions.

It constantly happens. Proceed with curiosity and caution.

The famous Oreo Super Bowl tweet, an outlier. The excellent Ocean
Spray cranberry TikTok, an outlier. The Pepsi (sad) moment, an
outlier.

In all instances, the one viral moment was followed by a considerable
increase in Social Spend internally and with Agencies. Close your
eyes. Clear your mind. Can you recall anything by any of these
companies on any platforms?

Outliers are not norms.

IDENTIFYING NORMS / NOT GETTING MISDIRECTED BY OUTLIERS.

So, how can you protect yourself when your CMO asks you to activate an
outlier they heard about at Cannes from one of their buddies during
the dinner on a nice boat?

Apply the THREE LAYERS OF COMMON-SENSE test:

Test Layer #1: Can we stress test if this an outlier, or the norm?

See above.

Go through the analysis, and identify if it is a norm.

As an example, a decade or so ago I’d shared that there are only
three things that work on Social Media with consistency, when it comes
to companies:

_INFORM ME. ENTERTAIN ME. PROVIDE UTILITY._

I’d distilled this from an analysis of a ton of companies, and
noting my Best Social Media Metrics: Amplification Rate, Conversation
Rate, Applause Rate. Economic Value.

Does your example, idea, Social Agency proposal pass the outlier or
norm test?

If no, stop. Save yourself a lot of grief/profits. If yes, awesome.
Proceed to the next layer.
Test Layer #2: Can our industry take advantage of this
tactic/strategy?

For the above three things that commonly are in the norm clusters, not
all of them can apply to every industry.

As an example, and I’m not poking at the Engineers, I am one, it
would be difficult for the SAAS industry to take advantage of
Entertain Me. I’m sure one of you is going to write to me with an
outlier. :) I’m sure there is one. In Estonia. It is just not the
norm.

But.

The SAAS industry can TOTALLY take advantage of _Inform Me_ and
_Provide Utility_. I offer that _Provide Utility_ is a massive
opportunity, given that an entire generation is over indexing on_ da
socials._

It is unlikely that the airline industry can take advantage of 75% of
the ideas that fit into _Provide Utility_ in terms of Social success
– their feeds are clogged with customer complaints!

Can the Car industry take advantage of _Inform Me_? Unlikely.

Does the example, idea, Social Agency proposal pass the applicability
test to our industry?

If no, stop. Save yourself significant profits. If yes, great. Proceed
to the next layer.
Test Layer #3: Can our company take advantage of this tactic/strategy?

This is common sense test is key.

Would it work for you?

Something that works for Dr. Pepper, might not work for Coca-Cola.

Mx. SVP in recommendation at the top of this note says: “_…on
TikTok, the only barrier to entry is creativity._”

That is not a barrier… That is a gap as wide as the distance from
where you are sitting to the moon!

Creativity is the rarest of the rare quality to find.

Try this: What was the last creative thing you remember seeing from
Google? Close your eyes for just 30 seconds?

Try it. Anything?

How about Nike? Marks & Spencer Christmas ad, remember it? How about
BYD? Sony? L’Oreal Paris?

Three of these brands I even LOVE.

But I can’t recall a single piece of creative they’ve done in the
past six months. And, all of these brands have Marketing budgets in
the HUNDREDS OF MILLIONS (even a few billion)  dollars. In that,
they are spending tens of millions, if not more, on creative.

Yet. _Kaput._

Not everyone is capable of everything others in the industry are
capable of.

I love the copywriting in Oatly ads. I can’t think of two other
companies who can emulate Oatly.

That’s what I want you to stress-test.

Even if the industry can do it… Can you do it?

Do you have the right culture?

Do you have the right people?

Do you have the right permissions?

Do you have a hyper-agile operating model?

If you do, YES! GO activate the shiny thing!!

If not… Don’t do anything… Why embarrass your brand (and waste
precious budget)?
There are brands that pass all three layers of _common sense_ test.
INNOCENT DRINKS. CHIPOTLE on Roblox or Tiktok. PATAGONIA on YouTube.
KLM on FB. COACH on WeChat. You'll note none are outliers because
you'll notice they all share patterns with each other!

BOTTOM LINE.

You all remember my fav quote: _It’s not the ink, it’s the think._

Today’s newsletter is one more reminder to focus on the _think_, and
not get distracted by the _ink__._

The three layers test is widely applicable. For any shiny object
thrown your way, for every new why don’t we, I just read x works
from your Agency Innovation Lead… Apply the three layers test, and
it’ll help improve the quality of decisions you make.

Carpe diem, and eat more cucumbers!

-Avinash.

PS: Bonus: Two TikTok Warnings.

Mx. SVP said: “_A channel where brands must entertain for commercial
gain, exactly like effective TV advertising_.”

This statement provides a valuable opportunity to learn two important
lessons before you commit a lot of money on Organic Social on TikTok,
and the money breaks your CFO's heart. Both these lessons apply to all
other Social channels as well.

A.

No one goes to TikTok to watch anything from Brands. Best-case
scenario: Content by Brands is an annoyance. Often, it is a nuisance.
In part because TikTok is a “PULL CHANNEL.”

Television is a “PUSH CHANNEL.” Even better, it is a push channel
with built-in non-skippable ads AND the expectation in humans that TV
= Have To Watch Ads.

In Pull channels, content (which sometimes can be ads) from brands can
have a very hard time being effective. Just look at your Brand Lift
studies or Cost Per metrics.

In Push channels, content from brands (which can be ads) has an easier
time being effective.

It is crucial to appreciate this difference, if you are comparing
Social to TV.

B.

Once upon a time, the Social Graph ruled the roost (thanks Mark). When
you posted something, chances were high it would be amplified for free
to many of your Followers.

TikTok broke the Social Graph (thanks Shou!), it uses the Content
Graph. Unless the piece of creative you are making is OMG INSANELY
VIRAL, nearly no one will see your content.

[Premium Member Bonus: TMAI #344: Bye Bye Social Graph [7]. [7]]

It is massively easier for your ads/content to be effective on TV
because you can simply buy Reach and Frequency, and humans are
pattered to watch ads. If it is a good ad, people will remember it
with a bit lower frequency x duration. If it is a bad ad, you need to
annoy people into remembering it, higher frequency x duration. Either
way. Success.

On TikTok, nearly Zero people will see your Organic content because
the Content Graph will kill any creative that it does not assess as
_viral material._

You can absolutely take your Organic content, make an ad out of it,
and buy Reach and Frequency, just as you would with TV. Unlike TV
though, it is very, very hard to make ads work on Tiktok (pull
channel, plus short ad formats, skippable, and TT's killer fatigue
index).

Look. I’m not saying TV is not dying. It is. Neither am I implying
that the audience on TV is not 30-40 years older than TT. It is.

But. Thinking technically about what delivering success requires (good
creative x frequency x duration x reach)… It is unwise to imply that
“_entertain for commercial gain_” is easy on TikTok, or say
“_exactly like effective TV advertising_.” It is not.
