TMAI #457: AI 2025 | TIME FOR ACTION.

[ Web Version [2] ]

Regardless of source, three broad patterns emerge re AI usage:

*
CATEGORY A: Handful percent of employees are using current AI
solutions to the max.

*
CATEGORY B: 8% - 10%  are using AI a few times a day.

*
CATEGORY C: The rest either once in a while or none at all (both of
which are the same as far as I’m concerned).

These stats used to break my heart.

Now, they freak me out.

AI is progressing at an insanely rapid clip. We are already feeling
the consequences on productivity (up), hiring (down), and acceleration
of business impact (faster). In 2025, Agents are starting to be almost
real, and tentatively smart.

[PREDICTION: In a handful of years, an unbelievable amount of content
will be created by Agents. It will be read by Agents. Agents will be
clicking on ads, and shopping will be done by Agents. Imagine the
consequences!]

If you and I are not in Category B at the very least, I worry we are
going to be left behind. If a company does not have 70% of their
employees in Category B or A (with most in the latter), the company
will be left behind.

You and I cannot be in Category C. I am sorry; it is no longer
feasible.

Not for Sales, not for HR, not for Engineering, not for Customer
Service, not for Product Design, not for Logistics, not for Finance,
not for User Research, not even for the wonderful crew cleaning
offices (remind me to tell you about this new AI robot I saw the other
day).

_Embrace, extend, thrive._

Earlier today, I used Deep Research in ChatGPT and Claude to complete
in half a day _close enough _analysis that had taken a team of
people four months to do. The team’s work had more detail, it was
better. My analysis drew out nuances and dimensions they had not
considered. It was organized and structured far better (likely my
prompting). If the team had started four months ago with that half a
day with LLMs, the project would have been done in six weeks – and
have been stronger.

Category A or Category B. No C.

My 83-year old aunt-in-law in China replies to my WeChats in English
using DeepSeek!

Category A!

I have two tabs constantly open with ChatGPT and Claude. My default
search engine in all browsers is set to Perplexity. As I work, those
tabs are a reminder to keep using the LLMs for all kinds of daily uses
cases - to get at least a first draft of anything I’m trying to get
done. 25% of the time, this ritual is a complete waste of time. The
rest of the time, it makes my work better. Sometimes, significantly
better.

Your work mode might be different, but I urge you to please
_embrace _and _extend._

Two strategic _higher order bits_ today, then clear recommendations
for AI activation.

FOUR STEPS TO SUPER GENERAL INTELLIGENCE (SGI).

My assessment from work of subject-matter experts is that there are
four relevant markers in the humanity timeline.

HUMAN: Pre-ChatGPT.

Sure, our Google Searches were powered by pretty smart algorithms,
sure scientists were using early AI to classify images, _yada, yada._
But most of us continued our lives without access to AI. This was like
two years ago!

HUMAN + AI: Post-ChatGPT 4.0.

This is today. We have access to more LLMs than we know what to do
with. Sora and Qwen and Mistral and more. Most humans, with most
_things they do,_ can incorporate AI to help them do that job smarter,
faster, or, at the very minimum, with better starting points. If you
have not used Deep Research for complex tasks, try it – it is often
incredible. AI is not broadly Agentic yet, but on the edges it is
booking restaurants, acting as an Executive Assistant, etc.

We are still in charge. We pull in narrow AI, when we need it.

AGI + HUMAN: Artificial General Intelligence arrives.

AGI Definition: “_An artificial system with the ability to achieve
goals across a wide range of environments and tasks, exhibiting
general cognitive abilities such as reasoning, planning, learning,
natural language understanding, perception, and creativity._”

Depending on who you ask, AGI will be here in a year or in 2027.
Basically, not sci-fi. The definition above does not specify this key
addition: “_….at a level equal to or surpassing that of an average
human._” My sense is AGI will be like the much heralded driverless
cars. It will be very good and self-sufficient for 90% of the use
cases, and the last 10% will take a long time (when humans will
continue to be helpful).

In most jobs we do today, AGI will be in charge. It will pull us in,
for the _last-mile use cases._

SGI: Super General Intelligence arrives.

SGI Definition: “_Super General Intelligence refers to a form of
intelligence that vastly exceeds the cognitive performance of humans
in virtually all domains of interest — including scientific
creativity, general wisdom, social skills, and problem-solving._”

AGI: Human-level intelligence. SGI: Beyond collective human
capability.

It is my hypothesis that SGI will arrive with error-free quantum
computing being available at scale. And, it will be here sooner than
you think, see above.

The AI we have today is running off state-of-the-art
GPUs/TPUs/Inferentias. They are extremely fast, parallel machines who
check millions and billions of possibilities, one by one. A quantum
computer can hold and process all combinations AT THE SAME TIME! I
believe that is what it will take for us to birth Super General
Intelligence – something what _vastly exceeds the cognitive
performance of all humans combined_.

SGI will do all jobs that exist today (as software, as software +
hardware). Workwise, I am not exactly sure what humans will do.

EMBRACE AI. NOW.

I do know what humans should do (workwise) today: EMBRACE, EXTEND,
THRIVE WITH NARROW AI.

And, by that, I don’t mean your company provides CoPilot and
declares the organization to have embraced AI. Or, Google forces
Gemini into your Gmail, you use it once in a while – and only when
Gemini UX steals your cursor due to an errant click!

I mean, the _two tabs open, and you are constantly in there_ pushing
the limits of the usefulness with narrow AI. If you are a Software
Engineer, then you are beating the _30% of the code is written by AI_
[3] standard already true at tech companies. If you are in Finance,
turn over your corporate forecasting platforms over to AI to move
accuracy from 85% to 98%! If you are in IT, then build out air gaped
versions of the latest LMMs into your Intranet so that company
employees are not _left behind_. Etc. Etc.

[If your company is getting in the way of your AI embrace: Buy a
personal laptop, and _embrace, extend, thrive _at home. Your company
might remain year 2020 savvy and be _left behind_, but you don’t
want to. I have a little desktop at home with a little GPU for this
purpose.]

Big Recommendation: DON'T CHASE 15 SHINY OBJECTS, DON'T HAVE YOUR
ORGANIZATION DO _100 ORGANIC AI EXPERIMENTS. _BE SMARTER.

There are three clusters of AI-Reality:

*
AI Implement Now (use cases already proven).

*
AI Bleeding Edge (use cases somewhat proven).

*
AI Speculative (promising use cases, don’t rely on yet).

There is extensive data accumulated re cluster #1. It should be a part
of your daily life. Commit to them being a part of _business as usual_
part of your life by the end of this year.

If your schedule permits now, or as you head into Q4 2025, start to
explore cluster #2. These use cases will likely be _business as usual_
mid/late 2026.

Unless you are in core tech, keep an eye on cluster #3. All of those
use cases will _sashay_ into cluster #2 and then cluster #1 over time.
React accordingly.

What you don’t want to do is jump into cluster #3, or even #2, use
cases first, if #1 is not anywhere close to _business as usual_ in
your daily work. Immense pain will follow.

AI 2025 ACTION PLAN: PROVEN, BLEEDING EDGE, SPECULATIVE.

To spark your imagination, as of May 2025, let me share specific ideas
from our ecosystem that fall into each cluster. Aim to action 100% of
Proven, 25% of Bleeding Edge, and monitor the Speculative (in case one
of them jumps the shark).

The list is not meant to be exhaustive, just specific starting points.

AI IMPLEMENT NOW. [AI + Human.]

These applications are delivering demonstratable ROI and efficiency
gains. Humans and companies not utilizing these are likely to fall
behind competitors.

N1. AI-DRIVEN ADVERTISING OPTIMIZATION: Utilizing AI within platforms
like Google Ads (Performance Max) or social media ad platforms
(Advantage+) to optimize bidding strategies, targeting, and creative
elements for better ROAS.

N2. AI-ENHANCED SEO TOOLS: Using AI for advanced keyword research,
content optimization suggestions, technical SEO audits, and
understanding search intent more deeply. (CHECKOUT EVERTUNE
[4]. Note: I'm a tiny, tiny, tiny, tiny investor.)

N3. BEHAVIOR RESPONSIVE PERSONALIZED EXPERIENCES: Leveraging AI to
deliver unique experiences, product recommendations, content, to
individual users in real-time based on a multitude of data points
(context, behavior, predicted needs) across multiple touchpoints. Does
your site/app start customizing the experience starting with the third
click of a brand-new human?

N4. PREDICTIVE ANALYTICS FOR CUSTOMER BEHAVIOR: Using AI to predict
customer lifetime value (CLV), churn likelihood, and propensity to
purchase. This is crucial for targeted retention and acquisition
campaigns.

N5. BASIC CONVERSATIONAL AI: Implementing chatbots for routine
customer service inquiries, lead qualification, and 24/7 support,
freeing up human agents for complex issues.

N6. AI-POWERED CYBERSECURITY: Employing AI for real-time threat
detection, anomaly identification in network traffic, user behavior
analytics, and faster incident response.

N7. DATA ANALYSIS AND BUSINESS INTELLIGENCE: Using AI tools to analyze
large datasets faster, uncover hidden patterns, generate automated
reports (and IAbI! [5]) and actionable insights, and provide natural
language querying of data.

N8. FRAUD DETECTION: Utilizing machine learning models to identify and
prevent fraudulent transactions, account takeovers, or anomalous
activities in real-time across finance, e-commerce, etc.

AI BLEEDING EDGE. [Human + AI]

These use cases are being activated by leading companies and early
adopters, demonstrating significant potential, but may require more
specialized skills, data infrastructure, or tolerance for refinement.
Some carry the risk that they might not pan out.

BE1. GENERATIVE AI FOR CONTENT CREATION AT SCALE: Using tools like pro
versions of ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc., to generate drafts of
diverse marketing copy (emails, blog posts, ad variants, social
posts), personalized messages, and images/video concepts. Requires
human oversight and refinement.

BE2. ADVANCED CONVERSATIONAL AI FOR SALES/SUPPORT: Deploying more
sophisticated virtual assistants capable of handling complex
multi-turn conversations, providing nuanced support, and even
assisting in sales processes.

BE3. AI IN HR FOR TALENT ANALYTICS: Applying AI to predict candidate
success, identify internal skills gaps, analyze employee sentiment,
forecast attrition risk, experience _optimization_ and hyper-custom
elements that create an enriched employee. (With vigilance around
bias.)

BE4. PREDICTIVE MARKET TREND ANALYSIS: Employing AI to analyze vast
datasets (market reports, news, social trends, economic indicators) to
forecast market shifts or emerging opportunities.

BE5. AI SOFTWARE DEVELOPMENT ASSISTANTS: Utilizing tools like Cursor
or similar AI code generators for code completion, bug detection,
automated testing script generation, and code summarization /
documentation (yes!).

BE6. AI-DRIVEN SUPPLY CHAIN OPTIMIZATION: Using AI for real-time
demand forecasting, inventory optimization, logistics planning, and
identifying potential disruptions proactively.

BE7. EDGE AI: Deploying AI models directly onto devices (sensors,
cameras, machinery) for real-time processing, reduced latency, and
enhanced privacy in specific applications like manufacturing quality
control or autonomous systems.

BE8. SYNTHETIC HUMANS TO POWER INSIGHTS: We test with organic humans
(you and I), we run brand trackers with organic humans, we run
behavior studies with organic humans. Switch to using a 8 billion
synthetic humans dataset, who are _cousins _of organic humans, to do
all of those activities… For 1/100th the cost, at 100x speed, with
99% accuracy. Oh, and imagine accurately predicting nuanced emotional
reactions to marketing stimuli beyond simple sentiment analysis.

AI SPECULATIVE. [AI + ?]

These are areas where AI holds significant future promise but are
currently largely experimental, lack proven ROI at scale, face
significant technical/ethical hurdles, or require further
breakthroughs. If you have a super expert in your team, request them
to invest time in understanding these.

S1. FULLY AUTONOMOUS AI MARKETING STRATEGISTS: AI systems that can
independently develop, execute, and optimize entire marketing
strategies with minimal human intervention. [Insane to imagine no!! It
is coming.]

S2. HYPER-REALISTIC SYNTHETIC MEDIA/INFLUENCERS: Generating highly
realistic, AI-created virtual spokespeople, creative talent,
influencers for campaigns.

S3. HIGH-FIDELITY COMPETITOR STRATEGY PREDICTION: AI models that can
reliably predict specific, complex strategic moves by competitors. (If
like me you do this with Agent-Based Models, imagine 1000x more data
and a million times more intelligence and compute.)

S4. AGENTIC AI SYSTEMS: Autonomous AI agents capable of complex
reasoning, planning, and execution of multi-step business processes or
managing entire systems with minimal to zero human input.

S5. GENERALIZED AI WITHIN BUSINESS DOMAINS: AI demonstrating
human-like adaptability and problem-solving across a wide range of
unforeseen tasks within specific business functions.

S6. EMOTION AI IN CUSTOMER EXPERIENCE: Technologies that detect and
respond to customer emotions through facial expressions, voice
tonality, and text sentiment analysis. [Speculative: Imagine this,
with Brain-Computer Interfaces!]

S7. AI FOR PREDICTIVE CULTURAL TRENDSPOTTING: This has been crapshoot
for centuries. But, now perhaps a massive infusion of science to
predicting macro cultural trends before they emerge using AI synthesis
of large-scale cultural data.

S8. QUANTUM AI: (Very long-term horizon.) Applications leveraging
quantum computing for AI tasks, offering potentially exponential
speedups.

By strategically investing across these clusters – solidifying the
proven, experimenting with the bleeding edge, and monitoring the
speculative – you can effectively leverage AI for sustained
competitive advantage in my spaces of Marketing and Technology.

BOTTOM LINE.

If you are _kinda_ scared and _crazy _excited… I’ve done my job.

It is ok to be scared, worried, vigilant, as you embrace, extend and
thrive with the Proven AI activities. Find comfort in the fact that it
is _once in many generations _opportunity to contribute to the future
of humanity – and do work that will trigger your passion chords AND
result in significant bumps in your compensation.

_Carpe Diem._

-Avinash.
