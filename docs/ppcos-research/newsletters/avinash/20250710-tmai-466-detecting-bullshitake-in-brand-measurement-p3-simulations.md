TMAI #466: DETECTING BULLSHITAKE IN BRAND MEASUREMENT. – P3 |
SIMULATIONS

[ Web Version [2] ]

We started this series reflecting on this slide presented by a major
brand at a major conference organized by a major ad platform. It
strongly suggested that the major ad platform was pulverizing other ad
platforms…

My _bsdar_ went _womp, womp, womp _because double digit Brand Lifts,
for any metrics, are extremely rare (if ever true) and they showed
lifts between +20 and +26!!

The second reason for an even more urgent _womp, womp, womp, womp_ was
that the major brand reported negative lifts, between -11 to -0.5!!!

The entire audience should have raised their hands and said: _Wait,
wait, wait… WHAT!_

In TMAI #464 [3], I’d outlined a dozen reasons, clustered in four
groups, as to how you can quickly look at analysis like this and
assess if it is fishy.

These were based on my experience analyzing a few billion dollars
worth of Brand Marketing – across countries, company types, and
campaign purposes (KPIs). I want you to trust that deep experience,
but I also want you to verify it for yourself.

In TMAI #465 [4], I’d started to build a Brand Marketing _Bsdar_
Model (BMBM!) that you can use to run simulations to verify your trust
in me – and more importantly, assess if what your Agency, Ad
Platform, Consultant is presenting to you is _bullshitake_.

While Brand Marketing is incredibly complex, the BMBM attempts to get
as close to real-world simulation as possible. It does this by
leveraging nine variables outlined in detail in TMAI #465:

*
Target Audience in Population.
_What is the size of your target audience?_

*
Reach of Target on Ad Platform.
_What percent of the target audience can the ad platform reach?_

*
Campaign Reach.
_How much of your Target Audience on Ad Platform will your campaign
reach?_

*
Success KPI Baseline.
_What’s the starting level of the KPI being measured, I.E., the
control group level?_

*
% Frequency 3/U/Wk.
_During the campaign, what percentage of the audience was exposed to
your ads 3 times Per User Per Week?_

*
Campaign Budget.
_How much are you spending on your campaign?_

*
KCV: 1 Frequency.
_How many individuals are delivered a frequency of 1/U/Wk?_

*
KCV: 3+ Effectiveness.
_How many individuals who see our ad the optimal 3/U/Wk but do not
change their mind?_

*
KCV: Lost Awareness.
_What is the decay rate?_

Let’s run some simulations!

#1. THE SNAP UK MILLENNIAL CAMPAIGN SIMULATION.

In TMAI #465, you’ll recall we were working on a campaign that would
target _millennial men interested in gender fluid fashion_ on Snap UK.

Our target audience was 7.2 million. Snap was able to reach 45% of
them. Due to budget reasons, for our campaign we were able to afford
to reach 50% of that 3.2 million. The baseline of our Success KPI,
Unaided Brand Awareness, on Snap is 35%. The amount of the audience we
reached who saw our ads an optimum Frequency of 3/User/Week (not Avg
Frequency) was 10%.

Let’s punch all of this into the Brand Marketing _Bsdar_ Model
(BMBM)…

Brand impact should be reported using just two KPIs:

*
Effectiveness: Individuals Lifted= 42,444

*
Efficiency: Cost Per Individual Lifted = $1.03

These two speak to the CFO. They cleanly allow comparison across
campaigns, channels, countries, business units, and time. Hence, I
love them.

This series was started when a major brand reported an improbable
double-digit brand lifts… Let’s also look at the lift for this
campaign: 3.3%.

Not 23. Just 3. A significantly more likely, and real, possibility.

When you report to your CFO and CMO, you can abstract all the
complexity away and report:

Simple. Clear. Action inspiring.

NOTE: This view is even more effective if you are running campaigns on

Now, let’s run additional simulations to illustrate A. how insanely
hard it is to get either negative lift or double-digit lifts, and
B. understand the role each variable plays in delivering impact –
so you can focus on that in your actual brand marketing campaigns.

#2. BUYING MORE REACH’S IMPACT SIMULATION.

I’m going to switch to America, larger numbers to play with. 😊

I’m also going to show you the full view across all the metrics so
you can absorb complete learnings. But remember, you’ll only show
the above simple view to your Sr. Leaders.

_What happens if you hold all else equal, but buy different amounts of
Reach on the ad platform? _

When all else is equal, the percentage of Brand Lift is the same: 2.6%

When you buy more Reach, you are getting more _Individuals Lifted_ –
a fact that would be completely hidden if you only report % Brand
Lift.

[NOTE: When you use the BMBM, please replace “Success KPI” in
every cell above with either Unaided Brand Awareness, Consideration,
or (Purchase) Intent – whatever your campaign’s objective.]

It might feel counter-intuitive that the CPIL also stays the same in
all three scenarios. But, all you are doing with Reach is showing your
ads to more and more of your target audience.

This also explains to you why when you are in Brand Strategy meetings
with me, I come in so deeply vehemently against campaigns with a 10%
or 15% or even 25% Reach. What’s the point of lifting a tiny number
of humans? Who may some day buy something from us?

Give me 50% or give me _death!_

#3. OPTIMIZING 3/U/WK FREQUENCY’S IMPACT SIMULATION.

_What happens if you hold all else equal, but OBSESS about optimizing
the campaign to get more humans to see your ads at an optimal
Frequency of 3/User/Week?_

MAGIC!

Magic happens.

The effectiveness KPI Individuals Lifted increases from 400k to 1,050k
to 1,700k!
The efficiency KPI CPIL _decreases_ from $1.69 to $0.75 to $0.53.
Oh, you don’t get double-digit brand lifts… But, in each scenario,
you do get more % Brand Lift as well.

If you learn nothing else today, learn the irreplaceable value of an
agency that knows how to deliver _frequency shaping _on key digital
platforms. Not _frequency capping _(that’s just _let’s not suck A
LOT_). _Frequency shaping._

Also learn the value of your TikTok/Meta/WeChat/YouTube Sales Account
team who knows how to use tactics on their platform to help you
_frequency shape_. You want as little 1 and 2 as possible, as much of
3 and 4 as possible, and to cap it at 5 so you don’t get any of the
horrible 5+/User/Week – that’s creating Net Detractors for your
brand.

#4. SUCCESS KPI BASELINE’S IMPACT SIMULATION.

_What happens to likely brand lift if the current baseline of your
success KPI is lower or higher?_

You’ll recall in TMAI #464 I’d shared that, in my extensive
experience, that I was suspicious of the major brand presenting double
dight brand lifts as I hypothesized that their Awareness and
Consideration pre-campaign were very high.

If your baseline is low, currently only 20% of your target audience is
aware of you, it is possible to get a high Brand Lift. (Still, not
double digit!)

As your baseline goes up, all else being equal, it is harder and
harder to get higher brand lift because more people are aware of you,
and the ones left to be persuaded are harder to persuade. Simple.

For the same spend above, you go from lifting 910k individuals to 570k
individuals. The _challenge _of a higher baseline.

It also becomes more expensive to lift individuals, as your baseline
goes higher.

BONUS LESSON: Every ad platform will have a different percentage of
the same target audience on it AND their baseline for your success KPI
will be different! There might be 90% of W 18 – 34 on TikTok and 60%
of W 18 – 34 on Snap. Fascinatingly, the UBA for your brand for the
same W 18 – 34 on TikTok might be 30% and on Snap it might be 70%.
When choosing where to advertise to deliver profits from Brand
Marketing, take both the Reach on the Platform AND THE AUDIENCE’S
CURRENT BASELINE FOR THE CAMPAIGN’S SUCCESS KPI. Sadly, many teams
and their Agencies miss this critical variable.

#5. REACH & BASELINE’S IMPACT SIMULATION.

Let’s play out the bonus lesson. 😊

_What happens if your choice is between platforms with different
baselines? Can you pay less, yet end up ahead?_

Yes. 😊

Disney+, YouTube, Meta can each reach different sized of our target
audience.

The important bit is that the baseline in our Success KPI
(Consideration in this case) is dramatically different. 5% on Disney+,
10% on YouTube, and 30% on Meta – for the same audience.

Assuming for the sake of simplicity that the campaign reaches the same
amount of audience on each platform, 50%, you can see the dramatic
difference in Individuals Lifted and CPIL!
Nearly double the effectiveness on Disney+ compared to Meta, for a
much lower CPIL.
If I was tight on budget, I can spend the same amount as Meta on
Disney+, Reach 38% of the audience on Disney+ vs. 50% above, and still
get  400k MORE Individuals lifted on Disney+ than on Meta!

The remarkable influence of the Success KPI’s baseline.

Ask for it before you decide where to spend your Brand Advertising
budget.

#6. DOUBLE-DIGIT BRAND LIFT SIMULATION.

_Is it possible to get double-digit percentage brand lift in Unaided
Brand Awareness or Consideration or (Purchase) Intent anywhere in the
world on any channel?_

Sure, but the likelihood is low. Because…

…an unusual set of facts would have to be true for double digit to
happen.

In the scenario above, on an ad platform with Very High Reach of your
target audience, you would pay a Very High Budget to Reach a Very High
percentage of the target audience, who would have to have a VERY LOW
Baseline for your Success KPI, and your tactical execution would
deliver an astonishing, hard to achieve, Very High 3/U/Wk Frequency.

Then, it is possible. 23.8%.

That would also imply an incredible 10 million Individuals Lifted, for
an difficult to believe $0.19 CPIL.

See why the whole thing feels fake.

It almost always is.

Next time Kantar reports your campaign delivered +20 points of lift in
UBA or Netflix reports a lift of +48 points in Consideration… Open
your copy of the BMBM, punch in the facts of the campaign, it will
take you ten seconds to choose between _bullshitake _or _no
bullshitake._

THREE CAUTIONS: FOR BMBM & ALL MODELS.

1.

If you are a long-term TMAI Premium subscriber, you know that the
_impact horizon _for the three big Brand Marketing KPIs are
different. Assuming you do the targeting right in each case, it is
easier to move (Purchase) Intent, a little harder to move
Consideration, and a little more harder to move Unaided Brand
Awareness.

My BMBM does not take this into account to keep things simple (the
more complex models I build do!). That means the predictions for
Individuals Lifted and CPIL are a little more optimistic for UBA and a
little bit pessimistic for Intent.

2.

You can make models say unintelligent nonsense. Keep common sense
handy.

Ex: I changed three variables at the same time, with a hypothetical
scenario in my head. The simulation showed that as my Reach appraoched
90%, my campaigns got A LOT MORE EFFICIENT! That is impossible. I
was so past the diminishing return curves.

3.

All models are wrong; it is simply a matter of degree.

I’ve worked diligently, using all of my experience across thousands
of campaigns in a dozen countries, to build a model that _sucks the
least_. It is why those three _key campaign variables _accounting for
hidden dimensions like _decay_ are included.

Use your own meta-analysis, your thousands of campaigns, your unique
circumstances, to make the model even less wrong. And, don’t forget
to send it to me so I can learn from you!

BOTTOM LINE.

So much data gets thrown at you these days, it has become insanely
critical to have a good _bsdar_ that can detect how much _bullshitake_
you are dealing with.

Leading with suspicion/curiosity is a god-like power. Not to be
negative, but to find the little positive, the little absolutely
reliable in the tsunami of data coming at you.

This question is magic: _Can you explain the methodology you used to
collect the data_?

Over time, like I’ve done above, codify your lessons from the
pattern matching into models. That allows you to scale your wisdom to
many, many more.

_Carpe diem._
