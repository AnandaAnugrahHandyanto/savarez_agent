TMAI #408: REACH OR FREQUENCY? THERE IS A WONG ANSWER.

[ Web Version [2] ]

This might seem hard to believe:

HUNDREDS OF MILLIONS OF DOLLARS OF MEDIA ARE PLANNED INCORRECTLY EVERY
SINGLE DAY?
I'm going to back up that big assertion today (or, reply and let me
know).

A simple life lesson that'll foundationally transform the impact of
your Marketing.

_Is Reach more important or Frequency, in the context of Brand
Marketing?_
There is a right answer.

HOW DID WE GET HERE?

When it came to effective and efficient media planning, for the last
thousand years, the answer was: Reach.

The number of channels were limited. The number of activities people
engaged in was limited. Human attention was massively concentrated.

In the beginning, you took an ad on the back cover of the Gutenberg
Bible. It was, for the most part, the only book accessible to the
masses. The masses (me!) had small footprint lives, radically fewer
distractions, religion was important, the Bible was important. Buy an
ad on the back cover of the Bible… BOOM! Brand awareness!!
Profits!!!

Over the centuries, little changed. We got three channels on TV, some
regional magazines and newspapers. You buy Reach via one or two of the
most popular mediums, and you were golden. Well, maybe not golden, but
green from all the greenbacks piling up as profits.

Fast-forward to the 20th century, the number of distractions increased
a tiny bit. But, nearly all Americans watched the finale of MASH and
when you buy all that Reach and pump out some decent ads… Another
sonic boom of profits.

It is not difficult to see why this Reach-centricity became a
religion.

It is embedded in how Agencies think. It is embedded in their Media
Planning tools. It is embedded in the CMOs who hand out cash for ads
to be delivered for humanity’s salvation.

Why do you think some companies trample over each other spending their
entire budgets on Super Bowl ads?

Super Bowl = Reaching every American human!

They believe that spending $7m on the ad AND $4mil on the Creative and
fees will deliver massive incremental profits - after all _think of
all that water cooler effect from reaching every human and powering
weeks of conversation from one SB ad!!_

That's the Reach religion in action.

If you’ve ever talked to someone in your Brand team, your Agency
account teams, to one of the hundreds of research/tools providers,
they will use the phrase Reach-first, Reach-centric, Reach-max
planning. A lot.

Frequency, if you’ve heard that word in a media planning context,
has been the step-child -  mentioned only in passing, to spend on if
any money is left after buying massive Reach.

AND, THEN THERE WAS LIGHT.

Around 25 years ago, something foundational changed.

Well, two bits.

First, the Internet arrived.

With it, instant fragmentation of our attention due to the incredible
explosion of content. From learning about travel to Ulaanbaatar to how
to make the best Tiramisu to definitive proof that the Earth is flat
and the moon landings were faked… It was all at your fingertips. Oh,
and those three TV channels had become 978 channels targeting every
niche (and no one!).

Second, the iPhone arrived.

Now while seeking definitive proof on YouTube on a laptop that the
Earth was flat, the human was also on the phone having an argument
with their girlfriend, and livestreaming the entire thing on Twitch!

Too much content, for every human niche imaginable, instantly
accessible, 24x7x365. Everything... Broken into little pieces.

Channels: _Uncountably _many.

Attention: Micro-fragmented.

Effort: Even swiping right/left feels like too much work. _Why can’t
the AI do it all?!_

Implication: It is impossible to escape the Super Bowl. Even if you
don’t watch football or the game, the damn ads have pre-ads on which
companies end up spending more money than the one SB ad slot. Even if
you don’t live in America (I was in London). So… A question for
you:

_How many Super Bowl ads do you remember?_
I suspect zero for most of you.

If you pause and really think hard: Two?

What did your CMO chasing Reach get them? Super Bowl or Times Square
or the Sphere?

IT’S A FREQUENCY WORLD, YOU JUST LIVE IN IT.

I’m using Internet + Mobile Phones + Content Explosion as three
gigantic proxies to illustrate the fragmentation of attention [and the
_suckification _of ad formats, which makes everything worse].

I’m open to your argument that there were other notable drivers as
well [political polarization – cause or outcome?].

The most important outcome due to attention fragmentation:

Reach-first media planning delivers abject incompetence when it comes
to results.
Few remember any Super Bowl ad or one from Grammys or the latest
YouTube Masthead or page three of the New York freaking Times or any
one of a thousand “mass Reach” platforms. Why? Three seconds after
the ad concluded, there are thirty-five thousand things competing for
our attention.

You could have told your boss you ran the ad on the Grammys,
transferred the money to your personal Swiss bank account, and the
impact on the business would be the same. Zero.

Since even your boss has thirty-five thousand things competing for her
attention, she has no time to tune into the boring Grammys, pay
attention to every ad break to check if the ad ran!

The secret to winning in these hyper-fragmented times?

Repetition. Repetition. Repetition.
Metaphorically speaking, you need to hit the human on the head with
the ad repeatedly.

Frequency.

You need Frequency. Loads of it.

If you can afford it... Absolutely positively buy a Super Bowl ad.
[Tip: Pre-test it!]

Then, _Frequency-first media planning _means you need to run that
same ad, targeting your desired audience [or ideally, intent!] for six
weeks, at a minimum frequency you'll earn with 150 GRPs per
week! That much repetition will get you two points of Brand Lift.
Two.

Or, if your desired audience/intent does not watch TV [why did you buy
the SB?], an avg frequency of at least 3 per week for Social ads, for
six to eight weeks straight!

Effective Frequency varies by channel, as it depends on the creative
quality, the native experience [nearly all ads on TikTok suck because
they are ads, and not TikToks], and the ad format [video, display,
gifs, interstitial etc.].

Facebook advices a Frequency of between two and three per week, for
multiple weeks depending on your campaign’s goal. Unaided Awareness
is much harder to move, will require significant duration [with great
Creative]. Purchase Intent is relatively easier to move compared to
Unaided Awareness, less duration [still, weeks].

It is not the Reach that'll deliver success, it is the repetition
you'll earn with Frequency that'll deliver success.

A Super Bowl ad without an additional $25 mil to _sustain _the
_spike_? A waste of money.

Two days of YouTube mastheads ad format that reaches 6.7 billion
people, without additional Frequency to achieve _wear-in? _A waste
of money.

THE RIGHT STRATEGY SINCE 2010.

STEP ONE: Identify the optimal norms for effective Frequency [per user
per week].

You'll do this through meta-analysis across years of your data, and/or
data that your Agency has accumulated re effectiveness across all
their clients, and/or industry norms you can purchase from Research
organizations or ad platforms.

Ex: For digital video, tech industry US norms, you would need a
Frequency of 3/week, for four weeks, with 90% Viewability, and AVOC
above 60%, as a minimum to deliver a point of Brand lift.

Your brand Agency will certainly have this data for all channels known
to humankind.

STEP TWO: Identify optimal duration.

Same meta-analysis as above, pouring over successful campaigns to
identify you need x frequency/user/week for y number of weeks/months.

STEP THREE: Compute the Reach.

Start with how much it would cost you to buy effective Frequency, for
the optimal duration.

Then, ask, _how much Reach can I buy for it?_

Let’s say you can only buy a Reach of 50k people for effective
Frequency and the optimal duration. That might feel  sad because
buying just one _measly _ Super Bowl would give you a Reach of
bazillion!

Here’s the reality: You DON’T HAVE THE MONEY to buy effective
Frequency and the optimal duration for a bazillion humans.

Reminder: Buying Reach-first and running out of money before you can
buy effective Frequency and the optimal duration will deliver the same
results as you transferring the campaign budget into your personal
Swiss bank account. Zero.

So, buy the Reach of 50k at effective Frequency levels for the optimal
duration.

Deliver the two points of lift in Consideration [success KPI]!

Now, go ask for money to reach 500k humans [at effective Frequency,
optimal duration].

Smash the pre-determined target for the success KPI.

Now, go ask for money to reach 50m humans. Rinse. Repeat. Based on,
repeated success.

If your campaigns since 2010 have not delivered results, it is not
because you did not Reach enough people… It is because you did not
have enough Frequency for them to remember you exist [aka,
achieve _wear-in_].

Why do you think Progressive, Verizon, Southwest, Campbell's Soup,
Apple, are everywhere you turn your head? Loads of Frequency first,
for optimal duration, and, in their cases with FAT budgets, massive
Reach second.

Why do you think Manhattan is plastered with billboards of every
luxury brand imaginable? Frequency, Frequency, Frequency!

Why do you think I see every single ad break on Hulu stuffed with a
DirecTV ad? Ok, that’s not Frequency, they are deliberately trying
to upset me. : )

CAN YOU EXPLAIN THIS SUPER CRITICAL LIFE-CHANGING GUIDANCE WITH A
CARTOON?

Sure…

[Reach - Frequency - Seeds]

Get it?

Frequency first, then Reach.

Prove success. Ask for more money. Rinse, repeat.

ONE LAST HIGH-IMPACT TIP?

Sure.

When you plan Frequency, plan it for per week per user.

One of my painful lessons in Frequency shaping was that, especially
with digital, you plan at a campaign level and most frequency could be
delivered up front or at the back end, defeating the entire purpose of
_reminders_ [achieving _wear-in_].

In the same vein, planning at an entire audience level often meant not
realizing that 10% of the target audience was receiving 90% of the
impressions – guaranteeing they will never forget my brand’s name,
and for sure take it in hatred for the rest of their lives. And, the
90% saw nothing, so my business results were zilch.

Plan Frequency per week per user.

It won’t always work, some channels like YouTube make Frequency
shaping presently not possible – unless you manually babysit the
platform. But, planning Frequency per week per user will expose that
fact, and maybe you want to spend money on YouTube or maybe somewhere
else with the right in-flight optimization capabilities.

BOTTOM LINE.

Humans have very busy, hyper-fragmented, lives, with a million more
important things to do than pay attention to a brand’s ad.

It is not relevant whether the human is in China or South Africa or
Brazil or Canada.

We have things to do.

For advertisers who want to have a fighting chance to break through
the fog of our lives, there are only three levers to pull:

Effective Creative + Effective Frequency + Optimal Duration

= Incredible Incremental Marketing-Driven Profits.
Carpe diem!
